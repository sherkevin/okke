# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding, attention control, and layer-contrast methods—are highly competitive and legitimate heuristics. However, they leave open the question of whether injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment can explicitly improve physical object grounding while mathematically preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, an evaluation framework and decode-time intervention protocol that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA operates on three structural commitments. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces global context with candidate-wise support. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** stabilizes spatial evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** deterministically protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via automated WordNet-based root-token lookup and BPE continuation inheritance. To enforce fair scientific boundaries, we separate a strict zero-shot probe (`TLRA_zero`) from a lightweight, base-model-frozen calibrated variant (`TLRA_calib`). 

Recognizing the empirical cliff of post-hoc intervention, we organize this paper strictly around a pre-registered experimental contract. The protocol locks all hyperparameters—including the critical spatial ratio $\rho$—on a single validation set, prohibiting per-dataset tuning. We introduce three explicit evidence chains: hallucination reduction (audited by Average Generation Length to reject truncation artifacts), structure preservation (using OCR tasks strictly as mathematical negative controls), and local-evidence parity (stratified by physical objects seen vs. unseen by the calibrator). All claims regarding spatial routing must survive a mandatory parity ablation against both `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` using the exact same calibrator weights. By auditing GPU memory-bandwidth latency (TPOT) and relegating spatio-temporal video to an exploratory appendix pilot, this paper serves as a rigorous, falsifiable protocol for evaluating decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable reasoning capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding remains vulnerable to hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and plausible but weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is acutely problematic in the grounding of visual physical entities—such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting—where the necessary visual evidence is highly localized in the patch space.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances in the field and serve as our primary legitimate baselines. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) contrast different input conditions to reshape distributions; attention-based control methods (`OPERA`) penalize abnormal generation patterns; and layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit differences between shallow and deep representations. These are highly competitive global decoding heuristics. However, they leave a specific, complementary proposition untested: 

**Can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment to outperform global heuristics, without destroying language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce physical entity hallucination more faithfully than purely global or language-side heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit, automated structure-preserving safeguards. 

Executing this intervention is mechanically and systemically non-trivial due to four explicit bottlenecks:
1. **Embedding Asymmetry:** Visual patch states and output logits do not naturally align in base models.
2. **Candidate Bound & Memory Bandwidth:** Post-hoc intervention can only reweight candidates the base model keeps alive in its top-$M$ predictions. Dynamically retrieving local spatial support for $M$ candidates per decoding step incurs a severe memory-bandwidth bottleneck.
3. **Structural Collapse:** Naive token-level suppression easily destroys function words, syntax, and complex entities.
4. **Modality Mismatch:** Spatial routing ($H \times W$ patches) does not natively translate to dense document text (OCR) or Spatio-Temporal tokens (video).

To address these, TLRA is built upon three specific design commitments: **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** (governed by a locked spatial ratio $\rho$), and **Automated VASM**, which mathematically protects linguistic structure via WordNet masking and BPE inheritance.

To enforce scientific rigor, TLRA establishes strict boundary conditions. First, `TLRA_zero` serves strictly as a **viability probe**; if native architectural alignment is insufficient, the paper contracts to `TLRA_calib`, transparently defined as a base-model-frozen plug-in. Second, the framework actively purges any claims of dense document understanding: because VASM algorithmically isolates physical entities and mathematically bypasses text-in-image tokens, document/OCR tasks are designated strictly as safety-oriented *negative controls*. Third, all temperature, scaling, and spatial variables ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M, \rho$) are permanently locked based on a single validation split.

Ultimately, the survival of the TLRA spatial hypothesis rests on passing a strict **parity ablation**. Any claim regarding "local evidence" must statistically outperform both a global pool (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and a random spatial subset (`TLRA_RandomK`) using the *exact same* calibrator weights, under an explicit audit separating physical objects seen vs. unseen during calibration. If spatial routing fails this unseen test, the paper's core claim mathematically devolves to a practical `MeanPool + VASM` baseline.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary and the Calibrator Blackbox: `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib`

Acknowledging that visual and lexical states often do not natively align, we explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks to prevent "calibrator blackbox" ambiguity:

1. **`TLRA_zero`**: A strict decode-time probe where the base model's native language modeling head is applied directly to contextualized final-layer visual states to derive lexical support.
2. **`TLRA_calib`**: A lightweight calibrated variant utilizing a 2-layer MLP projection, $\Phi_{calib}$, to bridge the embedding asymmetry. The base MLLM parameters remain strictly frozen.

**Transparency of $\Phi_{calib}$:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained exclusively to map final-layer visual patch representations to the base model's lexical embedding space via an InfoNCE contrastive loss. We train it on a pre-defined 50k subset of conceptual captions containing distinct physical objects. Crucially, any routing superiority claimed by `TLRA_calib` must be validated against `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` using the exact same frozen $\Phi_{calib}$ weights, paired with a strict "Category Leakage Audit" separating objects seen vs. unseen in this exact 50k subset.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set derived from the original logits: $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ is a strictly locked candidate window (e.g., $M=50$). We explicitly acknowledge that extracting patch-level support for $M$ candidates per step introduces a severe memory-bandwidth bottleneck, which we audit in Section 4.5.

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the contextualized final-layer visual states, with visual token volume $N_v = H \times W$. For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support via an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule. To prevent low-resolution inputs from degenerating into global pooling, $k$ is determined by a globally locked spatial ratio $\rho$:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big),
$$
followed by the raw support computation:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a logits intervention without destroying the base model's native softmax dynamics, TLRA computes a relative evidence score over the bounded candidate set:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
To prevent out-of-bounds suppression, TLRA applies a dynamic scaling penalty anchored strictly to the candidate window's logit range ($\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t) - \min(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t)$):
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \Delta_L \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Automated VASM

Naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses critical syntax alongside hallucinations. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect structure, where $\gamma(c) = 1$ for visually groundable physical entities, and $\gamma(c) = 0$ for all other tokens.

VASM is generated algorithmically, not via manual heuristics, operating in two stages:
1. **Automated Root-Token Lookup:** The vocabulary is processed via WordNet synsets (filtered exclusively for physical objects/nouns) and automated POS tagging. This mathematically isolates valid entity root tokens and rejects function words, verbs, and abstract syntax.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Because modern tokenizers fragment words arbitrarily, any subword continuation token deterministicly inherits the mask value ($\gamma$) of its preceding root token.

**The OCR Concession and BPE Fragility:** VASM's WordNet pipeline explicitly excludes arbitrary text strings. We explicitly concede that TLRA is mathematically blind to text-in-image tokens; tasks like `DocVQA` are purely negative controls. Furthermore, VASM exhibits linguistic fragility under certain BPE edge cases: if a physical entity word is hyphenated or fragmented such that the initial BPE token is not recognized as a valid WordNet root, the continuation inheritance fails. We document this as a known boundary condition.

### 3.5 Methodological Boundaries and The Fallback Clause

TLRA's claims are governed by explicit methodological boundaries:
- **The Fallback Clause:** If `AdaptiveTopK` fails to statistically outperform `MeanPool` on *unseen* objects (Section 4.4), the spatial routing claim is voided as a data-leakage artifact, and the method formally devolves to a `MeanPool + VASM` baseline.
- **Out-of-Candidate Limit:** Post-hoc reweighting cannot recover a correct entity token if the base model has already ejected it from the initial Top-$M$ set.
- **Single-Configuration Lock:** All critical parameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M, \rho$) are permanently locked after validation; per-dataset tuning is strictly prohibited.
- **Modality Limits:** TLRA is optimized strictly for spatial ($H \times W$) reasoning. Video (Spatio-Temporal) applications are mechanically unsupported by the core formulation and relegated to an exploratory appendix.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because this paper is structured as a rigorous falsifiable proposal, the experimental methodology is organized into a sanity pilot, three mandatory evidence chains, and strict boundary audits. **Crucially, all hyperparameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M, \rho$) are locked uniformly across all tasks based on a single held-out validation set.**

### 4.1 Stage 0: Zero-Shot Semantic Validity Pilot

**Goal:** Determine whether `TLRA_zero` is a viable independent branch or mere noise.
- **Protocol:** Measure Top-1/5/10 overlap between patch readout words and annotated object labels on a 1k held-out image subset.
- **Decision Rule:** If overlap is near-random, the zero-shot claim is abandoned. The paper explicitly contracts to position `TLRA_calib` as the sole operational method.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Truncation Audit

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, and strictly verify that this reduction is not an artifact of output truncation or calibrator memorization.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 1):** 
  - *Metrics:* POPE (Accuracy, F1), CHAIR (CHAIRi, CHAIRs), **Average Generation Length (AGL)**. (AGL is reported as a hard numerical column adjacent to F1).
  - *Rows:* Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Validation Criteria:** If `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` reduces AGL by >10% compared to the Base model, the hallucination reduction claim must be downgraded and discussed, as the model is artificially inflating precision by remaining silent. 

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure Preservation and Negative Controls

**Goal:** Verify that VASM mathematically protects general multimodal understanding and that the OCR concession operates correctly as a negative control.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 2):**
  - *Metrics:* MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard), **DocVQA**.
  - *Rows:* Base, `TLRA_full` (with VASM), `TLRA_no_VASM`.
- **Validation Criteria:** 
  1. `TLRA_no_VASM` must demonstrate a catastrophic performance drop on MMMU, proving VASM's absolute necessity.
  2. `DocVQA` performance must remain strictly flat between Base and `TLRA_full`, proving VASM safely bypasses out-of-scope textual entities without damage. Any claim of "improving OCR" is explicitly prohibited.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence Value and Category Leakage

**Goal:** Isolate and prove that spatial locality directly drives performance, rather than global calibration priors or randomized visual subset regularization.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 3):**
  - *Metrics:* FREAK (Spatial Relations, Object Existence, Counting). The dataset is strictly partitioned into **"Objects Seen in $\Phi_{calib}$"** vs. **"Objects Unseen in $\Phi_{calib}$"**.
  - *Rows:* Base, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK` (Mandatory), `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
  - *Supplementary:* A dedicated table detailing exactly how the 50k calibration captions were filtered to guarantee zero overlap with the Unseen test split.
- **Validation Criteria:** All three TLRA variants must use the *exact same* $\Phi_{calib}$ weights. `AdaptiveTopK` must statistically outperform both `MeanPool` and `RandomK` on *both* splits. If it only wins on the "Seen" split, the paper invokes the **Fallback Clause**: the spatial routing hypothesis is invalidated, and the contribution is reframed entirely as `MeanPool + VASM`.

### 4.5 Efficiency Pareto and Failure Audits

To audit systemic memory-bandwidth bottlenecks, a mandatory **Systemic Cost Pareto (Figure 1)** will plot actual measured Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT in milliseconds on a standardized A100 GPU, X-axis) against POPE F1 (Y-axis), tracing the latency penalty of TLRA across $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$ alongside baselines.

Furthermore, we document two explicit failure mode visualizations:
1. **Out-of-Candidate Bound:** A line graph plotting the raw logit rank of the correct entity token over sequence steps leading up to a hallucination, proving mathematically exactly when the token fell below rank $M=50$ and became unrecoverable.
2. **BPE Suffix-Collapse:** An instance where tokenizer fragmentation (e.g., hyphenation) defies WordNet linguistic boundaries, causing VASM inheritance to fail.

### 4.6 Bounded Secondary Video Pilot (Appendix)

Because TLRA is fundamentally optimized for $H \times W$ spatial routing, adapting it to Spatio-Temporal tokens lacks a dedicated temporal resonance theory. Therefore, an exploratory video pilot (e.g., `VIDHALLUC`) is executed strictly in the appendix to assess preliminary transferability without contaminating the core spatial claims.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest iteration of TLRA is the one bound by the cleanest scientific contract. By algorithmically generating VASM, strictly separating zero-shot probes from calibrated adjustments, enforcing exact-calibrator parity against global baselines, and treating OCR tasks exclusively as negative controls, this benchmark prioritizes methodological transparency over unconditional SOTA claims. 

The integrity of this method is bounded by several specific limitations:
1. **Calibrator Category Leakage:** External priors injected by $\Phi_{calib}$ present an ongoing risk. The mandatory Seen vs. Unseen audit and `TLRA_RandomK` ablation are the sole defenses ensuring gains stem from spatial routing, not data leakage.
2. **Hyperparameter Fragility:** The method relies on bounded parameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M, \rho$). If these are not strictly locked across datasets, the intervention collapses into an overfitted heuristic.
3. **Out-of-Candidate Bounds:** Decode-time reweighting cannot recover a true entity token that the base model has already discarded from its Top-$M$ candidate pool.
4. **Systemic Latency & Memory Bandwidth:** The adaptive Top-$k$ mechanism requires retrieving local support for $M$ candidates dynamically per token. This incurs a heavy memory-bandwidth bottleneck, translating to significant Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) overhead.
5. **BPE Linguistic Fragility:** WordNet-to-BPE inheritance fails when words are hyphenated or fragmented such that the initial subword is not a valid recognized root.
6. **Modality Transferability:** The core mechanism is explicitly spatial. Its application to dense document OCR is mathematically bypassed, and its extension to spatio-temporal video settings is mechanically unsound without further theoretical development, rendering it strictly an appendix exploration.

## 6. Revised Claims and Contract Execution

Pending empirical execution of the locked protocol, this paper restricts its claims strictly to:
- A structure-preserving masking mechanism (VASM) utilizing automated WordNet extraction and BPE inheritance.
- A controlled evaluation isolating token-local routing gains from global priors, protected by the Fallback Clause to `MeanPool + VASM`.
- The establishment of explicit boundary conditions regarding GPU latency (TPOT), out-of-candidate failures, and text-in-image bypass.

We explicitly do not claim architecture-invariant zero-shot success, dense document-grounding competence, or unified image-video superiority.
