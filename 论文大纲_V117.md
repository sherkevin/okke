# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Bounding Entity Verification via Shallow Late-Fusion Routing

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from entity hallucination when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during autoregressive decoding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations elegantly modulate general model confidence, they lack explicit, supervised spatial-to-lexical mapping. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a supervised late-fusion parametric module that projects post-connector visual features directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation, we explicitly restrict our scope to **visual entity existence verification** over an offline-curated physical noun dictionary ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). TLRA operates on three structural principles: (1) **Shallow Semantic Bypass**: We explicitly confront the representation depth gap by anchoring our lightweight Visual Alignment Adapter to the LLM's frozen Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$), acting as a direct layer-0 to layer-$N$ residual verification pathway, while ablating against input-space ($W_{in}$) anchoring; (2) **Dynamic Activation Pooling** to resolve spatial dilution; and (3) **Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM)** via an Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$), mathematically guaranteeing that hallucination suppression does not force irreversible grammatical collapse. To resolve BPE fragmentation across modern byte-level tokenizers (e.g., Tiktoken), we introduce **Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking**, isolating penalties to specific noun sequences without degrading homophonic prefixes. 

Crucially, this precise formulation forces us to confront and bound fundamental algorithmic fragilities. We pre-register a rigorous protocol to audit system-level vulnerabilities, including: the **Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) Shift**; the **Dataset Prior Trap** (verifying via a `TLRA_Blind` baseline that the adapter maps spatial features, not just Visual Genome class priors); the brittle **Negation Fallback Constraint**; and the **Batched Inference Latency Trap**. By separating Time-to-First-Token (TTFT) from Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) and tracking latency jitter (variance) across varying batch sizes, we benchmark TLRA against prompt-injected object detection to define the strict operational limits of decode-time visual routing.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to entity hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD) are powerful training-free methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or distribution shifts. However, as purely zero-shot interventions, they lack explicit, supervised spatial-to-lexical mapping pathways trained specifically to verify spatial presence at the token level. 

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is a late-fusion parametric routing module requiring targeted supervision. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit existence-checking supervision. 

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly downgrade our claim from universal "hallucination suppression" to **Entity Existence Verification**. Algorithmically, TLRA acts as an existence checker for an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). This precise formulation forces us to confront five hard theoretical boundaries:

First, **The Representation Depth Gap and Dataset Prior Memorization**: Extracting visual features post-connector (layer 0) and projecting them directly into the output logit space (layer $N$ $W_{out}$) bypasses the entire reasoning depth of the transformer. We hypothesize this acts as a "Shallow Semantic Bypass" sufficient for physical existence checking, but we must theoretically and empirically justify it against an input-embedding ($W_{in}$) anchor. Furthermore, we must prove via a `TLRA_Blind` baseline that the adapter actually utilizes spatial evidence rather than merely memorizing the long-tailed class distribution of its training dataset (Visual Genome).
Second, **The Batched Inference Latency & Jitter Trap**: Comparing late-fusion intervention against Prompt-Injected Object Detection requires strict latency disaggregation. If TLRA's Deterministic Trie Masking introduces severe TPOT degradation and branch-divergent jitter during batched inference (e.g., batch size 32) due to CPU-GPU synchronization, its architectural complexity is practically unjustified.
Third, **The Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) Synonym Shift**: Because TLRA penalizes only a finite $V_{noun}$ dictionary, it inherently biases the LLM’s hallucination distribution. We must prove the base LLM does not simply bypass the penalty by dynamically shifting to hallucinate obscure OOV synonyms (e.g., substituting the penalized "sofa" with an unpenalized "chesterfield").
Fourth, **The Negation Boundary**: A context-blind visual checker will disastrously penalize correct negative constraints (e.g., "The room is empty of..."). We explicitly relegate negation handling to a brittle **Fallback Constraint**, acknowledging it as a fundamental structural limit of late-fusion verification that will systematically fail on complex compositional negations.
Fifth, **The BPE Collateral Damage Fallacy**: Naive subword suppression risks morphological destruction. Penalizing the subword `_re` because "refrigerator" is missing visually arbitrarily destroys non-noun vocabulary like `_really` or `_return`.

To resolve these threats, TLRA introduces **Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking** to safely route byte-level BPE probabilities without collateral damage, and **Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM)** to gracefully concede a hallucination rather than forcing grammatical collapse. We prevent manifold drift by anchoring the projection to the LLM's frozen matrices, and replace static Top-$k$ pooling with **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**.

Our evaluation is a falsifiable protocol organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction, OOV Shifts, and the Negation Trap (POPE / CHAIR):** Evaluating entity grounding against zero-shot methods and `TLRA_Blind`, tracking the OOV hallucination rate, and explicitly auditing True Negative Accuracy and False Positive Negation Rates.
2. **Structure, Reasoning, and Hyperparameter Stability (MMBench / MME / DocVQA):** Auditing BPE collateral damage and the structural protection of VASM (`TLRA_no_VASM` vs Base).
3. **Local Evidence Value and Batched System Profiling (FREAK / System Latency):** Comparing Dynamic Pooling against spatial ablations, evaluating $W_{in}$ vs $W_{out}$ geometric anchors, and rigorously benchmarking TTFT, TPOT, and latency jitter across batched generation scales.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Connector Locus and the Representation Depth Gap

TLRA operates as a supervised parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage, strictly limited to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). 

**The Depth Gap and Shallow Semantic Bypass:** To map visual features directly into the LLM's vocabulary space, the spatial features $X_v$ are extracted **post-vision-language connector** (e.g., after the LLaVA MLP projector). Geometrically, these features reside in the LLM's input embedding space ($W_{in}$) at layer 0. However, TLRA anchors its projection to the LLM's highly contextualized **Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)** at layer $N$. 
We explicitly acknowledge this layer-0 to layer-$N$ translation bypasses the transformer's deep reasoning layers. We justify this as a **Shallow Semantic Bypass**: because entity existence is a fundamental, non-compositional visual primitive, layer-0 post-connector features retain sufficient raw semantic density to act as an independent logit-space verification signal. To rigorously test this, our protocol includes an architectural ablation, `TLRA_Win`, which anchors the adapter to $W_{in}$ instead of $W_{out}$.

**Manifold Preservation and The Dataset Prior Trap:** To enable zero-shot generalization, the target anchor matrix $W_{anchor} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ (sliced from $W_{out}$) is **strictly frozen**. We train only a lightweight Visual Alignment Adapter $f_{adapt}(X_v)$ on a 50k Visual Genome subset. 
A critical vulnerability is that $f_{adapt}$ may simply memorize the long-tailed prior of the VG dataset (e.g., unconditionally boosting "person" and penalizing "zebra") rather than utilizing actual token-local spatial resonance. We introduce a mandatory **`TLRA_Blind`** baseline—where $X_v$ is replaced by an average dataset embedding during inference—to definitively prove that hallucination reduction stems from dynamic spatial grounding, not static dataset prior injection.

### 3.2 Contrastive Calibration and Dynamic Activation Pooling

To calibrate $f_{adapt}$, we employ a **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** dynamically sampling hard negatives constrained by a **Semantic Margin $\phi_{sim}$** (via frozen CLIP text-text similarity) to prevent penalizing visual synonyms.

Visual patches are not bag-of-words. Static Top-$k$ pooling dilutes the existence signal of small objects. We utilize **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**. At decoding step $t$, for a candidate noun $c$, we identify the active spatial patches $\mathcal{A}_c = \{j \mid (f_{adapt}(X_{v, j}) \cdot W_{anchor})_c > \theta_{pool}\}$. The spatial support is:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{A}_c|} \sum_{j \in \mathcal{A}_c} (f_{adapt}(X_{v, j}) \cdot W_{anchor})_c \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
If $\mathcal{A}_c$ is empty, $S_{raw}(c) = 0$. The scaling factor $\beta$ is derived from the adapter's training distribution variance. (Its stability across highly divergent visual domains, such as medical vs. natural images, remains a pre-registered limitation). `TLRA_static_k` serves as a geometric baseline to prove this dynamic $\mathcal{A}_c$ outperforms fixed top-k patch selection.

### 3.3 Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking (Addressing Byte-Level BPE)

Modern tokenizers (e.g., Tiktoken used in Llama-3) shatter words irregularly, often heavily dependent on leading spaces (e.g., `_dog` vs `dog`). The naive approach of penalizing a subword prefix (e.g., `_re` for `_refrigerator`) causes unacceptable BPE collateral damage by suppressing homophonic non-nouns (e.g., `_really`). 

We replace probability-based lookahead with **Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking**. We construct a deterministic character-to-token Trie of $V_{noun}$, explicitly accounting for leading byte-level space encodings. At step $t$, TLRA only penalizes a subword logit if that subword deterministically routes *exclusively* to nodes within $V_{noun}$ lacking visual support $\hat S(c)$. If a subword branch contains *any* valid non-noun continuation, the penalty is deferred to the next token step. 

*The Hardware Reality:* While mathematically sound, dynamically traversing a Trie and applying token-specific, branch-divergent logit masks across a batched dimension inherently conflicts with standard GPU tensor parallelism. This risks catastrophic Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) jitter, which we explicitly audit in Section 4.3.

### 3.4 Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM) and The Fallback Constraint

**The $\tau$-Abort Syntax Floor:** Let $L_{floor}$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the top-$K$ window. Forcing the argmax to fall to $L_{floor}$ is fatal if $L_{floor}$ represents a grammatically invalid token (e.g., a comma). The **Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM)** module enforces an Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$):
$$
L_{final}(c) = 
\begin{cases} 
L_{orig}(c) & \text{if } (L_{orig}(c) - L_{floor}) > \tau \\
\max \Big( L_{floor} - \epsilon, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big) & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
If the entropy gap exceeds $\tau$, VASM aborts the penalty, conceding to the language prior to prevent irreversible structural collapse.

**The Lexical Negation Fallback Constraint:** A context-blind existence checker fundamentally disrupts accurate negative constraints (e.g., "The image lacks a dog"). We explicitly abandon attempting to "solve" negation via late-fusion, and instead implement a brittle **Lexical Fallback Constraint**: if an explicit negation token ("no", "not", "without") appears in the immediate local window $Y_{t-\delta:t}$, the penalty $\gamma(c)$ is forced to 0. We acknowledge this regular-expression-style heuristic is blind to compositionality. It will systematically fail on distant negations and trigger **False Positive Negations** (e.g., "Not only is there a dog..."), establishing a strict structural boundary for this architecture.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict structural, geometric, and hardware boundaries. By reframing TLRA as a geometrically grounded entity existence verifier, we identify mathematically rigid limitations that define the method's frontier:

1. **The Representation Depth Gap:** Our reliance on a "Shallow Semantic Bypass" (projecting layer-0 visual features into layer-$N$ unembedding weights) fundamentally assumes that existence checking is a non-compositional visual primitive. While `TLRA_Blind` and `TLRA_W_in` ablations are designed to validate this, it remains an architectural shortcut that bypasses deep transformer reasoning, permanently limiting the module's ability to verify complex object states or relations.
2. **The Hardware Reality of Trie Masking:** While Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking successfully prevents collateral damage to byte-level BPE subwords, dynamically updating per-sequence subword masks with branch-divergent logic across a batched dimension inherently conflicts with standard GPU tensor parallelism. If TPOT and Jitter at batch size 32 (evaluated in Chain C) degrade significantly, decode-time subword routing may be fundamentally unscalable for high-throughput production serving.
3. **The Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) Synonym Shift:** Because TLRA bounds its verification strictly to $V_{noun} \approx 4,500$, it acts as an asymmetric penalty surface. The base LLM's autoregressive nature may learn to bypass spatial constraints by dynamically shifting its hallucination distribution toward obscure, unpenalized OOV synonyms, rendering the targeted dictionary an incomplete defense.
4. **The Brittleness of the Negation Fallback Constraint:** We explicitly concede that a context-blind existence checker cannot natively solve linguistic negative constraints. The Lexical Fallback Constraint is a brittle heuristic that introduces measurable False Positive Negations and systematically fails on implicit or compositional phrasing, defining a strict parsing boundary for late-fusion verification.
5. **The Grammatical Trade-off (VASM):** The Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance threshold formally acknowledges that late-fusion logit manipulation cannot always elegantly override the language prior. If the sequence entropy is too low, the system must surrender and allow a hallucination to pass to prevent catastrophic grammatical destruction. The operational stability of the model therefore rests heavily on the resilience of the $\tau$ hyperparameter.

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head can safely navigate these morphological fragilities and latency bottlenecks without collapsing under standard deployment conditions.
