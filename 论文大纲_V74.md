# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa)—are highly competitive baselines for modulating global generation trajectories, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework specifically designed for physical entity grounding in natural scenes. TLRA reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states via a base-model-frozen calibrator.

To execute this safely and efficiently, TLRA operates on three operational commitments. First, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and abstract reasoning via a deterministic WordNet whitelist. We explicitly accept an "OCR Concession"—trading off intervention capability on arbitrary text to guarantee a 0% structural degradation on document-reading tasks. Second, we manage modern BPE fragmentation through a **Tokenizer-Aware Subword Boundary Heuristic**, intervening only on valid prefixes while relying on the base LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete suffixes, tracked via an Exact Semantic Match metric. Third, **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance** with relaxed candidate bounding explores the Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) Pareto frontier, supported by a universally fixed, prompt-agnostic normalized entropy fallback to catch prefill pruning blindspots.

We organize our validation blueprint around a strict experimental contract consisting of three evidence chains: Hallucination Reduction (mandating a rigorous Category Leakage Audit of seen vs. unseen entities), Structure Preservation (quantifying suffix survival via Exact Match BPE-CSR), and Local Evidence Value (isolating our Top-$k$ routing against global pooling baselines like `TLRA_MeanPool` across diverse prompt phrasings). This protocol offers a positive, mathematically falsifiable proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and attribute binding, where the required evidence is distinctly local.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are legitimate, highly competitive baselines that effectively modulate global generation trajectories. However, they do not directly answer the following positive hypothesis:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment for physical entities without damaging language structure, corrupting subword KV-cache momentum, or degrading downstream reasoning ability?**

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to embedding asymmetry, candidate limitation at the decoding head, the structural collapse risk of penalizing functional syntax, and the prohibitive system cost of computing patch-level support at high resolutions ($N_v > 4096$). 

We explicitly restrict the scope of our motivation: TLRA is designed exclusively for grounding physical entities in natural scenes. It actively trades off intervention capability on text-rich images (OCR) and abstract reasoning to ensure absolute grammatical safety. TLRA centers on three design commitments:

1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention via a Base-Model-Frozen Plug-in.** We bridge embedding asymmetry using a lightweight calibrator (`TLRA_calib`), contrasting against the near-random baseline of raw visual embeddings (`TLRA_zero`). While the base MLLM remains 100% frozen, TLRA introduces generic external distribution data. Through strict parity ablations against global pooling (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and random selection (`TLRA_RandomK`) using the *exact same calibrator*, we mathematically isolate the value of token-local decoding from this external prior.
2. **Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking).** Built on a strict WordNet whitelist, VASM deterministically bypasses intervention for structural and abstract tokens. We formalize two critical operational concessions: the *OCR paradox* (VASM natively ignores arbitrary text strings, serving as a zero-trigger negative control on document tasks) and *Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum*. VASM triggers on valid subword prefixes but relies on the base LLM to complete suffixes, a dynamic we rigorously audit via a formalized Exact Semantic Match BPE Completion Success Rate (BPE-CSR) to monitor suffix collapse risks.
3. **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance with Normalized Entropy Fallback.** To control latency, TLRA evaluates only the Top-$M$ logits. To counter the "prefill pruning blindspot"—where visually grounded objects absent from the prompt emerge late in open-ended generation—we incorporate a dynamic fallback mechanism. To prevent this fallback from hypersensitivity to varied prompt structures, we implement a prompt-agnostic normalized attention entropy metric with a universally fixed threshold $\theta_{fallback}$ that applies zero-shot across all evaluated datasets.

We commit to a definitive evaluation protocol mapped to 2D image-centric benchmarks (relegating temporal video extensions strictly to exploratory appendices, as our current calibrator lacks frame-specific temporal priors). We establish three distinct chains of evidence: Hallucination Reduction, Structure Preservation (proving VASM's safety via an OCR negative control), and Local-Evidence Validation (proving Top-$k$ anchoring statistically outperforms global pooling).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. A strict zero-shot readout (**`TLRA_zero`**) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yields near-random overlap due to massive embedding asymmetry, establishing the absolute floor of our intervention capability. 

Therefore, our intervention relies on **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain fair comparisons, the calibration protocol is strictly constrained:
1. **Plug-in Objective & Precomputation Cost:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{|V| \times D_v}$, mapping the MLLM's visual tokens $v_j \in \mathbb{R}^{D_v}$ into the LM head's pre-softmax vocabulary space ($|V|$ is the vocabulary size). **Crucially, during training, only $\Phi_{calib}$ is updated; the base MLLM remains 100% frozen.**
2. **Pseudo-Labeling Pipeline:** Pseudo-labels are generated using a deterministic extraction pipeline: GroundingDINO extracts bounding boxes from a generic subset of CC3M/Visual Genome; spaCy extracts noun chunks from the corresponding captions. The target distribution for $\Phi_{calib}$ is created by computing the cosine similarity between the CLIP text embedding of the noun chunk and the individual tokens in the LLM vocabulary.
3. **The Category Leakage Constraint:** We acknowledge that achieving "zero domain overlap" between calibrator data (CC3M/VG) and evaluation datasets (POPE/CHAIR on COCO) is a mathematical fiction in modern multimodal training. Instead, we mandate a strict **Category Leakage Audit**: all downstream evaluations must be stratified into entities *Seen* versus *Unseen* in the calibrator's pseudo-labels to quantify the exact extent of external prior memorization.

### 3.2 High-Resolution Efficiency: Bounding and Normalized Fallback

At decoding step $t$, evaluating the entire vocabulary against $>4096$ visual patches fundamentally ruins Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT). 

**1. Relaxed Candidate Bounding:** We restrict intervention strictly to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$. We actively relax bounds across $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$ to formalize the system overhead Pareto frontier.

**2. Prompt-Conditioned Pruning with Normalized Entropy Fallback:** We aggregate cross-attention from prefill prompt tokens to the visual sequence, pruning low-attention patches to an active subset $N_{active}$. To address the **Prefill Pruning Blindspot** (where unprompted objects emerge late), we employ **Prompt-Agnostic Normalized Entropy**:
$$
\hat{H}_{attn} = \frac{-\sum_{j \in N_{active}} p_j \log p_j}{\log(N_{active})} \in [0, 1]
$$
If $\hat{H}_{attn} > \theta_{fallback}$, attention is deemed too uniform, and pruning is bypassed ($N_{active} = N_v$). Crucially, $\theta_{fallback}$ is fixed universally across all datasets to prove the zero-shot validity of the normalization.

**3. Formalizing Token-Local Support:** For candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ with vocabulary index $idx(c)$, the projected visual logit vector for patch $v_j$ is $\hat{z}_j = \Phi_{calib}(v_j) \in \mathbb{R}^{|V|}$. The similarity score is the extracted scalar logit at that index, scaled by temperature $\tau_{sim}$:
$$
sim(\Phi_{calib}(v_j), c) = \frac{\hat{z}_{j, idx(c)}}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
TLRA computes local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule over the active patches:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_{active} \rceil\Big), \quad S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} sim(\Phi_{calib}(v_j), c).
$$

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw local support into a stable relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted as:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses functional syntax. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** via a binary mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0, 1\}$.

1. **Deterministic Whitelist:** $\gamma=1$ *if and only if* the token string maps to a lemma under structural WordNet synsets (e.g., `physical_entity.n.01`).
2. **Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum:** Modern tokenizers fragment words. VASM triggers $\gamma=1$ only on the valid initial prefix, relying on the base LLM's unmodified autoregressive momentum to complete suffix tokens ($\gamma=0$). This intentionally incurs a **Suffix Collapse Risk**—if the prefix intervention forces an uncalibrated path, the subsequent hidden states may shatter into repetition. We discard loose string matching and mandate an *Exact Semantic Match* tracking protocol to quantify this specific failure mode.
3. **The OCR Concession:** VASM explicitly defaults to $\gamma=0$ for out-of-vocabulary text, bypassing arbitrary OCR strings to prioritize grammatical safety over text-hallucination mitigation.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The scientific validity of TLRA hinges entirely on the explicit verification of three interlocking evidence chains, designed to isolate local evidence from external priors, track latency overhead, and ensure structural safety. The following sections outline our strict evaluation plan.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length & Leakage Checks)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces physical object hallucination, that the gain is not an artifact of shorter responses, and that it stems from decode-time routing rather than calibrator memorization.
- **Table 1 Planned Targets:** `POPE` and `CHAIR` metrics. Columns explicitly report: Model, Method, F1/Accuracy, Average Generated Length (AGL), AGL StdDev, and VASM Trigger Rate (%).
- **Baselines:** `Base`, `VCD`, `DoLa`, `OPERA`, **`TLRA_zero`**, **`TLRA_MeanPool`**, **`TLRA_RandomK`**, and **`TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`**.
- **The Asterisk Rule:** Any configuration achieving SOTA CHAIR scores by collapsing AGL variance by $> 5\%$ compared to the base model will be flagged (*) as an invalid artifact.
- **The Category Leakage Audit:** We strictly mandate a stratification of POPE F1 and CHAIR scores into `Entities Seen in Calibrator Pseudo-labels` vs. `Entities Unseen in Calibrator Pseudo-labels`. 
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will yield lower `CHAIR` and higher `POPE` F1 than all baselines, maintaining consistent performance across both `Seen` and `Unseen` entities without collapsing AGL.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The Safety Audit)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not degrade general cognition, explicitly validate the "OCR Concession" via a negative control, and quantify BPE suffix momentum survival.
- **Table 2 Planned Targets:** `MMBench`, `MMMU` (Hard), Language Perplexity (PPL), `DocVQA`, and `BPE-CSR`.
- **The Strict Negative Control:** Because VASM explicitly trades off OCR intervention for structural safety, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must demonstrate a `VASM Trigger Rate` statistically indistinguishable from $0\%$ on `DocVQA`.
- **Exact Semantic Match BPE-CSR:** We move past brittle edit-distance metrics. We procedurally track instances where a VASM-triggered prefix ($\gamma=1$) forces an intervention. A success (BPE-CSR=1) is recorded *if and only if* the completed generated subword sequence achieves an exact semantic match with the intended physical entity, mapping suffix preservation directly to grounding success.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will recover `MMBench`/`MMMU` scores comparable to the base model, keep PPL strictly $\le +5\%$, and maintain a high BPE-CSR.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Parity Ablation for Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate that *token-local adaptive selection* is statistically superior to coarse visual aggregation, controlling perfectly for external priors.
- **Figure 1 Target (The Parity Ablation):** A line chart plotting *Object Existence Accuracy* (`FREAK` / `Object HalBench`) on the Y-axis against *Intervention Strength ($\alpha$)* on the X-axis, complete with error bands aggregated over at least 3 distinct prompt phrasing variants.
- **Critical Parity Constraint:** `TLRA_MeanPool` ($k = N_{active}$) and `TLRA_RandomK` utilize the *exact same* $\Phi_{calib}$ weights as `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`. 
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must demonstrate a statistically significant gap ($p < 0.05$) above `TLRA_MeanPool` across all prompt variants. If MeanPool achieves parity with Top-$k$, the core scientific premise of "token-local resonance" is falsified.

To transparently map operational boundaries, the execution plan mandates the following diagnostics:

This transparently visualizes our theoretical risk model occurring in practice.

## 5. Conclusion and Limitations

TLRA formalizes a blueprint for precise, token-local visual grounding during MLLM decoding. By transparently acknowledging the structural risks of logit intervention and mitigating them via Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking and adaptive normalized entropy fallbacks, we establish a mathematically falsifiable framework. However, the strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its rigid operational boundaries:

1. **Calibrator External Priors and Parity Risks:** $\Phi_{calib}$ injects an external object-detection prior. The scientific validity of TLRA rests on a knife-edge, depending entirely on the `TLRA_MeanPool` parity ablation (Figure 1). If local routing does not statistically outperform global pooling using the same calibrator, the method acts merely as a stealth object detector.
2. **The Category Leakage Reality:** We mathematically concede that absolute "zero domain overlap" is impossible. TLRA's true generalizability is exclusively gated by the planned Category Leakage Audit separating entities *seen* vs. *unseen* by the calibrator pseudo-labels.
3. **The OCR Paradox and Negative Controls:** TLRA is strictly a mitigation strategy for *physical entities in natural scenes*. Its zero-trigger behavior on document tasks (`DocVQA`) is an intentional, strictly enforced limitation governed by the English WordNet dictionary.
4. **BPE Suffix Collapse and System Latency:** Artificially suppressing competing logits at step $t$ forces the model down a specific autoregressive path, inherently risking suffix stuttering if the KV-cache is pushed out of distribution. Furthermore, protecting Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) requires candidate bounding ($M$), which introduces a vulnerability where overwhelming language priors can push grounded tokens below the observable rank.

Our immediate future execution revolves around strictly validating these Evidence Chains—specifically enforcing the Exact Semantic Match BPE-CSR and the TPOT latency audits—ensuring the empirical footprint of TLRA remains precisely bounded by its positive hypotheses.
