# Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring: Escaping the Pooling Paradox in High-Resolution Image and Video MLLMs

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual or temporal evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate thousands of high-resolution spatial or temporal tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D/3D positional coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., Document VQA) and specific frame localization (e.g., Video Action). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space via localized visual matching. 

To resolve the compute-intensive trade-offs and structural bottlenecks inherent in zero-pooling logits interventions, BRA introduces three systemic upgrades: (1) **Dual-Track Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)**, offering a strictly zero-shot identity mapping alongside a formally defined linear projector trained offline via Visual Genome bounding boxes to prevent inference-time detector dependency; (2) **Bounded Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, isolating fine-grained $T \times H \times W$ features while mathematically managing the $\approx 10$ GFLOPs/step decode overhead via PagedAttention-inspired contiguous caching and optimized batched GEMV; and (3) **Stateful Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, replacing brittle predictive entropy with an offline syntactic mask equipped with strict BPE continuation rules. We establish a rigorous 5-tier "Defense Line" experimental protocol—spanning dense OCR heatmaps, temporal frame-hit rates, VRAM bandwidth stress-tests, and MMMU failure-case analysis—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy and inference efficiency of BRA against both image and video-adapted baselines.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model produces hallucinations—generating text that is linguistically fluent but physically or temporally absent from the multimedia input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on multi-pass contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD). While effective, these methods introduce severe latency overheads and VRAM bloat, rendering them computationally hostile to long-context generation. The second route employs hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution images or long videos, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool, MaxPool, or PCA) thousands of visual tokens. This irreversible aggregation destroys the fine-grained spatial-temporal coordinates that the model relies on to ground microscopic dense text or fleeting temporal actions, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or Video Action Recognition.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a localized match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial and temporal information. However, escaping the pooling paradox inherently introduces a severe inference compute trade-off: comparing vast spatio-temporal volumes ($N_v \to 50,000$) against the vocabulary at every step explodes both FLOPs and memory bandwidth. Furthermore, it exposes the unverified assumption of zero-shot modality alignment and the sub-word fragmentation vulnerability where multi-token visual entities escape penalization.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA, bounded by strict computational complexity controls, systems-level VRAM optimization, and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Our methodology features:
1. **Dual-Track Calibration and Bounded Resonance**: Transitioning from statistically dangerous absolute max-pooling to a dynamic Top-$k$ formulation over a sharply truncated candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$. To resolve the "alignment mirage" without breaking the zero-shot inference pipeline, we pair a zero-shot baseline with a localized linear projector, extracted entirely offline using dataset-grounded bounding boxes and hard negative sampling.
2. **Stateful Probabilistic VASM**: Replacing flawed predictive entropy with an offline, polysemy-aware masking mechanism structurally upgraded with a BPE inheritance heuristic to protect multi-token entities across diverse tokenizers (e.g., Tiktoken, SentencePiece).
3. **The 5-Line Defense Protocol**: Expanding the experimental blueprint into a strict five-stage defense. Crucially, this protocol mandates explicit evaluation of decoding-time FLOPs overhead, Average Generated Length (AGL) tracking to prevent `<EOS>` hacking, and Temporal Histogram visualizations against newly adapted video-specific baselines to prove true continuous Image+Video viability.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Bounded Candidate Filtering & Offline Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)

Comparing $N_v$ visual tokens against the entire vocabulary $|V|$ at every decoding step $t$ results in an intractable $O(|V| \times N_v \times d)$ complexity. To resolve this, we define a sharply bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=50$). We utilize the post-LLM contextualized hidden states corresponding to the visual token positions, denoted as $h_L^{(v_j)}$. To compute resonance with the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$, we formalize an alignment mapping $\Phi$:

1. **Zero-Shot BRA ($\text{BRA}_{zero}$)**: $\Phi$ is the identity function, relying purely on the LLM's intrinsic representation alignment.
2. **Calibrated BRA ($\text{BRA}_{calib}$)**: A lightweight single-layer linear projector trained entirely offline to bridge disjointed visual and textual spaces (e.g., in early Qwen-VL variants). To avoid introducing external object detectors during inference, the training utilizes ground-truth bounding boxes from the Visual Genome (VG) dataset. We extract stable bounding-box states ($h_{bbox}$) offline using a fixed dummy prompt (*"Describe the image in detail."*). Let $c^+$ be the ground-truth text entity, and $\{c^-\}$ be a composite set of *hard negatives* (co-occurring but incorrect objects within the same VG scene) and random vocabulary words:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = -\log \frac{\exp\left(\cos(W_{vocab}[c^+], \Phi_{calib}(h_{bbox})) / \tau\right)}{\sum_{c \in \{c^+\} \cup \{c^-\}} \exp\left(\cos(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi_{calib}(h_{bbox})) / \tau\right)}
$$
This precisely forces the projector to map localized visual concepts to lexical counterparts without overfitting to a generative language modeling objective.

### 3.2 Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance & Systems-Level Bandwidth Management

For continuous resolution models and videos, $N_v = T \times H \times W$ fluctuates drastically. Relying on global mean-pooling destroys spatial-temporal localization. We formulate **Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, defining an adaptive $k$ bounded by a lower threshold to isolate sparse entities without background dilution:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot (T \times H \times W) \rceil\Big)
$$
where $\rho$ is a small fractional threshold (e.g., $0.005$). The raw resonance score for a candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ is:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{\cos\left(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)})\right)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
**Overcoming the FLOPs and Memory Bandwidth Wall:** 
Even with bounded $\mathcal{C}_t$, computing cosine similarities for $N_v \approx 50,000$ tokens of dimension $d=4096$ demands $\approx 10.2$ GFLOPs per decoding step. Naive PyTorch implementations (e.g., relying on `.contiguous()` and high-level `torch.einsum`) fail to bypass the Python GIL and VRAM fragmentation, causing catastrophic Time Per Output Token (TPOT) degradation. 

BRA resolves this via **Pre-fill Contiguous Caching**: inspired by PagedAttention, $h_L^{(v_j)}$ are extracted during the initial pre-fill stage and cloned into a strictly pre-allocated, contiguous C++ tensor block in VRAM. During the autoregressive phase, the Top-$k$ resonance is computed via a custom batched GEMV (General Matrix-Vector Multiplication) operation. This decouples the algorithmic complexity from systemic memory read bottlenecks, maintaining $O(1)$ memory allocation overhead during generation.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty 

We map raw resonance scores into stable logits modifications by computing the relative evidence position within the Top-$M$ candidates via a Softmax operation, bounding the score $\hat S(c) \in [0,1]$:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_t)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
where $\alpha$ is a global penalty strength. Because $\hat S(c)$ is bounded, a lower resonance dynamically guarantees a higher penalty, intrinsically stabilizing cross-modality scaling.

### 3.4 Escaping the Entropy Trap: Stateful Probabilistic VASM

Previous iterations used predictive entropy to scale penalties, creating an **"Entropy Trap"**: strong language priors inherently produce low-entropy predictions for hallucinated objects. We guarantee mathematical stability via **Stateful Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**. 

We pre-compute a continuous syntactic weight based on POS probability distributions: $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = \sum P(pos \mid c) \cdot \gamma_{pos}$. However, naive token-level masking introduces a **Sub-word Fragmentation Vulnerability**: if a tokenizer fractures a visual entity (e.g., "refrigerator" $\to$ "re", "friger", "ator"), the fragments lack corpus POS tags and default to $0.0$, escaping the penalty.

VASM solves this via a **BPE Continuation Heuristic**:
If a token $c_t$ is morphologically identified as a sub-word continuation (e.g., lacking the `_` (U+2581) prefix in SentencePiece, or matching continuation byte-patterns in Tiktoken), it inherits the expected semantic weight of its root word:
$$
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_t)] = \begin{cases} 
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_{t-1})], & \text{if } c_t \text{ is a BPE continuation} \\ 
\sum P(pos \mid c_t) \cdot \gamma_{pos}, & \text{otherwise} 
\end{cases}
$$
This stateful tracking structurally protects functional syntax while ensuring long, multi-token visual entities are rigorously penalized if unsupported by visual resonance.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

To definitively validate the structural advantages of BRA—and explicitly address the computational and architectural assumptions inherent in logits-space interventions—we define a rigorous, hypothesis-driven Evaluation Protocol. All planned experiments will be conducted across distinct model families (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT and Qwen-VL), comparing BRA against both image-standard and newly adapted video-specific baselines.

### 4.1 Preliminary Ablations & Systems Complexity Profiling
**Objective:** Prove memory bandwidth optimization claims and isolate the Pooling Paradox.
**Protocol:**
- **Complexity Analysis:** Compare theoretical and empirical overhead.
  | Method | Compute (FLOPs/step) | VRAM Allocation | Context Degradation |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Base | $O(1)$ | $O(N_{ctx})$ | None |
  | VCD | $O(N_{ctx} \cdot d^2)$ | $2 \times O(N_{ctx})$ | High (Multi-pass) |
  | DoLa | $O(L \cdot d)$ | $O(L \cdot N_{ctx})$ | Spatial Loss (Pooling) |
  | **BRA** | $O(M \cdot N_v \cdot d)$ | $O(N_v)$ | None (Zero-Pooling) |
- **Latency Scaling Curve:** Plot Time Per Output Token (TPOT) vs. Visual Token Count $N_v$ ($1k \to 50k$). 
- **Hyperparameter Robustness:** Grid search mapping $\alpha$ and $\rho$ to performance, proving $\alpha$ does not require per-image tuning.
- **Hypothesis:** Despite requiring $\approx 10$ GFLOPs/step at $N_v=50k$, BRA's custom contiguous caching will yield a linear, manageable TPOT trajectory that is Pareto-superior to multi-pass methods (VCD) and robust across standard $\alpha$ values.

### 4.2 Defense Line 1: Core Hallucination Suppression & Length-Bias Control
**Objective:** Prove BRA mitigates object hallucinations without inducing length collapse.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** POPE, CHAIR (MSCOCO).
- **Baselines:** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa. 
- **Key Metric Requirements:** F1 (POPE), CHAIR$_S$, CHAIR$_I$, and strictly **Average Generated Length (AGL)**.
- **Hypothesis:** Hidden-state interventions often artificially inflate CHAIR scores by forcing premature `<EOS>`. BRA will lower hallucination rates while maintaining an AGL within 5% of the Base model, refuting any `<EOS>` hacking. $\text{BRA}_{calib}$ will heavily outscore $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ on early Qwen variants.

### 4.3 Defense Line 2: General Intelligence & Language Prior Integrity
**Objective:** Ensure visual logit penalization does not lobotomize instructional compliance.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME.
- **Reporting:** Total score, explicitly isolating "Perception" vs. "Cognition/Reasoning".
- **Hypothesis:** Thanks to VASM shielding functional syntax ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma] \approx 0$), BRA will improve Perception without degrading Cognition, bypassing the catastrophic reasoning degradation typical of contrastive decoding.

### 4.4 Defense Line 3: "Zero-Pooling" Supremacy on Dense Spatial Tasks
**Objective:** Qualitatively and quantitatively expose the "Pooling Paradox" on high-resolution targets.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR / Position subset) and DocVQA.
- **Spatial Heatmap Mandate:** Render a side-by-side visualization: (1) Original dense image, (2) DoLa MeanPool spatial activations (expected: blurry global blob), and (3) BRA Top-$k$ activations.
- **Hypothesis:** BRA's Adaptive Top-$k$ will dynamically produce sharp, isolated activation points precisely over the exact microscopic text patches being decoded, visually proving the necessity of zero-pooling.

### 4.5 Defense Line 4: Spatio-Temporal Grounding in Video Narrative
**Objective:** Validate that Adaptive Top-$k$ scales across $T \times H \times W$ without destroying temporal action localization.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** VIDHALLUC and Video-MME.
- **Baselines:** Must include explicitly adapted temporal variants (e.g., Temporal-DoLa utilizing temporal mean-pooling, and Video-VCD).
- **Temporal Histogram Mandate:** Plot Top-$k$ Activation Count (Y) against Video Frames (X), with a shaded region indicating the ground-truth action timeframe. 
- **Hypothesis:** Temporal pooling averages out single-frame actions, causing flat activations. BRA's Top-$k$ tokens will autonomously spike strictly within the shaded ground-truth temporal window, yielding a $>85\%$ Frame Hit Rate.

### 4.6 Defense Line 5: VASM Ablation & Boundary Failure Cases
**Objective:** Test limits where Language Inertia is beneficial and analyze algorithmic edge-case failures.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMMU (Hard Subset) for VASM ablation; custom tiny-object dataset for failure analysis.
- **Hypothesis (VASM):** The "No-VASM" ablation ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma] = 1.0$) will crash MMMU reasoning scores, mathematically proving the "Entropy Trap" claim and validating VASM.
- **Hypothesis (Failure Case):** For microscopically small objects where true spatial token count $< k_{min}$, BRA will be forced to absorb background noise, diluting the signal. Documenting this explicitly bounds the operational limits of $k_{min}$.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to localized logits-space resonance anchoring resolves a critical structural bottleneck in high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly addressing the computational overhead via bounded candidate sets and PagedAttention-inspired contiguous caching, and by resolving sub-word fragmentation via Stateful VASM, BRA provides a mathematically robust pathway to intervene safely without destroying fragile 2D/3D encodings. 

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Hardware Bandwidth Limits for Extreme Videos:** Although mathematically optimized via batched GEMV and caching, as sequence lengths push toward $>100K$ tokens in ultra-long videos, VRAM bounds and block-cache updating overheads during generation could eventually become a strict hardware bottleneck, necessitating localized chunking or temporal offloading strategies.
2. **The $k_{min}$ Dilution Vulnerability:** As mapped out in Defense Line 5, if an object is microscopically small (occupying fewer tokens than $k_{min}$), the Top-$k$ selection is forced to include background tokens, diluting the localized signal. Future dynamic $k_{min}$ bounds based on attention-head entropy or dynamic thresholding could address this.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until the 5-Tier Defense Protocol is fully executed, we limit our claims to the theoretical and algorithmic soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial-temporal bottlenecks of MLLM interventions, offering a mathematically bounded, compute-aware, and BPE-safe blueprint to validate zero-pooling decoding across both static and dynamic multimedia architectures.
