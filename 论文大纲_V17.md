# Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Token-Local Logits Intervention for Multimodal Hallucination Mitigation

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the local visual evidence needed for the next decoding step. Existing mitigation strategies have shown that hallucination can be reduced through contrastive decoding, attention control, or inter-layer comparison, but they do not directly provide a general framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that operates in the terminal logits space by reweighting candidate tokens using localized visual support.

BRA is built around three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse global correction with candidate-wise support computed directly from the contextualized visual states at the LLM's final layer. Second, **Bounded Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance** extracts stable local evidence from high-resolution token fields without disrupting the underlying decoding distribution, strictly operating within a safe candidate window. Third, **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via dynamic subword inheritance, preventing hallucination suppression from degrading grammar or long lexical units. To maintain methodological transparency, we explicitly separate a zero-shot variant (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated upper-bound (`BRA_calib`).

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize this paper around a falsifiable, three-chain evaluation protocol: hallucination reduction, structure and reasoning preservation, and the specific value of local evidence over coarse aggregation. Spanning `POPE`, `CHAIR`, `MMBench`, `MMMU`, `FREAK`, `DocVQA`, and spatio-temporal video metrics, this protocol presents a positive, rigorously bounded proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual evidence control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual image or video input. This failure is especially damaging in dense grounding scenarios such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, GUI understanding, and frame-sensitive video reasoning, where the relevant visual evidence is local rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These families are all important competitive baselines, but none of them by themselves define a general answer to the following question:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This question is the actual center of BRA. This paper does not attempt to frame mainstream baselines as sharing a single flawed mechanism. Instead, it advances a direct, defensible positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided that it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards.

Executing this is nontrivial for three mathematical and structural reasons. First, the input-output embedding asymmetry: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, making direct cosine similarity with output vocabularies mathematically meaningless unless properly projected via the model's native `lm_head`. Second, the candidate generation limit: decode-time intervention can only reweight existing language priors; it cannot resurrect a completely suppressed visual token. Third, the structural collapse risk: hallucination suppression easily overfires on Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities unless explicitly protected. 

To address these challenges, BRA centers on three core mechanisms:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, utilizing final-layer visual states to bridge the embedding asymmetry.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance**, extracting localized support from bounded candidate subsets without distribution collapse.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, a structure-preserving protection layer utilizing BPE continuation inheritance.

To ensure rigorous evaluation, we preserve a strict fairness boundary between `BRA_zero` and `BRA_calib`, organizing the experimental design as a stringent test of effectiveness, side effects, and local-evidence value.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary and the Embedding Asymmetry: `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`

A critical vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is the embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens typically enter at the input embedding layer. Unless an architecture uses strict weight tying, directly comparing input-aligned visual features to output lexical unembeddings is mathematically invalid.

To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* visual hidden states at the LLM's final transformer layer, $h_L^{(v_j)}$. Because these states have passed through the entire residual stream alongside text tokens, they occupy the same manifold as the pre-unembedding text states. We therefore split BRA into two distinct tracks:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute the support by evaluating what vocabulary each visual patch would independently activate: 
   $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`BRA_calib`**: A calibrated upper-bound variant where a lightweight matrix $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained on a small alignment set to correct semantic drift between the final visual states and the lexical space.

`BRA_zero` serves as the fair comparison against baseline decoding methods; `BRA_calib` probes the theoretical upper bound of local evidence alignment.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, BRA acknowledges a fundamental limitation: decode-time logits intervention cannot rescue a target token if the base model assigns it near-zero probability. We therefore strictly bound the intervention to a candidate set:
$$
\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})
$$
where $M \ll |V|$ is a safe candidate window (e.g., $M=100$).

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, BRA computes local visual support from the visual token volume $N_v = T \times H \times W$. To prevent spatial noise from washing out small objects, we apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)
$$
The raw support is the average activation of candidate $c$ across its top-$k$ most resonant visual patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c]}{\tau_{sim}}
$$

**Spatio-Temporal Dilution in Video:** For video ($T > 1$), flattening $T \times H \times W$ risks selecting noisy, disjointed patches across irrelevant frames. To test if BRA naturally isolates temporal events, we deliberately retain the flattened Top-$k$ search but introduce a **Frame Hit Rate** metric (Section 4.3) to rigorously audit whether the selected patches temporally cluster around the relevant action.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into stable logits adjustment, BRA normalizes the evidence over the bounded candidate set:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens that lack local visual backing relative to their peers:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Probabilistic VASM and BPE Inheritance

Hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing structural tokens (which lack visual grounding) or fragmenting multi-token entities. We introduce Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) to modulate the intervention strength $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)]$.

Because BPE subwords are context-dependent, a static corpus-level prior $P(pos \mid c)$ is insufficient. VASM utilizes a two-tier mechanism:
1. **Root Token Expectation:** For tokens initiating a word (e.g., identified by SentencePiece `_` prefix or absence of WordPiece `##`), we query a static prior dictionary to assign high $\gamma$ to visual nouns/adjectives and low $\gamma$ to functional syntax.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the dictionary and inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its most recent root token. 

This inference-time inheritance prevents the mathematical collapse of multi-token entities (e.g., complex OCR strings or scientific terms) during decoding.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces a specific architectural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence, we organize the evaluation plan into three falsifiable evidence chains. The following outlines the hypothesis-driven protocol and the planned benchmarks.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction

**Hypothesis:** `BRA_zero` mitigates object hallucination without causing pathological output truncation or length collapse.

- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR` (Image).
- **Baselines:** Base Model, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `BRA_zero`, `BRA_calib`.
- **Mandatory Metric:** `AGL` (Average Generated Length).
- **Execution Rule:** Table 1 must report Accuracy/F1 alongside `AGL`. If BRA improves `CHAIR` metrics solely by generating significantly shorter responses, the hallucination reduction claim is falsified.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via local evidence does not degrade complex reasoning, provided that VASM protects structural and functional tokens.

- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard Subset)`.
- **Primary Ablation:** `BRA` (with VASM) vs. `BRA_no_VASM`.
- **Execution Rule:** Table 2 must demonstrate that `BRA_no_VASM` suffers a severe reasoning drop on `MMMU (Hard)`, while `BRA` (with VASM) preserves or improves upon the Base model's performance. This isolates VASM as a strictly necessary component rather than a marginal heuristic.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Value of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting (Adaptive Top-$k$) is significantly more faithful than coarse global visual aggregation.

- **Benchmarks:** `FREAK` (OCR, Position, Counting), `DocVQA`.
- **Primary Ablation:** Base vs. `BRA_MeanPool` vs. `BRA_zero`. 
  *(Note: `BRA_MeanPool` replaces the Top-$k$ extraction with a global average of $h_L^{(v_j)}$).*
- **Execution Rule:** Table 3 must show `BRA_zero` outperforming `BRA_MeanPool`. If `BRA_zero` cannot beat the global average on dense document or spatial tasks, the core premise of "token-local necessity" is falsified.
- **Qualitative Audit (Figure 1):** We mandate spatial heatmaps tracking the Top-$k$ visual tokens selected during the generation of specific localized entities (e.g., small objects, OCR text) to prove geometric alignment.

#### Defense Line 4: Spatio-Temporal Audit (Video)
To test if token-local control survives the shift to video without spatio-temporal dilution, we define the **Frame Hit Rate**: the percentage of Top-$k$ visual tokens that temporally align with the ground-truth action timeframe of the generated token.
- **Benchmarks:** `VIDHALLUC`, `Video-MME`.
- **Execution Rule:** We will plot a temporal histogram of the Top-$k$ concentration. If the Frame Hit Rate is near-random, the spatio-temporal extension claim will be downgraded to a documented limitation.

### 4.4 Efficiency Audits

To ensure the bounded candidate filtering and Top-$k$ search do not introduce prohibitive overhead, the protocol includes:
- **Scaling Curve:** Decoding Latency (Tokens/Sec) plotted against Visual Token Count ($N_v$).
- **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Variance over candidate window $M$, Top-$k$ ratio $\rho$, and penalty strength $\alpha$.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of BRA relies on a clean scientific contract: it intervenes at decode-time, leverages token-local visual evidence, preserves structural integrity via BPE-aware VASM, and maintains a transparent zero-shot baseline. However, execution of this framework carries specific theoretical limitations:

1. **Embedding Alignment Dependency:** `BRA_zero` assumes that applying the LLM's native `lm_head` to the final-layer visual hidden states produces a meaningful semantic distribution. If the architecture's visual states remain too abstract or geometrically entangled at the final layer, `BRA_zero` will yield noisy local support, necessitating the `BRA_calib` variant.
2. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** BRA fundamentally operates by reweighting the Top-$M$ language priors. It cannot generate a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$.
3. **Static Dictionary Edge Cases:** While BPE continuation inheritance mitigates subword fragmentation, VASM's reliance on root-token prior dictionaries may still misclassify highly contextual homographs.
4. **Spatio-Temporal Dilution:** The flat $T \times H \times W$ Top-$k$ search in video risks scattering attention across noisy frames. If empirical Frame Hit Rates prove low, explicit temporal locality constraints will be required in future work.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring**, a positive, falsifiable framework for mitigating multimodal hallucinations through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By explicitly identifying the embedding asymmetry risk, formalizing VASM with BPE inheritance, and separating `BRA_zero` from calibrated bounds, we establish a rigorous foundation for decode-time control. The claims herein are strictly bounded by the proposed five-line evaluation protocol spanning hallucination reduction, reasoning preservation, and the empirical necessity of local over coarse visual evidence.
