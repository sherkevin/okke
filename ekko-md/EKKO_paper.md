## Title 
EKKO: Mitigating Hallucinations in Multimodal Large Language Models via Evaluative Knowledge-Kernel Oracles

## Abstract
Fundamentally, Multimodal Large Language Models remain text-native autoregressive systems. While they accept various modalities, non-text inputs often serve merely as conditioning signals rather than first-class languages of admission. Consequently, hallucination is a structural temporal collapse: premature semantic commitments enter the autoregressive prefix before establishing robust causal links to the input context. Existing decode-time mitigation methods mainly address historical failures through retrospective penalties, but they do not explicitly evaluate whether a candidate token is supported by query-relevant visual regions and whether its short-horizon continuation will remain visually grounded. In this paper, we propose EKKO, a novel inference-time decoding framework with Evaluative Knowledge-Kernel Oracles, for mitigating hallucinations in vision-language generation. EKKO evaluates candidate tokens through three coordinated signals: a past signal that preserves rollback-based protection against historical textual inertia, a current signal that measures query-conditioned visual support via anchor-aware region weighting, and a future signal that probes short-horizon continuation tendency through prospective rollout. This design enables EKKO to suppress risky candidates that appear locally plausible at the current step but are likely to trigger text-dominated continuation. To keep the method practical, EKKO performs anchor generation before autoregressive decoding and uses shared-KV batched rollout during candidate evaluation. Experiments on multiple MLLM benchmarks, including POPE, CHAIR, and MMBench, demonstrate that EKKO improves visual grounding and reduces hallucination across diverse multimodal generation settings.
