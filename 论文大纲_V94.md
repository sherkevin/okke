# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Hybrid Auxiliary Routing Head for MLLM Grounding via Intermediate Visual States

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as visual-noise contrastive decoding (VCD), Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG), or layer-contrast methods (DoLa)—are training-free and operate globally. In contrast, we present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a hybrid trained auxiliary routing head that directly injects token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment while employing explicit heuristics to protect language structure.

TLRA is built on three architectural corrections. First, to avoid the "local evidence illusion" where final-layer LLM visual tokens are irreversibly contaminated by global text-attention, TLRA extracts visual representations from intermediate LLM layers dynamically profiled across architectures. Second, to bridge the manifold mismatch between static mid-layer states and the dynamic lexical space, we train a lightweight MLP projector ($\Phi_{calib}$). Crucially, we enforce discrete logit-space routing rather than continuous hidden-state addition, mathematically isolating the routing gain against a rigorous, zero-initialized gated continuous-fusion baseline to prevent manifold collapse and preserve text perplexity. Third, we address the GPU kernel latency bottleneck of dynamic autoregressive Top-K sorting by capping the intervention window, and we manage the "BPE subword prefix trap" through Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM). 

To enforce an unforgiving evaluation contract, we separate our hybrid architectural mechanism from the training objective. Any claim about TLRA's routing superiority must survive a mandatory parameter-and-data-matched parity against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the identical cross-entropy alignment objective, as well as zero-shot visual CFG. Furthermore, we explicitly audit the true wall-clock latency of our Top-K routing kernel, quantify the mathematical ceiling of bounded logit interventions (the "Hijacking Problem"), and expose the collateral fragility of the English-centric VASM heuristic on multilingual benchmarks. The resulting paper establishes a positive, falsifiable protocol for structure-preserving, hybrid visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model frequently generates text that is fluent but decoupled from localized visual evidence. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained physical grounding scenarios, such as spatial positioning and small-object recognition.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) inject visual noise to compare distributions; image-free Classifier-Free Guidance (`CFG`) directly penalizes unconditional priors; attention-based control (`OPERA`) penalizes over-trust in attention matrices to break repetition and summary bottlenecks; layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep language representations. Crucially, these are **training-free, plug-and-play techniques** relying on zero-shot distribution shifts. They leave a complementary architectural question unanswered:

**How can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence directly into logits adjustment using a trained auxiliary head, without conflating the routing gains with training-objective superiority, and without falling victim to the globalization of final-layer representations?**

This paper introduces a hybrid auxiliary routing head, Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA), advancing a narrow, falsifiable hypothesis: a hybrid framework scoring candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than zero-shot global visual perturbations or trivial continuous fusion, provided it extracts truly local states and explicitly bounds its own grammatical interference.

Executing this intervention requires overcoming four specific architectural and hardware barriers:
1. **The Local Evidence Illusion:** In decoder-only MLLMs, visual tokens processed during prefill undergo deep bidirectional self-attention. By the final layer, they are heavily contextualized, text-contaminated global mixtures. TLRA extracts visual states from intermediate layers. Rather than hardcoding a naïve $L/2$ assumption, we systematically profile the layer depth across backbones (e.g., LLaMA-2 vs. LLaMA-3).
2. **Manifold Mismatch & The Continuous Fusion Strawman:** Static mid-layer visual states and dynamic pre-LM-head lexical states do not natively share an embedding space. We train a lightweight projector ($\Phi_{calib}$) to bridge this. However, naïvely adding continuous vectors to the LLM's final hidden state often causes grammar collapse. To prove discrete logit routing is fundamentally superior, we must defeat a rigorous, zero-initialized gated continuous fusion baseline, measuring strict Textual Perplexity (PPL) preservation.
3. **Hardware Latency and The Top-M Ceiling:** Computing dynamic visual similarity inside the autoregressive loop threatens GPU memory bandwidth. Retrieving local support during generation requires $O(M \cdot N_v)$ dot products and a dynamic Top-$K$ sort at every step. We bound this with a Top-$M$ candidate window ($M=50$). However, this introduces a hard mathematical ceiling (the "Hijacking Problem"): if the base LLM hallucination is so severe that the ground truth token falls outside the Top-50, TLRA is structurally blind to it. We explicitly quantify this failure boundary.
4. **The BPE Subword Trap vs. The VASM Crutch:** A perfect Next-Token Prediction objective should natively teach $\Phi_{calib}$ to ignore grammatical syntax and fire only on visual entities. In practice, manifold bleeding occurs. We introduce Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)—a static, English-derived statistical mask—as an explicit engineering fallback to protect syntax. We formally audit whether $\Phi_{calib}$ learns semantic separation natively, and quantify the severe collateral damage of the VASM heuristic across multilingual boundaries (e.g., Chinese MMBench).

TLRA is a *trained hybrid method*. We therefore enforce a mandatory parity baseline: a LoRA model trained on the exact same dense region-caption data using the exact same alignment objective. If TLRA fails to outperform this objective-matched control and standard zero-shot CFG, its routing claim is mathematically falsified.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: Isolating the Objective in Hybrid Routing

TLRA is a hybrid method utilizing a lightweight MLP projector $\Phi_{calib}$ to map static, mid-layer visual states into the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical space. During training, the base LLM and Vision Encoder are **strictly frozen**; only $\Phi_{calib}$ is updated using a standard localized Next-Token Prediction objective over a dense region-caption subset.

**Objective Matching for Fair Evaluation:**
Confounding the routing mechanism with the training objective is a fatal flaw. Any claim about decode-time local routing must survive a mandatory parity ablation against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset **using identical Cross-Entropy**. 

**Defeating the Continuous Fusion Strawman:**
To justify discrete logit-space routing, we enforce parity against a robust `ContinuousAdd_Gated` baseline. Rather than a naïve addition, this baseline projects $\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ and continuously adds it to the final hidden state via a learned, zero-initialized gating scalar $\gamma$: $h_{t, final} + \gamma \sum \alpha_j \Phi_{calib}(v_j)$. If TLRA's discrete logit routing outperforms this optimally initialized continuous baseline on pure Textual Perplexity (WikiText PPL), we prove that avoiding the pre-trained LM-head manifold shift is an architectural necessity.

### 3.2 Bridging the Mismatch: Layer Profiling and The Top-M Window

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, locked at $M=50$ to optimize CUDA thread synchronization (though Top-$p$ nucleus bounding is theoretically superior for handling varying entropy). 

**The Local Evidence Illusion:**
By the final layer, visual tokens ($N_v \approx 4000$) have become text-contaminated global mixtures. TLRA identifies the optimal extraction layer $L_{mid}$ through empirical layer-sweep profiling to capture representations before spatial resolution is irreversibly diluted.

**Hardware Lifecycle and The Top-K Kernel Bottlenecks:**
$\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is executed **exactly once** during prefill, caching an $N_v \times D$ projected tensor ($\approx 32.7$ MB in FP16). During autoregression, raw local support for $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ is computed via:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}), c)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
We explicitly audit the hardware cost of performing a Top-$k$ sort over $N_v=4000$ elements for $M=50$ candidates *at every decoding step* to transparently document the true ms/token overhead. Furthermore, extracting $\Phi_{calib}$ purely from prefill states assumes visual grounding is completely static. We acknowledge this misses dynamic cross-attention contextualization, forming a structural limitation.

### 3.3 Temperature-Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw support into a relative evidence score: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)$. To prevent the visual head from blowing up the logits and overriding confident structural tokens, we anchor the penalty to the local logit spread $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)) - \min(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t))$. 

Because logit spread is highly sensitive to the generation temperature $T$, we adjust the spread boundary dynamically. Bounded by hyperparameter $\beta$, the logit adjustment is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L / T, \beta) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 BPE Subword Trap and the VASM Crutch

Ideally, the localized Next-Token Prediction objective should natively teach $\Phi_{calib}$ to output low visual resonance scores for abstract syntax. In practice, continuous manifold bleeding causes the projector to occasionally spike on functional subwords (e.g., `[car]` in `[car, dinal]`), leading to syntax collapse. 

To protect syntax without inline parsing, TLRA introduces Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM), a static $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$ mask. We employ **Frequency-Weighted Subword Resolution**: if a BPE subword maps to a groundable root entity in $\ge \gamma$ of its occurrences in a C4 corpus, it retains $M_{VASM}=1$. 

We explicitly acknowledge VASM is a brittle, English-centric engineering hack masking a limitation in the mapping objective. We formally audit this by plotting $\Phi_{calib}$'s raw entity vs. syntax score distributions *before* masking, and by treating VASM's collateral fragility on non-English benchmarks as a strict pass/fail threshold.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around three evidence chains and strict boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper.

### 4.1 Stage 0: The Gated Continuous Fusion Validation

**Goal:** Mathematically verify that discrete logit-space routing is structurally superior to continuous hidden-state addition without destroying pure language modeling.
- **Protocol:** Compare TLRA against `TLRA_ContinuousAdd_Gated` (using a zero-initialized gating parameter $\gamma$). Evaluate on standard Textual Perplexity (WikiText PPL).
- **Hypothesis:** Despite optimal initialization, continuous addition will still exhibit grammar degradation (worse PPL) or fail to adequately boost visual entities compared to discrete logit routing + VASM.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Objective Fairness

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, verifying gains derive from routing rather than calibration data.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Partitioned Baselines:** 
  - *Training-Free (Zero-Shot):* Base, `Visual CFG`, `VCD`, `MM-DoLa` (0 extra parameters, 0 extra data).
  - *Matched Objective (Hybrid):* `Base + LoRA` (trained on identical data/cross-entropy).
- **Hard Rule:** TLRA *must* significantly outperform both the zero-shot methods (including Visual CFG) and the objective-matched `Base + LoRA`.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Native Separation and Multilingual VASM Audit

**Goal:** Measure capability preservation, visually prove the manifold bleed, and quantify VASM's cross-lingual collateral damage.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench`, `MMBench-CN` (Chinese), `MMMU`.
- **Figure A (Native Separation Scatter Plot):** Plot raw $\Phi_{calib}$ output scores (Y-axis) vs. token frequency in C4 (X-axis) *before* VASM. Visual entities colored blue, syntax red. (Hypothesis: proves $\Phi_{calib}$ learns partial separation, but the overlapping tails justify VASM).
- **The Pass/Fail OOD Audit:** Evaluate on `MMBench-CN`. Tests the catastrophic failure boundaries of the C4-English-derived VASM heuristic. If VASM degrades Chinese performance by >5% compared to Base, it is flagged as a fatal structural limitation.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Layer Sweep and Visual Resolution Limits

**Goal:** Prove intermediate visual states retain more token-local grounding than final layers.
- **Protocol:** Systematic layer-sweep ($L_1$ to $L_N$) across LLaMA-2-7B and LLaMA-3-8B measuring `POPE F1`. Stratify performance by Ground Truth bounding box size to determine if $L_{mid}$ loses resolution for extremely small (<10x10 pixel) objects.

### 4.5 Efficiency and Mathematical Ceilings (The Hijacking Audit)

**Goal:** Transparently document computational overhead and the structural limits of bounded routing.
- **Table 2 (Latency Micro-benchmark):** Decode Time (ms/tok) explicitly split into: [Standard LLM Forward] + [Dot Product Time] + [Top-$k$ Sort Time]. 
- **Figure B (The Hijacking CDF):** A Cumulative Distribution Function plot where X = Logit Rank of the GT Token in Base model during CHAIR hallucination instances, Y = Cumulative %. A hard vertical line at X=50 demonstrates TLRA's maximum theoretical salvage rate. If the GT token is heavily expelled ($>50$), TLRA cannot recover it.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA explicitly bounds its claims. By defining itself as a *hybrid trained routing head*, it acknowledges its parameter and data costs relative to pure zero-shot methods like CFG or VCD. It isolates its routing gains against an unforgiving objective-matched `Base + LoRA` baseline and a text-perplexity-audited `ContinuousAdd_Gated` baseline to prevent conflating architectural superiority with alignment data or trivial scaling artifacts.

The primary unresolved risks and limitations are:
1. **The VASM Crutch and Multilingual Fragility:** A perfectly calibrated objective should teach $\Phi_{calib}$ to ignore syntax natively. In reality, manifold bleeding necessitates VASM. By frequency-weighting BPE subwords using an English C4 corpus, TLRA relies on a statistical hack that fails ungracefully when BPE statistics shift in multilingual contexts (e.g., Chinese MMBench).
2. **The Top-M Ceiling (The Hijacking Problem):** As demonstrated by our Hijacking CDF (Figure B), bounded routing has a hard mathematical ceiling. If the base LLM's language prior completely expels the correct visual entity from the candidate window (Rank $> 50$), TLRA cannot recover it. 
3. **Static Prefill Assumption:** By extracting $\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ exactly once during prefill, TLRA assumes the required visual representation is perfectly static. This ignores dynamic cross-attention contextualization, potentially missing complex vision-text interplay generated deep into the sequence. 
4. **GPU Kernel Latency:** Sorting over $N_v \approx 4000$ elements for $M=50$ candidates at every decoding step creates a CUDA thread synchronization bottleneck, introducing a non-trivial wall-clock latency hit per token.
