## Summary
The paper proposes Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA), a decode-time intervention for MLLMs to reduce hallucination. It uses a lightweight trained calibrator ($\Phi_{calib}$) to project visual tokens into the LM head's pre-softmax space. To maintain efficiency, it employs Prompt-Conditioned Pruning and an Adaptive Top-$k$ visual matching exclusively on the Top-$M$ candidate tokens. To prevent language structure collapse, it introduces VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking), a deterministic WordNet-based fail-safe that restricts intervention to physical entities and colors, explicitly bypassing function words, arbitrary OCR, and relying on the LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete BPE subwords. The paper outlines a rigorous, three-chain experimental protocol to validate hallucination reduction, structure preservation, and the specific value of token-local evidence versus global pooling.

## MajorWeaknesses
1. **Catastrophic BPE-WordNet Collisions:** VASM checks the string representation of the first BPE token against WordNet. You cite "strawberry" $\rightarrow$ "straw" (a valid physical entity) as a success case. However, consider the word "bulletin". The BPE tokenizer might split this as `['bullet', 'in']`. "Bullet" is a physical entity in WordNet. VASM will trigger $\gamma=1$, force the model to look for a physical bullet in the image, suppress the token because there is no bullet, and completely destroy the generation of the abstract word "bulletin". This is a severe lexical blind spot.
2. **Attention Sink Vulnerability in Pruning:** You propose pruning visual patches based on prefill cross-attention. In modern transformer-based MLLMs (e.g., LLaVA, Qwen-VL), attention often collapses into "attention sinks" (e.g., the `<image>` token, or the first patch, or a newline character) rather than semantically meaningful visual regions during the prefill stage. If you prune based on raw prefill attention weights, you risk discarding 90% of the actual visual evidence.
3. **Arbitrary Fallback Heuristics:** The Dynamic Fallback rule ($L_{prompt} < 5$ tokens) is mechanically weak. "Where is the dog?" is 4 tokens (highly specific). "Please describe this image in detail" is 7 tokens (highly generic). A hard token-count threshold will fail in the wild.

Do not claim state-of-the-art on DocVQA.
