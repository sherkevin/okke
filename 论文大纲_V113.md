# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Late-Fusion Visual Existence Checker for Autoregressive Decoding

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from entity hallucination when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during autoregressive decoding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations (e.g., DoLa, VCD) elegantly modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways to route fine-grained visual evidence. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects post-connector visual features directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual entity existence verifier**. It reliably answers "Is this noun anywhere in the image?" establishing a strict theoretical boundary between global entity grounding and targeted relational VQA vulnerabilities. To resolve parameter bloat, TLRA projects strictly to an offline-filtered physical noun subset ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$), reducing the structural footprint to $\sim$18M parameters. TLRA operates on three structural principles: (1) A semantically coherent initialization tied to the LLM's **Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)**, explicitly bypassing the Layer 0-to-Layer $N$ residual distortion that plagues input-embedding initializations to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap; (2) **Dynamic Activation Pooling** to resolve the spatial dilution inherent in static Top-$k$ patch selection across varying object sizes; and (3) A **Dynamic Syntax Floor equipped with an Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$)**, mathematically guaranteeing that hallucination suppression does not force irreversible grammatical collapse in high-entropy states. To resolve BPE fragmentation, we introduce a Subword-Trie Lookahead, pre-registering an evaluation protocol that rigorously contrasts greedy "Pivot Survival" against theoretical Beam Search Rollback. We explicitly benchmark against prompt-injected object detection (e.g., Grounding DINO) to justify the necessity of late-fusion intervention over pure context-window prompt engineering.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), such as LLaVA-1.5 and Llama-3-Vision variants, exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to entity hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding, attention-penalty heuristics, or distribution shifts. However, they do not introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways trained specifically to verify spatial presence at the token level. 

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit existence-checking supervision.

To maintain intellectual rigor, we downgrade our scope from general "hallucination suppression" to **Entity Existence Verification**. Algorithmically, TLRA acts as a context-blind existence checker for an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). By pooling patch features independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. This establishes two hard theoretical boundaries. First, against **Prompt-Injected Object Detection**: if TLRA cannot outperform an off-the-shelf detector (e.g., Grounding DINO) appending a text list of objects to the prompt, its architectural complexity is unjustified. We hypothesize that TLRA's token-level logit control prevents the attention dilution inherent in long context prompts. Second, TLRA risks **exacerbating relational hallucinations**. If a prompt asks "Is the dog on the sofa?" and the image contains a dog on the floor, verifying "sofa" exists will validate the token, inherently relying on the baseline LLM's self-attention to manage the spatial *relation*. 

Furthermore, we confront three existential threats to late-fusion routing: **Geometric Incoherence**, **Spatial Dilution**, and **Grammatical Collapse**. 
First, initializing a projection matrix from Layer 0 input embeddings assumes the residual stream preserves the embedding manifold. Modern LLMs heavily distort this space via repeated RMSNorms and Swish-GLU MLPs. We resolve this by extracting TLRA's initialization directly from the LLM's **final Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)**. 
Second, static Top-$k$ spatial pooling fundamentally fails across varying bounding-box sizes; we replace it with **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**, mathematically scaling the evidence pool based on object size. 
Finally, naive logit suppression risks grammatical destruction. Forcing an autoregressive trajectory to abandon a noun when no viable syntactic alternative exists creates semantic absurdity ("The man opened the *comma*"). We implement an **Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$)**, formally conceding a hallucination rather than irreversibly destroying the sentence structure.

Our evaluation is a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Semantic Absurdity:** Evaluating entity grounding (POPE/CHAIR) against zero-shot methods and **Prompt-Injected Object Detection**, while explicitly auditing Semantic Coherence via a "Pivot Survival Rate." We contrast greedy subword pivots against the theoretical gold-standard of **Beam Search Rollback**.
2. **Context-Blindness and Targeted VQA:** Mapping the exact failure boundaries of history-independent routing by splitting evaluation into Global Existence (POPE) and Relational Vulnerability (MMHal-Relations).
3. **Structure Preservation & The Manifold Assumption:** Auditing geometric initialization (Unembedding Matrix vs. Layer 0 vs. Random) alongside Connector-Locus ablations (Pre- vs. Post-MLP visual features), anchored by an untrained `TLRA_zero` representation check.
4. **Hardware Reality and The Latency Bottleneck:** A formal evaluation of the Trie-Lookahead's computational overhead, mapping Tokens/Second throughput on GPU at Batch Size $\ge 8$ to measure true warp divergence.
5. **The Syntax Floor and $\tau$-Abort Audit:** Quantifying the exact trade-off between hallucination penalty execution and Forced Syntactic Error Rates across nucleus (Top-$p$) sampling distributions.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Connector Locus and Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$) Alignment

TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage, strictly limited to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ maps cached spatial visual states to this entity subset. 

**The Connector Locus Requirement:** To successfully map visual features to the LLM's vocabulary space, the spatial features $X_v$ *must* be extracted **post-vision-language connector** (e.g., after the LLaVA MLP projector). Features extracted directly from the raw vision encoder (e.g., CLIP/SigLIP) exist in a fundamentally incompatible dimensional and semantic manifold, rendering late-fusion logit projection mathematically incoherent.

**The Sparsity Trap and $W_{out}$ Initialization:** To enable zero-shot generalization to unseen tokens, $W_{calib}$ must inherit semantic structure. Previous late-fusion models assumed initializing from Layer 0 input embeddings was sufficient. However, modern MLLMs featuring deep non-linearities (Swish-GLU MLPs, RMSNorms) induce severe rotation and scaling, destroying the Layer 0-to-Layer $N$ manifold alignment. To mathematically bypass this, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)** corresponding to $V_{noun}$. Because $W_{out}$ projects the final hidden state to logits, it is geometrically aligned with the Layer $N$ space by definition. 

**`TLRA_zero`:** A strict baseline computing cosine similarity directly between frozen post-connector features $X_v$ and the $W_{out}$ targets without parametric training, isolating whether explicit contrastive supervision ($W_{calib}$) is necessary.

### 3.2 Contrastive Calibration and Semantic Negative Sampling

To calibrate $W_{calib}$ and prevent mode collapse, we employ a **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** ($\mathcal{L}_{calib}$). For a given image with ground-truth physical nouns $\mathcal{P}$, we dynamically sample hard negative nouns $\mathcal{N}$ from $V_{noun}$ using corpus co-occurrence matrices. 
Crucially, to prevent shattering the visual manifold by penalizing visually indistinguishable synonyms (e.g., ground-truth "mug" vs. negative "cup"), we introduce a **Semantic Margin $\phi_{sim}$**. A candidate negative $n$ is only accepted if its frozen CLIP text-text cosine similarity to all $p \in \mathcal{P}$ is below $\phi_{sim}$. The LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen during this calibration.

### 3.3 Dynamic Activation Pooling and the $\tau$-Abort Syntax Floor

Visual patches are not bag-of-words. Static Top-$k$ pooling critically fails because it dilutes the existence signal of small objects (1 patch) while missing semantic context for massive objects (20 patches). We replace Top-$k$ with **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**. 
At decoding step $t$, for a candidate noun $c$, we identify the subset of active spatial patches $\mathcal{A}_c = \{j \mid (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_c > \theta_{pool}\}$. The raw spatial support is the mean of these dynamically selected active patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{A}_c|} \sum_{j \in \mathcal{A}_c} (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_c \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
If $\mathcal{A}_c$ is empty, $S_{raw}(c) = 0$. This ensures size-invariant existence checking.

**The Dynamic Syntax Floor and the $\tau$-Abort Mechanism:** 
To penalize a hallucination without destroying grammar, we enforce a Dynamic Syntax Floor. Let $L_{floor}$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the Top-$M$ candidate window. However, forcing the argmax to fall to $L_{floor}$ is mathematically fatal if $L_{floor}$ represents a grammatically invalid token with near-zero probability (e.g., a comma with logit -15). 
We introduce the **Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$)**. The final logit modification is applied *before* softmax/Top-$p$ truncation:
$$
L_{final}(c) = 
\begin{cases} 
L_{orig}(c) & \text{if } (L_{orig}(c) - L_{floor}) > \tau \\
\max \Big( L_{floor} - \epsilon, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big) & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
If the LLM has no viable syntactic alternative (entropy gap $> \tau$), TLRA aborts the intervention. Permitting a hallucinated noun is strictly preferable to irreversible structural collapse.

### 3.4 BPE Fragmentation: Subword-Trie Lookahead & Top-$p$ Compatibility

Modern tokenizers shatter words (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`). A naive "First-Subword Penalty" introduces Prefix Collisions (penalizing `_re` due to the lack of a "refrigerator" simultaneously penalizes "return"). TLRA implements a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification**: the penalty $\gamma(c)$ is strictly deferred (set to 0) until the autoregressive trajectory reaches a subword that *uniquely identifies* a noun stem.

Because $L_{final}(c)$ is computed pre-softmax, it integrates seamlessly with **Nucleus (Top-$p$) Sampling**. If a penalized noun drops below $L_{floor}$, the subsequent softmax distribution organically elevates the valid syntactic alternatives into the nucleus.

**Greedy Pivot vs. Beam Search Rollback:** If the image lacks a refrigerator, TLRA drops the logit for `friger`. Under greedy decoding, this forces a mid-word forward pivot (e.g., completing `_re` + `alize`). We explicitly contrast this highly latency-optimized but dangerous "Greedy Pivot" against the theoretical gold-standard of **Beam Search Rollback**, where a triggered penalty explicitly prunes the entire prefix (`_re`) from the active beam, forcing a resample without mid-word textual corruption.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict linguistic, structural, and hardware boundaries. By reframing TLRA as an explicitly-trained, context-blind entity existence verifier, we identify mathematically rigid limitations that future research must confront:

1. **The Prompt-Injection Threat:** If appending a list of objects detected by Grounding DINO to the system prompt achieves comparable POPE and MMHal scores, the architectural complexity of TLRA is functionally invalidated for offline tasks. TLRA's primary defense relies on its token-level latency control and its ability to bypass attention-dilution limits inherent to massive prompt contexts.
2. **Relational Exacerbation vs. Global Existence:** Because TLRA pools visual patches independently of text context ($Y_{<t}$), it excels at global entity verification but actively risks *exacerbating* relational hallucinations. If the base LLM fails to correctly apply its self-attention to spatial structures, TLRA's validation of an object's existence may inadvertently strengthen an incorrect positional claim.
3. **The Grammatical Trade-off ($\tau$-Abort):** The $\tau$-Abort threshold is a formal concession that late-fusion logit manipulation cannot always gracefully recover from hallucination triggers. If the sequence entropy is too low, the system must allow a hallucination to pass to prevent catastrophic grammatical destruction. 
4. **Hardware Latency and Warp Divergence:** Traversing a subword Trie for candidate tokens introduces branching logic. In diverse batched generation, sequences will hit ambiguous prefixes at completely different steps, causing severe GPU warp divergence that threatens practical throughput limits.

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a sparse, geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head—anchored by the Unembedding Matrix and dynamic pooling—can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities, or if the algorithmic limits of late-fusion projection represent an insurmountable theoretical ceiling.
