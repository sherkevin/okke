# Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring: Escaping the Pooling Paradox in High-Resolution Image and Video MLLMs

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual or temporal evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate thousands of high-resolution spatial or temporal tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D/3D-RoPE coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., Document VQA) and specific frame localization (e.g., Video Action). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space via localized visual matching. 

To resolve critical mathematical, structural, and hardware bottlenecks in prior interventions, BRA introduces three systemic upgrades: (1) **Dual-Track Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)**, offering a strictly zero-shot identity mapping alongside a formally defined, localized InfoNCE-trained linear projector extracted via stable dummy-prefixes for unaligned architectures; (2) **Bounded Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, isolating fine-grained $T \times H \times W$ features while mathematically bounding FLOPs ($O(M \times N_v)$) and overcoming the VRAM memory bandwidth wall via pre-fill contiguous caching; and (3) **Stateful Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, replacing brittle predictive entropy with an offline syntactic mask equipped with strict BPE continuation rules to prevent multi-token entity fragmentation. We establish a rigorous 5-tier "Defense Line" experimental protocol—spanning dense OCR heatmaps, temporal frame-hit rates, VRAM bandwidth stress-tests, and failure-case analysis—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy and inference efficiency of BRA across continuous and dynamic-resolution MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model produces hallucinations—generating text that is linguistically fluent but physically or temporally absent from the multimedia input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on multi-pass contrastive decoding or backtracking (e.g., VCD, OPERA). While effective, these methods introduce severe latency overheads and VRAM bloat, rendering them computationally hostile to long-context generation. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution images or long videos, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool, MaxPool, or PCA) thousands of visual tokens. This irreversible aggregation destroys the fine-grained spatial-temporal coordinates (like 2D/3D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects, dense text, or fleeting temporal actions, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or Video Action Recognition.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a localized match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial and temporal information. However, implementing this exposes severe mathematical and systemic vulnerabilities: the computational explosion of matching vast spatio-temporal volumes against the entire vocabulary $|V|$, the hardware memory bandwidth wall of accessing high-dimensional states step-by-step, the unverified assumption of zero-shot modality alignment, and the sub-word fragmentation vulnerability where multi-token visual entities escape penalization.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA, bounded by strict computational complexity controls, VRAM optimization, and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Our methodology features:
1. **Dual-Track Calibration and Bounded Resonance**: Transitioning from statistically dangerous absolute max-pooling to a dynamic Top-$k$ formulation over a sharply truncated candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$. To resolve the "alignment mirage," we pair a zero-shot baseline with a localized InfoNCE-trained linear projector, extracted via stable prefix-conditioning.
2. **Stateful Probabilistic VASM**: Replacing flawed predictive entropy with an offline, polysemy-aware masking mechanism based on corpus-level Part-of-Speech (POS) distributions, structurally upgraded with a BPE inheritance heuristic to protect multi-token entities across diverse tokenizers.
3. **The 5-Line Defense Protocol**: Expanding the experimental blueprint into a strict five-stage defense. Crucially, this protocol mandates explicit cross-architecture validation, VRAM memory bandwidth stress-testing on extreme video lengths ($N_v \to 50,000$), tracking Average Generated Length (AGL) to prevent `<EOS>` hacking, and temporal Frame Hit Rates to prove true Image+Video viability.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Bounded Candidate Filtering & Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)

Comparing $N_v$ visual tokens against the entire vocabulary $|V|$ at every decoding step $t$ results in an intractable $O(|V| \times N_v \times d)$ complexity. To resolve this compute explosion, we define a sharply bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=50$). We strictly utilize the **post-LLM contextualized hidden states** corresponding to the visual token positions, denoted as $h_L^{(v_j)}$. To compute resonance with the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$, we formalize an alignment mapping $\Phi$, decoupled into two variants:

1. **Zero-Shot BRA ($\text{BRA}_{zero}$)**: $\Phi$ is the identity function. This relies on the LLM's intrinsic representation alignment and serves as the primary fair baseline.
2. **Calibrated BRA ($\text{BRA}_{calib}$)**: A lightweight single-layer linear projector trained offline to bridge disjointed visual and textual spaces (e.g., in early Qwen-VL variants). Because $h_L^{(v_j)}$ are highly sensitive to the text prefix, extracting stable bounding-box states ($h_{bbox}$) offline requires a fixed, neutral dummy prompt (e.g., *"Describe the image in detail."*). Let $c^+$ be the corresponding text entity token, and $\{c^-\}$ be a set of randomly sampled incongruent visual nouns:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = -\log \frac{\exp\left(\cos(W_{vocab}[c^+], \Phi_{calib}(h_{bbox})) / \tau\right)}{\sum_{c \in \{c^+\} \cup \{c^-\}} \exp\left(\cos(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi_{calib}(h_{bbox})) / \tau\right)}
$$
This precisely forces the linear projector to map localized visual concepts to their lexical counterparts without overfitting to a generative language modeling objective.

### 3.2 Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance and Bandwidth Management

Operating over high-resolution videos produces massive spatio-temporal token volumes defined by $N_v = T \times H \times W$. For continuous resolution models (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT) where patches are dynamic, $H$ and $W$ represent the flattened dynamically cropped grids per frame. Relying on global mean-pooling (the Pooling Paradox) destroys spatial-temporal localization.

We formulate **Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**. Because $N_v$ fluctuates drastically, a static ratio $\rho$ might cause pooling dilution on extremely dense images. Therefore, we define an adaptive $k$ bounded by an absolute lower threshold to isolate sparse entities:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot (T \times H \times W) \rceil\Big)
$$
where $\rho$ is a small fractional threshold (e.g., $0.005$). For a candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, the raw resonance score is:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{\cos\left(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)})\right)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
**Overcoming the Memory Bandwidth Wall:** While bounding $\mathcal{C}_t$ reduces FLOPs to $O(M \times N_v \times d)$, loading $N_v \approx 50,000$ high-dimensional hidden states from VRAM *at every decoding step* creates a severe memory read bottleneck. To resolve this, BRA utilizes **Pre-fill Contiguous Caching**: during the initial pre-fill stage, $h_L^{(v_j)}$ are extracted and cloned into a contiguous, cache-friendly memory block in VRAM. This optimizes tensor coalescing during the autoregressive phase, preventing TPOT (Time Per Output Token) degradation.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty 

We map the raw resonance scores into stable logits modifications by computing the relative evidence position within the Top-$M$ candidates via a Softmax operation, strictly bounding the normalized score $\hat S(c) \in [0,1]$:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_t)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
where $\alpha$ is a global penalty strength. Because $\hat S(c)$ is bounded, a lower resonance dynamically guarantees a higher penalty, intrinsically stabilizing cross-modality scaling between dense images and long videos.

### 3.4 Escaping the Entropy Trap: Stateful Probabilistic VASM

Previous iterations used predictive entropy to scale penalties, creating an **"Entropy Trap"**: strong language priors inherently produce low-entropy predictions for predictable *hallucinated* objects. We guarantee mathematical stability via **Stateful Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**. 

We pre-compute a continuous syntactic weight based on POS probability distributions in a large corpus: $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = \sum P(pos \mid c) \cdot \gamma_{pos}$. However, naive token-level masking introduces a **Sub-word Fragmentation Vulnerability**: if a tokenizer fractures a visual entity (e.g., "refrigerator" $\to$ "re", "friger", "ator"), the fragments lack corpus POS tags and default to $0.0$, escaping the penalty.

To solve this, VASM introduces a **BPE Continuation Heuristic**:
If a token $c_t$ is morphologically identified as a sub-word continuation (e.g., lacking the `_` (U+2581) prefix in SentencePiece, or matching continuation byte-patterns in Tiktoken), it inherits the expected semantic weight of its root word:
$$
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_t)] = \begin{cases} 
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_{t-1})], & \text{if } c_t \text{ is a BPE continuation} \\ 
\sum P(pos \mid c_t) \cdot \gamma_{pos}, & \text{otherwise} 
\end{cases}
$$
This stateful tracking structurally protects functional syntax while ensuring long, multi-token visual entities are rigorously penalized if unsupported by visual resonance.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

To definitively validate the structural advantages of BRA—and explicitly address the computational and architectural assumptions inherent in logits-space interventions—we define a rigorous, hypothesis-driven Evaluation Protocol. All planned experiments must be conducted across at least two fundamentally distinct model families (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT [Vicuna] and Qwen-VL [Qwen]), strictly comparing zero-shot and calibrated variants against optimally adapted baselines.

### 4.1 Preliminary Ablations & Bandwidth Stress-Testing
**Objective:** Isolate BRA mechanisms and empirically prove memory bandwidth optimization claims.
**Protocol:**
- **Pooling Paradox Ablation:** Evaluate Base vs. Top-$k$ vs. MeanPool vs. MaxPool vs. PCA on the FREAK (Position) subset.
- **Latency Scaling Curve (Crucial):** Plot Time Per Output Token (TPOT) on the Y-axis against Visual Token Count $N_v$ (from 1k scaling up to extreme 50k tokens) on the X-axis. Compare Base, VCD, and BRA.
- **Hypothesis:** BRA will strictly outperform MeanPool on positional metrics. On the latency curve, while continuous memory caching mitigates bandwidth limits, BRA will exhibit a slight upward curve at $N_v \to 50k$ compared to Base, but its TPOT trajectory will remain substantially flatter and Pareto-superior to multi-pass methods like VCD.

### 4.2 Defense Line 1: Core Hallucination Suppression & Length-Bias Control
**Objective:** Prove BRA mitigates object hallucinations without inducing length collapse.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** POPE, CHAIR (MSCOCO).
- **Baselines:** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa. *Note: Baselines must use their best-known temporal adaptations.*
- **Key Metric:** F1, CHAIR$_S$, and mandatorily **Average Generated Length (AGL)**. 
- **Hypothesis:** Hidden-state interventions often inflate CHAIR scores by forcing premature `<EOS>`. Both BRA variants will lower hallucination rates while maintaining Base AGL. $\text{BRA}_{calib}$ will heavily outscore $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ on architectures with disjointed multimodal spaces.

### 4.3 Defense Line 2: General Intelligence & Language Prior Integrity
**Objective:** Ensure visual logit penalization does not lobotomize instructional compliance.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME.
- **Hypothesis:** Thanks to VASM shielding functional syntax ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma] \approx 0$), BRA will maintain or slightly improve overall multimodal reasoning scores, bypassing the catastrophic degradation typical of contrastive decoding.

### 4.4 Defense Line 3: "Zero-Pooling" Supremacy on Dense Spatial Tasks
**Objective:** Qualitatively and quantitatively expose the "Pooling Paradox" on high-resolution targets.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR) and DocVQA.
- **Spatial Heatmap Mandate:** Extract the Top-$k$ selected spatial coordinates for a specific decoded text token and map them back to the original image grid. 
- **Hypothesis:** DoLa will show severe degradation and blurry global heatmaps due to spatial pooling. BRA's Adaptive Top-$k$ will dynamically produce sharp activation points over exact microscopic text patches, visually proving zero-pooling efficacy.

### 4.5 Defense Line 4: Spatio-Temporal Grounding in Video Narrative
**Objective:** Validate that Adaptive Top-$k$ scales across $T \times H \times W$ without destroying temporal action localization.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** VIDHALLUC and Video-MME.
- **Temporal Histogram & Frame Hit Rate:** Over an aggregate of 50+ video samples, plot the temporal histogram (X: Frame Index $T_i$, Y: Number of Top-$k$ selected tokens). Calculate the **Frame Hit Rate** (percentage of Top-$k$ tokens that fall within the ground-truth temporal window).
- **Hypothesis:** Temporal MeanPool averages out single-frame actions. BRA's Top-$k$ tokens will autonomously cluster exactly at the $T_i$ frame where the action occurs, yielding a $>85\%$ Frame Hit Rate.

### 4.6 Defense Line 5: VASM Ablation & Boundary Failure Cases
**Objective:** Test limits where Language Inertia is beneficial and analyze algorithmic edge-case failures.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMMU (Hard Subset) for VASM ablation; custom tiny-object dataset for failure analysis.
- **Hypothesis (VASM):** The "No-VASM" ablation ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma] = 1.0$) will show catastrophic degradation on MMMU, proving VASM is essential for protecting valid reasoning pathways.
- **Hypothesis (Failure Case):** When evaluating ultra-tiny objects where the true object token count is strictly less than $k_{min}$, BRA will be forced to pull in background noise tokens, potentially causing false resonance. Documenting this bounds the operational limits of $k_{min}$.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to localized logits-space resonance anchoring resolves a structural bottleneck in high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly addressing the computational explosion via bounded candidate sets ($O(M \times N_v)$), overcoming the memory bandwidth wall via pre-fill contiguous caching, and resolving sub-word fragmentation via Stateful VASM, BRA provides a robust pathway to intervene safely without destroying fragile 2D/3D encodings. 

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Bandwidth Limits for Extreme Videos:** Although mathematically optimized via caching, as sequence lengths push toward $>100K$ tokens in ultra-long videos, VRAM memory limits and cache updating overheads during generation could eventually become a hardware bottleneck, necessitating localized chunking strategies.
2. **The $k_{min}$ Dilution Vulnerability:** As mapped out in Defense Line 5, if an object is microscopically small (occupying fewer tokens than $k_{min}$), the Top-$k$ selection is forced to include background tokens, diluting the localized signal. Future dynamic $k_{min}$ bounds based on attention-head entropy could address this.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until the 5-Tier Defense Protocol is fully executed, we limit our claims to the theoretical and algorithmic soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial-temporal bottlenecks of MLLM interventions, offering a mathematically bounded, bandwidth-aware, and BPE-safe blueprint to validate zero-pooling decoding across both static and dynamic multimedia architectures.
