# Bounded Resonance Anchoring: Token-Local Visual Evidence Extraction for Structure-Preserving MLLM Decoding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are prone to hallucination when language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for grounded decoding. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalty) act as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers. However, they do not provide a framework for injecting strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

BRA operates on three concrete mechanisms. First, **token-local logits intervention** maps final-layer visual states into the LLM's latent dimension to evaluate candidate-wise spatial support. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence while allowing irrelevant background patches to mathematically abstain, explicitly avoiding the dilution of global averaging or the noise of forced probability distributions. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via an expanded WordNet-lemmatized dictionary and dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation inheritance. To address the input-output embedding asymmetry inherent in prefix-conditioned MLLMs, we establish a strict evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot probe (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated bound (`BRA_calib`). 

To validate BRA, we propose a rigorous, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol strictly bounded to Image-LLMs and dense 2D spatial grids. The protocol is organized into three definitive evidence chains: (A) Hallucination Reduction (measured alongside Average Generated Length to prevent truncation gaming), (B) Structure Preservation (isolating the necessity of VASM and BPE inheritance), and (C) Local Evidence Value (proving Adaptive Top-$k$ strictly outperforms global mean-pooling). Evaluated across benchmarks including `POPE`, `CHAIR`, `MMBench`, `MMMU`, `FREAK`, and `DocVQA`, this pre-registered protocol establishes a scientifically sound proposition for calibrated, structure-preserving visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is especially damaging in dense grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is fundamentally token-local rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), attention-based pattern penalty (e.g., OPERA), or layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa). These families represent distinct, highly competitive baselines that effectively regularize the language-side or attention-side generation process in orthogonal ways. However, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, defensible hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than pure language-side heuristics, provided that it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We term this framework **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**. In this work, we explicitly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, recognizing that spatiotemporal localization (e.g., video) requires distinct mathematical formulations beyond static grids.

Executing BRA is nontrivial for three structural reasons. First, the **input-output embedding asymmetry**: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, making direct dot-product similarity with the terminal output vocabulary matrix mathematically invalid unless properly projected. Second, the **post-hoc entanglement risk**: assuming the final-layer hidden states of visual patches retain spatial locality requires overcoming deep unidirectional self-attention mixing. Third, the **structural collapse risk**: hallucination suppression easily overfires on Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities unless mathematically protected. 

To address these challenges, BRA centers on three core mechanisms:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, mapping final-layer visual states to the LLM's pre-vocabulary latent dimension to evaluate localized patch support for text candidates.
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations. This prevents background patches from casting forced votes and strictly avoids the spatial dilution of global average pooling.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, a deterministic, structure-preserving protection layer utilizing a WordNet-augmented, lemmatized visual-concept lookup and dynamic BPE continuation inheritance to protect multi-token words.

To ensure rigorous evaluation, we maintain a transparent boundary regarding embedding projection. We deploy a zero-shot probe (`BRA_zero`) to test native alignment. However, acknowledging the high likelihood of spatial washout in 1D-sequence architectures, we back this with a calibrated variant (`BRA_calib`). `BRA_calib` utilizes a lightweight 5k-image projector to overcome the semantic gap without altering the base LLM. We define our experimental design as a stringent protocol testing five lines of defense across three primary evidence chains: 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Proving mitigation against length collapse on POPE/CHAIR alongside Average Generated Length (AGL), explicitly separating zero-shot from calibrated baselines.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving VASM and BPE Inheritance's necessity on MMBench/MME/MMMU(Hard) against unconstrained ablations.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Proving the strict superiority of local evidence on FREAK/DocVQA against functionally identical `BRA_MeanPool` baselines.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Overcoming Post-Hoc Entanglement and Embedding Asymmetry: `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`

A critical vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is the embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input embedding layer. To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states of the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$. 

We explicitly acknowledge the gamble of **post-hoc entanglement**: because these states have passed through deep self-attention, they implicitly mix with the prefix context. For architectures utilizing 1D sequences (e.g., LLaVA), the visual sequence of length $N$ is deterministically reshaped back into a $\sqrt{N} \times \sqrt{N}$ spatial grid. To handle vocabulary projection cleanly, we define two distinct tracks:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support by evaluating what vocabulary each visual patch would independently activate: $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`BRA_calib`**: If `BRA_zero` fails due to spatial washout, we explicitly pivot to a lightweight calibrated decoding method. $\Phi_{calib}: \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{D_{llm}}$ is a single linear layer that projects the visual state to the LLM's final hidden dimension *prior* to $W_{vocab}$. It is trained on a frozen set of 5,000 COCO image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. **Crucially, the base LLM remains entirely frozen.** 

We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. To resolve the multi-token label ambiguity (e.g., "fire hydrant" tokenizing into `_fire`, `_hyd`, `rant`), we define the positive target $W_{vocab}[c^+]$ as the L2-normalized mean of the subword embeddings comprising the ground-truth label $T$:
$$
W_{vocab}[c^+] = \frac{\sum_{i \in T} W_{vocab}[i]}{\|\sum_{i \in T} W_{vocab}[i]\|_2}
$$
The loss is aligned strictly to the terminal output space:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = -\log \frac{\exp(\text{sim}(\Phi_{calib}(h_L^{(v_j)}), W_{vocab}[c^+]) / \tau_{nce})}{\exp(\text{sim}(\Phi_{calib}(h_L^{(v_j)}), W_{vocab}[c^+]) / \tau_{nce}) + \sum_{neg} \exp(\text{sim}(\Phi_{calib}(h_L^{(v_j)}), W_{vocab}[c^-]) / \tau_{nce})}
$$
Negative samples ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are strictly drawn from intra-image patches with an Intersection over Union (IoU) $< 0.1$ with the target bounding box. Unlike pure training-free methods, `BRA_calib` requires a minimal calibrated parameter space, which we visually and categorically separate in our experimental protocols.

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling

At decoding step $t$, BRA strictly bounds the intervention to a safe candidate set to prevent distribution collapse: $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=100$). For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, BRA computes local visual support. 

A naive Softmax over $\mathcal{C}_t$ forces every visual patch to distribute a sum of 1.0, artificially inflating the voting power of irrelevant background patches (e.g., empty sky) for the "least bad" candidate. To prevent this noise injection, we apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation. This allows patches with low semantic overlap to mathematically abstain from voting:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{noise}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{noise}\Big)
$$
To prevent small objects from being washed out by background noise (which occurs in global average pooling), we apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$, where $k_{min}$ acts as an absolute floor to capture single-patch objects. The raw localized support for a candidate $c$ aggregates only its top-$k$ most supportive activated patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c)
$$
The localized evidence is then re-normalized into a relative distribution across the bounded candidate set: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}_{c \in \mathcal{C}_t}(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{norm})$.

### 3.3 VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens that lack local visual backing relative to their peers. Crucially, this penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$ to protect structural integrity:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
The penalty weight is dynamically tied to the entropy of the original candidate distribution: $\alpha = \alpha_0 \cdot \mathcal{H}(\text{Softmax}(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)))$.

Hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing structural tokens or fragmenting multi-token entities. VASM deterministically gates the intervention via a strictly pre-computed dual-tier architecture:
1. **Root Token Expectation (WordNet-Augmented Dictionary):** VASM employs a static $O(1)$ binary mask $\gamma$ where $\gamma=1$ *only* for an unambiguous dictionary of visual object nouns. This dictionary initializes from COCO/LVIS categories (empirically covering $>95\%$ of standard visual benchmark targets) and is systematically expanded using WordNet synsets and lemmatization to cover plurals and common synonyms. Out-of-dictionary esoteric terms safely default to $\gamma=0$, ensuring structural safety over absolute object coverage.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance (Dynamic):** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the dictionary and inherits the exact deterministic $\gamma$ weight of its most recent root token. For instance, the word "rhinoceros" might tokenize as `_rhi`, `no`, `cer`, `os`. VASM matches `_rhi` to the dictionary ($\gamma=1$), and structurally inherits $\gamma=1$ to the subsequent fragments, adjusting their logits synchronously. For byte-level BPE (e.g., Qwen-VL), inheritance triggers via `Ġ`. This completely prevents the mathematical collapse of multi-token entities or hyphenated visual concepts.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces a specific structural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence, we organize the evaluation plan into three falsifiable evidence chains supported by five lines of defense. The protocol mandates explicit architecture specification, utilizing **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector, 1D sequence) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE) to prove generalization. The following structure serves as the registered protocol for pending empirical execution.

### 4.1 Defense Line 1: Locality Verification and Zero-Shot Semantic Validity

**Hypothesis:** The final-layer visual hidden states $h_L^{(v_j)}$ retain spatial locality despite post-hoc entanglement, and occupy a semantically meaningful subspace.
- **Protocol A (Spatial Washout Check):** Generate a side-by-side Cosine Similarity heatmap between the projected hidden states of an image and the text embedding of a known object. For LLaVA (1D sequence reshaped to 2D), we explicitly compare the native `lm_head` projection against the $\Phi_{calib}$ projection.
- **Protocol B (Semantic Overlap Check):** Measure the **Token Overlap Rate** between natively decoded words of $\Phi_{zero}(h_L^{(v_j)})$ and ground-truth object labels.
- **Execution Rule:** If the native Token Overlap Rate is $<5\%$ on 1D-sequence models, or if the `lm_head` heatmaps show diffuse activation, it proves post-hoc entanglement scrambles native alignment. In this scenario, we immediately drop `BRA_zero` and proceed with `BRA_calib` on the frozen LLM.

### 4.2 Chain A (Defense Line 2): Hallucination Reduction vs. Length Collapse

**Hypothesis:** BRA mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting without causing pathological output truncation or length collapse.
- **Planned Execution (Table 1):**
  - **Columns:** Method | Training Images | POPE (Acc/F1) | CHAIRi | CHAIRs | **AGL (Average Generated Length - Mandatory)**
  - **Zero-Shot Rows (Training=0):** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `BRA_zero`
  - **Calibrated Row (Training=5k):** `BRA_calib`
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA_calib` must strictly reduce hallucination metrics compared to the Base model and 0-shot baselines, *without* the Average Generated Length (AGL) dropping by $>10\%$. Improving accuracy solely by dodging generation via truncation falsifies the core mitigation claim.

### 4.3 Chain B (Defense Line 3): Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via local evidence does not degrade complex reasoning, provided that VASM actively protects structural and functional tokens, and BPE Inheritance protects multi-token entities.
- **Planned Execution (Table 2):**
  - **Columns:** MMBench V1.1 | MME | MMMU (Hard Subset)
  - **Rows:** Base | `BRA_no_VASM` | `BRA_no_BPE_Inherit` | `BRA_full`
- **Execution Rule:** `BRA_no_VASM` applies the exact same visual penalty weight $\alpha$ to *all* tokens (removing the $\gamma(c)$ gate). `BRA_no_BPE_Inherit` uses VASM but forces $\gamma=0$ for all BPE continuations (masking only the root token).
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA_no_VASM` must demonstrate a catastrophic reasoning drop on logic-heavy subsets like `MMMU (Hard)`, proving unconstrained visual suppression destroys language structures. `BRA_no_BPE_Inherit` must show degradation due to multi-token word fragmentation. Conversely, `BRA_full` must match or slightly beat the Base model, isolating VASM and BPE Inheritance as mathematically indispensable.

### 4.4 Chain C (Defense Line 4): The Necessity of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting is significantly more faithful than coarse global visual aggregation.
- **Planned Execution (Table 3):**
  - **Columns:** FREAK (OCR/Position) | DocVQA (ANLS)
  - **Rows:** Base | `BRA_RandomK` | `BRA_MeanPool` | `BRA_AdaptiveTopK`
- **Execution Rule:** `BRA_MeanPool` *must* use the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$ projector and VASM mask as `BRA_AdaptiveTopK`. The *only* difference must be that `MeanPool` averages all $N_v$ normalized patches, while `AdaptiveTopK` selects the subset.
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA_AdaptiveTopK` must strictly and significantly outperform both global aggregation (`BRA_MeanPool`) and arbitrary noise injection (`BRA_RandomK`).

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of BRA relies on a clean, verifiable scientific contract: it intervenes at decode time, leverages token-local visual evidence over random noise or global pooling, preserves structural integrity via VASM, and establishes transparent performance boundaries. However, execution of this framework carries specific theoretical limitations:

1. **Embedding Alignment and Parametric Asymmetry:** `BRA_zero` tests the absolute limits of the residual stream's ability to overcome post-hoc entanglement. If spatial washout in 1D-sequence architectures destroys native alignment, `BRA_calib` becomes a strict requirement. We explicitly brand this as a *calibrated decoding method*, requiring a 5,000-image frozen projector projecting from $D_{vision}$ to $D_{llm}$. This introduces a minor parametric asymmetry compared to purely training-free heuristics (VCD, DoLa), which we mandate separating in all empirical reporting.
2. **VASM's Closed-Vocabulary Ceiling:** By relying on a predefined WordNet-augmented dictionary for $\gamma=1$, BRA mathematically cannot ground completely novel, out-of-dictionary concepts. While empirical coverage of standard benchmarks (COCO/LVIS) exceeds 95%, highly esoteric domain-specific visual entities inherently default to $\gamma=0$ and receive no localized anchoring. This is a known limit accepted to guarantee structural safety over absolute object coverage.
3. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** BRA fundamentally operates by reweighting the Top-$M$ language priors. It mathematically cannot generate a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$. 
4. **Restriction to Image Spatial Grids:** The current formulation of Adaptive Top-$k$ pooling relies strictly on 2D spatial extraction. We explicitly bound this paper to Image-LLMs and dense spatial reasoning, leaving the formulation of spatiotemporal local evidence (for video) to future work.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines **Bounded Resonance Anchoring**, a positive, falsifiable framework for mitigating multimodal hallucinations through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By explicitly mapping the embedding asymmetry risk, fixing patch magnitude dominance via threshold-gated Top-$k$ pooling, and formalizing VASM with tokenizer-aware BPE inheritance, we establish a rigorous foundation for calibrated decode-time control. The claims herein are strictly bounded by the proposed five-line evaluation protocol spanning Hallucination Reduction, Structure Preservation, and Local Evidence Value.
