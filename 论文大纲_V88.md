# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Structure-Preserving Hybrid Routing Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, including visual-noise contrastive decoding (VCD), attention-penalty control (OPERA), and layer-contrast methods (DoLa), are strong competitive baselines. However, as primarily language-side heuristics or global-noise interventions, they do not directly provide a framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, an intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from static prefill visual states.

TLRA is built on three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse visual contexts with candidate-wise support computed dynamically against the frozen visual KV-cache. Second, **Adaptive Top-k resonance** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via an offline, pre-computed WordNet-based boolean mask. To solve subword bleeding (where a subword like "ator" belongs to both physical entities and abstract concepts), VASM employs conservative conflict resolution, ensuring syntax preservation without catastrophic online parsing latency. 

To enforce fair comparison, we explicitly separate a zero-shot architectural preliminary (`TLRA_zero`) from the main calibrated variant (`TLRA_calib`). Acknowledging that `TLRA_calib` requires a lightweight, InfoNCE-trained visual-to-lexical projector, it is explicitly classified as a **hybrid projection-routing method**, not a purely training-free intervention. Consequently, its evaluation contract mandates a direct, parameter-matched comparison against a LoRA fine-tuning of the base MLLM using the exact same calibration data.

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize the paper around a strictly pre-registered experimental contract. The protocol locks all intervention hyperparameters on a single validation set and evaluates three evidence chains: hallucination reduction audited against Average Generation Length (AGL) and Peak VRAM, structure preservation with OCR-heavy tasks treated as strict negative controls, and local-evidence parity under identical calibrator weights. All localized-routing claims must pass parity ablations against `TLRA_MeanPool` and the `Base+LoRA` data-matched control. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable proposal for structure-preserving hybrid visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible but weakly supported by the visual input. This failure is especially damaging in **fine-grained physical grounding** scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the relevant evidence is localized rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances in the field. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) inject visual noise to compare distributions under perturbed and clean inputs. Attention-based control methods (`OPERA`) penalize pathological autoregressive attention patterns. Layer-contrast methods (like MM-adapted `DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep language-model representations. These are all legitimate, training-free baselines. However, because they rely primarily on language-side distribution shifts or global visual perturbations, they leave a specific, complementary question unanswered:

**How can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence into logits adjustment without destroying token generation speed or damaging language structure?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a hybrid framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics or global visual pooling, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. The proposal is intentionally bounded. It targets physical-entity grounding under a controlled image-centric scientific contract.

Executing this intervention is nontrivial for four reasons. First, **the causal attention barrier**: in decoder-only MLLMs, visual tokens are processed during the prefill stage and remain causally isolated (frozen) from the dynamically unfolding text, meaning their representations do not natively adapt to the generated prefix. Second, **embedding asymmetry**: these static visual states and dynamic lexical logits do not natively share an embedding space without a bridging projection. Third, **system cost ($O(M \times N_v)$)**: computing dense similarity between $M$ candidates and thousands of static visual tokens inside the autoregressive loop threatens to hit severe memory-bandwidth limits, destroying `tokens_per_second` and spiking VRAM. Fourth, **structural collapse risk**: naive intervention destroys syntax and long entities, exacerbated by "subword bleeding" where Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) tokens are shared across protected and non-protected words.

To address these issues, TLRA is built upon four explicit commitments:

1. **Hybrid projection-routing intervention** replacing purely training-free claims, enforced by a mandatory data-matched LoRA baseline.
2. **Adaptive Top-k resonance** over static prefill visual states, evaluated under strict latency and Peak VRAM accounting.
3. **Offline, pre-computed VASM** featuring conservative BPE conflict resolution, functioning as a zero-latency structure-preserving shield.
4. **A strict zero/calibrated split** acknowledging the architectural limitations of direct readout (`TLRA_zero`) and focusing evaluation on the calibrated `TLRA_calib`.

To keep the paper scientifically honest, TLRA enforces strict boundary rules. If `TLRA_calib` fails to outperform a standard LoRA trained on the exact same calibration subset, the complex decode-time routing claim is falsified. Furthermore, TLRA accepts an **OCR concession**: because VASM intentionally masks arbitrary text strings to protect syntax, OCR-heavy tasks are treated purely as negative controls.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: The `TLRA_zero` Preliminary vs. `TLRA_calib`

We explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks to isolate architectural viability from calibration gains:

1. **`TLRA_zero` (Architectural Preliminary)**: A strict zero-shot probe where the model-native LM head is applied directly to prefill visual states. Due to the severe embedding asymmetry in decoder-only architectures—where the LM head expects deeply processed text states, not shallow visual encodings—this stage mathematically expects near-random performance. It is included purely to prove the necessity of a bridging projection.
2. **`TLRA_calib` (Main Intervention)**: A hybrid projection-routing variant where a lightweight, 2-layer MLP projection `Phi_calib` maps static prefill visual states into the dynamic lexical space. The base MLLM remains strictly frozen.

Because `Phi_calib` is trained via an InfoNCE-style contrastive objective on a conceptual-caption subset, `TLRA_calib` is **not a training-free method**. Any claim about decode-time local routing must therefore survive a mandatory parity ablation against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset, in addition to `TLRA_MeanPool` using the identical frozen `Phi_calib`.

### 3.2 Static Prefill Visual States and Adaptive Top-k Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M$ is a globally locked candidate window.

Crucially, in decoder-only MLLMs utilizing KV-caching, visual tokens are processed exclusively during the prefill stage. Due to the causal mask, **visual representations do not adapt as the sentence unfolds**. Let $h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}$ denote the static final-layer LLM hidden states at the visual token positions, cached from the prefill stage. The visual token volume is $N_v = H \times W$.

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support by comparing the candidate's projected semantic embedding against these static visual states:
$$
k = \max(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil)
$$
where $\rho$, $k_{min}$, $\tau_{sim}$, and $\tau_{evidence}$ are locked from a single validation split. 

The raw local support is:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}), c)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
This introduces an $O(M \times N_v)$ complexity per generation step. For modern high-res MLLMs where $N_v > 4000$, this dense operation is heavily memory-bound. We execute this via highly optimized batched tensor operations, and its latency and Peak VRAM overhead are explicitly modeled in our evaluation.

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw local support into a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
To avoid uncontrolled distortion, TLRA applies a scaled penalty anchored to the local logit range $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)) - \min(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t))$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \Delta_L \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Offline VASM and Subword Conflict Resolution

A naive online POS tagger would cause catastrophic latency and fail on partial autoregressive prefixes. TLRA solves this using an **offline, pre-computed Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Mask (VASM)**.

VASM is a static boolean mask $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$ over the entire vocabulary $V$:
- $M_{VASM}(c) = 1$ for visually groundable physical-entity root tokens and their BPE continuations.
- $M_{VASM}(c) = 0$ for structurally protected functional tokens, syntax, and excluded concepts.

The mask is generated offline:
1. **Automated root-token lookup:** We use WordNet synsets to isolate physical entities, explicitly excluding abstract concepts.
2. **Deterministic BPE inheritance:** Subword continuations inherit the mask state of their root token. 
3. **Conservative Subword Conflict Resolution:** A critical vulnerability is "subword bleeding" (e.g., the BPE token "ator" appearing in "refrigerator" [groundable] and "senator" [abstract]). To prevent structural damage, VASM applies **conservative intersection**: if a subword is shared between a groundable entity and *any* protected functional/abstract word, its mask is forced to $0$ (protected).

**Inherent Limitations:** Because VASM is static and conservative, it inherently suffers from polysemy failures (e.g., masking out "mouse" as an animal if the computer device sense was dominant/protected). Furthermore, arbitrary text-in-image strings receive zero coverage, cementing our **OCR concession** where `DocVQA` serves as a strict negative control.

### 3.5 Fallback Claims

TLRA explicitly does not claim zero-shot superiority without a projector, nor does it claim OCR competence. Crucially, if `TLRA_calib` is outperformed by `Base + LoRA (Phi_calib data)`, the paper must contract its claim entirely, acknowledging that the performance gains stem from the conceptual-caption training data rather than the token-local decode-time routing mechanism.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around one architectural preliminary, three main evidence chains, and strict efficiency/boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Architectural Preliminary (`TLRA_zero`)

**Goal:** Mathematically verify the embedding asymmetry between static prefill visual states and dynamic LM heads in decoder-only models.
- **Protocol:** Evaluate direct dot-product zero-shot routing on a miniature held-out subset.
- **Expected Outcome:** Near-random performance due to dimensional and semantic drift, confirming the absolute necessity of `Phi_calib` and immediately transitioning the evaluation to `TLRA_calib`.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Baseline Fairness

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, and strictly verify that gains are not merely artifacts of the calibration data or sequence truncation.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Core Baselines:** Base, `VCD` (Visual Noise), `MM-DoLa` (Layer Contrast).
- **Mandatory Data-Matched Baseline:** `Base + LoRA (Phi_calib data)` using the exact same training subset and parameter budget.
- **Metrics:** Hallucination score, `AGL`, `AGL StdDev`.
- **Hard Rule:** If `TLRA_calib` loses to the `LoRA` baseline, the hybrid routing claim is falsified.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention preserves general capability and that VASM functions as a zero-overhead structural shield.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard)`.
- **Negative Control:** `DocVQA` (VASM trigger coverage must approach zero).
- **Ablation Rows:** Base, `TLRA_full` (with VASM), `TLRA_no_VASM`.
- **Latency Audit:** Explicit comparison of `tokens/s` between `TLRA_full` and `TLRA_no_VASM` to prove the offline mask $M_{VASM}$ adds near-zero latency overhead.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence Value and Leakage Control

**Goal:** Isolate whether the $O(M \times N_v)$ local routing computation provides value over global pooling, given the exact same projector.
- **Benchmark:** `FREAK` (Spatial Relations, Object Existence, Counting).
- **Routing Parity Rows:** `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Strict Rule:** Both variants must share the identical frozen `Phi_calib`.
- **Leakage Rule:** Results must be segmented into Seen vs. Unseen categories based on the calibrator catalog to prove routing generalization.

### 4.5 Efficiency and Failure-Boundary Audits

**Goal:** Establish the actual deployment cost (memory and compute) and document qualitative failure modes.
- **Mandatory Metrics:** `ITL` (Inter-Token Latency), `tokens_per_second`, and crucially, **`Peak VRAM (GB)`**.
- **Data Scaling Hypothesis (Appendix):** An ablation of `Phi_calib` trained on 10k, 50k, and 100k samples compared to equivalent LoRA models to test if routing superiority holds at scale.
- **Failure Analysis Requirements:** 
  1. Documentation of VASM polysemy failures.
  2. **Out-of-candidate Hijacking:** Documentation of at least one case where the correct entity is absent from the Top-$M$ candidates, causing TLRA to wrongly anchor a hallucinated candidate to a visually salient but incorrect image region.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is the one with the cleanest, most unforgiving scientific contract:
- It explicitly frames itself as a hybrid projection-routing method, bounding its claims against a matched-data `Base + LoRA` baseline to prevent attribution errors.
- It utilizes an offline, conservative VASM to preserve structure without destroying autoregressive generation speed.
- It corrects the causal attention fallacy by routing against static, prefill-extracted visual states, avoiding $O(N^2)$ recomputation.
- It acknowledges and models both the latency ($O(M \times N_v)$) and the heavy memory-bandwidth (Peak VRAM) costs of dense dot-products in the decoding loop.

The primary unresolved risks and acknowledged limitations are:
1. **The LoRA Substitution Risk:** `TLRA_calib` may fail to significantly outperform `Base + LoRA (Phi_calib data)`, indicating that the training data—not the decode-time routing—drives the performance.
2. **VASM Polysemy and Conservative Bleeding:** Because VASM employs conservative conflict resolution for subword bleeding, it may over-protect certain valid physical entities if they share BPE tokens with abstract syntax, reducing intervention recall.
3. **Out-of-Candidate Hijacking:** If the true visual entity is missing from the LM's Top-$M$ candidate window, TLRA's dynamic routing acts as a forced multiplier on the remaining incorrect candidates, potentially anchoring a hallucination to visual noise.
4. **Hardware Bottlenecks:** For extremely high-resolution models ($N_v \gg 4000$), the dense $O(M \times N_v)$ dot-product imposes strict memory bandwidth limits, restricting TLRA to small $M$ values to prevent VRAM overflow.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)

Until the protocol is fully executed, the paper claims only:
- A falsifiable, data-matched hybrid routing framework for token-local visual evidence operating on static prefill KV-states.
- An offline masking mechanism (VASM) with conservative conflict resolution that avoids catastrophic online parsing latency.
- A strict parity test of local routing vs. coarse pooling and data-matched LoRA baselines under explicit VRAM accounting.

The paper strictly does not claim:
- Superiority over baselines without accounting for the calibration data advantage.
- Online contextual understanding of syntax (VASM is static).
- Dynamic temporal adaptation of visual states during generation.
