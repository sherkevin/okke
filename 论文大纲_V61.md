# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Logits Intervention for Multimodal Hallucination Mitigation

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for the next decoding step. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa), are important competitive baselines, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual patches. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window, making the method robust for spatial grounding, small-object recognition, and counting. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via deterministic root-token lookup and BPE continuation inheritance. Because strict zero-shot intervention often collapses due to visual-lexical embedding asymmetry, TLRA utilizes a lightweight, domain-agnostic calibrator (`TLRA_calib`) trained purely on generic image-text pairs, leaving the base MLLM entirely frozen to ensure fair comparison against training-free baselines. 

We organize the paper around a strict experimental contract: three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction (measured alongside average generated length to prevent length-collapse artifacts), structure and reasoning preservation, and the specific value of local over global evidence. To guarantee practical viability, we incorporate explicit efficiency limits and out-of-candidate failure audits. Furthermore, we retain a bounded secondary video pilot to test whether the token-local control principle can extend into spatio-temporal multimodal decoding. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the relevant evidence is distinctly local rather than global. 

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate, highly competitive baselines, but none of them directly answer the following question:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

That question is the center of this paper. We advance a direct positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided it includes explicit structure-preserving safeguards. 

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to four foundational challenges. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and output logits do not naturally live in the same space, making strict zero-shot lookup noisy. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc logits intervention can only reweight candidates that the base model already keeps alive. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression of visually unsupported tokens easily damages function words, syntax, and long entities. Fourth, **system cost**: patch-level support extraction at every generation step scales poorly at high visual resolutions.

TLRA therefore centers on three design commitments:

1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention** utilizing a lightweight, domain-agnostic calibrator to bridge embedding asymmetry without altering the base MLLM.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** as the local evidence extractor over a strictly bounded candidate set to ensure computational feasibility.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)** as the structure-preserving protection layer. To guarantee syntax safety, VASM relies on precomputed lexical filtering (e.g., POS-tagged vocabularies targeting visual nouns/adjectives). We explicitly acknowledge the *OCR paradox*: by relying on dictionaries to protect syntax, VASM natively masks out out-of-vocabulary (OOV) arbitrary OCR strings. Consequently, TLRA scopes its core claim strictly to spatial object grounding and counting, deliberately omitting OCR-heavy documents from its primary motivation.

To maintain scientific rigor, this paper avoids "fishing expeditions" and commits to a definitive evaluation protocol. The primary scientific contract is image-centric, establishing three distinct chains of evidence: hallucination reduction, structural preservation, and local-evidence validation against coarse pooling. A bounded **secondary video pilot** tests early transferability to spatio-temporal inputs, serving as a secondary diagnostic rather than an unsupported claim of complete image-video unification.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. In our preliminary viability probes, we found that a strict zero-shot readout (`TLRA_zero`) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yields near-random overlap. The embedding asymmetry between the vision encoder's output space and the LM's vocabulary space is too large. 

Therefore, the definitive intervention is **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain a fair comparison against zero-shot inference baselines (like DoLa and VCD), the calibration protocol is strictly constrained:
1. **Lightweight & Domain-Agnostic:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib}$, to map final-layer visual states to the LM's embedding space. 
2. **Generic Data Only:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained solely on a generic image-caption dataset (e.g., CC3M subset) using a standard token-level contrastive loss. It is *never* trained on downstream task data (e.g., POPE, MMBench, or specific reasoning instructions).
3. **Frozen Base Model:** The vision encoder and the LLM remain 100% frozen. During inference, $\Phi_{calib}$ acts purely as a plug-in state-translator for the decode-time intervention.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA must avoid the $O(|V| \times H \times W)$ cost of evaluating the entire vocabulary against all visual patches. We first restrict intervention to a strictly bounded candidate set:

$$
\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig}),
$$

where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=10$) is a small candidate window derived from the original logits.

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the contextualized visual states from the final layer, translated via $\Phi_{calib}$, with a total visual token volume $N_v = H \times W$. For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes candidate-wise local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule:

$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big),
$$

followed by:

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

Here, `score(v_j, c)` is the cosine similarity between the projected patch representation and the embedding of candidate $c$. Adaptive Top-$k$ preserves spatial locality while resisting the noise sensitivity of pure max-matching. Crucially, calculating this exclusively for $M$ candidates bounds the decode-time overhead to $O(M \times N_v)$.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a stable logits intervention, TLRA computes a relative evidence score over the bounded candidate set:

$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$

The final candidate logits are adjusted as:

$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

This relative formulation emphasizes the suppression of weakly supported candidates relative to stronger local competitors, rather than arbitrarily rewarding tokens and destabilizing the softmax distribution.

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses useful language structure. If a function word like "the" or "is" lacks localized visual support, TLRA would normally penalize it, leading to grammar collapse. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect these tokens.

VASM operates via a binary mask $\gamma(c)$:
- $\gamma(c) = 1$: Intervention applied.
- $\gamma(c) = 0$: Intervention bypassed ($L_{final} = L_{orig}$).

To guarantee reproducibility, VASM is constructed via two rules:
1. **Precomputed Lexical Lookup:** We construct an intervention whitelist by POS-tagging the tokenizer's vocabulary (e.g., via NLTK/WordNet), explicitly targeting visual nouns, colors, and physical adjectives. All functional syntax, stopwords, and unrecognized arbitrary text default to $\gamma = 0$.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Language models frequently fragment words into subwords (e.g., "sk" + "ate" + "board"). BPE continuation fragments (often denoted by `##` or `Ġ`) automatically inherit the $\gamma$ mask value of their immediate root token to prevent partial-word corruption.

**The OCR Concession:** Because VASM defaults to masking out out-of-vocabulary and non-dictionary text to protect syntax, it inherently bypasses arbitrary OCR strings. We consider this a necessary trade-off: ensuring structural integrity is prioritized over intervening on in-the-wild text. Consequently, TLRA explicitly does not claim to solve OCR-heavy hallucinations.

### 3.5 What TLRA Does Not Claim

To maintain scientific integrity, this framework establishes strict boundary conditions:
- `TLRA_calib` is an inference-time intervention, but it is *not* 100% training-free (it requires $\Phi_{calib}$).
- VASM sacrifices intervention on OOV/OCR tokens to guarantee zero structural collapse.
- TLRA cannot recover a correct token if it falls outside the initial Top-$M$ candidate set.
- The secondary video pilot probes temporal transferability but does *not* claim a solved unified video-image architecture.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Rather than presenting cherry-picked subsets, our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The evaluation is structured into three interlocking evidence chains, an efficiency scaling audit, and a bounded secondary video pilot.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length-Collapse Check)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces object hallucination and that this reduction is not a deceptive artifact of the model generating shorter, truncated responses.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE` (Accuracy / F1), `CHAIR` ($CHAIR_s$, $CHAIR_i$).
- **Baselines:** Base MLLM, `VCD`, `OPERA`, `DoLa`.
- **Mandatory Control Metric:** Average Generated Length (`AGL`).
- **Hypothesis:** TLRA will yield a lower `CHAIR` score and higher `POPE` F1 than the base model and purely linguistic baselines (DoLa), while maintaining an `AGL` within 5% of the Base model.

*Execution Contract for Table 1:* Columns must strictly follow `Method | POPE (Acc/F1) | CHAIR_s | CHAIR_i | AGL`. Any mitigation method that achieves state-of-the-art CHAIR scores purely by collapsing AGL will be flagged as a false positive.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The VASM Ablation)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not irreparably degrade general multimodal cognition, and prove that VASM is the critical mechanism preventing structural collapse.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1` (Overall), `MME` (Cognition Subset), `MMMU` (Hard).
- **Ablation Setup:** Base model vs. `TLRA_no_VASM` vs. `TLRA_full`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_no_VASM` will suffer catastrophic performance drops on cognition and complex reasoning tasks due to the penalization of functional syntax and partial BPE tokens. `TLRA_full` must recover these scores to equal or slightly exceed the Base model.

*Execution Contract for Table 2:* This table isolates the specific contribution of VASM. If `TLRA_no_VASM` performs adequately, the VASM component will be conceded as redundant.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate the core thesis that *token-local adaptive selection* is superior to coarse visual aggregation for fine-grained spatial grounding tasks.
- **Benchmarks:** `FREAK` (Position / Counting subsets), `Object HalBench`. (DocVQA is explicitly excluded due to the OCR paradox detailed in Section 3.4).
- **Ablation Setup:** Base vs. `DoLa` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool` (where $k = N_v$) vs. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Hypothesis:** For localized phenomena like counting or spatial relation, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will demonstrate a statistically significant margin over both language-only layer-contrast (`DoLa`) and global visual aggregation (`TLRA_MeanPool`).

*Execution Contract for Table 3:* Top-$k$ must cleanly beat MeanPool to justify the computational overhead of candidate-patch similarity tracking.

### 4.4 Efficiency Hard Bounds and Failure Analysis

TLRA introduces step-wise patch-candidate computations. To prove practical viability, we enforce an explicit efficiency audit.
- **Figure 1 (Efficiency Scaling):** A line chart plotting `Tokens/Second` (Y-axis) against increasing `Visual Token Volume` $N_v$ (X-axis) for Base, VCD, and TLRA. 
- **Efficiency Constraint:** If TLRA reduces tokens-per-second by more than 50% compared to base decoding at standard resolutions ($N_v \approx 576$), it will be declared computationally unviable for real-time applications.

**Qualitative Failure Audits:** We mandate the inclusion of two specific failure modes:
1. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** An instance where the correct visual token was ranked $> M$ by the base model, demonstrating the absolute limitation of post-hoc logits reweighting.
2. **VASM Masking Error:** An instance where VASM conservatively masked a valid visual concept (treating it as an OOV or structural token), causing TLRA to bypass intervention and fail to prevent a hallucination.

### 4.5 Bounded Secondary Video Pilot

**Goal:** Test whether the token-local Top-$k$ principle exhibits early transferability to spatio-temporal inputs.
- **Protocol:** A secondary probe on `VIDHALLUC` or `Video-MME` evaluating whether localized frame-level evidence provides a similar hallucination reduction trend. 
- **Boundary:** This pilot serves purely as a transferability diagnostic. Positive results strengthen the broader multimedia relevance, but video unification is not a co-equal claim required for the validation of the primary spatial TLRA framework.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its actual empirical execution. The core scientific contribution is a positive, decode-time framework that leverages token-local visual evidence while deploying VASM to prevent the structural collapse of generated language.

However, several hard limitations define the boundaries of this method:
1. **The OCR / OOV Paradox:** To guarantee the safety of functional syntax and multi-token entities, VASM relies on precomputed lexical anchors. This determinism explicitly sacrifices intervention on out-of-vocabulary strings, making TLRA ineffective for arbitrary OCR transcription tasks.
2. **Reliance on Calibration:** Due to severe embedding asymmetry in standard MLLMs, a strict zero-shot implementation (`TLRA_zero`) functions poorly. The requirement of `TLRA_calib` ($\Phi_{calib}$) means the framework is not 100% training-free, though the base model remains entirely frozen and the calibrator requires no downstream task data.
3. **Computational Overhead at High Resolutions:** The $O(M \times N_v)$ step-wise complexity creates an inherent trade-off between the size of the Top-$M$ candidate window and decoding latency, especially as vision encoders push toward extreme resolutions ($N_v > 2000$).
4. **Candidate-Bound Ceiling:** Like all post-hoc logits interventions (including DoLa and VCD), TLRA cannot resurrect a correct token if the base model's internal probability assigns it a rank lower than $M$. 

By explicitly declaring these limitations, we establish TLRA not as a panacea for all multimodal errors, but as a scientifically principled, highly targeted mechanism for correcting spatial and object-level visual grounding during generation.
