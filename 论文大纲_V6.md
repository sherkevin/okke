# Breaking the Language Inertia: Localized Resonance Anchoring for High-Resolution MLLM Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate high-resolution visual tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D-RoPE spatial coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., Document VQA or GUI navigation). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space via localized top-$k$ visual matching. 

To resolve critical mathematical flaws in prior logits-based interventions, BRA incorporates three core mechanisms: (1) **Mixed-Domain Modality Projection**, establishing a universal semantic mapping robust to artificial domains; (2) **Localized Top-$k$ Resonance**, which isolates fine-grained spatial features without the extreme statistical vulnerability of pure max-pooling; and (3) **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, replacing brittle predictive entropy with a static, pre-computed syntactic mask to protect continuous BPE fragments and functional syntax from unwarranted penalization. We establish a rigorous 6-stage experimental protocol—including dense OCR tasks, Syntax Preservation metrics, and False-Positive evaluations—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy, the cross-domain validity of latent alignment, and the Pareto-optimal system efficiency of BRA across continuous and dynamic-resolution MLLM architectures.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model hallucinates—producing text that is linguistically fluent but physically absent from the visual input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on contrastive decoding or backtracking (e.g., VCD, OPERA). While effective, they introduce significant latency overheads (Time-Per-Output-Token spikes) and VRAM bloat. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution MLLMs, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool or PCA) thousands of high-resolution visual tokens. This operation irreversibly destroys the fine-grained spatial coordinates (like 2D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects and dense text, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or artificial GUI navigation.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a localized match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial information. However, implementing this principle exposes several mathematical vulnerabilities: the unproven assumption of zero-shot modality alignment across diverse domains, the extreme noise sensitivity of pure max-pooling over thousands of visual tokens, and the "Entropy Trap" where contextually predictable hallucinations evade penalization.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol designed to resolve these vulnerabilities. Our methodological structural upgrades include:
1. **Localized Top-$k$ Resonance**: Transitioning from a statistically dangerous absolute max-pooling to a Top-$k$ mean formulation, smoothing out high-frequency visual noise while explicitly avoiding the spatial destruction of global pooling.
2. **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**: Replacing flawed predictive entropy with an offline, POS-guided masking mechanism to mathematically guarantee the structural protection of functional syntax and BPE fragments.
3. **Rigorous 6-stage Evaluation Protocol**: Expanding the experimental blueprint to explicitly test cross-domain modality alignment ($\Phi$), quantitative syntax preservation, dense spatial preservation (DocVQA/ChartQA), false-positive noise rejection, and the true Time/VRAM Pareto frontier across both continuous (e.g., LLaVA-1.5) and dynamic-resolution (e.g., InternVL) architectures.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 The Modality Gap and Mixed-Domain Resonance Formulation

The core hypothesis of BRA is that intervention should occur in the logits space to avoid pooling high-resolution spatial tokens. Given a candidate token set $\mathcal{C}_t = \{c_1, \dots, c_K\}$ at decoding step $t$, we measure the visual support for each candidate. 

A critical vulnerability in naive logits-space intervention is the assumption of topological orthogonality—whether the last-layer visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ reside in the exact same semantic manifold as the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$. To bridge this, we formalize the raw resonance score $S_{raw}(c)$ through an alignment mapping $\Phi$. To prevent the "Protocol 0 Fallacy" (where an adapter trained solely on natural images fails on dense OCR), $\Phi$ is designed as a lightweight linear projector trained via contrastive loss on a **mixed-domain dataset** (comprising MSCOCO, TextCaps, and a subset of GUI elements).

### 3.2 Localized Top-$k$ Resonance for Dense Spatial Preservation

Unlike hidden-state methods (e.g., DoLa) that compute a globally pooled visual vector $\bar{v} = \frac{1}{N_v} \sum h_L^{(v_j)}$ to establish an intervention direction, BRA explicitly isolates fine-grained features. However, operating over $1024 \times 1024$ images produces thousands of tokens; relying on a pure $\max_{j}$ operator creates extreme statistical vulnerability, where a hallucinated text token is highly likely to find spurious high cosine similarity with at least one background noise patch.

To resolve this, we formulate **Localized Top-$k$ Resonance**. We compute the mean resonance of the most highly activated $k$ local patches (where $k$ is an ultra-small localized cluster, e.g., $k \in \{2, 3, 4\}$):

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \cos\left(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)})\right)
$$

This strictly avoids the Pooling Paradox by isolating the specific region of interest (e.g., a tiny bounding box containing text) while successfully smoothing out spurious, single-patch visual noise.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty 

To map the raw resonance scores into stable logits modifications without introducing over-parameterized hyper-sensitivity, we compute the relative evidence position within the Top-K candidates:

$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}_{\tau}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$

The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:

$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

where $\alpha$ is a global penalty strength, and $\gamma(c)$ is a token-aware semantic mask. Tokens with high relative visual support ($\hat S(c) \approx 1$) receive near-zero penalty, while ungrounded tokens are linearly suppressed.

### 3.4 Escaping the Entropy Trap: Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)

Applying visual resonance blindly at the terminal token level causes semantic collapse for multi-token entities (e.g., splitting "play" and "ing") and destroys functional syntax ("therefore", "however"). Previous iterations attempted to use predictive entropy $H_t$ to scale the penalty, operating under the flawed assumption that low entropy correlates strictly with syntax. This creates an **"Entropy Trap"**: strong language priors inherently produce low-entropy predictions for predictable *hallucinated* objects (e.g., generating "keyboard" after "mouse and..."), effectively turning off the visual penalty for the most stubborn hallucinations.

We abandon runtime statistical heuristics in favor of mathematical guarantees via **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**. We introduce a static, one-time computed vocabulary mask mapping $W_{vocab}$ tokens to their syntactic weight $\gamma(c) \in [0, 1]$ based on extensive corpus POS (Part-of-Speech) tagging:

1. **Semantic Entities ($\gamma = 1.0$):** Tokens classified primarily as Nouns, Verbs, and Adjectives. Full resonance penalty is applied, enforcing strict visual grounding for objects and actions.
2. **Functional Syntax & BPE Fragments ($\gamma = 0.0$):** Tokens classified as prepositions, conjunctions, punctuation, and dependent BPE sub-words (e.g., tokens lacking a leading space like `##ing`). The penalty naturally drops to zero ($L_{final} = L_{orig}$), ensuring tokenizer-agnostic structural preservation with $O(1)$ runtime overhead.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

This section outlines the rigorous, hypothesis-driven evaluation blueprint required to empirically validate BRA. To stress-test spatial preservation, baselines will explicitly include both a continuous-resolution MLLM (e.g., LLaVA-1.5) and a dynamic-resolution MLLM (e.g., InternVL or LLaVA-UHD).

### 4.1 Protocol 0: Mixed-Domain $\Phi$ Transferability Probe
**Hypothesis:** A linear $\Phi$ trained on a diverse domain mixture can bridge the modality gap without memorizing dataset-specific visual priors, generalizing to unseen artificial datasets.
**Experiment:** 
- Train $\Phi$ using contrastive loss on a mixed dataset (MSCOCO + TextCaps + UI subsets).
- **Evaluation:** Apply the *frozen* $\Phi$ directly to unseen datasets: ChartQA and VisualWebBench. 
- **Success Criteria:** Beyond qualitative t-SNE, we will compute the **Recall@K** of ground-truth visual-text token pairs. $\Phi$ must achieve a Recall@5 significantly higher than random projection on unseen domains to justify the plug-and-play claim.

### 4.2 Protocol 1: Static Hallucination Suppression
**Hypothesis:** BRA suppresses object hallucination in standard natural images.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: POPE, CHAIR, MME.
- Baselines: Base decoding, VCD, OPERA, DoLa.
- **Success Criteria:** BRA must achieve equivalent or superior F1 scores to DoLa and VCD while maintaining the Average Generation Length (AGL) within 5% of the Base model to prove it does not cause length collapse.

### 4.3 Protocol 2: The "Pooling Paradox" Proof via Dense Spatial Tasks
**Hypothesis:** Standard hidden-state interventions destroy 2D spatial features. BRA’s localized Top-$k$ match preserves them.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: **DocVQA, ChartQA**, and VisualWebBench.
- **Mandatory Ablation Table:** Compare (1) Base Model, (2) DoLa, (3) `BRA-MeanPool` (where $S_{raw}$ uses globally pooled visual tokens), and (4) `BRA-TopK`.
- **Success Criteria:** While DoLa and `BRA-MeanPool` may perform well on POPE (Protocol 1), they must show significant degradation on dense spatial tasks. `BRA-TopK` must dramatically outperform Rows 2 and 3 on DocVQA, definitively proving the Pooling Paradox.

### 4.4 Protocol 3: Quantitative Syntax Preservation (VASM Validation)
**Hypothesis:** VASM mathematically prevents semantic collapse by protecting syntax and BPE fragments from unwarranted visual penalties.
**Experiment:**
- **Execution:** Generate responses over 1,000 queries. Parse the outputs using a POS tagger (e.g., SpaCy).
- **Metric:** Calculate the "Syntax Preservation Rate"—measuring the probability mass perturbation ($\Delta P$) applied by BRA specifically to Verbs/Nouns vs. Conjunctions/Prepositions/Sub-words.
- **Success Criteria:** The perturbation drop for functional syntax must mathematically approximate zero, proving VASM's superiority over raw entropy heuristics.

### 4.5 Protocol 4: False Positive Vulnerability Study (Cluttered Backgrounds)
**Hypothesis:** Localized Top-$k$ mean prevents high-frequency visual noise from triggering false-positive visual resonance.
**Experiment:**
- Construct a synthetic "Cluttered Background" image benchmark prompting the model for *absent* objects.
- **Evaluation:** Compare the hallucination rate (false object generation) using `BRA-PureMax` vs. `BRA-Top3`. 
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA-PureMax` will theoretically show higher hallucination rates by incorrectly finding resonance in visual noise. `BRA-Top3` must significantly lower the hallucination bypass rate.

### 4.6 Protocol 5: The Latency/VRAM Pareto Frontier
**Hypothesis:** Operating strictly in the logits space via localized dot products is strictly Pareto-superior to multi-pass contrastive methods.
**Experiment:**
- Construct a 2D scatter plot: X-axis is Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT in ms), Y-axis is DocVQA Accuracy, and Bubble Size represents Peak VRAM overhead.
- **Success Criteria:** VCD and OPERA must sit on the far right (high latency/VRAM). BRA must occupy the top-left quadrant, delivering SOTA dense spatial accuracy with near-zero latency overhead over the Base model.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to localized logits-space resonance anchoring addresses a structural bottleneck in high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly exposing and resolving the "Pooling Paradox", BRA provides a pathway to intervene safely without destroying the fragile 2D spatial encodings established by Vision-RoPE. Furthermore, replacing flawed statistical heuristics (the "Entropy Trap") with Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) mathematically grounds the structural preservation of language generation.

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Modality Alignment Dependency:** Although Protocol 0 aims to prove robust cross-domain generalization, the necessity of the $\Phi$ projector means BRA is not strictly zero-parameter out of the box. It requires a one-time calibration over a mixed-domain corpus for each new MLLM unembedding space (e.g., LLaVA vs. InternVL). 
2. **Top-$k$ Sensitivity Across Dynamic Resolutions:** As models dynamically crop images into variable numbers of patches (e.g., InternVL), a static $k$ value for localized resonance may require adaptive scaling based on the total token sequence length. This hyper-parameter behavior will be investigated during empirical execution.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until Protocols 0-5 are fully executed, we strictly limit our claims to the theoretical soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial bottlenecks of high-resolution MLLM interventions, offering a concrete mathematical blueprint to validate the supremacy of localized Top-$k$, logits-space decoding.
