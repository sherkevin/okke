# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Structure-Preserving Hybrid Routing Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, including contrastive decoding, attention control, and layer-contrast methods, are strong competitive baselines. However, they do not directly provide a framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, an intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built on three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse visual contexts with candidate-wise support computed dynamically. Second, **Adaptive Top-k resonance** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via an offline, pre-computed WordNet-based boolean mask and deterministic BPE continuation inheritance, avoiding catastrophic online parsing latency. 

To enforce fair comparison, we explicitly separate a zero-shot architectural probe (`TLRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated variant (`TLRA_calib`). Acknowledging that `TLRA_calib` requires an InfoNCE-trained visual-to-lexical projector, it is explicitly classified as a **hybrid projection-routing method**, not a purely training-free intervention. Consequently, its evaluation contract mandates a direct comparison against a lightweight LoRA fine-tuning of the base MLLM using the exact same calibration data.

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize the paper around a strictly pre-registered experimental contract. The protocol locks all intervention hyperparameters on a single validation set and evaluates three evidence chains: hallucination reduction audited against Average Generation Length (AGL), structure preservation with OCR-heavy tasks treated as strict negative controls, and local-evidence parity under identical calibrator weights. All localized-routing claims must pass parity ablations against `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK`, and the `Base+LoRA` data-matched control. Efficiency modeling ($O(M \times H \times W)$ overhead) and failure-boundary audits are mandatory. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable proposal for structure-preserving hybrid visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible but weakly supported by the visual input. This failure is especially damaging in **fine-grained physical grounding** scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the relevant evidence is localized rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances in the field. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) inject visual noise to compare distributions under perturbed and clean inputs. Attention-based control methods (`OPERA`) penalize pathological autoregressive attention patterns. Layer-contrast methods (like MM-adapted `DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep language-model representations. These are all legitimate, training-free baselines. However, they leave a specific, complementary question unanswered:

**How can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence into logits adjustment without destroying token generation speed or damaging language structure?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a hybrid framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics or global visual pooling, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. The proposal is intentionally bounded. It targets physical-entity grounding under a controlled image-centric scientific contract.

Executing this intervention is nontrivial for four reasons. First, **embedding asymmetry**: final-layer visual states and lexical logits do not natively share an embedding space in most autoregressive architectures without projection. Second, **system cost ($O(M \times H \times W)$)**: computing dense similarity between $M$ candidates and thousands of visual tokens inside the autoregressive loop threatens to destroy `tokens_per_second`. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive intervention destroys syntax and long entities. Fourth, **baseline fairness**: introducing a trained visual-to-lexical projector means the method can no longer be compared solely to training-free baselines like VCD.

To address these issues, TLRA is built upon four explicit commitments:

1. **Hybrid projection-routing intervention** replacing purely training-free claims, enforced by a mandatory data-matched LoRA baseline.
2. **Adaptive Top-k resonance** as the primary local evidence extractor, evaluated under strict latency-overhead accounting.
3. **Offline, pre-computed VASM** as the structure-preserving protection layer, eliminating the need for online POS taggers.
4. **A strict zero/calibrated split** separating the architectural probe `TLRA_zero` from the calibrated `TLRA_calib`.

To keep the paper scientifically honest, TLRA enforces strict boundary rules. If `TLRA_calib` fails to outperform a simple standard LoRA trained on the exact same calibration subset, the complex decode-time routing claim is falsified. Furthermore, TLRA accepts an **OCR concession**: because VASM intentionally protects arbitrary text strings, OCR-heavy tasks are treated purely as negative controls. The primary scientific contract is image-centric, with video retained solely as a bounded appendix pilot.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib`

We explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks to isolate architectural viability from calibration gains:

1. **`TLRA_zero`**: A strict zero-shot probe where the model-native readout is applied directly to contextualized visual states. We acknowledge a severe embedding asymmetry here; if the base model's architecture does not naturally align visual hidden states with output vocabulary logits, this stage acts purely as an architectural failure-boundary test.
2. **`TLRA_calib`**: A hybrid projection-routing variant where a lightweight projection `Phi_calib` maps visual states into the lexical space. The base MLLM remains frozen.

Because `Phi_calib` is trained via an InfoNCE-style contrastive objective on a conceptual-caption subset, `TLRA_calib` is **not a training-free method**. Any claim about decode-time local routing must therefore survive a mandatory parity ablation against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset, in addition to `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` using the identical frozen `Phi_calib`.

### 3.2 Contextualized Visual States and Adaptive Top-k Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M$ is a globally locked candidate window.

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the **final-layer LLM hidden states at the visual token positions**. Importantly, these states are already heavily contextualized by the generated text prefix through autoregressive self-attention, meaning the visual representation adapts as the sentence unfolds. The visual token volume is $N_v = H \times W$.

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support by selecting:
$$
k = \max(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil)
$$
where $\rho$, $k_{min}$, $\tau_{sim}$, and $\tau_{evidence}$ are locked from a single validation split. 

The raw local support is:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(h_L^{(v_j)}, c)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
This introduces an $O(M \times N_v)$ complexity per generation step. For modern high-res MLLMs where $N_v > 4000$, this dense operation must be highly optimized, and its latency overhead is explicitly modeled in our evaluation.

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw local support into a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
To avoid uncontrolled distortion, TLRA applies a scaled penalty anchored to the local logit range $\Delta_L$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \Delta_L \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Offline VASM

A naive online POS tagger would cause catastrophic latency and fail on partial autoregressive prefixes. TLRA solves this using an **offline, pre-computed Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Mask (VASM)**.

VASM is a static boolean mask $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$ over the entire vocabulary $V$:
- $M_{VASM}(c) = 1$ for visually groundable physical-entity root tokens and their BPE continuations.
- $M_{VASM}(c) = 0$ for structurally protected functional tokens, syntax, and excluded concepts.

The mask is generated offline:
1. **Automated root-token lookup:** We use WordNet synsets to isolate physical entities, explicitly excluding abstract concepts.
2. **Deterministic BPE inheritance:** Subword continuations recursively inherit the mask state of their root token (e.g., "refriger" $\to$ "ator").

**Inherent Limitations:** Because VASM is static, it cannot resolve context-dependent polysemy (e.g., "mouse" as an animal vs. a computer device). This is accepted as a known failure boundary. Furthermore, arbitrary text-in-image strings receive zero coverage, cementing our **OCR concession** where `DocVQA` serves as a strict negative control.

### 3.5 Fallback Claims

TLRA explicitly does not claim zero-shot superiority without a projector, nor does it claim OCR competence. Crucially, if `TLRA_calib` is outperformed by `Base + LoRA (Phi_calib data)`, the paper must contract its claim entirely, acknowledging that the performance gains stem from the conceptual-caption training data rather than the token-local decode-time routing mechanism.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around one sanity pilot, three main evidence chains, and strict efficiency and boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Zero-Shot Architectural Probe

**Goal:** Determine whether `TLRA_zero` can extract meaningful signal despite embedding asymmetry.
- **Protocol:** Patch-level lexical overlap on a held-out image subset.
- **Decision Rule:** If the base model's pre-trained VL connector does not support direct spatial-lexical alignment, `TLRA_zero` is downgraded to an architectural failure note, and the evaluation proceeds entirely with `TLRA_calib`.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Baseline Fairness

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, and strictly verify that gains are not merely artifacts of the calibration data or sequence truncation.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Core Baselines:** Base, `VCD`, `MM-DoLa`.
- **Mandatory Data-Matched Baseline:** `Base + LoRA (Phi_calib data)` using the exact same training subset and parameter budget.
- **Metrics:** Hallucination score, `AGL`, `AGL StdDev`, `Relative Latency Multiplier (xBase)`.
- **Hard Rule:** If `TLRA_calib` loses to the `LoRA` baseline, the hybrid routing claim is falsified.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention preserves general capability and that VASM functions as a zero-overhead structural shield.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard)`.
- **Negative Control:** `DocVQA` (VASM trigger coverage must approach zero).
- **Ablation Rows:** Base, `TLRA_full` (with VASM), `TLRA_no_VASM`.
- **Latency Audit:** Explicit comparison of `tokens/s` between `TLRA_full` and `TLRA_no_VASM` to prove the offline mask $M_{VASM}$ adds near-zero latency overhead.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence Value and Leakage Control

**Goal:** Isolate whether the $O(M \times H \times W)$ local routing computation provides value over global pooling, given the exact same projector.
- **Benchmark:** `FREAK` (Spatial Relations, Object Existence, Counting).
- **Routing Parity Rows:** `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Strict Rule:** All three TLRA variants must share the identical frozen `Phi_calib`.
- **Leakage Rule:** Results must be segmented into Seen vs. Unseen categories based on the calibrator catalog to prove routing generalization.

### 4.5 Efficiency and Failure-Boundary Audits

**Goal:** Establish the actual deployment cost and mathematical bounds of the intervention.
- **Mandatory Metrics:** `ITL`, `tokens_per_second`, `peak_vram`, and Relative Latency Multiplier.
- **Complexity Ablation:** Candidate-window sensitivity over $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$.
- **Failure Analysis:** Documentation of VASM polysemy failures (e.g., misclassifying context-dependent nouns) and out-of-candidate failures.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is the one with the cleanest, most unforgiving scientific contract:
- It explicitly frames itself as a hybrid projection-routing method, not a pure training-free anomaly.
- It audits routing gains against a matched-data `Base + LoRA` baseline to prevent attribution errors.
- It utilizes an offline, pre-computed VASM to preserve structure without destroying autoregressive generation speed.
- It defines visual states accurately as contextualized hidden states at visual positions.
- It acknowledges and models the $O(M \times H \times W)$ latency overhead directly.

The primary unresolved risks and acknowledged limitations are:
1. **The LoRA Risk:** `TLRA_calib` may fail to significantly outperform `Base + LoRA (Phi_calib data)`, indicating that the conceptual-caption training data—not the decode-time routing—drives the performance.
2. **VASM Polysemy:** Because VASM is a static offline mask, it inherently misclassifies context-dependent polysemy (e.g., masking out "mouse" when referring to an animal if the dominant WordNet sense was excluded).
3. **Architectural Asymmetry:** `TLRA_zero` is highly likely to fail on architectures lacking native spatial-lexical alignment.
4. **Latency Ceiling:** For extremely high-resolution models ($N_v \gg 4000$), the dense $O(M \times N_v)$ dot-product may impose an unacceptable `tokens_per_second` penalty, restricting TLRA to $M < 10$.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)

Until the protocol is fully executed, the paper claims only:
- A falsifiable, data-matched hybrid routing framework for token-local visual evidence.
- An offline masking mechanism (VASM) that avoids catastrophic online parsing latency.
- A strict parity test of local routing vs. coarse pooling and data-matched LoRA baselines.

The paper strictly does not claim:
- Superiority over baselines without accounting for the calibration data advantage.
- Online contextual understanding of syntax (VASM is static).
- Zero-overhead generation (latency is explicitly modeled).
