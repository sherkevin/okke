# Decode-Time Token-Local Visual Intervention for Hallucination Reduction in Image-LLMs

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to object hallucination when overarching language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense spatial grounding. While existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalties) serve as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers for broad language or attention dynamics, they lack a mechanism to inject strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present a functional **Token-Local Visual Intervention (TLVI)** framework, a decode-time adjustment that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

Strictly scoped to 2D Image-LLMs to secure defensible claims regarding spatial locality, TLVI operates on three core mechanisms. First, acknowledging spatial washout in deep self-attention, we establish an evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot diagnostic (`TLVI_zero`) from a lightweight, dynamically calibrated bound (`TLVI_calib`). We explicitly formalize `TLVI_calib` as a **hybrid test-time adaptation** requiring an isolated 5k-sample prior, rather than a purely training-free method. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence; we utilize an exact prefill-derived visual activation tensor to establish a static threshold, isolating heavy dictionary dot-products to the Time to First Token (TTFT) phase. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via a dual-tier semantic filter: an ~85k WordNet physical entity dictionary alongside deterministic regex triggers for OCR structures (e.g., numbers, proper nouns) to prevent intervention bypass on dense document tasks. VASM is instrumented with dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) greedy string-matching continuation tracking. 

To rigorously assess algorithmic overhead, we propose a pre-registered evaluation protocol across three definitive evidence chains: (A) Hallucination Reduction isolating mechanisms against a spatially-aligned `Base + 5k LoRA` control, (B) Structure Preservation explicitly profiling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) bypass rates, and (C) Local Evidence Value comparing `TLVI_calib` against global pooling (`TLVI_MeanPool`) with explicit Inter-Token Latency (ITL) multiplier and Intervention Trigger Rate profiling. Alongside transparent defense lines dissecting $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ CUDA sorting bottlenecks and TTFT context scaling, this framework establishes a scientifically rigorous proposition for dictionary-gated token-local intervention.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable visual-linguistic capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to establish that these methods serve as state-of-the-art, orthogonal regularizers that effectively discipline overarching language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. They are highly competitive alternatives; however, because they operate on broader generation dynamics rather than explicit spatial feature grounding, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination faithfully, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We functionalize this intervention as **Token-Local Visual Intervention (TLVI)**. In this work, we strictly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, deliberately discarding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims surrounding spatial locality.

Executing TLVI requires acknowledging and mathematically overcoming three structural barriers. First, **input-output embedding asymmetry** and **spatial washout**. Deep self-attention inevitably diffuses localized patch coordinates by the final layer. Second, the computational complexity of **dynamic spatial pooling** at every decoding step. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or OCR-critical entities (e.g., numbers, proper nouns).

TLVI resolves these barriers via a structured framework:
1. **Bi-Level Logits Intervention (`TLVI_zero` / `TLVI_calib`)**: We establish a strict offline evaluation boundary testing whether final-layer visual states natively align with the terminal vocabulary (`TLVI_zero`). Acknowledging that architectures like LLaVA-1.5 often fail this zero-shot diagnostic due to spatial washout, we pivot to `TLVI_calib`—explicitly shifting the method into a **hybrid test-time adaptation** requiring a lightweight, isolated 5k-sample prior.
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations. We mathematically define the activation threshold solely during the prefill stage. While this induces a measurable Time to First Token (TTFT) spike that scales with image resolution, it guarantees stability in sparse images and removes redundant threshold approximations during autoregressive decoding.
3. **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, utilizing dynamic BPE inheritance and a dual-tier structural shield: an ~85k WordNet physical entity dictionary and deterministic regular expressions for OCR constructs (dates, numbers, capitalized entities). This expansion ensures TLVI does not remain mathematically dormant on dense spatial tasks like DocVQA. We directly confront VASM's conflation of "preservation" and "inaction" by transparently tracking explicit Intervention Trigger Rates (%) against Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) terms.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, our experimental design is organized into an execution protocol defining three primary evidence chains. 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mechanism value on POPE, CHAIR, and AGL against a strictly parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline optimized with equivalent spatial signaling.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving VASM dynamics via MMBench and MMMU(Hard), openly profiling OOV intervention bypass rates.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the definitive superiority of localized evidence on dense tasks (FREAK, DocVQA) by strictly comparing against a global average baseline (`TLVI_MeanPool`), bound by a strict Inter-Token Latency (ITL) multiplier constraint.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Embedding Asymmetry and Spatial Washout

A fundamental challenge in decode-time visual intervention is embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input layer. To construct a valid local support metric, TLVI evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states corresponding to the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$.

We explicitly address "spatial washout"—the reality that deep self-attention often destroys localized visual coordinates by the final layer. We define a strict operational boundary governed by a feature-level **Washout Threshold**, evaluated offline once per architecture:

1. **`TLVI_zero` (The Diagnostic Null-Hypothesis)**: A pure zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support via $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$. 
2. **The Washout Threshold**: We test zero-shot bounding box retrieval on a held-out 500-sample spatial validation set, explicitly stratified to match downstream resolutions. If the native projection fails to achieve an average bounding box $\text{IoU} > 0.15$ using Otsu's thresholding, native spatial fidelity is mathematically declared lost. 
3. **`TLVI_calib` (Hybrid Test-Time Adaptation)**: Upon triggering the threshold, we pivot to a hybrid adaptation. $\Phi_{calib}$ is a 2-layer MLP projection ($\approx 4.2$M parameters) optimized on 5,000 image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. **Crucially, this is not a training-free method**; these pairs are size-stratified and sampled from Visual Genome subsets strictly disjoint from test datasets. The base LLM remains entirely frozen.

**Strict Semantic & Spatial Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. To prevent feature collisions in dense images, negative patches ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are strictly filtered. A valid negative must satisfy two conditions: (1) Membership in a differing semantic super-category, and (2) An absolute spatial exclusion zone of $\text{IoU} < 0.1$ with the positive anchor. *Fallback mechanism:* If no valid negative patch satisfies the absolute spatial exclusion zone (e.g., in highly dense crowds), the anchor is explicitly skipped during the contrastive step to prevent forced feature collision penalties.

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling

At decoding step $t$, a naive Softmax over all visual patches artificially inflates noise. We apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{active}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{active}\Big)
$$
To calculate $\theta_{active}$ reliably without the vulnerability of step-by-step moving medians in sparse images, we derive it **strictly once during the prefill stage**. For each final-layer visual token $v_j$, we compute its maximum visual-to-semantic activation across the targeted dictionary $\mathcal{V}_{dict}$:
$$
A(v_j) = \max_{c \in \mathcal{V}_{dict}} \left( W_{vocab}[c] \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)}) \right)
$$
The absolute threshold $\theta_{active}$ is provisionally defined as the 85th percentile of the set $\{A(v_1), \dots, A(v_{N_v})\}$. We explicitly acknowledge **Prefill Threshold Attrition**: contextualized visual states may drift during highly extended autoregressive generation. However, isolating this $\sim 85$k dictionary dot product to prefill prevents prohibitive latency spikes during decoding. 

We then apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$, aggregating only the top-$k$ most supportive activated patches. The mathematical sorting operation operates on a tensor of size $M \times N_v$, where $M$ is the pre-selected candidate vocabulary window. 

### 3.3 Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing. This penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

To prevent structural collapse while ensuring the intervention does not remain completely dormant on dense OCR or reasoning tasks, VASM utilizes a **Dual-Tier Protection Architecture**:
1. **Tier 1: Root Dictionary Expectation:** We define the visual noun dictionary offline using the WordNet *physical entity* subtree ($\approx 85,000$ synsets). 
2. **Tier 2: Deterministic Format Triggers:** Acknowledging that WordNet excludes numbers, dates, and proper nouns—rendering visual grounding null on datasets like DocVQA—we supplement the mask with deterministic regex logic. Tokens conforming strictly to structural formats (e.g., numerical strings, standalone capitalized entities) are assigned $\gamma=1$.
3. **Dynamic Continuation Inheritance:** Tokenizers like LLaMA-2/3 frequently split common nouns using prefix spaces (`_` or `Ġ`). For subword tokenizers merging characters unpredictably (e.g., Qwen's `tiktoken`), VASM decodes the rolling token window and applies greedy string matching against dictionary/regex entries to resolve continuation inheritance. Any subword fragment inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its root via a pre-compiled GPU-side bitmask.

**The Polysemy & OOV Concession:** We explicitly recognize that deterministic gating cannot resolve polysemy natively (e.g., the financial "bank" vs. a river "bank"). Furthermore, domain-specific jargon missing from both tiers will inherently bypass intervention ($\gamma=0$). We commit to empirically quantifying explicit Out-Of-Vocabulary bypass rates.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because TLVI introduces specific structural hypotheses—and acknowledges severe risks of Inter-Token Latency (ITL) bottlenecks and intervention bypass if unverified—we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains. The protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). All subsequent sections outline our planned, hypothesis-driven execution. 

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction via Step-by-Step Attribution

**Hypothesis:** TLVI mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting. These gains derive from the decode-time mechanism, not merely parameter exposure to calibration data.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Acc/F1, and False Positive Rate), CHAIR (Object vs. Attribute), and AGL (Attention-Grounded Localization).
  - **Planned Output (Table 1 - Strict Format):** 
    - *Columns:* Model | POPE (F1) | **POPE (FPR)** | CHAIR (Obj) | AGL (Acc)
    - *Rows:* Base | `Base + 5k LoRA` | DoLa* | VCD* | OPERA* | `TLVI_MeanPool` | `TLVI_calib (Base)` | `TLVI_calib (+VASM)`
  - **The Comparison Fairness Context:** We explicitly note (*) that DoLa, VCD, and OPERA are zero-shot, orthogonal regularizers. `TLVI_calib` utilizes a 5k-sample prior. 
  - **The Strict 5k LoRA Control:** To prevent baseline disparity, the `Base + 5k LoRA` control is not merely fine-tuned on next-token prediction. It is optimized on the exact same 5k VG pairs with an equivalent auxiliary InfoNCE spatial objective, matching optimizer states and a LoRA rank equivalent to $\Phi_{calib}$'s 4.2M parameters. This definitively isolates inference-time spatial grounding gains. 
  - **False Positive Defense:** Reporting the False Positive Rate (FPR) for POPE is mandatory to ensure TLVI is not merely inflating accuracy by artificially penalizing all "Yes" tokens.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure Preservation and the Inaction Conflation

**Hypothesis:** VASM protects structural tokens, but reliance on explicit dictionaries introduces a vulnerability where Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) terms bypass intervention entirely, conflating "preservation" with mere "inaction."
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard Subset).
  - **Intervention Trigger Rate Profiling (Figure 1):** We will construct a scatterplot with **X-axis = OOV Rate (%)** and **Y-axis = Accuracy Drop relative to Base**. Point sizes explicitly represent the **Intervention Frequency (%)**. If the intervention rate on MMMU drops below 5%, we will explicitly state: *"TLVI maintains reasoning performance on MMMU primarily by remaining safely dormant due to out-of-vocabulary terms."*
  - **$\theta_{active}$ Threshold Ablation:** We will ablate the prefill threshold percentile $\in \{70, 80, 85, 90, 95\}$ against POPE F1 to empirically justify the 85th percentile default and measure threshold sensitivity.

### 4.3 Chain C: Local Evidence Value & Dense Spatial Evaluation

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting isolates specific evidence that diffuse global pooling natively washes out.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (Accuracy and ANLS) and DocVQA (ANLS). *Note: The Tier 2 VASM Regex expansion specifically enables active intervention on DocVQA's dense numerical/OCR answers.*
  - **Baseline Comparison (Table 2):** Strictly isolating `TLVI_zero` (if viable), `TLVI_MeanPool` (naive global averaging), and `TLVI_calib` (Top-$k$). 
  - **The Strict Latency/Trigger Constraint:** Table 2 will include a mandatory **ITL Multiplier** column (e.g., MeanPool = 1.0x, Top-$k$ = 3.8x) and an **Intervention Trigger Rate (%)** column. We establish a strict execution threshold: if the trigger rate is >15% but `TLVI_MeanPool` remains within 1-3% of the Top-$k$ variant, the massive $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ sorting overhead is scientifically unjustified. A >3% gap is required to validate the core spatial locality hypothesis.

### 4.4 Defense Lines: Inter-Token Latency (ITL) and Context Scaling Transparency

**Hypothesis:** Executing $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorts at every decoding step fundamentally bottlenecks ITL, while the $85k \times N_v$ dictionary dot-product bottlenecks prefill TTFT for high-resolution images.
- **Evaluation Protocol (Figure 3):** Executed on a standardized single A100 (80GB) GPU using BF16 precision and FlashAttention-2. 
- **Latency Profiling:** We plot **Time to First Token (TTFT)** and **Inter-Token Latency (ITL)** against an expanding Candidate Window Size **$M \in \{10, 50, 100, 200\}$**. 
- **Context Length Scaling:** We include a secondary plot showing TTFT explosion as Context Length / Image Resolution ($N_v$) increases, explicitly tracking Batched Out-of-Memory (OOM) failures for Batch Sizes > 1 to formally bound TLVI's operational envelope.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of Token-Local Visual Intervention (TLVI) lies in its structurally bounded proposition: intervening at decode-time using token-local visual evidence while protecting language integrity via dictionary-gated tracking (VASM). However, its execution is governed by theoretical and operational constraints:

1. **The `TLVI_calib` Hybrid Shift:** Deep self-attention demonstrably washes out localized patch fidelity by final layers. For architectures triggering the offline Washout Threshold, the premise of zero-shot alignment is mathematically invalid. This explicitly shifts the method into a hybrid test-time adaptation requiring a 5k-sample prior, fundamentally differentiating it from orthogonal zero-shot regularizers.
2. **Prefill Threshold Attrition & VASM Brittleness:** Deriving $\theta_{active}$ solely during prefill prevents autoregressive latency spikes, but risks threshold attrition during extended long-context generation where contextualized states drift. Furthermore, while the dual-tier VASM dictionary covers physical entities and regex formats, niche domain jargon will safely default to $\gamma=0$, conflating "structure preservation" with "intervention bypass"—an outcome we openly track.
3. **The Inter-Token Latency (ITL) Bottleneck:** Executing spatial pooling and $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorting at every single decoding step incurs severe CUDA kernel overheads. While the dictionary dot-product induces a TTFT spike that scales quadratically with image resolution ($N_v$), ITL becomes a strict bottleneck during autoregressive generation. Testing large batch sizes risks VRAM OOM failures, positioning TLVI strictly as a high-precision analytical tool rather than a high-throughput serving API.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines Token-Local Visual Intervention (TLVI), a rigorously defined framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through dictionary-gated, token-local logits intervention. By formalizing spatial washout (`TLVI_zero` vs `TLVI_calib`), computing a prefill-derived visual activation threshold, and engineering robust language protection via VASM, we establish a technically precise foundation for decode-time visual control. To prevent conflating inaction with preservation and to objectively assess algorithmic bottlenecks, the claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol—mandating exact Intervention Trigger Rate logging, strict spatial `Base + 5k LoRA` isolation, and aggressive ITL transparency—establishing a verifiable roadmap for empirical execution.
