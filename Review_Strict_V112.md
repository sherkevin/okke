## Verdict
This is an exceptionally self-aware, mathematically rigorous, and structurally sound paper draft. The framing of the "Sparsity Trap" and the explicit decision to initialize the calibration matrix from the Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$) rather than Layer 0 embeddings is a profound, theoretically justified insight that correctly addresses the manifold distortion in modern Swish-GLU/RMSNorm LLMs. The pre-registered evaluation protocol is brutally honest and highly falsifiable. 

However, beneath the excellent philosophical framing, there are three severe methodological fragilities that threaten to destroy the method once the experiments are actually run: (1) The spatial pooling mechanism (Top-$k$) is physically naive; (2) The Dynamic Syntax Floor lacks a safety abort threshold, guaranteeing catastrophic grammatical collapse in high-entropy states; and (3) The "Context-Blind" framing, while intellectually honest, may result in unacceptable relational hallucination degradation that renders the method unusable in practice. My score is high because the *science* is excellent, but your path to acceptance requires bulletproofing the methodology against the exact failures you have predicted.

## Summary
The paper proposes Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA), a late-fusion parametric head designed to suppress entity hallucination in MLLMs during autoregressive decoding. It explicitly acts as a context-independent visual existence checker for ~4,500 physical nouns. To solve manifold misalignment, it initializes its projection matrix using the LLM’s Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$). It introduces a Dynamic Syntax Floor to prevent logit over-penalization and a Subword-Trie Lookahead to handle BPE fragmentation. The evaluation plan is highly structured, pitting the method against prompt-injected object detection and explicitly auditing the semantic and hardware costs of the intervention.

## WhatShouldBeKept
*   **The Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$) Initialization:** This is your strongest technical contribution. Do not dilute this. The theoretical argument against Layer 0 initialization due to modern LLM non-linearities is spot on.
*   **The Prompt-Injected Object Detection Baseline:** Comparing against Grounding DINO in the system prompt is a masterclass in fair evaluation. It directly challenges the necessity of your architectural complexity. Keep this exact framing.
*   **The Hardware Audit (Tokens/Sec at BS $\ge 8$):** Your acknowledgment of GPU warp divergence due to asynchronous Trie traversals in batched generation is extremely rare in decoding papers. This must remain a core metric.
*   **The `TLRA_zero` representation check:** This is a perfect ablation.

## MajorWeaknesses
1.  **The Naivety of Top-$k$ Spatial Pooling (Eq. 2):** Your formulation $S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_{c}$ is structurally defective for visual representation. Visual patches are not bag-of-words. If an object is small (1 patch), averaging the top $k=5$ patches dilutes the existence signal by 80%, potentially causing false negatives. If the object is massive (20 patches), top-5 misses semantic context. Furthermore, grid-based patch representations in models like CLIP/SigLIP suffer from severe spatial entanglement. You need a dynamic pooling mechanism or a max-pooling fallback. If you run this with a static $k$, your POPE recall will likely collapse on small objects.
2.  **The Dynamic Syntax Floor guarantees irreversible collapse:** Your equation $L_{final} = \max(L_{floor} - \epsilon, \dots)$ is a suicide pact for the LLM. If the autoregressive context strictly demands a noun (e.g., "The man drives a"), the highest non-noun logit ($L_{floor}$) might be a comma with a raw logit of -15 (near-zero probability). By strictly forcing the argmax to fall to $L_{floor}$, you are forcing the model to select garbage, destroying the rest of the generation. You are planning to *measure* this via the "Forced Syntactic Error Rate" (Chain B), but you must *fix* it. You need an **Intervention Abort Threshold**: if $L_{orig}(c) - L_{floor} > \tau$ (i.e., the model has no viable syntactic alternative), TLRA must back off and allow the hallucination, because a hallucinated noun is better than irreversible structural destruction.
3.  **The "Context-Blindness" Defense is a Double-Edged Sword:** You define TLRA as a context-independent checker ($Y_{<t}$ independent) to establish a theoretical boundary. But if evaluating an image with a dog on the floor, and the prompt asks "Is the dog on the sofa?", verifying "sofa" exists in the background will validate the token and exacerbate relational hallucinations (as you noted). If MMHal-Relation scores tank by 10+ points, reviewers will reject the paper regardless of POPE improvements. You must demonstrate that the baseline LLM's own attention mechanism can handle the relational aspect if TLRA handles the existence aspect.
4.  **Incompatibility with Nucleus/Top-$p$ Sampling:** Your entire Subword-Trie and Greedy Pivot vs Beam Search Rollback discussion assumes greedy argmax decoding. MLLMs heavily utilize temperature and Top-$p$ sampling for diverse captioning. If you modulate logits pre-softmax, how does this interact with the Top-$p$ truncation? If the penalized noun was the *only* token in the nucleus, does your syntax floor drag low-probability tokens into the nucleus? This must be addressed.

## SectionBySectionComments
*   **Abstract & Intro:** You write "We reject the premise that forcing a subword pivot is inherently 'graceful'." This is fantastic prose and highly persuasive. 
*   **Section 3.1 ($W_{out}$ Alignment):** You state $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$. Ensure you clarify if the hidden dimension $D$ of the vision encoder matches the LLM $D$. If there is an MLP connector (like in LLaVA), are you applying $W_{calib}$ *before* or *after* the vision-language connector? It must be *after* the connector to align with the LLM's $W_{out}$ space. Specify this explicitly.
*   **Section 3.2 (Hard Negative Sampling):** Using co-occurrence matrices for hard negatives is smart, but ensure you don't penalize objects that are visually indistinct or synonymous in the CLIP latent space (e.g., "cup" vs "mug"). If "mug" is GT and "cup" is the hard negative, BCE loss will shatter the visual manifold. You need a semantic threshold for negative sampling.
*   **Section 3.4 (Trie Lookahead):** You discuss Beam Search Rollback as a theoretical upper bound. Note that standard Beam Search in LLMs is highly penalized by length. Make sure your rollback mechanism doesn't accidentally trigger standard beam search length-penalties causing early EOS generation.

## RequiredRevisions
1.  **Redesign the Spatial Pooling:** Replace or augment the static Top-$k$ pooling in Eq. 2. Introduce dynamic thresholding (e.g., mean of all patches where logit $> \theta$) or provide a rigorous ablation showing that static Top-$k$ does not destroy small-object recall.
2.  **Implement an Abort Mechanism for the Syntax Floor:** Introduce a threshold $\tau$. If the entropy of the non-noun distribution is too low, disable the penalty mask. You cannot simply document the model outputting commas instead of nouns; you must actively prevent it.
3.  **Specify Connector Location:** Explicitly state where the visual features $X_v$ are extracted (pre- or post-VL connector) and prove the dimensional/geometric alignment with $W_{out}$.
4.  **Sampling Compatibility:** Add a section or algorithm block explaining how TLRA behaves under Top-$p$/Temperature sampling, not just greedy/beam search.

## SuggestedFiguresTablesExperiments

2.  **The Safety Threshold ($\tau$) Experiment:** In Chain B, alongside your planned Argmax Retention Rate, plot the "Grammatical Survival Rate" as a function of an introduced $\tau$ threshold. Show the trade-off curve between Hallucination Suppression and Grammatical Collapse.
3.  **Connector vs. Vision-Only Ablation:** Add an experiment comparing $X_v$ taken directly from the Vision Encoder (e.g., CLIP/SigLIP) vs. $X_v$ taken after the LLaVA-style MLP connector. $W_{out}$ alignment theoretically *requires* post-connector features. Prove this empirically.
4.  **Failure Case Addition:** For the "Semantic Failure Case" table, add a column showing the *confidence* (softmax probability) of the forced alternative token. This will visually prove why the grammar collapses when the model is forced into the low-probability syntax floor.

## AcceptanceOutlook
This paper has the bones of a top-tier oral presentation. The theoretical deconstruction of late-fusion alignment (specifically the critique of Layer 0 initialization) is highly compelling. However, you have designed an evaluation protocol so strict that it will likely break your own method (specifically the Top-$k$ and Syntax Floor mechanisms). If you patch these two algorithmic holes *before* running the final experiments, and your results validate the $W_{out}$ initialization claim, I will strongly advocate for Accept. If you run the experiments with the current equations and the syntax floor triggers mass grammatical failure, the strictness of your protocol will force me to Reject. Fix the math, run the protocol, and this will be a standout paper.
