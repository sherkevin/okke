# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Sparse Decode-Time Visual Existence Checker for Hallucination Suppression

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs, e.g., LLaVA-1.5) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during entity grounding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate general model confidence, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens during autoregressive decoding. 

Recognizing the mathematical limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual existence verifier** rather than a complex spatial reasoner. It reliably answers "Is this noun in the image?" to suppress pure hallucinations. To resolve geometric incoherence and parameter bloat, TLRA projects strictly to an offline-filtered physical noun subset ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$), reducing the structural footprint to $\sim$18M parameters and significantly mitigating gradient noise during training. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A semantically coherent, bias-free initialization tied directly to the LLM's **Layer 0 input token embeddings**—matching the geometric space of the visual projector—to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring trained via a strict contrastive negative-sampling objective, featuring an **in-window syntactic logit floor** to prevent structural collapse and model timidity; and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) equipped with a **First-Subword Anchoring Fallback** to survive the existential threat of BPE fragmentation in modern large-vocabulary tokenizers (e.g., Llama-3's 128k tiktoken). This work establishes a pre-registered, boundary-aware protocol for parameter-efficient decode-time visual routing, strictly mapping its structural, relational, and tokenization failure boundaries.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), such as LLaVA-1.5 and Llama-3-Vision variants, exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, Image-Level Classifier-Free Guidance) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts. However, they cannot introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways. Conversely, standard parametric approaches operate entirely in the prefill phase, compressing spatial evidence into the global context window without explicitly routing it to specific token generation steps. This leaves a critical gap: **How can we inject fine-grained, token-specific visual evidence directly into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying morphological syntax, suffering geometric mismatch, or succumbing to the fragmentation of modern BPE tokenizers?**

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit supervision that zero-shot methods lack. 

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly reject the framing of TLRA as a "context-aware spatial reasoner." Mathematically, TLRA acts as a **Global Visual Existence Checker**. By pooling patch features independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. While this effectively suppresses pure hallucinations, it establishes a hard theoretical boundary: TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. We embrace this constraint, demonstrating that context-blind existence checking is highly effective for core hallucination reduction if explicitly trained with strict negative sampling to prevent mode collapse.

Furthermore, we fundamentally correct the structural bloat of vocabulary-space projections. Projecting to a full 32,000 or 128,000-token vocabulary incurs massive parameter costs ($\sim$131M to $\sim$524M parameters), the vast majority of which maps to non-visual abstract tokens. TLRA instead utilizes a **Sparse Deterministic Projection** ($D \times V_{noun}$), reducing the trainable footprint to an ultra-lightweight $\sim$18M parameters by strictly targeting an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems. Unlike previous claims that centered on dynamic inference latency (which are often masked by kernel launch overheads on high-end GPUs like the A100), TLRA's sparsity primarily delivers **drastic VRAM footprint reduction and the elimination of gradient noise** during training, ensuring stable convergence.

Finally, we confront two existential threats to late-fusion routing: **Geometric Incoherence** and **BPE Fragmentation**. Training a projection matrix from scratch on 50k captions mathematically guarantees failure on unseen tokens due to random initialization. TLRA solves this by initializing its sparse projection head strictly from the LLM's pre-trained **Layer 0 input token embeddings** (omitting bias terms to preserve pure dot-product geometry). Simultaneously, modern tokenizers aggressively shatter common words into non-semantic fragments. We explicitly audit this **VASM Coverage Drop** across Llama-2 and Llama-3 tokenizers and introduce a "First-Subword Anchoring" fallback to ensure the existence checker remains viable even as vocabulary sizes scale.

Our evaluation is structured as a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Generic Collapse:** Evaluating entity grounding (POPE, AMBER) against VCD, DoLa, and Image-Level CFG, while auditing for Morphological Error Rate (MER), False Negative Rate (FNR), and **Response Length Timidity**.
2. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general capabilities (MMBench) and explicitly testing geometric initialization (Layer 0 vs. Random vs. Layer $N$) on a held-out "Unseen Objects" split.
3. **Local Evidence Value & Relational Boundaries:** Isolating spatial advantage on FREAK, explicitly documenting TLRA's failure boundary on a Multi-Instance/Relational stress test, and mapping spatial heatmaps to prove the extraction of localized visual evidence.
4. **Semantic Boundaries and Fragment Leakage:** Bounding the method via strict BPE Fragmentation Profiles across multiple tokenizer generations.
5. **Hardware Physics:** A formal evaluation of static memory utilization (VRAM) and parameter-efficiency ratios, avoiding exaggerated latency claims.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Sparse Architectural Scope and Geometrically Coherent Initialization

TLRA targets standard MLLM architectures (e.g., LLaVA-1.5) where spatial provenance is preserved across visual tokens $X_v$. TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage:

**1. `TLRA_calib` (The Sparse Parametric Routing Head):** Instead of a full $D \times V$ matrix, TLRA strictly limits its projection to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ maps cached spatial visual states of dimension $D$ to this subset. 
- **The Sparsity Trap and Geometric Coherence:** Crucially, post-MLP visual features $X_v$ are geometrically aligned to the LLM's **input embedding space** (Layer 0), *not* the output `lm_head` (Layer $N$). To enable zero-shot generalization without forcing a single linear layer to simulate 32 transformer blocks, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained Layer 0 input token embeddings**. To strictly preserve this pre-trained dot-product manifold, $W_{calib}$ is implemented **without a bias term**.
- **`TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** A baseline variant that computes cosine similarity directly between frozen $X_v$ and the LLM's discrete text embeddings, serving as an ablation to prove whether decode-time spatial routing requires the explicitly supervised alignment of `TLRA_calib`.

### 3.2 Training Formulation and Contrastive Negative Sampling

A critical flaw in naive "existence checkers" is mode collapse: if trained solely on caption data (which only provides *positive* occurrences of nouns), the scalar output will blindly saturate to 1.0 for all patches. To calibrate $W_{calib}$, we employ a strict **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** ($\mathcal{L}_{calib}$).

For a given image, let $\mathcal{P}$ be the set of ground-truth physical nouns present in the caption. We dynamically sample a set of **hard negative nouns** $\mathcal{N}$ from $V_{noun}$ (objects frequently co-occurring in the dataset corpus but explicitly absent from the current image). The base LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen; gradients flow *only* into $W_{calib}$:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{P}} \log(\hat{S}(c)) - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{N}} \log(1 - \hat{S}(c))
$$
This negative sampling explicitly teaches TLRA to push $S_{raw}$ toward 0 when visual evidence is absent, establishing the critical threshold for the existence verification.

### 3.3 The Top-$k$ "Existence Checker" and the Absolute Syntax Floor

At each decoding step $t$, TLRA computes a raw spatial support score by pooling the **Top-$k$** matching patches for a candidate token $c$ from the candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_{c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
Here, $k$ is a critical hyperparameter dictating the definition of "existence" (ablated in Section 4). If $k=1$, the method is highly sensitive but risks occlusion blindness; if $k=|X_v|$, it loses all spatial resolution. $S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$ outside of the candidate proposal window. 

To apply this evidence without forcing behavioral collapse or extreme timidity, we enforce an **Absolute Syntax Floor**. Rather than searching the entire vocabulary (which is vulnerable to obscure Unicode noise), we bound the penalty strictly within the current step's top candidates. Let $L_{floor} = \max_{v \in \mathcal{C}_t \setminus \mathcal{V}_{noun}} L_{orig}(v) + \epsilon$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token *currently in the Top-$M$ window*. The final adjusted logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = \max \Big( L_{floor}, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big)
$$
**Noun-Only Fallback Guarantee:** By bounding the penalty to never drop below $L_{floor}$, TLRA mathematically cannot alter the grammatical structure of the sentence (e.g., it cannot force the model to output a verb when a noun is required), isolating the intervention strictly to physical grounding.

### 3.4 BPE Fragmentation and the First-Subword Fallback

VASM utilizes a deterministic binary mask ($\gamma \in \{0, 1\}^V$) extracting viable physical noun stems from WordNet. However, modern tokenizers (e.g., Llama-3 128k) shatter everyday words into non-semantic fragments (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`). 

**The Existential Threat:** Any subword that does not match a semantic dictionary stem becomes mathematically un-penalizable. To survive the VASM Coverage Drop, TLRA implements a **First-Subword Anchoring Strategy**. For any multi-token noun $w \to [t_1, t_2, \dots, t_n]$, the mask $\gamma$ identifies and activates *only* the initial subword $t_1$ (if it carries the semantic stem). By applying the existence penalty $\hat{S}$ strictly to $t_1$, TLRA prevents the generation of the hallucinated object at its inception, leaving subsequent subwords $t_{>1}$ to be naturally handled by the base LLM's autoregressive language priors. We explicitly profile this fragmentation limit in Section 4.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict computational, structural, and relational boundaries. By explicitly reframing TLRA as a targeted, explicitly-trained visual existence verifier operating in a sparse lexical space, we identify the following mathematically rigid limitations:

1. **VASM Coverage Drop and BPE Aggression:** Despite the introduction of the First-Subword Anchoring fallback, BPE fragmentation remains an existential threat to vocabulary-gated interventions. If a tokenized sequence shatters a noun such that even its initial fragment lacks a semantic match in WordNet, it mathematically escapes TLRA's jurisdiction. As tokenizers become more granular (e.g., Llama-3 128k) or multilingual, the percentage of un-penalizable fragments establishes a hard ceiling on dictionary-guided methods.
2. **Geometric Coherence vs. Deep Fusion:** While initializing $W_{calib}$ from Layer 0 input embeddings respects the geometric space of the visual projector, it forces a single linear layer to execute a cross-modal alignment that deep-fused architectures handle over multiple transformer blocks. If visual features lack linearly separable existence boundaries at Layer 0, TLRA will inherently underperform deep-fusion LoRAs.
3. **The "Existence Checker" Fallacy (Relational Blindness):** The mathematical formulation of $S_{raw}$ pools patches independently of text context. TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. It explicitly trades complex relational reasoning for robust, lightweight, decode-time hallucination suppression.
4. **Top-$k$ Occlusion and Resolution Limits:** The hyperparameter $k$ fundamentally dictates the existence checker's field of view. A static $k$ struggles with extreme scale variances in images—small objects require highly localized patches, while large objects dominate the feature map, risking occlusion blindness or spatial blurring if $k$ is miscalibrated.

Future execution of this pre-registered protocol will definitively confirm whether a sparse, geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head, equipped with strict negative sampling, can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities and relational blindness to provide scalable, targeted visual grounding.
