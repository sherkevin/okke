# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Hybrid Auxiliary Routing Head for MLLM Grounding via Intermediate Visual States

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as visual-noise contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa)—are training-free and operate globally. In contrast, we present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a hybrid trained auxiliary routing head that directly injects token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment while strictly preserving language structure. 

TLRA is built on three architectural corrections. First, to avoid the "local evidence illusion" where final-layer LLM visual tokens are irreversibly contaminated by global text-attention, TLRA extracts visual representations from intermediate LLM layers. Rather than assuming a universal depth, we profile this semantic transition dynamically across architectures (e.g., LLaMA-2 vs. LLaMA-3). Second, to bridge the manifold mismatch between static mid-layer states and the dynamic lexical space, we train a lightweight MLP projector ($\Phi_{calib}$). Crucially, this projection is computed exactly once during the prefill phase, caching a tensor for retrieval that requires only $O(M \cdot N_v)$ dot products per decode step within a bounded Top-$M$ candidate window. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via a pre-computed frequency-weighted mask. Acknowledging the "BPE subword prefix trap," VASM operates as a statistical heuristic, which we actively audit across different tokenizer scales and multilingual/out-of-domain boundaries.

To enforce an unforgiving evaluation contract, we separate the architectural mechanism from the training objective. Any claim about TLRA's decode-time routing must survive a mandatory parameter-matched parity against: (1) a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the identical cross-entropy alignment objective, proving the gain comes from late-fusion routing rather than training data; and (2) a trivial continuous additive-fusion baseline, proving that discrete logit-space routing prevents the manifold collapse inherent to simple hidden-state addition. The resulting paper establishes a positive, falsifiable protocol for structure-preserving, hybrid visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model frequently generates text that is fluent but decoupled from localized visual evidence. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained physical grounding scenarios, such as spatial positioning and small-object recognition.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) inject visual noise to compare distributions; attention-based control (`OPERA`) penalizes pathological autoregressive patterns; layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep language representations. Crucially, these are **training-free, plug-and-play techniques** that rely on language-side distribution shifts or global visual perturbations. They leave a complementary architectural question unanswered:

**How can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence directly into logits adjustment using a trained auxiliary head, without conflating the routing gains with training-objective superiority, and without falling victim to the globalization of final-layer representations?**

This paper introduces a hybrid auxiliary routing head, Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA), advancing a narrow, falsifiable hypothesis: a hybrid framework scoring candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than global visual pooling or trivial continuous fusion, provided it extracts truly local states and incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards.

Executing this intervention requires overcoming four specific architectural barriers:
1. **The Local Evidence Illusion:** In decoder-only MLLMs, visual tokens processed during prefill undergo deep bidirectional self-attention. By the final layer, they are heavily contextualized, text-contaminated global mixtures. TLRA solves this by extracting visual states from intermediate layers. Rather than hardcoding a naïve $L/2$ assumption, we systematically profile the layer depth across different backbones to pinpoint where spatial locality collapses into textual semantics.
2. **Manifold Mismatch & The Continuous Fusion Collapse:** Static mid-layer visual states and dynamic pre-LM-head lexical states do not natively share an embedding space. While a lightweight trained projector ($\Phi_{calib}$) bridges this gap, naïvely adding these continuous vectors to the LLM's final hidden state shifts the generation out of its natural manifold, causing grammar collapse. We thus require *discrete logit-space routing*.
3. **Computational Throttling:** Computing dynamic visual similarity at every generation step threatens memory bandwidth. We solve this by computing the projection exactly once during prefill, caching a lightweight $\approx 32$ MB tensor. During autoregression, retrieval costs only $O(M \cdot N_v)$ lightweight dot products per step, restricted to a bounded Top-$M$ candidate window.
4. **The BPE Subword Prefix Trap:** Naïve intervention destroys syntax, exacerbated by subword bleeding where tokens (e.g., "car") are shared across physical entities ("automobile") and abstract language ("cardinal"). We introduce a frequency-weighted statistical heuristic (VASM) tuned on a held-out corpus. We explicitly acknowledge this is an English-centric engineering heuristic, and formally audit its brittleness across multilingual and technical OOD benchmarks.

To address these issues, TLRA is built upon strict scientific commitments. Because TLRA is a *trained hybrid method*, it is inherently more expensive to deploy initially than VCD or OPERA. We therefore enforce a mandatory baseline: a LoRA model trained on the exact same dense region-caption data using the exact same alignment objective as our TLRA projector. We also enforce parity against a trivial "continuous additive fusion" baseline. If TLRA fails to outperform these objective-matched controls, its routing claim is mathematically falsified.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: Isolating the Objective in Hybrid Routing

TLRA is a hybrid method utilizing a lightweight MLP projector $\Phi_{calib}$ to map static, mid-layer visual states into the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical space. 

**Objective Matching for Fair Evaluation:**
Confounding the routing mechanism with the training objective is a fatal flaw in hybrid interventions. To ensure mathematical fairness, $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained using a standard localized Next-Token Prediction objective over a dense region-caption subset. Consequently, any claim about decode-time local routing must survive a mandatory parity ablation against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset **using identical Cross-Entropy**. 

**The Continuous Fusion Collapse:**
To justify discrete logit-space routing, we enforce parity against a trivial `ContinuousAdd` baseline, where $\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is attention-weighted and continuously added to the final hidden state before the LM head ($h_{t, final} + \sum \alpha_j \Phi_{calib}(v_j)$). Continuous addition often shifts the autoregressive state out of its natural manifold, causing rapid grammar degradation. TLRA avoids this by intervening exclusively in the discrete logit space.

### 3.2 Bridging the Manifold Mismatch: Layer Profiling and $O(M \cdot N_v)$ Retrieval

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, globally locked at $M=50$. Limiting routing to Top-$M$ guarantees computational efficiency while purposefully acknowledging the "Hijacking Problem"—where overwhelming language priors might push the correct visual entity beyond the candidate window.

**The Local Evidence Illusion and Dynamic Layer Profiling:**
A common misconception is treating final-layer LLM hidden states as inherently "visual." By the final layer, visual tokens have undergone dense self-attention with the text prompt, becoming text-contaminated global mixtures. Let $h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}$ denote the static hidden states at the visual token positions ($N_v \approx 4000$). Instead of hardcoding a depth (e.g., $L/2$), TLRA identifies the optimal extraction layer $L_{mid}$ through empirical layer-sweep profiling across target architectures (e.g., LLaMA-2 vs. LLaMA-3) to capture representations before spatial locality is irreversibly diluted.

**Hardware Lifecycle Protection:**
$\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is executed **exactly once** at the conclusion of the prefill stage. The $N_v \times D$ projected tensor ($\approx 32.7$ MB in FP16) is stored in SRAM. During the autoregressive loop, raw local support for $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ is computed via $O(M \cdot N_v)$ batched dot-products:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}), c)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw support into a relative evidence score: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)$. To prevent the visual head from blowing up the logits distribution and overriding confident structural tokens, we anchor the penalty to the local logit spread $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)) - \min(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t))$. Bounded by hyperparameter $\beta$, the adjustment is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L, \beta) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 The BPE Subword Prefix Trap and Frequency-Weighted VASM

To preserve syntax without inline parsing delays, TLRA uses a pre-computed Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Mask (VASM), $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$. 

**The Subword Prefix Trap:** Short physical entities serve as subwords for abstract concepts (e.g., `[car]` in `[car, dinal]`). A strict conservative mask causes catastrophic physical-entity recall collapse. We employ **Frequency-Weighted Subword Resolution**: if a BPE subword maps to a groundable root entity in $\ge \gamma$ of its occurrences in a C4 corpus (tuned on a held-out validation set), it retains $M_{VASM}=1$. 

**Acknowledging Tokenizer and Domain Brittleness:** 
This statistical heuristic dictates *which* semantic direction is sacrificed. It anchors the multimodal system to English-centric BPE statistics. We accept this collateral damage and formally audit its fragility across different tokenizer scales (32k vs. 128k) and out-of-domain (Multilingual/Code) negative controls.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around three evidence chains and strict boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper.

### 4.1 Stage 0: The Continuous Fusion Manifold Collapse

**Goal:** Mathematically verify that discrete logit-space routing is structurally superior to trivial hidden-state addition.
- **Protocol:** Compare TLRA against `TLRA_ContinuousAdd` on a subset of held-out dense captions.
- **Hypothesis/Expected Outcome:** `ContinuousAdd` will exhibit rapid grammar collapse because adding auxiliary visual vectors directly to $h_{t, final}$ shifts the representation out of the pre-trained LM-head manifold. Discrete logit routing prevents this degradation.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Objective Fairness

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, verifying gains derive from the auxiliary routing mechanism rather than the calibration data.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Training-Free Baselines:** Base, `VCD`, `MM-DoLa` (Require 0 extra parameters, 0 extra data).
- **Matched Objective Baseline (Hybrid):** `Base + LoRA` (trained on identical data/cross-entropy objective).
- **Hard Rule:** TLRA must outperform the objective-matched `Base + LoRA` to justify the complexity of the $\Phi_{calib}$ projector and Top-$M$ routing.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure Preservation and Tokenizer/Domain Audit

**Goal:** Measure general capability preservation and explicitly quantify the collateral damage of VASM across different BPE structures and Non-English domains.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench`, `MME`, `MMMU`.
- **Cross-Tokenizer & OOD Collateral Damage Audit:** 
  1. `DocVQA` & `GSM8K` (English Text/Math): Measures degradation of abstract reasoning.
  2. `Multilingual/Code Queries`: Evaluates the catastrophic failure boundaries of the C4-English-derived VASM heuristic.
- **Ablation Rows:** Base, `TLRA_full`, `TLRA_no_VASM`.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: The Layer Sweep Heatmap

**Goal:** Prove that intermediate visual states retain significantly more token-local grounding than text-contaminated final layers, and identify where the "Illusion" begins.
- **Protocol:** A systematic layer-sweep analysis.
  - **X-axis:** Extraction Layer ($L_1$ to $L_N$) across at least two different backbone architectures (e.g., LLaMA-2-7B vs. LLaMA-3-8B).
  - **Y-axis:** Hallucination Metric (`POPE F1`).
- **Hypothesis:** Performance will peak in the intermediate layers and sharply degrade in the final layers as dense self-attention globalizes the visual tokens.

### 4.5 Efficiency and Failure-Boundary Audits

**Goal:** Document computational costs and topological failure modes.
- **Failure Analysis 1 (The "Hijacking" Bounding Box):** A histogram tracking the generation-step rank of the ground-truth physical entity token. This visually demonstrates how often the GT token falls outside the Top-50 window, mathematically proving TLRA's upper intervention limits.
- **Failure Analysis 2 ($\Delta_L$ Restraint):** A scatter plot of $\Delta_L$ (logit spread) vs. visual penalty applied, proving the bounded penalty prevents the model from overriding confident structural tokens.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA explicitly bounds its claims. By defining itself as a *hybrid trained routing head*, it acknowledges its parameter and data costs relative to pure training-free methods like VCD or OPERA. It isolates its routing gains against an unforgiving objective-matched `Base + LoRA` baseline and a trivial `Continuous Additive Fusion` baseline to prevent conflating architectural superiority with alignment data or continuous state drift. Crucially, it abandons the "local evidence illusion" of final-layer representations, shifting extraction to intermediate layers dynamically profiled to preserve spatial locality.

The primary unresolved risks and limitations are:
1. **The Multilingual VASM Fragility:** VASM is a brittle statistical heuristic. By frequency-weighting BPE subwords using an English C4 corpus to prevent visual trigger starvation, TLRA actively accepts collateral damage to abstract and non-English language. As our OOD Audit intends to show, this heuristic fails ungracefully when BPE statistics shift in multilingual or code-generation contexts.
2. **The Top-$M$ Ceiling (The Hijacking Problem):** By limiting routing to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ to guarantee a lightweight $O(M \cdot N_v)$ footprint, TLRA is mathematically bounded. If the base LLM's language prior is so overwhelmingly wrong that the correct visual entity is completely expelled from the candidate window (e.g., rank 51), TLRA cannot recover it. This represents a hard ceiling for discrete, bounded late-fusion interventions.
