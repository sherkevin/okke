## Overall Score
Score: 3/5

## Verdict
The paper currently presents a highly disciplined, scientifically rigorous pre-registration of a methodology. It correctly identifies a positive methodological proposition (injecting token-local visual evidence at decode-time while preserving structure) rather than relying on a grand, often inaccurate, critique of existing baselines. The experimental blueprint is excellent. However, there are fundamental misalignments between the proposed training of the calibrator and the hypotheses you intend to test, particularly regarding spatial reasoning and BPE autoregressive momentum. If the proposed experimental plan is executed flawlessly with the corrections outlined below, this paper has a strong trajectory for acceptance.

## Summary
The paper proposes Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA), a decode-time intervention to reduce hallucinations in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs). Because pure zero-shot local patch intervention fails due to embedding asymmetry, the authors introduce a lightweight, frozen-base-model calibrator (`TLRA_calib`) trained on generic noun-chunk image pairs. To handle decode-time complexity and long-horizon blindness, TLRA employs prompt-conditioned pruning with an entropy-driven fallback. To prevent the destruction of linguistic structure, the authors introduce Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM), a deterministic WordNet-based whitelist with tokenizer-aware BPE collision checks. The paper lays out a strict three-chain evaluation protocol focusing on hallucination reduction, structure preservation, and isolating the value of local evidence against global pooling.

## WhatShouldBeKept
1. **The Positive Framing and Baseline Treatment:** You have successfully avoided the trap of mischaracterizing `DoLa`, `VCD`, and `OPERA`. Treating them as competitive, legitimate baselines rather than falsely claiming they "depend on global pooling" is intellectually honest and strengthens your paper. Do not regress on this framing.
2. **The `TLRA_MeanPool` Parity Baseline:** Using the exact same trained $\Phi_{calib}$ weights for a global mean-pooling baseline is the most critical scientific control in this paper. It perfectly isolates the contribution of *local* extraction from the *external data* injected via the calibrator. 
3. **The "OCR Concession" as a Negative Control:** Acknowledging that VASM bypasses arbitrary OCR strings and using `DocVQA` as an exact negative control (expecting 0% trigger rate and 0% improvement) is a brilliant, falsifiable test of your structural preservation mechanism.
4. **The Three Evidence Chains:** The interlocking logic of Hallucination Reduction (Chain A), Structure Preservation (Chain B), and Local Evidence Value (Chain C) is the exact level of rigor expected at ACM Multimedia.

## MajorWeaknesses
1. **Contradiction Between Calibrator Training and Spatial Hypotheses (Chain C):** You explicitly state in Section 3.1 that $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained on spaCy *noun chunks* aligned via GroundingDINO (capturing objects and attribute-noun pairs). Yet, in Section 4.3 (Chain C), your primary hypothesis is that TLRA will excel on fine-grained **Spatial Relations** in `FREAK`. Noun chunks notoriously lack complex spatial prepositions (e.g., "behind", "partially occluded by", "to the left of"). If the calibrator is blind to spatial relations during its InfoNCE training, it is mathematically highly improbable that it will magically recover them at decode-time just because you use Top-$k$ selection. 
2. **Unverified Assumption of "BPE Momentum":** In Section 3.4, you claim that triggering VASM on a BPE prefix will allow the base LLM's "autoregressive momentum" to safely complete the suffix. This assumes that an intervention on step $t$ does not corrupt the hidden states required to predict the suffix at step $t+1$. In practice, heavily penalizing competing logits at the prefix step can alter the KV-cache trajectory or subsequent embeddings, leading to the exact "BPE stuttering" you fear. This is a massive systemic risk that lacks quantitative bounding in your plan.
3. **Entropy Fallback Tuning is Unspecified:** The threshold $\theta_{fallback}$ acts as a binary switch between local pruning and dense evaluation. If tuned on the test set, it constitutes a severe leak. Your protocol lacks a definition of the validation split used to establish this hyperparameter.
4. **Video Modality Creep:** While the current draft is strictly image-centric (which is correct), if you harbor any intentions of resurrecting a "secondary video pilot" from earlier developmental phases of this work, abandon it or banish it entirely to the appendix. Your token-local evidence theory is currently built entirely on 2D spatial patches. Attempting to shoehorn temporal visual evidence into this specific narrative will break the tightness of your claims.

## SectionBySectionComments
*   **Abstract & Intro:** Excellent clarity. You explicitly acknowledge the use of generic external data, distinguishing your method from purely training-free approaches. Keep this transparency.
*   **Section 3.1 (Calibration):** The dense InfoNCE objective is sound, but the reliance purely on noun chunks is a vulnerability (see Weakness 1). You must either augment the training data with relation graphs/spatial captions or explicitly downgrade your spatial reasoning claims.
*   **Section 3.2 (Entropy-Driven Fallback):** The mathematical formulation is logical, but masking "attention sinks" before calculating entropy requires a robust definition. How are sinks identified dynamically across different vision encoders? 
*   **Section 3.4 (VASM):** The OCR Concession is logically sound, but you must ensure that your introduction does *not* bait the reader into thinking TLRA solves dense document understanding. If the method bypasses OCR, do not motivate the paper with document-heavy scenarios.
*   **Section 4.1-4.3 (Evaluation Protocol):** The requirement to report AGL variance and VASM trigger rates alongside standard metrics is exceptional and sets a high bar for reproducibility.

## RequiredRevisions
1. **Resolve the Spatial vs. Noun-Chunk Paradox:** Either modify the $\Phi_{calib}$ training pipeline to include spatial relation triplets (e.g., using a scene graph parser instead of just spaCy noun chunks), OR rewrite the hypothesis for Chain C to focus on *fine-grained object attributes/states* rather than explicitly spatial relations.
2. **Define the Validation Protocol:** Add a sub-section or paragraph explicitly detailing how hyperparameters ($M$, $\alpha$, $\theta_{fallback}$) are calibrated using a strictly held-out validation set (e.g., a subset of VisDial or a generic multimodal validation set not used in test benchmarks).
3. **Solidify the Scope:** Maintain the current bounded claims: "token-local logits intervention + VASM + fair zero-shot/calibrated split". Do not attempt to expand the paper into a generalized "cure for all multimodal hallucinations."

## SuggestedFiguresTablesExperiments
Since your experiments are in the execution phase, you must integrate the following into your experimental design:
1. **BPE Completion Success Rate (Quantitative):** Add a metric to Evidence Chain B that explicitly measures suffix survival. Extract a list of 500 multi-token physical entities. Force the model to generate them with and without TLRA. Report the exact percentage of times the prefix intervention successfully leads to the correct suffix vs. grammatical collapse/stuttering. Figure 3's qualitative heatmap is not enough; we need the aggregate survival rate.
2. **The "Zero-Shot" Baseline Enforcement:** Ensure that `TLRA_zero` is included in Chain C as planned. It is critical to visually prove the embedding asymmetry you claim in the introduction.
3. **Fallback Activation Scatter Plot:** In your planned Figure 1, ensure the scatter plot clearly differentiates tasks by their token length (e.g., POPE vs MMMU). We need to see a clear correlation: as generated length increases, Fallback Activation Rate approaches 100%, proving the system correctly reverts to dense evaluation to avoid the prefill blindspot.
4. **Isolate Object Existence vs Spatial:** In Table 3 (FREAK), if your calibrator remains noun-chunk only, I expect `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` to crush `TLRA_MeanPool` on Object Existence, but potentially tie or fail on Spatial Relations. If this happens, report it honestly as a limitation of the calibrator's training data, not as a failure of the Top-$k$ hypothesis.

## AcceptanceOutlook
This is a highly promising, methodologically sound framework. If the authors strictly execute the proposed 3-chain experimental protocol, honestly report the negative controls (DocVQA) and the parity baselines (`TLRA_MeanPool`), and address the risks regarding BPE momentum and spatial relations, this paper will be a strong candidate for acceptance at ACM Multimedia. The focus must remain entirely on empirical execution and maintaining the tightly bounded claims presented in this draft.
