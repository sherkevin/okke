# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Late-Fusion Visual Entity Existence Checker for Autoregressive Decoding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from entity hallucination when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during autoregressive decoding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways to route fine-grained evidence. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual entity existence verifier**. It reliably answers "Is this noun anywhere in the image?" establishing a strict theoretical boundary between global entity grounding and targeted relational VQA vulnerabilities. To resolve parameter bloat, TLRA projects strictly to an offline-filtered physical noun subset ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$), reducing the structural footprint to $\sim$18M parameters. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A semantically coherent initialization tied to the LLM's **Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)**, explicitly bypassing the Layer 0-to-Layer $N$ residual distortion that plagues input-embedding initializations to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring trained via a hard-negative contrastive objective, featuring an **in-window Dynamic Syntax Floor** audited for grammatical validity to prevent structural stuttering; and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) equipped with a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification**. Crucially, we reject the premise that forcing a subword pivot is inherently "graceful." Instead, we pre-register a boundary-aware evaluation protocol that explicitly compares greedy "Pivot Survival" against theoretical Beam Search Rollback, audits relational hallucination exacerbation, and benchmarks against prompt-injected object detection (e.g., Grounding DINO) to justify the necessity of late-fusion intervention.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), such as LLaVA-1.5 and Llama-3-Vision variants, exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to entity hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, OPERA) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding, attention-penalty heuristics, or distribution shifts. However, they do not introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways trained specifically to verify spatial presence at the token level. 

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit existence-checking supervision.

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly downgrade our scope from general "hallucination suppression" to **Entity Existence Verification**. Algorithmically, TLRA acts as a context-blind existence checker for an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). By pooling patch features independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. This establishes two hard theoretical boundaries. First, against **Prompt-Injected Object Detection**: if TLRA cannot outperform an off-the-shelf detector (e.g., Grounding DINO) appending a text list of objects to the prompt, its architectural complexity is unjustified. We hypothesize that TLRA's token-level logit control prevents the attention dilution inherent in long context prompts. Second, TLRA risks **exacerbating relational hallucinations**. If a prompt asks "Is the dog on the sofa?" and the image contains a dog on the floor, TLRA will validate the existence of "sofa" and may inadvertently reinforce the language prior's incorrect relational claim. 

Furthermore, we confront two existential threats to late-fusion routing: **Geometric Incoherence** and **BPE Subword Collisions**. Training a projection matrix from scratch on 50k captions functionally guarantees failure on unseen tokens. While initializing from Layer 0 input embeddings assumes the residual stream preserves the embedding manifold, modern LLMs (e.g., LLaMA-3) heavily distort this space via repeated RMSNorms and Swish-GLU MLPs. We resolve this by extracting TLRA's initialization directly from the LLM's **final Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)**, guaranteeing exact geometric alignment with the Layer $N$ output logits.

Simultaneously, modern tokenizers shatter common words (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`). Penalizing the first subword (`_re`) destroys valid non-nouns ("return"). We introduce a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** to defer penalties to unique semantic identifiers. However, forcing an autoregressive trajectory to abandon a noun mid-word is a text-corruption risk, often leading to semantic absurdity ("The man opened the *realize*") or forcing the model to select a grammatically invalid syntax token (e.g., a comma) via our Dynamic Syntax Floor.

Our evaluation is a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Semantic Absurdity:** Evaluating entity grounding (POPE/CHAIR) against zero-shot methods and **Prompt-Injected Object Detection**, while explicitly auditing Semantic Coherence via a "Pivot Survival Rate" LLM Judge. We contrast greedy subword pivots against the theoretical gold-standard of **Beam Search Rollback**.
2. **Context-Blindness and Targeted VQA:** Mapping the exact failure boundaries of history-independent routing by splitting evaluation into Global Existence (POPE) and Relational Vulnerability (MMHal-Relations).
3. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general reasoning (MMBench) and geometric initialization (Unembedding Matrix vs. Layer 0 vs. Random), alongside an untrained `TLRA_zero` representation check.
4. **Hardware Reality and The Latency Bottleneck:** A formal evaluation of the Trie-Lookahead's computational overhead, mapping Tokens/Second throughput on GPU at Batch Size $\ge 8$ to measure true warp divergence.
5. **The Dynamic Syntax Floor Audit:** Quantifying the exact trigger rates, Argmax Retention limits, and the critical **Forced Syntactic Error Rate** (Part-of-Speech tracking) when the hallucination penalty clashes with weak non-noun distributions.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Sparse Architectural Scope and Unembedding Matrix Initialization

TLRA targets standard MLLM architectures and operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage, strictly limited to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ maps cached spatial visual states to this entity subset. 

**The Sparsity Trap and $W_{out}$ Alignment:** To enable zero-shot generalization to unseen tokens, $W_{calib}$ must inherit semantic structure. Previous iterations of late-fusion models assumed that initializing from Layer 0 input token embeddings was sufficient. However, modern MLLMs featuring deep non-linearities, repeated RMSNorms, and Swish-GLU MLPs (e.g., LLaMA derivatives) induce severe rotation and scaling, destroying the Layer 0-to-Layer $N$ manifold alignment. To mathematically bypass this, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)** corresponding to the $V_{noun}$ vocabulary. Because $W_{out}$ projects the final hidden state to logits, it is perfectly aligned with the Layer $N$ geometric space by definition. 

**`TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** A strict baseline computing cosine similarity directly between frozen visual features $X_v$ and the $W_{out}$ target embeddings without parametric training. This lower-bound isolates whether explicit contrastive supervision ($W_{calib}$) is necessary, or if inherent MLLM visual-textual feature alignment suffices.

### 3.2 Training Formulation and Hard Negative Sampling

To calibrate $W_{calib}$ and prevent mode collapse (saturating outputs to 1.0), we employ a strict **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** ($\mathcal{L}_{calib}$). For a given image with ground-truth physical nouns $\mathcal{P}$, we dynamically sample **hard negative nouns** $\mathcal{N}$ from $V_{noun}$ using corpus co-occurrence matrices (objects frequently associated with $\mathcal{P}$ but explicitly absent from the current image). The LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{P}} \log(\hat{S}(c)) - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{N}} \log(1 - \hat{S}(c))
$$

### 3.3 The Top-$k$ "Existence Checker" and the Dynamic Syntax Floor

At decoding step $t$, TLRA computes a spatial support score by pooling the **Top-$k$** matching patches for a candidate noun token $c$ from the candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_{c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
Because $S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$, TLRA functions strictly as a context-blind *Global Entity Existence Checker*. 

To apply this penalty without inducing catastrophic grammatical stuttering, we enforce a **Dynamic Syntax Floor**. Let $L_{floor} = \max_{v \in \mathcal{C}_t \setminus \mathcal{V}_{noun}} L_{orig}(v)$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the Top-$M$ window. The final logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = \max \Big( L_{floor} - \epsilon, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big)
$$
**The Grammatical Collapse Risk:** We subtract a microscopic $\epsilon$ from the floor comparison so the alternative syntax token $L_{floor}$ wins the argmax tie. However, this introduces a fatal vulnerability: if the autoregressive context strongly demands a noun (e.g., "The man opened the [refrigerator]"), $L_{floor}$ might be a grammatically invalid preposition or punctuation mark. Forcing the model to output "The man opened the ," is semantic destruction. We pre-register a **Forced Syntactic Error Rate (PoS Audit)** to rigorously measure how often this mechanism trades a hallucinated noun for a structural grammatical collapse.

### 3.4 Resolving BPE Fragmentation: Subword-Trie Lookahead & Beam Search Rollback

Modern tokenizers shatter words. A naive "First-Subword Penalty" introduces a Prefix Collision Threat (penalizing `_re` due to the lack of a "refrigerator" simultaneously penalizes valid non-nouns like "return"). TLRA implements a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** via batched tensor masking:
1. **Deferred Penalty:** If a candidate matches an ambiguous prefix node, the penalty mask $\gamma(c)$ is set to 0.
2. **Unique Resolution:** The penalty is strictly deferred until the trajectory reaches a subword that *uniquely identifies* a noun stem (e.g., `friger` conditioned on `_re`). 

**Greedy Pivot vs. Beam Search Rollback:** If the image lacks a refrigerator, TLRA drops the logit for `friger`. Under greedy decoding, this forces the LLM to execute a forward pivot (e.g., completing `_re` + `alize`). We explicitly reject the notion that this is inherently "graceful." To isolate the exact cost of greedy decoding, our evaluation protocol defines two operational modes:
*   **TLRA (Greedy Pivot):** Highly latency-optimized, but carries immense text-corruption risk. Audited via the "Pivot Survival Rate."
*   **TLRA (Beam Search Rollback):** A theoretical upper bound. When a noun penalty triggers on a unique subword, the algorithm explicitly prunes the entire prefix (e.g., `_re`) from the active beam, forcing the model to resample from the point of initial divergence. This prevents fluent nonsense at the cost of severe throughput degradation.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict linguistic, structural, and hardware boundaries. By reframing TLRA as an explicitly-trained, context-blind entity existence verifier, we identify mathematically rigid limitations that future research must confront:

1. **The Prompt-Injection Threat:** If appending a list of objects detected by Grounding DINO to the system prompt achieves comparable POPE and MMHal scores, the architectural complexity of TLRA is functionally invalidated for purely offline tasks. TLRA's primary defense relies on its token-level latency control and ability to bypass attention-dilution limits inherent to long prompt contexts.
2. **Relational Exacerbation and Context-Blindness:** The checker pools patches independently of text context ($Y_{<t}$). If an object physically exists in the background, TLRA validates it. While TLRA excels at global entity captioning, it actively risks *exacerbating* relational hallucinations (validating the existence of "dog" and "sofa" even if the dog is not on the sofa).
3. **The "Graceful Pivot" vs. Grammatical Collapse:** The Subword-Trie Lookahead successfully prevents catastrophic prefix collisions. However, the Greedy Pivot is a severe semantic hazard. If forced to pivot mid-word, or if the Dynamic Syntax Floor forces the selection of a comma over a noun, grammatical structure collapses. While Beam Search Rollback theoretically solves this by pruning the entire prefix, it incurs massive computational overhead. 
4. **Hardware Latency and Warp Divergence:** Traversing a subword Trie for candidate tokens introduces branching logic. In diverse batched generation, sequences will hit ambiguous prefixes at completely different steps, causing severe GPU warp divergence that threatens Tokens/Second throughput.

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a sparse, geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head—anchored by the Unembedding Matrix—can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities, or if the algorithmic limits of late-fusion vocabulary projection represent an insurmountable theoretical ceiling.
