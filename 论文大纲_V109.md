# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Sparse Decode-Time Visual Existence Checker for Hallucination Suppression

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during entity grounding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate general model confidence, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens during autoregressive decoding. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual existence verifier** rather than a complex spatial reasoner. It reliably answers "Is this noun in the image?" to suppress pure hallucinations. To resolve geometric incoherence and parameter bloat, TLRA projects strictly to an offline-filtered physical noun subset ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$), reducing the structural footprint to $\sim$18M parameters and significantly mitigating gradient noise during training. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A semantically coherent, bias-free initialization tied directly to the LLM's **Layer 0 input token embeddings** to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring trained via a strict contrastive negative-sampling objective, featuring an **in-window Dynamic Syntax Floor** to prevent structural collapse; and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) equipped with a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** to survive the existential threat of BPE fragmentation—avoiding the catastrophic prefix collisions inherent in naive first-subword penalization. This work establishes a pre-registered, boundary-aware protocol for parameter-efficient decode-time visual routing, explicitly mapping its structural, relational, linguistic fluency, and latency boundaries.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), such as LLaVA-1.5 and Llama-3-Vision variants, exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, Image-Level Classifier-Free Guidance) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts. However, they cannot introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways. Standard parametric approaches, conversely, operate entirely in the prefill phase, compressing spatial evidence into the global context window without explicitly routing it to specific token generation steps. This leaves a critical gap: **How can we inject fine-grained, token-specific visual evidence directly into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying morphological syntax, suffering geometric mismatch, or succumbing to the prefix-collision trap of modern BPE tokenizers?**

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit supervision that zero-shot methods lack. 

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly reject the framing of TLRA as a "context-aware spatial reasoner." Algorithmically, TLRA acts as a **Global Visual Existence Checker**. By pooling patch features independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. While this effectively suppresses pure hallucinations, it establishes a hard theoretical boundary: TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. We embrace this constraint, demonstrating that context-blind existence checking is highly effective for core hallucination reduction if explicitly trained with strict negative sampling to prevent mode collapse.

Furthermore, we structurally correct the bloat of vocabulary-space projections. Projecting to a full 32,000 or 128,000-token vocabulary incurs massive parameter costs ($\sim$131M to $\sim$524M parameters), the vast majority mapping to non-visual abstract tokens. TLRA instead utilizes a **Sparse Deterministic Projection** ($D \times V_{noun}$), reducing the trainable footprint to $\sim$18M parameters by strictly targeting an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems. 

Finally, we confront two existential threats to late-fusion routing: **Geometric Incoherence** and **BPE Subword Collisions**. Training a projection matrix from scratch on 50k captions functionally guarantees failure on unseen tokens due to random initialization. TLRA solves this by initializing its sparse projection head strictly from the LLM's pre-trained **Layer 0 input token embeddings** (omitting bias terms). Simultaneously, modern tokenizers aggressively shatter common words into non-semantic fragments (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`). Penalizing the first subword (`_re`) catastrophicly penalizes valid non-nouns (e.g., "red", "return"). We explicitly audit this **Prefix Collision Threat** across Llama-2 and Llama-3 tokenizers and introduce a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** to ensure the existence checker remains viable without crippling general linguistic fluency.

Our evaluation is structured as a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Linguistic Collapse:** Evaluating entity grounding (POPE, AMBER) against VCD, DoLa, Image-Level CFG, and Woodpecker, while strictly auditing for Fluency (Perplexity) to ensure the intervention does not induce grammatical stuttering.
2. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general capabilities (MMBench) and explicitly testing geometric initialization (Layer 0 vs. Random vs. Layer $N$) on an "Unseen Objects" split alongside a fully untrained `TLRA_zero` baseline.
3. **Local Evidence Value & Relational Boundaries:** Isolating spatial advantage on FREAK, explicitly documenting TLRA's failure boundary on a Multi-Instance stress test, and mapping spatial heatmaps (including strict **Negative Controls**) to prove the extraction of localized visual evidence.
4. **Semantic Boundaries and The Prefix Collision:** Bounding the method via strict BPE Fragmentation Profiles and Trie-Lookahead resolution across tokenizer generations.
5. **Decode-Time Latency Physics:** A formal evaluation mapping the true memory bandwidth bottleneck, reporting Tokens/Second (throughput) alongside VRAM utilization.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Sparse Architectural Scope and Geometrically Coherent Initialization

TLRA targets standard MLLM architectures where spatial provenance is preserved across visual tokens $X_v$. TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage:

**1. `TLRA_calib` (The Sparse Parametric Routing Head):** Instead of a full $D \times V$ matrix, TLRA strictly limits its projection to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ maps cached spatial visual states to this subset. 
- **The Sparsity Trap and Geometric Coherence:** Post-MLP visual features $X_v$ are geometrically aligned to the LLM's **input embedding space** (Layer 0), *not* the output `lm_head` (Layer $N$). To enable zero-shot generalization, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained Layer 0 input token embeddings**. To strictly preserve this pre-trained dot-product manifold, $W_{calib}$ is implemented **without a bias term**.
- **`TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** A critical baseline that computes cosine similarity directly between frozen $X_v$ and the LLM's discrete Layer 0 text embeddings without any parametric training. This serves as the fundamental lower bound to prove whether decode-time spatial routing requires explicit contrastive supervision ($W_{calib}$), or if inherent feature alignment is sufficient.

### 3.2 Training Formulation and Contrastive Negative Sampling

If trained solely on standard captions (positive occurrences), an existence checker will suffer mode collapse, saturating scalar outputs to 1.0 for all patches. To calibrate $W_{calib}$, we employ a strict **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** ($\mathcal{L}_{calib}$). For a given image, let $\mathcal{P}$ be the ground-truth physical nouns. We dynamically sample a set of **hard negative nouns** $\mathcal{N}$ from $V_{noun}$ (objects frequently co-occurring in the corpus but absent from the current image). The base LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{P}} \log(\hat{S}(c)) - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{N}} \log(1 - \hat{S}(c))
$$
This explicitly forces $\hat{S}$ toward 0 when visual evidence is absent.

### 3.3 The Top-$k$ "Existence Checker" and the Dynamic Syntax Floor

At each decoding step $t$, TLRA computes a raw spatial support score by pooling the **Top-$k$** matching patches for a candidate token $c$ from the candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_{c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
$S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$. To apply this penalty without inducing catastrophic grammatical stuttering (e.g., forcing the model into a loop of prepositions or EOS tokens when a hallucinated noun is suppressed), we enforce a **Dynamic Syntax Floor**. Let $L_{floor} = \max_{v \in \mathcal{C}_t \setminus \mathcal{V}_{noun}} L_{orig}(v) + \epsilon$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the Top-$M$ window. The final adjusted logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = \max \Big( L_{floor}, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big)
$$
While this structural bound mitigates behavioral collapse by preventing the noun logit from falling below the best non-noun alternative, it introduces the risk of the model pivoting to an awkward syntactic path. We explicitly audit for this induced stuttering via Perplexity (PPL) metrics in our evaluation protocol.

### 3.4 Resolving BPE Fragmentation: Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification

VASM utilizes a deterministic binary mask ($\gamma \in \{0, 1\}^V$) targeting physical noun stems. However, modern tokenizers aggressively shatter words. A naive "First-Subword Penalty" introduces a fatal **Prefix Collision Threat**: if "refrigerator" tokenizes to `_re`, `friger`, `ator`, penalizing `_re` due to the lack of a refrigerator simultaneously penalizes valid words like "red" or "return," decimating general linguistic fluency.

To survive BPE fragmentation without prefix collisions, TLRA implements a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** strategy during the candidate window generation.
1. **Trie Construction:** We compile $V_{noun}$ into a subword Trie offline.
2. **Deferred Penalty:** At step $t$, if a candidate token matches an ambiguous prefix node in the Trie (e.g., `_re`), $\gamma(c)$ is temporarily set to 0 (no penalty is applied).
3. **Unique Resolution Pivot:** The penalty $\hat{S}$ is strictly deferred until the autoregressive trajectory reaches a subword that *uniquely identifies* a physical noun stem in the Trie (e.g., generating `friger` conditioned on `_re`). If the image lacks a refrigerator, TLRA penalizes `friger`, dropping its logit. 
Crucially, this allows the base LLM a **Graceful Pivot**: having generated `_re` but being blocked from `friger`, the LLM naturally falls back to strong language priors to complete a valid, non-hallucinated word (e.g., completing `_re` + `alize`), preserving grammatical fluency while suppressing the visual hallucination. We empirically profile this collision boundary in Section 4.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict linguistic, structural, and relational boundaries. By reframing TLRA as an explicitly-trained visual existence verifier operating in a sparse lexical space, we identify mathematically rigid limitations:

1. **The Trie-Lookahead Overhead and Latency:** Decode-time memory bandwidth is the true bottleneck of autoregressive generation. While TLRA boasts a tiny $\sim$18M parameter footprint, the operations required at every decoding step—extracting features, Top-$k$ pooling, and traversing the Subword-Trie to defer ambiguous prefixes—incur non-trivial latency costs. If the Tokens/Second throughput drops below viable interactive thresholds, parameter efficiency becomes irrelevant.
2. **Grammar Pivots vs. Stuttering:** The Dynamic Syntax Floor algorithmically prevents the noun logit from dropping below the best non-noun token. However, if the intended (but hallucinated) noun is heavily penalized, the LLM is forced to sample the next highest token. While our Trie-Lookahead enables "Graceful Pivots" at the subword level, phrase-level pivots can still induce unnatural stuttering or grammatical collapse, necessitating strict continuous auditing via Perplexity.
3. **Geometric Coherence vs. Deep Fusion:** Initializing $W_{calib}$ from Layer 0 input embeddings forces a single linear layer to execute cross-modal alignment that deep-fused architectures handle over multiple blocks. If visual features lack linearly separable existence boundaries at Layer 0, `TLRA_calib` will struggle to meaningfully outperform the untrained `TLRA_zero` baseline.
4. **Relational Blindness:** The "Existence Checker" pools patches independently of text context. TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries, trading complex spatial reasoning for targeted decode-time suppression.

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a sparse, geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head—equipped with strict negative sampling and subword-trie verification—can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities to provide scalable visual grounding.
