# Token-Local Visual Evidence for Structure-Preserving MLLM Decoding via Bounded Region Alignment

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to object hallucination when overarching language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense spatial grounding. While existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalties) serve as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers for broad language or attention dynamics, they lack a mechanism to inject strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bounded Region Alignment (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

Strictly scoped to 2D Image-LLMs to secure defensible claims regarding spatial locality, BRA operates on three core mechanisms. First, acknowledging spatial washout in deep self-attention, we establish an evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot diagnostic (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight, dynamically calibrated bound (`BRA_calib`). We explicitly formalize `BRA_calib` as a **hybrid test-time adaptation** requiring a strictly isolated 5k-sample prior. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence, utilizing an exact prefill-derived visual activation tensor to establish a static threshold, avoiding the collapse of moving medians in sparse images. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via a WordNet spatial dictionary and Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation tracking. To avoid conflating "structure preservation" with mere "intervention bypass" in out-of-vocabulary domains, VASM is instrumented to transparently log exact intervention trigger rates.

To rigorously assess algorithmic overhead, we propose a pre-registered evaluation protocol across three definitive evidence chains: (A) Hallucination Reduction isolating mechanisms against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` control, (B) Structure Preservation explicitly profiling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) bypass rates and quantifying polysemy failures, and (C) Local Evidence Value comparing `BRA_zero/calib` against global pooling (`BRA_MeanPool`). Alongside transparent defense lines dissecting Inter-Token Latency (ITL) bottlenecks for the $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ CUDA sorting overheads, this framework establishes a scientifically rigorous proposition for dictionary-gated token-local intervention.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable zero-shot capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to establish that these methods serve as state-of-the-art, orthogonal regularizers that effectively discipline overarching language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. They are highly competitive alternatives; however, because they operate on broader generation dynamics rather than explicit spatial feature grounding, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination faithfully, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We conceptualize this intervention as **Bounded Region Alignment (BRA)**. In this work, we strictly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, deliberately discarding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims surrounding spatial locality.

Executing BRA requires acknowledging and mathematically overcoming three structural barriers. First, **input-output embedding asymmetry** and **spatial washout**. Deep self-attention inevitably diffuses localized patch coordinates by the final layer. Second, the computational complexity of **dynamic spatial pooling** at every decoding step. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities.

BRA resolves these barriers via a structured framework:
1. **Bi-Level Logits Intervention (`BRA_zero` / `BRA_calib`)**: We establish a strict offline evaluation boundary testing whether final-layer visual states natively align with the terminal vocabulary (`BRA_zero`). Acknowledging that architectures like LLaVA-1.5 will likely fail this zero-shot diagnostic due to spatial washout, we pivot to `BRA_calib`—explicitly shifting the method into a **hybrid test-time adaptation** requiring a lightweight, isolated 5k-sample prior.
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations. We mathematically define the activation threshold solely during the prefill stage to maintain stability in sparse image contexts.
3. **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, utilizing dynamic BPE continuation inheritance and an explicit ~85k WordNet physical entity dictionary to shield structural language. Crucially, we directly confront VASM's conflation of "preservation" and "inaction" by transparently tracking explicit Intervention Trigger Rates (%) against Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) terms.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, our experimental design is organized into an execution protocol defining three primary evidence chains. 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mechanism value on POPE, CHAIR, and AGL against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline to definitively isolate inference-time gains from parameter exposure.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving VASM dynamics via MMBench and MMMU(Hard), openly profiling OOV intervention bypass rates, and numerically quantifying polysemy false positives.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the definitive superiority of localized evidence on dense tasks (FREAK, DocVQA) by strictly comparing against a global average baseline (`BRA_MeanPool`).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Embedding Asymmetry and Spatial Washout

A fundamental challenge in decode-time visual intervention is embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input layer. To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states corresponding to the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$.

We explicitly address "spatial washout"—the reality that deep self-attention often destroys localized visual coordinates by the final layer. We define a strict operational boundary governed by a feature-level **Washout Threshold**, evaluated offline once per architecture:

1. **`BRA_zero` (The Diagnostic Null-Hypothesis)**: A zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support via $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$. 
2. **The Washout Threshold**: We test zero-shot bounding box retrieval on a held-out 500-sample spatial validation set. If the native projection fails to achieve an average bounding box $\text{IoU} > 0.15$ against ground truth using Otsu's thresholding, native spatial fidelity is mathematically declared lost. We anticipate models like LLaVA-1.5 will trigger this threshold, rendering `BRA_zero` primarily a diagnostic tool rather than a viable decoding strategy.
3. **`BRA_calib`**: Upon triggering the threshold, we pivot to a hybrid test-time adaptation. $\Phi_{calib}$ is a 2-layer MLP projection ($\approx 4.2$M parameters) optimized on 5,000 image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. **Crucially, these pairs are size-stratified and sampled exclusively from Visual Genome subsets strictly disjoint from test datasets.** The base LLM remains entirely frozen.

**Strict Semantic & Spatial Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. To prevent feature collisions in dense images (e.g., mistakenly penalizing a cat's tail when the anchor is the cat's head), negative patches ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are strictly filtered. A valid negative must satisfy two conditions: (1) Membership in a differing semantic super-category, and (2) An absolute spatial exclusion zone of $\text{IoU} < 0.1$ with the positive anchor.

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling

At decoding step $t$, a naive Softmax over all visual patches artificially inflates noise. We apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{active}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{active}\Big)
$$
To calculate $\theta_{active}$ reliably without the vulnerability of step-by-step moving medians in sparse images, we derive it **strictly once during the prefill stage** (processing the prompt image and text prefix). 
For each final-layer visual token $v_j$, we compute its maximum visual-to-semantic activation across the targeted dictionary $\mathcal{V}_{dict}$:
$$
A(v_j) = \max_{c \in \mathcal{V}_{dict}} \left( W_{vocab}[c] \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)}) \right)
$$
The absolute threshold $\theta_{active}$ is defined as the 85th percentile of the set $\{A(v_1), \dots, A(v_{N_v})\}$. This provides a mathematically stable, image-conditioned absolute threshold that does not require re-computation during autoregressive generation.

We then apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$, aggregating only the top-$k$ most supportive activated patches. The mathematical sorting operation operates on a tensor of size $M \times N_v$, where $M$ is the pre-selected candidate vocabulary window. 

### 3.3 Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing. This penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

VASM prevents structural collapse via a dual-tier protection architecture:
1. **Explicit Root Dictionary Expectation:** We define the visual noun dictionary offline using the WordNet *physical entity* subtree ($\approx 85,000$ synsets). $\gamma=1$ *only* for tokens matching this dictionary. 
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Tokenizers like LLaMA-2/3 frequently split common nouns using prefix spaces (`_` or `Ġ`). VASM matches the root text explicitly after stripping these prefixes. Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its root token via a pre-compiled GPU-side bitmask.

**The Polysemy Concession:** We explicitly recognize that a deterministic dictionary cannot resolve polysemy natively. Non-visual usages of physical nouns (e.g., the financial "bank" vs. a river "bank") risk triggering false positive suppression. Furthermore, modern proper nouns (e.g., "Cybertruck") missing from WordNet will inherently bypass intervention ($\gamma=0$). We commit to empirically quantifying both polysemy failures and Out-Of-Vocabulary bypass rates in our evaluation protocol, having completely excised initial designs for "confidence-conditioned gating" to eliminate pipeline bloat.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces specific structural hypotheses—and acknowledges severe risks of Inter-Token Latency (ITL) bottlenecks and intervention bypass if unverified—we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains. The protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). All subsequent sections outline our planned, hypothesis-driven execution. 

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction via Step-by-Step Attribution

**Hypothesis:** BRA mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting. These gains derive from the decode-time mechanism, not merely parameter exposure to calibration data.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Acc/F1, and False Positive Rate), CHAIR (Object vs. Attribute), AGL (Attention-Grounded Localization), and RefCOCO (Zero-shot bounding box accuracy).
  - **Planned Output (Table 1 - Strict Format):** 
    - *Columns:* Model | POPE (F1) | **POPE (FPR)** | CHAIR (Obj) | RefCOCO (Acc)
    - *Rows:* Base | `Base + 5k LoRA` | DoLa* | VCD* | OPERA* | `BRA_MeanPool` | `BRA_calib (Base)` | `BRA_calib (+VASM)`
  - **The Comparison Fairness Context:** We explicitly note (*) that DoLa, VCD, and OPERA are zero-shot, orthogonal regularizers. `BRA_calib` utilizes a 5k-sample prior. 
  - **The Strict 5k LoRA Control:** The `Base + 5k LoRA` control is fine-tuned on the exact same 5k VG pairs used by $\Phi_{calib}$, matching optimizer states, epochs, and utilizing a LoRA rank equivalent to $\Phi_{calib}$'s 4.2M parameters. This definitively isolates inference-time spatial grounding gains. 
  - **False Positive Defense:** Reporting the False Positive Rate (FPR) for POPE is mandatory. If BRA merely lowers the overall confidence of "Yes" tokens, inflating accuracy by becoming overly conservative, the FPR will expose this failure.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure Preservation and the Inaction Conflation

**Hypothesis:** VASM protects structural tokens, but reliance on an explicit WordNet dictionary introduces a vulnerability where Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) terms bypass intervention entirely, conflating "preservation" with mere "inaction."
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard Subset).
  - **Intervention Trigger Rate Profiling (Figure 1):** We will construct a scatterplot with **X-axis = OOV Rate (%)** and **Y-axis = Accuracy Drop relative to Base**. Point sizes explicitly represent the **Intervention Frequency (%)**. If the intervention rate on MMMU drops below 5%, we will explicitly state: *"BRA maintains reasoning performance on MMMU primarily by remaining safely dormant due to out-of-vocabulary terms, rather than actively protecting complex reasoning."*
  - **Polysemy Measurement:** We introduce a manually annotated 100-sample *Polysemy Disambiguation* subset from Visual Genome to numerically quantify the false positive suppression rate of abstract usages of physical nouns (e.g., "bank"), moving beyond anecdotal visual grids.

### 4.3 Chain C: Local Evidence Value & Dense Spatial Evaluation

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting isolates specific evidence that diffuse global pooling natively washes out.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (Accuracy and ANLS) and DocVQA (ANLS).
  - **Baseline Comparison (Table 2):** Strictly isolating `BRA_zero`, `BRA_MeanPool` (naive global averaging), and `BRA_calib` (Top-$k$). We establish a strict threshold: if `BRA_MeanPool` is within 1% of the Top-$k$ variant, the $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ sorting overhead is completely unjustified. A 3-5% gap is required to validate the core spatial locality hypothesis.

### 4.4 Defense Lines: Inter-Token Latency (ITL) and OOM Transparency

**Hypothesis:** Executing $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorts at every decoding step fundamentally bottlenecks Inter-Token Latency (ITL), limiting the method to offline, high-precision applications.
- **Evaluation Protocol (Figure 3):** Executed on a standardized single A100 (80GB) GPU using BF16 precision and FlashAttention-2. We plot **Time to First Token (TTFT)** and **Inter-Token Latency (ITL)** against an expanding Candidate Window Size **$M \in \{10, 50, 100, 200\}$**, across Batch Sizes = 1, 4, and 8.
- **Baseline Context:** We explicitly partition TTFT (where $\theta_{active}$ is computed) and ITL (where the sorting bottleneck occurs). We will plot Batched Out-of-Memory (OOM) failures, formally defining whether BRA can function beyond Batch Size 1.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of Bounded Region Alignment (BRA) lies in its structurally bounded proposition: intervening at decode-time using token-local visual evidence while protecting language integrity via dictionary-gated tracking (VASM). However, its execution is governed by theoretical and operational constraints:

1. **The `BRA_calib` Hybrid Shift:** Deep self-attention demonstrably washes out localized patch fidelity by final layers. For architectures triggering the offline Washout Threshold (IoU < 0.15), the premise of zero-shot alignment is mathematically invalid. This explicitly shifts the method into a hybrid test-time adaptation requiring a 5k-sample prior, fundamentally differentiating it from orthogonal zero-shot regularizers.
2. **VASM Brittleness and the Inaction Conflation:** An explicitly defined ~85k WordNet superset cannot capture modern proper nouns or domain-specific jargon. In specialized tasks like MMMU, if critical visual nouns are OOV, VASM safely defaults to $\gamma=0$. "Structure preservation" in niche domains intrinsically conflates with "intervention bypass," an outcome we openly track. Furthermore, polysemy false positives remain an inherent vulnerability.
3. **The Inter-Token Latency (ITL) Bottleneck:** Executing spatial pooling and $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorting at every single decoding step incurs severe CUDA kernel overheads. While Time to First Token (TTFT) is minimally impacted due to prefill-only $\theta_{active}$ derivation, ITL becomes a strict bottleneck. Testing large batch sizes risks VRAM OOM failures, positioning BRA strictly as a high-precision analytical tool rather than a high-throughput serving API.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines Bounded Region Alignment (BRA), a rigorously defined framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through dictionary-gated, token-local logits intervention. By formalizing spatial washout (`BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`), computing a prefill-derived visual activation threshold, and engineering robust language protection via VASM, we establish a technically precise foundation for decode-time visual control. To prevent conflating inaction with preservation and to objectively assess algorithmic bottlenecks, the claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol—mandating exact Intervention Trigger Rate logging, polysemy quantification, strict `Base + 5k LoRA` isolation, and aggressive ITL transparency—establishing a verifiable roadmap for empirical execution.
