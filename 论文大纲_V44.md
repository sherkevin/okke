# Token-Local Visual Evidence for Structure-Preserving MLLM Decoding via Bounded Region Alignment

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to object hallucination when overarching language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense spatial grounding. While existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalties) serve as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers for broad language or attention dynamics, they lack a mechanism to inject strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bounded Region Alignment (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

Strictly scoped to 2D Image-LLMs to secure defensible claims regarding spatial locality, BRA operates on three core mechanisms. First, to address embedding asymmetry and spatial washout in deep layers, we establish an evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot projection (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight, dynamically calibrated bound (`BRA_calib`). We explicitly acknowledge `BRA_calib` as a **hybrid test-time adaptation** requiring a strictly isolated 5k-sample prior. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence, replacing brittle moving medians with a text-prefix-derived activation threshold to handle sparse images. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via an explicitly defined WordNet spatial dictionary. To avoid conflating "structure preservation" with mere "intervention bypass" in out-of-vocabulary domains, VASM is instrumented to transparently log exact intervention trigger rates.

To validate BRA and rigorously assess algorithmic overhead, we propose a pre-registered, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Evaluated across three definitive evidence chains—(A) Hallucination Reduction isolating mechanisms against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` control and tracking RefCOCO zero-shot grounding, (B) Structure Preservation explicitly profiling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) bypass rates, and (C) Local Evidence Value comparing `BRA_zero/calib` against global pooling (`BRA_MeanPool`). Alongside transparent defense lines logging exact $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ CUDA kernel overheads, this framework establishes a scientifically rigorous proposition for dictionary-gated token-local intervention.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable zero-shot capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to clarify that these methods serve as state-of-the-art orthogonal regularizers that effectively discipline the overarching language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. However, because they operate on broader generation dynamics rather than explicit spatial grounding, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination more faithfully than pure language/attention heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We conceptualize this intervention as **Bounded Region Alignment (BRA)**. In this work, we strictly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, deliberately discarding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims surrounding spatial locality.

Executing BRA requires acknowledging and mathematically overcoming three structural barriers. First, **input-output embedding asymmetry** and **spatial washout**. We hypothesize that for architectures like LLaVA-1.5, deep self-attention diffuses localized patch fidelity by the final layer. Second, the computational complexity of **dynamic spatial pooling** at every decoding step. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities.

BRA resolves these barriers via a structured framework:
1. **Bi-Level Logits Intervention (`BRA_zero` / `BRA_calib`)**: A strict evaluation boundary testing whether final-layer visual states natively align with the terminal vocabulary. If a formalized spatial washout threshold is crossed, we pivot to `BRA_calib`—explicitly shifting from a pure zero-shot method to a **hybrid test-time adaptation** requiring a lightweight, isolated 5k-sample prior.
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations bounded by text-prefix-derived thresholds to maintain stability in both dense and sparse image contexts.
3. **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, utilizing dynamic BPE continuation inheritance and an explicit ~85k WordNet physical entity dictionary to shield structural language. Crucially, we directly confront VASM's conflation of "preservation" and "inaction" by transparently tracking explicit Intervention Trigger Rates (%).
4. **Confidence-Conditioned Gating**: An optional heuristic targeting extreme linguistic confidence on visual nouns, subject to a strict pruning mandate: if it fails to yield $\ge 2\%$ absolute gain, it is excised to prevent pipeline bloat.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, our experimental design is organized into an execution protocol defining three primary evidence chains. 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mechanism value on POPE, CHAIR, and AGL against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline, isolating inference-time gains from parameter exposure, while tracking False Positive Rates and RefCOCO grounding.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving VASM dynamics via MMBench and MMMU(Hard), openly profiling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) intervention bypass rates and analyzing polysemy false positives.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the definitive superiority of localized evidence on dense tasks (FREAK, DocVQA) by strictly comparing against a global average baseline (`BRA_MeanPool`).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Embedding Asymmetry and Spatial Washout

A fundamental challenge in decode-time visual intervention is embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input layer. To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states corresponding to the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$.

We explicitly address the potential for "spatial washout"—the assumption that deep self-attention preserves localized visual support in final layers. To prevent falsely claiming zero-shot alignment if an architecture natively destroys spatial fidelity, we define a strict operational boundary governed by a feature-level **Washout Threshold**, evaluated offline once per architecture:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support via $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$. 
2. **The Washout Threshold**: We test zero-shot bounding box retrieval on a held-out 500-sample spatial validation set. A predicted bounding box is extracted by binarizing a Gaussian-smoothed heatmap of patch logits using Otsu's thresholding. If the native projection fails to achieve an average bounding box $\text{IoU} > 0.15$ against ground truth, native spatial fidelity is mathematically declared lost. We define 0.15 as the absolute minimum overlap required for basic object localization; falling below this indicates the final-layer representations are effectively spatially uniform. We anticipate architectures like LLaVA-1.5 will trigger this threshold.
3. **`BRA_calib`**: Upon triggering the threshold, we pivot to a hybrid test-time adaptation. $\Phi_{calib}$ is a 2-layer MLP projection ($\approx 4.2$M parameters) optimized on 5,000 image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. **Crucially, these pairs are size-stratified to prevent bias towards large objects and are sampled exclusively from Visual Genome subsets strictly disjoint from POPE/CHAIR test datasets.** The base LLM remains entirely frozen.

**Strict Semantic Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. Negative patches ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are cross-referenced to ensure an $\text{IoU} < 0.1$ with the target *and* membership in a differing semantic super-category. We acknowledge that bounding-box level contrastive learning is inherently noisy; dense, crowded patches causing feature collisions remain a limitation of this calibration phase.

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling

At decoding step $t$, a naive Softmax over all visual patches artificially inflates noise. We apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{active}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{active}\Big)
$$
Previously, utilizing a moving median for $\theta_{active}$ failed in sparse images where background patches dominate (reducing the median to noise levels). Instead, $\theta_{active}$ is derived **strictly once per prompt** as the 85th percentile of visual activations elicited by the text prefix, providing a stable, image-conditioned absolute threshold.

We then apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$, aggregating only the top-$k$ most supportive activated patches. The candidate vocabulary window $M$ is pre-selected by taking the Top-$M$ tokens from the base LLM's original logits. The mathematical sorting operation then operates on a tensor of size $M \times N_v$. Executing this sort per-token incurs an unavoidable $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ CUDA kernel launch overhead, necessitating strict latency tracking.

### 3.3 VASM and Confidence-Conditioned Gating

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing. This penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

VASM prevents structural collapse via a dual-tier protection architecture:
1. **Explicit Root Dictionary Expectation:** We define the visual noun dictionary offline using the WordNet *physical entity* subtree ($\approx 85,000$ synsets). $\gamma=1$ *only* for tokens matching this dictionary. 
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Tokenizers like LLaMA-2/3 frequently split common nouns using prefix spaces (`_` or `Ġ`). VASM matches the root text explicitly after stripping these prefixes. Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its root token via a pre-compiled GPU-side bitmask.

**The Polysemy Concession:** We explicitly recognize that a deterministic dictionary cannot resolve polysemy natively. Non-visual usages of physical nouns (e.g., the financial "bank" vs. a river "bank") risk triggering false positive suppression. Furthermore, modern proper nouns (e.g., "Cybertruck") missing from WordNet will inherently bypass intervention ($\gamma=0$).

**Confidence-Conditioned Gating:** 
Intervening across all confidence bands introduces unnecessary computational overhead. We define an optional heuristic strictly targeting extreme confidence ($Entropy < H_{low}$) on visual nouns ($\gamma=1$). High confidence on a visual noun warrants explicit visual scrutiny. To maintain scientific hygiene, this module is governed by a strict pruning mandate: if empirical ablations fail to demonstrate $\ge 2\%$ absolute performance gain across core benchmarks, it will be discarded from the final protocol.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces specific structural hypotheses—and acknowledges risks of computational bottlenecks and intervention bypass if unverified—we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains and strict defense lines. The protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector, 1D sequence) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). All subsequent sections outline our planned, hypothesis-driven execution. 

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction via Step-by-Step Attribution

**Hypothesis:** BRA mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting. These gains derive from the decode-time mechanism, not merely parameter exposure to calibration data.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Acc/F1, and False Positive Rate), CHAIR (Object vs. Attribute), AGL (Attention-Grounded Localization, IoU), and RefCOCO/RefCOCO+ (Zero-shot bounding box accuracy).
  - **Planned Output (Table 1 - Strict Format):** 
    - *Columns:* Model | POPE (F1) | POPE (FPR) | CHAIR (Obj) | RefCOCO (Acc) | **Tok/Sec** | **% Tokens Intervened**
    - *Rows:* Base | `Base + 5k LoRA` | DoLa* | VCD* | OPERA* | `BRA_MeanPool` | `BRA_calib (Base)` | `BRA_calib (+VASM)` | `BRA_calib (Full)`
  - **The Comparison Fairness Context:** We explicitly note (*) that DoLa, VCD, and OPERA are fully zero-shot and training-free orthogonal regularizers. `BRA_calib`, conversely, utilizes a 5k-sample prior. 
  - **The 5k LoRA Control & Strict Isolation:** The `Base + 5k LoRA` control is fine-tuned on the exact same 5k VG pairs used by $\Phi_{calib}$ to definitively isolate inference-time spatial grounding gains from parameter exposure. The inclusion of the False Positive Rate (FPR) for POPE ensures BRA is not merely suppressing visual generation to artificially inflate the "Yes/No" ratio.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure Preservation and the Inaction Conflation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via visual suppression does not degrade complex reasoning if VASM protects structural tokens. However, reliance on an explicit WordNet dictionary introduces a vulnerability where Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) terms bypass intervention entirely, conflating "preservation" with mere "inaction."
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard Subset).
  - **Intervention Trigger Rate Profiling (Figure 1):** To enforce methodological honesty, we construct a scatterplot. **X-axis = OOV Rate (%)** (percentage of dataset visual nouns missing from WordNet), and **Y-axis = Accuracy Drop relative to Base**. Point sizes explicitly represent the **Intervention Frequency (%)**. If MMMU performance is maintained simply because BRA bypasses highly specialized vocabulary, this limitation will be explicitly documented.

### 4.3 Chain C: Local Evidence Value & Dense Spatial Evaluation

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting isolates specific evidence that diffuse global pooling natively washes out.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (Accuracy and ANLS for OCR/Position) and DocVQA (ANLS).
  - **Baseline Comparison (Table 2):** Strictly isolating the performance of `BRA_zero`, `BRA_MeanPool` (naive global averaging), and `BRA_calib`. If `BRA_MeanPool` outperforms the Adaptive Top-$k$ variant in dense document contexts, the core spatial locality hypothesis fails.
  - **Visualizing Local Evidence (Figure 2):** We will provide side-by-side heatmaps comparing: (1) The raw `lm_head` projection, (2) The $\Phi_{calib}$ projection, and (3) The specific tokens being penalized versus rewarded.

### 4.4 Defense Lines: Latency Reality and OOM Transparency

**Hypothesis:** Executing $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorts at every decoding step incurs significant $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ CUDA kernel launch overheads, fundamentally bottlenecking generation throughput.
- **Evaluation Protocol (Figure 3):** Executed strictly on a standardized single A100 (80GB) GPU using BF16 precision and FlashAttention-2. We plot **Tokens/Sec** (left y-axis, with standard deviation error bars to capture kernel launch jitter across generation lengths) and **Peak VRAM Usage** (right y-axis) against an expanding Candidate Window Size **$M \in \{10, 50, 100, 200\}$**, across **Batch Sizes = 1, 4, and 8**.
- **Baseline Context:** A baseline horizontal line representing the vanilla LLM generation speed will be included so the exact percentage of throughput degradation is visually immediate. We will explicitly plot Batched Out-of-Memory (OOM) failures, formally assessing BRA's viability as a high-precision analytical tool versus a high-throughput serving mechanism.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of Bounded Region Alignment (BRA) lies in its structurally bounded proposition: intervening at decode-time using token-local visual evidence while protecting language integrity via dictionary-gated tracking (VASM). However, its execution is governed by theoretical and operational constraints:

1. **The `BRA_calib` Hybrid Shift:** Deep self-attention likely degrades localized patch fidelity by final layers. For architectures triggering the offline Washout Threshold (IoU < 0.15), the premise of zero-shot alignment is invalid, forcing reliance on $\Phi_{calib}$. This explicitly shifts the method from pure training-free inference to a hybrid test-time adaptation requiring a 5k-sample prior, fundamentally differentiating it from orthogonal zero-shot regularizers like DoLa or VCD.
2. **VASM Brittleness and the Inaction Conflation:** An explicitly defined ~85k WordNet superset cannot capture modern proper nouns (e.g., "Cybertruck") or domain-specific jargon. In specialized tasks like MMMU, if critical visual nouns are OOV, VASM defaults to $\gamma=0$. "Structure preservation" in niche domains intrinsically conflates with "intervention bypass." Furthermore, polysemy false positives (suppressing abstract usages of physical nouns) remain an inherent vulnerability.
3. **Latency Bottleneck from CUDA Overheads:** Executing spatial pooling and $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorting at every single decoding step triggers measurable kernel launch jitter and overheads. Isolating decode-time latency reveals a strict $\mathcal{O}(M \cdot N_v \log k)$ bottleneck. Testing large batch sizes risks VRAM OOM failures, positioning BRA primarily for high-precision offline applications rather than real-time generation APIs.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines Bounded Region Alignment (BRA), a rigorously defined framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through dictionary-gated, token-local logits intervention. By formalizing embedding asymmetry (`BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`) via offline spatial washout thresholds, utilizing Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ pooling, and engineering robust language protection via VASM, we establish a technically precise foundation for decode-time visual control. To prevent conflating inaction with preservation and to objectively assess algorithmic overhead, the claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol—mandating exact Intervention Trigger Rate logging, RefCOCO grounding verification, polysemy failure analysis, and aggressive latency transparency—establishing a verifiable roadmap for empirical execution.
