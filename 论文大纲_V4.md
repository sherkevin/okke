# Breaking the Language Inertia: Zero-Pooling Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring for MLLM Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate high-resolution visual tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D-RoPE spatial coordinates necessary for dense grounding. In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a zero-pooling, decode-time intervention operating directly in the logits space. 

To address critical topological constraints, BRA incorporates three core mechanisms: (1) **Relative Resonance Reshaping**, deploying a stabilized additive shift based on local evidence calibration; (2) **Dynamic Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)**, transitioning from brittle tokenizer heuristics to an entropy-guided damping mechanism that universally protects BPE fragments and functional syntax; and (3) **Cross-Frame Attention Displacement**, substituting mathematically flawed latent-space subtraction with robust attention-weight tracking to isolate temporal actions. This manuscript establishes a rigorous 6-stage experimental protocol—anchored by a Modality Alignment Probe and dense OCR tasks—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy, modality alignment requirements, and system overhead of logits-space interventions.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model hallucinates—producing text that is linguistically fluent but physically absent from the visual input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on contrastive decoding or backtracking (e.g., VCD, OPERA, Woodpecker). While effective, they introduce significant latency overheads and VRAM spikes. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution MLLMs, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool or PCA) thousands of high-resolution visual tokens. This operation irreversibly destroys the fine-grained spatial coordinates (like 2D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects and dense text, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or GUI navigation.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a zero-shot max-pooling match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial information. However, implementing this principle exposes mathematical and engineering vulnerabilities: the unproven assumption of modality alignment in the unembedding space, the catastrophic fragmentation of continuous BPE sub-words, and the non-linear instability of naive temporal differencing in video tasks.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Our contributions are methodological and structural:
1. We formulate **Relative Resonance Reshaping**, mitigating the hyper-sensitivity of logits scaling by introducing a conservative, normalized additive shift.
2. We design an **Entropy-Guided Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)**, replacing tokenizer-specific heuristic rules with a universal, dynamic mechanism to protect structural language components.
3. We resolve the mathematical fallacy of latent-space spatial filtering by introducing **Cross-Frame Attention Displacement** to capture video actions.
4. We define a stringent **6-stage Evaluation Protocol**, testing modality alignment ($\Phi$), dense spatial preservation (DocVQA/TextVQA), and Pareto-optimal system efficiency.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 The Modality Gap and Resonance Formulation

The core hypothesis of BRA is that intervention should occur in the logits space to avoid pooling high-resolution spatial tokens. Given a candidate token set $\mathcal{C}_t = \{c_1, \dots, c_K\}$ at decoding step $t$, we measure the visual support for each candidate. 

A critical vulnerability in naive logits-space intervention is the assumption of topological orthogonality—whether the last-layer visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ reside in the exact same semantic manifold as the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$. We formalize the raw resonance score $S_{raw}(c)$ through an alignment mapping $\Phi$:

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \max_{j \in \{1 \dots N_v\}} \cos(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)}))
$$

where $\Phi$ defaults to the identity mapping $I$ if late-layer self-attention naturally aligns modalities. If Protocol 0 proves this alignment insufficient, $\Phi$ acts as a lightweight linear projector trained via contrastive loss on a minimal dataset (e.g., MSCOCO validation set) in under 1 hour on a single GPU. This ensures the deployment cost remains strictly marginal compared to heavy decoding baselines.

### 3.2 Relative Resonance Reshaping (Additive Shift)

Directly multiplying the logits by absolute resonance scores introduces extreme hyper-sensitivity. To stabilize the intervention, BRA utilizes a normalized **Additive Shift**.

We compute the relative evidence position within the Top-K candidates:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}_{\tau}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final logits are reshaped via additive penalties and rewards:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) + \alpha \cdot \eta(c) \cdot \hat S(c) - \beta \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
where $\alpha, \beta$ are additive constants, and $\eta(c), \gamma(c)$ are token-aware damping coefficients. This formulation operates as a local ranking calibrator rather than a global magnitude disruptor.

### 3.3 Dynamic Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)

Applying visual resonance blindly at the token level causes semantic collapse for multi-token entities (e.g., splitting "play" and "ing") and destroys functional syntax. Relying on hardcoded tokenizer prefixes (e.g., `Ġ`) is brittle and non-scalable. We upgrade SMI to an **Entropy-Guided Dynamic Weighting** mechanism.

Linguistic continuation tokens and functional syntax exhibit exceptionally low predictive entropy $H_t$ given the prior context, whereas new visual entities (nouns/verbs) exhibit higher entropy. We dynamically define the penalty weight $\gamma(c)$ and reward weight $\eta(c)$ as a function of the normalized candidate entropy:

$$
\gamma(c) = 1.0 - \exp(-\lambda \cdot H_t(c))
$$
$$
\eta(c) = \max(\gamma(c), \epsilon)
$$

1. **Visual Entities (High Entropy):** $\gamma \to 1.0$. Full resonance mapping is applied, enforcing strict visual grounding.
2. **BPE Fragments & Syntax (Low Entropy):** $\gamma \to 0.0$. Words like "ing" or "therefore" naturally drop to near-zero penalties ($L_{final} \approx L_{orig}$), ensuring universal, tokenizer-agnostic syntax preservation.

### 3.4 Cross-Frame Attention Displacement for Video

Naive linear subtraction $h_L^{(t)} - h_L^{(t-1)}$ mathematically fails because the LLM latent space does not possess continuous, linear spatial properties; subtracting non-linear features causes unpredictable semantic drift rather than isolating actions. 

BRA mitigates this by shifting from latent subtraction to **Cross-Frame Attention Displacement**. We track the shift in the final-layer self-attention weights $A_{t}^{(j)}$ of visual patch $j$ across consecutive frames. The temporal resonance focuses strictly on tokens where the attention displacement exceeds a background threshold:

$$
\Delta A^{(t, j)} = \text{KL-Divergence}(A_{t}^{(j)} \parallel A_{t-1}^{(j)})
$$

$S_{video}(c)$ is then computed using only the visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ that exhibit high $\Delta A$, safely isolating dynamic actions from static camera pans without relying on mathematically ungrounded latent spatial mean-pooling.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

This section outlines the rigorous, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol required to empirically validate BRA.

### 4.1 Protocol 0: The Modality Alignment Probe (Crucial Prerequisite)
**Hypothesis:** The final-layer visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ align sufficiently with the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$, or can be aligned using an ultra-lightweight projector $\Phi$.
**Experiment:** 
- Extract $h_L^{(v_j)}$ for 100 random MSCOCO images.
- **Success Criteria:** A side-by-side t-SNE visualization (Raw vs. Projected). The heatmap must demonstrate that the Top-5 text tokens for specific physical patches (e.g., bounding box of a dog) naturally decode to semantically relevant words (e.g., "dog", "fur"). If raw alignment fails, we train $\Phi$ on MSCOCO-val ($<1$ hour, 1 GPU) and prove its effectiveness.

### 4.2 Protocol 1: Static Hallucination and Length-Bias Avoidance
**Hypothesis:** BRA suppresses object hallucination without causing length collapse.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: POPE, CHAIR, MME.
- Baselines: Base decoding, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, Woodpecker, LURE.
- **Key Metric:** F1 Score / Accuracy and **Average Generation Length (AGL)**. The target is improved F1 while maintaining AGL within 5% of the Base model.

### 4.3 Protocol 2: The "Pooling Paradox" Proof via Dense Spatial Tasks
**Hypothesis:** Standard hidden-state interventions destroy 2D spatial features. BRA’s zero-pooling max-match preserves them.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: **DocVQA, TextVQA**, and VisualWebBench.
- **Mandatory Ablation:** Compare `BRA (Zero-Pooling)` against `BRA-MeanPool` and DoLa.
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA (Zero-Pooling)` must demonstrate a statistically significant margin on dense OCR and GUI navigation tasks, definitively proving the Pooling Paradox.

### 4.4 Protocol 3: Video Temporal Robustness Validation
**Hypothesis:** Cross-Frame Attention Displacement isolates true actions accurately without non-linear semantic drift.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: MVBench, VidHalluc.
- **Mandatory Ablation:** Base vs. `BRA (Latent Subtraction)` vs. `BRA (Attention Displacement)`.
- **Criteria:** Attention Displacement must prevent the degradation seen in Latent Subtraction during camera-pan subsets.

### 4.5 Protocol 4: The Latency/Overhead Pareto Frontier
**Hypothesis:** Operating strictly in the logits space via dot products is Pareto-superior to multi-pass methods.
**Experiment:**
- Construct a 2D bubble chart: X-axis is Latency (ms/token), Y-axis is POPE F1, Bubble Size represents Peak VRAM overhead.
- **Success Criteria:** BRA must occupy the top-left quadrant (high F1, low latency, minimal bubble size) compared to VCD and OPERA.

### 4.6 Protocol 5 & 6: SMI Case Audit and Sensitivity Analysis
**Hypothesis:** Entropy-guided SMI protects BPE continuity, and Additive Shift is hyperparameter-stable.
**Experiments:**
- **Protocol 5 (SMI Audit):** Provide a concrete generation trace showing a BPE word (e.g., "play" + "ing") where naive logits scaling breaks the word, but dynamic SMI reduces the visual penalty on "ing" to maintain fluency.
- **Protocol 6 (Sensitivity):** A line chart plotting POPE F1 against varying values of $\alpha$ and $\beta$ (from 0.0 to 3.0), proving the method does not require dataset-specific grid searches.

## 5. Discussion & Future Work

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to logits-space resonance anchoring is a structural necessity for AgentOS and high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly addressing the "Pooling Paradox", BRA provides a pathway to intervene safely without destroying the fragile 2D spatial encodings established by Vision-RoPE. The evolution from heuristic tokenizer rules to Entropy-Guided SMI, alongside the correction of video temporal features via Attention Displacement, bridges this gap from theory to a mathematically sound methodology. 

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Modality Alignment Dependency:** If $\Phi$ requires continuous retraining for newly released models, it slightly diminishes the "training-free" plug-and-play appeal, necessitating standardized alignment adapters.
2. **Temporal Window Limits:** While Attention Displacement resolves spatial filtering fallacies, tracking attention shifts over extremely long temporal context windows may still encounter phase-shift noise, requiring further investigation into hierarchical temporal modeling.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until Protocols 1-6 are fully executed, we strictly limit our claims to the theoretical soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific bottlenecks of high-resolution MLLM interventions, offering a concrete blueprint to validate the supremacy of zero-pooling, logits-space decoding.
