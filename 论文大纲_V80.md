# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding, attention control, and layer-contrast methods—are highly competitive sequence-level and distribution-level heuristics. However, they leave open the question of whether injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment can explicitly improve physical object grounding while mathematically preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, an evaluation framework and decode-time intervention protocol that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA operates on three structural commitments. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces sequence-level penalties with candidate-wise spatial support. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** stabilizes evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window. Third, to avoid the prohibitive latency of autoregressive part-of-speech tagging, we introduce **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**—an *offline, pre-computed* static vocabulary mask derived from WordNet, coupled with dynamic $O(1)$ BPE continuation inheritance, to deterministically protect functional syntax, abstract reasoning, and multi-token entities. 

Recognizing that visual and lexical states often lack native alignment, we structurally separate our evaluation tracks. We define `TLRA_zero` as the true zero-shot probe, and classify `TLRA_calib`—which utilizes a frozen projection trained on 50k captions—explicitly as a lightweight Test-Time Adaptation (TTA) module, preventing unfair comparisons against pure zero-shot baselines. We organize this paper strictly around a pre-registered experimental contract. The protocol mandates three explicit evidence chains: hallucination reduction (audited by Average Generation Length to reject truncation artifacts), structure preservation (using OCR/DocVQA strictly as mathematical negative controls), and local-evidence parity (stratified by physical objects seen vs. unseen by the calibrator). By auditing GPU memory-bandwidth latency (TPOT) and committing to a "Fallback Clause" if spatial routing fails the unseen test against a calibrated mean pool, this paper serves as a rigorous, falsifiable protocol for evaluating decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable reasoning capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding remains vulnerable to hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and plausible but weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is acutely problematic in the grounding of visual physical entities—such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting—where the necessary visual evidence is highly localized in the patch space.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances in the field and serve as our primary legitimate baselines. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) contrast different input noise conditions to reshape distributions; attention-based control methods (`OPERA`) penalize abnormal generation patterns; and layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit differences between shallow and deep representations. These are highly competitive *sequence-level or distribution-level language heuristics*. However, they leave a specific, complementary proposition untested: 

**Can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment to outperform sequence-level heuristics, without destroying language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce physical entity hallucination more faithfully than purely distribution-level heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit, automated structure-preserving safeguards. 

Executing this intervention is mechanically and systemically non-trivial due to three explicit bottlenecks:
1. **Embedding Asymmetry:** Visual patch states and output logits do not naturally align in base models. Bridging this gap often requires external priors, structurally shifting the method from pure zero-shot to lightweight adaptation.
2. **The Autoregressive Latency Paradox:** Running external NLP pipelines (e.g., POS taggers) to isolate entity tokens at decode time is fundamentally flawed, as taggers require complete sentences to resolve ambiguity, and executing them on partial prefixes for $M$ candidates per step incurs catastrophic latency.
3. **Candidate Bound & Memory Bandwidth:** Post-hoc intervention can only reweight candidates the base model keeps alive in its top-$M$ predictions. Dynamically retrieving local spatial support for $M$ candidates introduces a severe memory-bandwidth (TPOT) bottleneck.

To address these, TLRA is built upon three specific design commitments: **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** (governed by a locked spatial ratio $\rho$), and an **Offline VASM** protocol, which mathematically protects linguistic structure via a pre-computed static WordNet dictionary and dynamic BPE inheritance, bypassing decode-time NLP latency entirely.

To enforce scientific rigor, TLRA establishes strict boundary conditions. First, we explicitly classify `TLRA_calib` as a Test-Time Adaptation (TTA) module, acknowledging that its 50k calibration captions introduce external priors; `TLRA_zero` remains the sole pure zero-shot baseline. Second, the framework actively purges any claims of broad multimodal superiority: because VASM algorithmically isolates physical entities and mathematically bypasses non-physical nouns, OCR tasks (`DocVQA`) are designated strictly as *flat-line negative controls*. Third, all hyperparameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M, \rho$) are permanently locked based on a single validation split to prevent dataset-specific overfitting.

Ultimately, the survival of the TLRA spatial hypothesis rests on passing a strict **parity ablation**. The core scientific conclusion cannot be drawn from comparisons with zero-shot sequence heuristics (like DoLa/VCD); instead, any claim regarding "local evidence" must statistically outperform a calibrated global pool (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and a random spatial subset (`TLRA_RandomK`) using the *exact same* calibrator weights, under an explicit audit separating physical objects seen vs. unseen during calibration. If spatial routing fails this unseen test, the paper executes a pre-registered **Fallback Clause**, mathematically devolving the core claim to a practical `MeanPool + VASM` baseline.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary and Methodological Identity: `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib`

Acknowledging that visual and lexical states often do not natively align, we explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks to prevent "calibrator blackbox" ambiguity and ensure fair baseline comparisons:

1. **`TLRA_zero` (Pure Zero-Shot Probe)**: The base model's native language modeling head is applied directly to contextualized final-layer visual states to derive lexical support. This is the only variant directly comparable to methods like DoLa or VCD in terms of strict zero-shot parameter isolation, serving primarily as a viability probe.
2. **`TLRA_calib` (Lightweight TTA / PEFT)**: A calibrated variant utilizing a 2-layer MLP projection, $\Phi_{calib}$, to bridge embedding asymmetry. We explicitly acknowledge that training $\Phi_{calib}$ on 50k conceptual captions injects external priors. Consequently, `TLRA_calib` is structurally a Test-Time Adaptation module. 

Crucially, the primary antagonist for `TLRA_calib` is not VCD or DoLa, but `TLRA_MeanPool`. Any routing superiority claimed by `TLRA_calib` must be validated against `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` using the exact same frozen $\Phi_{calib}$ weights, paired with a strict "Category Leakage Audit" separating objects seen vs. unseen in this exact 50k subset.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set derived from the original logits: $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ is a strictly locked candidate window (e.g., $M=50$). We explicitly acknowledge that extracting patch-level support for $M$ candidates per step introduces a severe memory-bandwidth bottleneck, which we audit via TPOT in Section 4.5.

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the contextualized final-layer visual states, with visual token volume $N_v = H \times W$. For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support via an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule, determined by a globally locked spatial ratio $\rho$:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big),
$$
followed by the raw support computation:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a logits intervention without destroying the base model's native softmax dynamics, TLRA computes a relative evidence score over the bounded candidate set:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
To prevent out-of-bounds suppression, TLRA applies a dynamic scaling penalty anchored strictly to the candidate window's logit range ($\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t) - \min(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t)$):
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \Delta_L \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Offline VASM

Naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses critical syntax. A critical flaw in existing decode-time control is the **autoregressive paradox**: running NLP taggers (e.g., POS parsing) on incomplete generation prefixes is highly inaccurate, and executing them dynamically for $M$ candidates per step incurs catastrophic latency. 

TLRA resolves this by introducing **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, an *offline, pre-computed* static structure combined with dynamic $O(1)$ state-machine updates. VASM guarantees $\gamma(c) = 1$ for visually groundable physical entities, and $\gamma(c) = 0$ for all other tokens, operating in two stages:

1. **Offline Static Dictionary Construction:** Prior to inference, the entire MLLM tokenizer vocabulary ($|V| \approx 32k - 128k$) is cross-referenced against WordNet synsets. Tokens unambiguously mapped to physical objects/nouns are assigned $\gamma = 1$ in a static binary lookup table. Function words, verbs, and abstract syntax are assigned $\gamma = 0$. This reduces decode-time root identification to an $O(1)$ memory lookup.
2. **Dynamic BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Because modern tokenizers fragment words arbitrarily, decode-time intervention must handle subwords. If step $t$ generates a continuation token (e.g., indicated by `##` or lack of a space prefix `Ġ`), it deterministically inherits the $\gamma$ value of the active root token at step $t-1$. This requires only a lightweight state variable to track the current active root, adding negligible latency. *Limitation Concession:* Hyphenated words or severe BPE fragmentation that break exact WordNet root matches safely default to $\gamma=0$, ensuring the base model is not corrupted, even if intervention is missed.

**The OCR Concession and Lexical Rigidity:** VASM introduces deliberate limitations. The static dictionary enforces lexical rigidity; borderline ambiguous words rely strictly on their primary WordNet definition. Furthermore, VASM explicitly ignores abstract text-in-image strings. We strictly concede that TLRA is mathematically blind to OCR; tasks like `DocVQA` are utilized purely as flat-line negative controls to prove VASM does not erroneously destroy text reading capabilities.

### 3.5 Methodological Boundaries and The Fallback Clause

TLRA's claims are governed by explicit methodological boundaries:
- **The Fallback Clause:** If `AdaptiveTopK` fails to statistically outperform `MeanPool` on *unseen* objects (Section 4.4), the spatial routing claim is voided as a data-leakage artifact, and the method formally devolves to a `MeanPool + VASM` baseline.
- **Out-of-Candidate Limit:** Post-hoc reweighting cannot recover a correct entity token if the base model has already ejected it from the initial Top-$M$ set.
- **Modality Limits:** TLRA is optimized strictly for spatial ($H \times W$) reasoning. Spatio-Temporal tokens (video) are mechanically unsupported by the core formulation and relegated to an exploratory appendix pilot.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because this paper is structured as a rigorous falsifiable proposal, the experimental methodology is organized into three mandatory evidence chains, a strict systemic latency audit, and explicit boundary testing. **Crucially, all hyperparameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M, \rho$) are locked uniformly across all tasks based on a single held-out validation set.** 

### 4.1 Stage 0: Zero-Shot Semantic Validity Pilot

**Goal:** Determine whether `TLRA_zero` is a viable independent branch or mere noise.
- **Protocol:** Measure Top-1/5/10 overlap between patch readout words and annotated object labels on a 1k held-out image subset.
- **Decision Rule:** If overlap is near-random, the zero-shot claim is strictly downgraded to a viability probe, and `TLRA_calib` is formally evaluated as the operational adaptation method, benchmarked primarily against `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Truncation Audit

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, and strictly verify that this reduction is not an artifact of output truncation (i.e., artificially inflating precision by remaining silent).
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 1):** 
  - *Metrics:* POPE (Accuracy, F1), CHAIR (CHAIRi, CHAIRs), and a hard column for **Average Generation Length (AGL)**.
  - *Rows:* Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `TLRA_zero` (if viable), `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Validation Criteria:** If `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` reduces AGL by >10% compared to the Base model (e.g., dropping from 15 tokens to 3 tokens), the hallucination reduction claim must be downgraded, proving it functions as a truncation engine rather than a grounding engine. 

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure Preservation and Negative Controls

**Goal:** Verify that VASM mathematically protects general multimodal understanding and that the OCR concession operates correctly as a negative control.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 2):**
  - *Metrics:* MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard), and **DocVQA**.
  - *Rows:* Base, `TLRA_full` (with offline VASM), `TLRA_no_VASM`.
- **Validation Criteria:** 
  1. `TLRA_no_VASM` must demonstrate a catastrophic performance drop on reasoning benchmarks (MMMU), proving VASM's absolute necessity.
  2. `DocVQA` performance must remain strictly *flat* between Base and `TLRA_full`. If `TLRA_full` drops significantly (e.g., Base 65.2 $\to$ TLRA 40.0), VASM is erroneously destroying text tokens. Any claim of "improving OCR" is explicitly prohibited.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence Value and Category Leakage Parity

**Goal:** Isolate and prove that spatial locality directly drives performance, rather than global calibration priors or randomized visual subset regularization.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 3):**
  - *Metrics:* FREAK (Spatial Relations, Object Existence, Counting). The dataset is strictly partitioned into **"Objects Seen in $\Phi_{calib}$"** vs. **"Objects Unseen in $\Phi_{calib}$"**.
  - *Rows:* `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK` (Mandatory Ablation), `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
  - *Supplementary:* A dedicated script detailing the exact WordNet synset cross-referencing used to guarantee absolute zero category overlap between the 50k calibration captions and the Unseen test split.
- **Validation Criteria:** All three TLRA variants must use the *exact same* frozen $\Phi_{calib}$ weights. The core scientific conclusion derives solely from this table. `AdaptiveTopK` must statistically outperform both `MeanPool` and `RandomK` on the *Unseen* split. If it only wins on the "Seen" split, the **Fallback Clause** is enacted: the spatial routing hypothesis is invalidated as a data-leakage artifact, and the paper's contribution is reframed entirely as `MeanPool + VASM`.

### 4.5 Efficiency Pareto and Failure Mode Audits

To rigorously audit the systemic memory-bandwidth bottlenecks introduced by spatial routing, we mandate a **Systemic Cost Pareto (Figure 1)**. 
- **Protocol:** A scatter plot mapping Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT in milliseconds on a standardized A100 GPU, X-axis) against POPE F1 (Y-axis). 
- **Rows mapped:** Base, VCD, DoLa, `TLRA_M=10`, `TLRA_M=50`, `TLRA_M=100`. This visually quantifies the latency penalty of extracting support for $M$ candidates dynamically.

Furthermore, we document one explicit mathematical failure mode:
- **Out-of-Candidate Bound (Logit Rank Track):** A sequence probability graph plotting the raw logit rank of a correct physical entity token leading up to a hallucination. This must mathematically prove the limitation by showing exactly when the correct token drops below rank $M=50$ at step $t-1$, rendering it unrecoverable by any post-hoc intervention.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest iteration of TLRA is the one bound by the cleanest scientific contract. By architecting VASM as an offline pre-computed static dictionary to resolve the autoregressive latency paradox, strictly defining `TLRA_calib` as a lightweight Test-Time Adaptation module, enforcing exact-calibrator parity against MeanPool baselines, and treating OCR tasks exclusively as flat-line negative controls, this protocol prioritizes methodological transparency over unconditional SOTA claims. 

The integrity of this method is bounded by several explicit limitations:
1. **Calibrator Category Leakage & Identity:** Because $\Phi_{calib}$ uses 50k captions, TLRA ceases to be a pure zero-shot heuristic. The mandatory Seen vs. Unseen audit is the sole defense ensuring gains stem from spatial routing rather than PEFT memorization.
2. **Out-of-Candidate Bounds:** Decode-time reweighting mathematically cannot recover a true entity token that the base model has already discarded from its Top-$M$ candidate pool.
3. **Systemic Latency (TPOT Tax):** The adaptive Top-$k$ mechanism requires retrieving local support for $M$ candidates dynamically per token. Despite VASM bypassing NLP pipeline latency, the memory-bandwidth bottleneck of spatial retrieval remains significant, translating to an unavoidable TPOT overhead.
4. **Lexical Rigidity:** The offline static nature of VASM guarantees speed but introduces lexical rigidity, relying heavily on primary WordNet exact-matches and safely (but conservatively) defaulting to $\gamma=0$ on extreme BPE fragmentations or hyphenations.
5. **Modality Transferability:** The core mechanism is explicitly spatial. Its application to dense document OCR is mathematically bypassed by design, and its extension to spatio-temporal video settings is relegated strictly to an appendix exploration.

## 6. Revised Claims and Contract Execution

Pending empirical execution of the locked protocol, this paper restricts its claims strictly to:
- A mathematically sound, latency-optimized structure-preserving masking mechanism (Offline VASM + BPE inheritance).
- A controlled evaluation isolating token-local routing gains from calibrator priors, protected by a strict Fallback Clause to `MeanPool + VASM`.
- The establishment of explicit falsifiable boundary conditions regarding GPU latency (TPOT), out-of-candidate failures, and text-in-image negative controls.

We explicitly do not claim architecture-invariant zero-shot success, dense document-grounding competence, or unified image-video superiority.
