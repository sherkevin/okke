## Verdict
This paper presents a refreshingly disciplined, falsifiable protocol for decode-time MLLM intervention. The explicit structural commitments—specifically the Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) and the pre-registered parity ablations—demonstrate a high degree of methodological maturity. However, the current draft still flirts with narrative contradictions regarding its scope (e.g., OCR limits) and faces a steep empirical cliff. My evaluation focuses strictly on the validity of your proposed experimental matrix. If the authors execute this contract exactly as proposed and tighten the boundaries around their claims, the resulting paper will be a rigorous, highly valuable contribution, regardless of whether it achieves unconditional State-of-the-Art status. Acceptance is strictly contingent upon the uncompromising execution of the proposed ablations.

## Summary
The paper proposes Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA), a decode-time intervention framework designed to mitigate MLLM hallucinations by reweighting candidate logits based on localized visual evidence. To prevent structural collapse during intervention, the authors introduce VASM, an automated masking strategy relying on WordNet root lookup and BPE continuation inheritance. Recognizing that the experimental results are pending, the paper is structured around a strict evaluation contract: separating zero-shot viability (`TLRA_zero`) from calibrated projection (`TLRA_calib`), enforcing a locked-hyperparameter regime, and proposing three distinct evidence chains (Hallucination Reduction, Structure Preservation, and Local Evidence Value) audited by specific negative controls and pareto efficiency curves.

## WhatShouldBeKept
1. **The `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib` Split:** This is a mathematically honest defense against the "calibrator blackbox" critique. Using `TLRA_zero` strictly as a viability probe sets the right scientific baseline.
2. **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM):** This is currently your strongest positive asset. The algorithmic derivation via WordNet and deterministic BPE inheritance is a significant step up from manual heuristic lists. 
3. **The Evidence Chain Architecture:** The design of Chain A (auditing Average Generation Length to catch truncation artifacts), Chain B (using DocVQA mathematically as a negative control), and Chain C (the Seen vs. Unseen leakage audit) is exceptionally well-constructed. 
4. **The "Exact Same Calibrator" Parity Ablation:** Forcing `AdaptiveTopK` to compete against `MeanPool` and `RandomK` using the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$ weights is the only valid way to prove the spatial routing hypothesis. Do not compromise on this.

## MajorWeaknesses
1. **Contradictory OCR Narrative:** You explicitly state that VASM mathematically bypasses text-in-image tokens and designate DocVQA as a negative control (Section 3.4/4.3). Yet, you still use phrases like "fine-grained physical grounding" that often bleed into dense document contexts. You cannot have it both ways. If the method explicitly ignores OCR, you must aggressively purge any motivational framing that implies your method is suitable for dense document understanding. 
2. **Misplaced Multimedia Burden (Video):** You propose a "bounded secondary video pilot" in the appendix. TLRA is heavily optimized for spatial routing ($H \times W$ patches, spatial ratio $\rho$). Attempting to force Spatio-Temporal tokens into this framework without a dedicated temporal resonance theory is mechanically unsound. The multimedia track at ACM MM does not mechanically require video. Retain it strictly as an appendix pilot or drop it entirely; do not let it dilute the core image-centric spatial claims.
3. **Baseline Framing Risks:** While Section 1 correctly identifies DoLa, VCD, and OPERA as legitimate baselines, be extremely careful not to frame their mechanisms (e.g., reliance on global pooling or layer contrast) as "failures" that define your problem. They are simply alternative, competitive heuristics. Your core legitimate proposition is: *Can token-local evidence injection + VASM + a fair calibrated split outperform global decoding heuristics without destroying language structure?* Keep the focus on proving this positive proposition.
4. **Hyperparameter Completeness:** You state that ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M$) are permanently locked after validation. However, the spatial ratio $\rho$ defining the Adaptive Top-$k$ window is missing from this locked list. $\rho$ is a critical systemic parameter and must be subjected to the same strict single-configuration lock.

## SectionBySectionComments
- **Abstract & Introduction:** The tone is appropriately restrained. Ensure you stick to the "narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis." If `AdaptiveTopK` ultimately fails to convincingly beat `MeanPool` in Chain C, be prepared to immediately shrink your core claim to "MeanPool + VASM" without attempting to invent a new overarching narrative.
- **Section 3.1 & 3.2:** The definition of the bounded candidate window ($M$) is sound. However, acknowledge the severe memory-bandwidth bottleneck this introduces at decode time. Retrieving local support for $M=50$ candidates dynamically per token is a massive computational overhead compared to static global pooling.
- **Section 3.4:** The BPE Continuation Inheritance is clever but linguistically fragile. What occurs when a physical entity word is hyphenated or fragmented such that the initial BPE token is not recognized as a valid WordNet root? Explicitly document this as a known boundary condition.
- **Section 4.4 (Chain C):** This is the crux of the paper. If `AdaptiveTopK` only outperforms `MeanPool` on the "Seen" objects, your spatial routing claim is dead, and the gains are entirely a data-leakage artifact from $\Phi_{calib}$. The paper's survival hinges on winning the "Unseen" split.

## RequiredRevisions
1. **Shrink the Scope:** Explicitly remove any lingering implications that TLRA is designed for or beneficial to OCR-heavy document tasks. Re-write the introduction to strictly bound the method to visual physical entities.
2. **Lock $\rho$:** Add the spatial ratio $\rho$ to the list of explicitly locked hyperparameters across all benchmarks.
3. **Downgrade Video:** Explicitly state in the introduction that video is entirely out-of-scope for the primary spatial formulation, justifying its placement as a purely exploratory appendix item. Do not attempt to weave it into the main narrative.
4. **Prepare the Fallback:** Explicitly state in your methodology that if `AdaptiveTopK` is not statistically superior to `MeanPool` in Chain C, the method devolves to a `MeanPool+VASM` baseline, and you will report it as such.

## SuggestedFiguresTablesExperiments
To successfully execute your proposed protocol, ensure the following constraints are met in your upcoming data collection:
1. **Table 1 (Chain A):** Average Generation Length (AGL) must be reported as a hard numerical column right next to POPE/CHAIR F1. If AGL drops by more than 10%, you must include a discussion on whether the model is artificially avoiding hallucination via truncation.
2. **Table 3 (Chain C):** For the FREAK dataset, provide a supplementary table detailing exactly how the 50k calibration conceptual captions were filtered to guarantee zero overlap with the "Unseen" test split. Data leakage here invalidates the entire paper.
3. **Figure 1 (Systemic Cost Pareto):** The X-axis *must* be actual measured Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) in milliseconds on a standardized GPU (e.g., A100), not theoretical FLOPs. The dynamic tensor slicing required for Top-$k$ extraction per candidate token will incur a heavy latency penalty that FLOPs will hide.
4. **Failure Analysis:** For the "Out-of-Candidate Bound" visualization, plot a line graph showing the raw logit rank of the correct entity token over the sequence steps leading up to the hallucination. Prove mathematically exactly when the token fell below rank $M=50$ and became unrecoverable.
