## Verdict
This is a highly mature, scientifically rigorous draft that successfully bounds its claims and presents a positive, falsifiable proposition. The methodology for Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA) is sound, and the introduction of Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) with tokenizer-aware BPE checks addresses a critical flaw in prior decode-time interventions. Most importantly, the experimental protocol is flawlessly designed to isolate the exact value of token-local extraction while explicitly controlling for confounding variables (AGL collapse, external data leakage, structural degradation). Assuming the authors execute the proposed experimental plan without deviating from this strict contract or massaging the results, this paper presents a compelling, high-quality contribution to ACM Multimedia.

## Summary
The paper proposes TLRA, a decode-time intervention to reduce hallucinations in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs). It reweights candidate tokens using token-local visual evidence extracted via a lightweight, frozen-base-model calibrator (`TLRA_calib`). To maintain efficiency, it employs Prompt-Conditioned Pruning with an entropy-driven fallback. To prevent structural and morphological collapse during generation, it introduces VASM, a deterministic WordNet-based whitelist with BPE subword boundary awareness. The paper explicitly outlines a three-chain evaluation protocol focusing on hallucination reduction (controlling for length), structure preservation (using OCR tasks as a negative control), and isolating local evidence against global/random pooling.

## WhatShouldBeKept
1. **The Positive Framing and Baseline Taxonomy:** Keep the exact current framing. You correctly treat `DoLa`, `VCD`, and `OPERA` as highly competitive baselines without attacking them with false strawman arguments (e.g., you successfully avoided claiming they "rely on global pooling"). 
2. **The `TLRA_MeanPool` Internal Control:** This is the strongest scientific element of the paper. By comparing your adaptive Top-$k$ selection against a mean-pooling baseline that uses the *exact same* external calibrator weights ($\Phi_{calib}$), you perfectly isolate the decode-time architectural contribution from the external data prior. Do not remove or dilute this ablation.
3. **The "OCR Concession" and DocVQA Negative Control:** Openly admitting that VASM bypasses arbitrary OCR strings and using `DocVQA` to prove an exact ~0% trigger rate (zero improvement, zero degradation) is brilliant and honest. Keep this. Do not attempt to retrospectively claim OCR capabilities.
4. **Tokenizer-Aware Subword Boundary Check:** Transitioning from a blunt $L_{char} \ge 4$ filter to a BPE-aware boundary check safely preserves short valid words ("cat") while avoiding false-prefix collisions ("bulletin"). This is a robust engineering choice.
5. **The AGL Variance Check:** Using Average Generated Length (AGL) variance to police "hallucination reduction via length collapse" is a gold standard. Keep it mandatory.

## MajorWeaknesses
1. **The Prefill Pruning Blindspot:** Your methodology computes the attention distribution from the *text prompt tokens* during the prefill stage to prune visual patches. This is architecturally perilous. Visual grounding often relies on attention generated by *previously decoded output tokens*, not just the static prefill prompt. If an object is not mentioned in the prompt but becomes relevant during a long-form open-ended generation, the prefill attention will have already pruned its visual patch. 
2. **Entropy Fallback Hyper-sensitivity:** Because of the aforementioned blindspot, your entropy fallback ($H_{attn} > \theta_{fallback}$) is doing extreme heavy lifting. I am highly skeptical that this fallback won't just trigger near 100% of the time on any complex multimodal reasoning task, effectively rendering your "high-resolution efficiency" claim null. 
3. **InfoNCE Calibrator Bias:** Training $\Phi_{calib}$ specifically on generic spaCy noun phrases aligned to bounding boxes risks heavily biasing the calibrator toward physical object nouns and ignoring localized spatial prepositions or attributes. If $\Phi_{calib}$ cannot score localized spatial relations, `TLRA` will fail on the fine-grained spatial relation split of `FREAK`.

## SectionBySectionComments
- **Abstract & Introduction:** Excellent bounding of claims. You propose "token-local logits intervention + VASM + fair zero-shot/calibrated split" and nothing more. Resist any future temptation to invent a grander narrative.
- **Section 3.1 (Calibration Protocol):** The transparency regarding the embedding asymmetry and the introduction of generic external data is commendable. However, explicitly define how $\Phi_{calib}$ handles plural nouns or attribute-noun pairs (e.g., "red car" vs "car"). 
- **Section 3.2 (Pruning):** The mechanism is well-defined mathematically, but as noted in the weaknesses, you must explicitly state how you handle cross-attention from *decoded* tokens. If you strictly use prefill, you must empirically prove (via Figure 1) that it does not destroy long-horizon reasoning.
- **Section 3.4 (VASM):** The logic here is highly airtight. Polysemy delegation to base logits is a very smart concession. 
- **Section 4 & 5 (Evaluation & Limitations):** The inclusion of an explicit failure analysis and limitation section outlining English-centric brittleness and BPE stuttering demonstrates strong scientific maturity.

## RequiredRevisions
1. **Address the Prefill Pruning Flaw:** You must either (A) update the methodology to dynamically re-compute or update active visual patches based on a rolling window of decoded tokens, or (B) heavily restrict the pruning claim, acknowledging that for long generations, the entropy fallback defaults the system to dense Top-$k$ lookup.
2. **Video Pilot Strict Isolation:** Currently, your benchmark scope is entirely image-centric. If you plan to add a secondary video pilot as the experiments progress, it must *not* infect the main narrative. Do not mechanically force a "temporal local evidence" claim into the main text unless it is as rigorous as the spatial one. Keep any video experiments constrained to a secondary pilot or appendix.
3. **Calibrator Specificity:** Clarify the exact parameter count and inference overhead of $\Phi_{calib}$. It must remain strictly lightweight (e.g., a single linear layer) to justify the "plug-in" claim.
