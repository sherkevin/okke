# Breaking the Language Inertia: Adaptive Resonance Anchoring for High-Resolution MLLM Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate high-resolution visual tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D-RoPE spatial coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., Document VQA). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space via localized visual matching. 

To resolve critical mathematical flaws in prior logits-based interventions, BRA incorporates three structural upgrades: (1) **Patch-to-Vocab Modality Calibration**, utilizing a lightweight contrastive projector ($\Phi$) to bridge the topological gap between visual patches and the text unembedding matrix; (2) **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, which scales logarithmically with dynamic visual resolutions to isolate fine-grained spatial features without the statistical vulnerability of pure max-pooling; and (3) **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, replacing brittle predictive entropy with an offline, frequency-weighted syntactic mask that robustly handles polysemy and continuous BPE fragments. We establish a rigorous 6-stage experimental protocol—including dense OCR ablation, False-Positive evaluations, and Pareto-frontier analysis—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy and cross-domain validity of BRA across continuous and dynamic-resolution MLLM architectures.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model hallucinates—producing text that is linguistically fluent but physically absent from the visual input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on contrastive decoding or backtracking (e.g., VCD, OPERA). While effective, they introduce significant latency overheads (Time-Per-Output-Token spikes) and VRAM bloat. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution MLLMs, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool or PCA) thousands of high-resolution visual tokens. This operation irreversibly destroys the fine-grained spatial coordinates (like 2D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects and dense text, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or artificial GUI navigation.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a localized match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial information. However, implementing this principle exposes several mathematical vulnerabilities: the topological gap between visual tokens and the text vocabulary, the extreme noise sensitivity of pure max-pooling over thousands of tokens, and the "Entropy Trap" where contextually predictable hallucinations evade penalization.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol designed to resolve these vulnerabilities. Acknowledging that true zero-shot modality alignment is empirically unverified, our methodology introduces a highly calibrated approach:
1. **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**: Transitioning from a statistically dangerous absolute max-pooling to a resolution-aware Top-$k$ mean formulation, smoothing out high-frequency visual noise while explicitly scaling to dynamic-resolution models like InternVL.
2. **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**: Replacing flawed predictive entropy with an offline, polysemy-aware masking mechanism based on corpus-level Part-of-Speech (POS) distributions, guaranteeing the structural protection of functional syntax and unidentifiable BPE fragments.
3. **Rigorous 6-stage Evaluation Protocol**: Expanding the experimental blueprint to explicitly test patch-to-vocab modality alignment (including a "No $\Phi$" baseline), quantitative syntax preservation, dense spatial preservation (DocVQA/ChartQA), false-positive noise rejection, and the true Time/VRAM Pareto frontier.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Patch-to-Vocab Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)

The core hypothesis of BRA is that intervention should occur in the logits space to avoid pooling high-resolution spatial tokens. Given a candidate token set $\mathcal{C}_t = \{c_1, \dots, c_K\}$ at decoding step $t$, we measure the visual support for each candidate. 

A critical vulnerability in naive logits-space intervention is the assumption of topological orthogonality—whether the last-layer visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ reside in the exact same semantic manifold as the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$. To bridge this, we formalize the raw resonance score $S_{raw}(c)$ through an alignment mapping $\Phi$. Unlike highly parameterized adapters, $\Phi$ is a lightweight linear projector calibrated strictly to align patch-level visual features with vocabulary-level text embeddings. 

To achieve this, $\Phi$ is trained via a localized InfoNCE contrastive loss using visual grounding datasets (e.g., RefCOCO, where specific bounding boxes $v_b$ map to distinct noun tokens $c^*$):
$$
\mathcal{L}_{\Phi} = - \log \frac{\exp(\cos(\Phi(h_L^{(v_b)}), W_{vocab}[c^*]) / \tau_{cal})}{\sum_{c' \in \mathcal{N}} \exp(\cos(\Phi(h_L^{(v_b)}), W_{vocab}[c']) / \tau_{cal})}
$$
This calibration establishes a universal semantic mapping robust to artificial domains, ensuring $\Phi$ functions as a plug-and-play projector during inference without requiring full LLM fine-tuning.

### 3.2 Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance for Dynamic Resolutions

Unlike hidden-state methods (e.g., DoLa) that compute a globally pooled visual vector $\bar{v} = \frac{1}{N_v} \sum h_L^{(v_j)}$ to establish an intervention direction, BRA explicitly isolates fine-grained features. However, operating over high-resolution images produces thousands of tokens; relying on a pure $\max_{j}$ operator creates extreme statistical vulnerability, where a hallucinated text token is highly likely to find spurious high cosine similarity with at least one background noise patch.

To resolve this, we formulate **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**. Crucially, modern MLLMs (e.g., InternVL) utilize dynamic cropping, meaning the number of visual tokens $N_v$ fluctuates drastically (e.g., from 256 to 4096). A static $k$ would behave inconsistently. We define an adaptive $k$ that scales proportionally to the visual sequence length:
$$
k = \max(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil)
$$
where $\rho$ is a fractional threshold (e.g., $\rho = 0.01$) and $k_{min} \ge 2$. We then compute the temperature-scaled mean resonance of these localized patches:

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{\cos\left(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)})\right)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$

This formulation strictly avoids the Pooling Paradox by isolating the specific region of interest (e.g., a tiny bounding box containing text) while successfully smoothing out spurious, single-patch visual noise.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty 

To map the raw resonance scores into stable logits modifications without introducing over-parameterized hyper-sensitivity, we compute the relative evidence position within the Top-K candidates:

$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$

The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:

$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

where $\alpha$ is a global penalty strength, and $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)]$ is the probabilistically-weighted semantic mask. Tokens with high relative visual support ($\hat S(c) \approx 1$) receive near-zero penalty, while ungrounded tokens are linearly suppressed.

### 3.4 Escaping the Entropy Trap: Probabilistic VASM

Applying visual resonance blindly at the terminal token level causes semantic collapse for multi-token entities (e.g., splitting "play" and "ing") and destroys functional syntax. Previous iterations attempted to use predictive entropy $H_t$ to scale the penalty. This creates an **"Entropy Trap"**: strong language priors inherently produce low-entropy predictions for predictable *hallucinated* objects, effectively turning off the visual penalty for the most stubborn hallucinations.

We abandon runtime statistical heuristics in favor of mathematical guarantees via **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**. Recognizing that static binary $\{0, 1\}$ assignments fail for polysemous words (e.g., "watch" as a noun vs. verb), we pre-compute a continuous syntactic weight $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \in [0, 1]$ based on the probability distribution of POS tags in a large corpus (e.g., the C4 dataset):

$$
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = \sum_{pos} P(pos \mid c) \cdot \gamma_{pos}
$$

1. **Semantic Entities ($\gamma_{pos} = 1.0$):** Nouns, Verbs, and Adjectives. 
2. **Functional Syntax ($\gamma_{pos} = 0.0$):** Prepositions, conjunctions, punctuation.
3. **BPE Fallback Heuristic:** Sub-words that cannot be reliably POS-tagged in isolation (e.g., dependent tokens lacking a leading space like `##ing`) are explicitly forced to $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = 0.0$.

This mathematically guarantees structural preservation of the tokenizer's syntax with $O(1)$ runtime overhead, while remaining robust to contextual linguistic ambiguity.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

This section outlines the rigorous, hypothesis-driven evaluation blueprint required to empirically validate BRA. To stress-test spatial preservation, baselines will explicitly include both a continuous-resolution MLLM (e.g., LLaVA-1.5) and a dynamic-resolution MLLM (e.g., InternVL).

### 4.1 Protocol 0: Patch-to-Vocab Calibration Probe
**Hypothesis:** A linear $\Phi$ calibrated on grounding data can bridge the modality gap without memorizing dataset-specific visual priors, and is strictly superior to assuming zero-shot identity between the visual and text manifolds.
**Experiment:** 
- Train $\Phi$ using the InfoNCE loss (Eq 1) on RefCOCO/MSCOCO bounding boxes.
- **Evaluation:** Apply the *frozen* $\Phi$ directly to unseen datasets (e.g., ChartQA). Compute the **Recall@K** of ground-truth visual-text token pairs.
- **Mandatory Baseline:** Compare against the **"No $\Phi$" (Zero-Shot Identity) baseline**, where visual tokens are mapped directly to $W_{vocab}$ via identity or default linear projection.
- **Success Criteria:** $\Phi$ must achieve a Recall@5 significantly higher than the "No $\Phi$" baseline on unseen domains to justify its inclusion.

### 4.2 Protocol 1: The "Pooling Paradox" Proof via Dense Spatial Tasks
**Hypothesis:** Standard hidden-state interventions destroy 2D spatial features. BRA’s adaptive Top-$k$ match preserves them.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: **DocVQA, ChartQA**, and VisualWebBench.
- **Mandatory Ablation Table:** Compare (1) Base Model, (2) DoLa, (3) `BRA-MeanPool` (where $S_{raw}$ uses globally pooled visual tokens), (4) `BRA-PureMax` ($k=1$), and (5) `BRA-TopK` (Adaptive $k$).
- **Success Criteria:** While DoLa and `BRA-MeanPool` may perform adequately on natural images, they must show severe degradation on dense spatial tasks. `BRA-TopK` must dramatically outperform Rows 2, 3, and 4 on DocVQA, definitively proving the Pooling Paradox. We will support this with **Figure 1**, a qualitative heatmap overlaid on high-resolution images showing exactly which patches the Top-$k$ mechanism selected when generating tiny visual text.

### 4.3 Protocol 2: General Hallucination Suppression
**Hypothesis:** BRA suppresses object hallucination in standard natural images without length collapse.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: POPE, CHAIR, MME.
- Baselines: Base decoding, VCD, OPERA, DoLa.

### 4.4 Protocol 3: Quantitative Syntax Preservation (VASM Validation)
**Hypothesis:** Probabilistic VASM mathematically prevents semantic collapse by protecting syntax and BPE fragments, significantly outperforming heuristic predictive entropy.
**Experiment:**
- **Execution:** Generate responses over 1,000 queries. Parse outputs using SpaCy.
- **Metric:** Calculate the "Syntax Preservation Rate"—measuring the probability mass perturbation ($\Delta P$) applied by BRA specifically to Verbs/Nouns vs. Conjunctions/Prepositions/Sub-words.
- **Success Criteria:** The perturbation drop for functional syntax and BPE fallbacks must mathematically approximate zero. We will document instances where contextual polysemy is successfully resolved by the $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)]$ formulation.

### 4.5 Protocol 4: False Positive Vulnerability Study (Cluttered Backgrounds)
**Hypothesis:** Adaptive Top-$k$ prevents high-frequency visual noise from triggering false-positive visual resonance, a known vulnerability of pure max-pooling.
**Experiment:**
- Construct a synthetic "Cluttered Background" image benchmark prompting the model for *absent* objects.
- **Evaluation:** Compare hallucination rates using `BRA-PureMax` vs. `BRA-TopK`. 
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA-PureMax` will theoretically show higher hallucination rates by incorrectly finding resonance in visual noise. `BRA-TopK` must significantly lower this false-positive bypass rate.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to localized logits-space resonance anchoring addresses a structural bottleneck in high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly exposing and resolving the "Pooling Paradox", BRA provides a pathway to intervene safely without destroying the fragile 2D spatial encodings established by Vision-RoPE. Furthermore, replacing flawed statistical heuristics (the "Entropy Trap") with Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) mathematically grounds the structural preservation of language generation while robustly handling polysemy.

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Modality Alignment Dependency:** Although Protocol 0 aims to prove robust cross-domain generalization, the necessity of the $\Phi$ projector means BRA is not strictly zero-parameter out of the box. It requires a one-time localized calibration phase over bounding-box grounding data.
2. **Extreme Polysemy Edge Cases:** While Probabilistic VASM handles corpus-level polysemy via expected values ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)]$), highly contextual deviations (e.g., a statistically rare usage of a word) could still result in suboptimal penalization.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until Protocols 0-5 are fully executed, we strictly limit our claims to the theoretical soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial bottlenecks of high-resolution MLLM interventions, offering a concrete mathematical blueprint to validate the supremacy of adaptive Top-$k$, logits-space decoding.
