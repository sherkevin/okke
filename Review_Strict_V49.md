## Verdict
The methodological framework and evaluation protocol are mathematically rigorous, structurally sound, and theoretically falsifiable. By strictly scoping the proposition to 2D Image-LLMs and framing existing global regularizers (DoLa, VCD, OPERA) as orthogonal baselines rather than direct mechanistic competitors, the paper establishes a highly defensible narrative. However, as the empirical execution is currently pending, the paper represents a high-quality *pre-registration* rather than a proven scientific contribution. The score strictly reflects the quality of the proposed experimental design, which is exceptional, but ultimate acceptance at ACM Multimedia hinges entirely on whether the empirical data meets the uncompromising success boundaries defined herein.

## Summary
The paper proposes a decode-time "Token-Local Visual Intervention" framework to mitigate object hallucination in MLLMs. It explicitly argues that diffuse global pooling (or overarching language heuristics) washes out the localized spatial evidence required for dense grounding. To resolve this, the authors introduce a hybrid approach: a zero-shot diagnostic (`Local_Zero`) that, upon failure, pivots to a lightweight, isolated 5k-sample prior (`Local_Calib`) trained with hard-negative spatial overlap sampling. The intervention dynamically routes visual support to logits using an Entropy-Scaled Adaptive Top-$k$ mechanism (derived during prefill) and protects language structure via a Dual-Tier Dictionary-Gated Semantic Mask (WordNet + Regex). The paper outlines a strict, pre-registered evaluation protocol across three evidence chains (Hallucination Reduction, Structure Preservation, Local Evidence Value) to empirically validate the framework.

## WhatShouldBeKept
1. **The Strict 2D Scoping:** Deliberately discarding the spatiotemporal video domain to secure defensible claims regarding 2D spatial locality is an excellent, scientifically mature decision. Do not add video back in; the current boundary is tight.
2. **The Framing of DoLa/VCD/OPERA:** Treating these purely as "orthogonal zero-shot regularizers managing global heuristics" is correct. Retain this framing. Do not slip back into claiming your method "beats" them on their own turf; your method solves a *different* mechanistic problem (spatial routing).
3. **The `Base + 5k LoRA` Falsifiability Boundary:** This is the strongest experimental design choice in the paper. Isolating the parameter exposure from the decode-time routing ensures that any claimed $\Delta$ is genuinely derived from the intervention, not just data contamination.
4. **The Intervention Trigger Rate vs. OOV Concession:** Openly acknowledging that "preservation" is often conflated with "inaction" (due to OOV bypasses) shows high AC-level awareness. Figure 1's planned scatterplot is mandatory.

## MajorWeaknesses
1. **The `Local_Zero` Strawman:** Deep self-attention almost universally diffuses spatial coordinates by the final layer in standard LLM decoders. If you already know that `Local_Zero` fails for LLaVA-1.5 and Qwen-VL, framing it as a "Bi-Level" system is slightly theatrical. It is highly likely this is strictly a test-time adaptation method (`Local_Calib`). If empirical tests show universal `Local_Zero` failure, compress this narrative and simply state calibration is structurally required.
2. **Hard-Negative Sampling Blindspot:** Your InfoNCE design utilizes Hard Negatives ($\text{IoU} > 0.5$ with differing semantics). However, you have not accounted for *compositional coherence*. If a localized patch contains a "rider" and a "horse" ($\text{IoU} > 0.5$), pushing their representations apart in the semantic space might severely harm relational reasoning. 
3. **Threshold Attrition Fallback:** In Section 4.4, you plan to track prefill threshold drift (Fig 4). However, the methodology lacks a *fallback mechanism*. If the activation threshold $\theta_{active}$ becomes mathematically obsolete by step $t=80$ of a 250-token sequence, what does the model do? A descriptive graph of failure is insufficient; the algorithm needs a prescriptive recovery state.
4. **Missing Baseline in Chain C:** In Table 2 (FREAK, DocVQA), you ablate masking tiers against `Local_MeanPool`. You *must* also include the `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline here. If the 5k Visual Genome prior accidentally learned OCR or dense spatial heuristics, it might outperform the Top-$k$ routing natively. 

## SectionBySectionComments
- **Abstract & Intro:** Excellent claim shrinkage. The proposition is focused strictly on "how to inject token-local visual evidence at decode-time." Maintain this discipline.
- **Section 3.1 (Calibration):** Clarify the exact implementation of the 2-layer MLP. Is it injected as a parallel stream during generation, or does it replace a specific projection? Ensure the FLOP count overhead of $\Phi_{calib}$ is documented alongside parameter count.
- **Section 3.2 (Entropy-Scaled Threshold):** Normalizing visual entropy by $\log(N_v)$ assumes entropy scales logarithmically with patch count. For dynamic resolution models (e.g., LLaVA-AnyRes) where patches represent different physical granularities (thumbnail vs. high-res crops), this normalization might skew $\theta_{active}$. You must empirically verify this scaling factor.
- **Section 3.3 (Semantic Masking):** The polarity of $\gamma$ needs extreme clarity. Based on the formula $L_{orig} - \alpha \gamma (1-\hat{S})$, $\gamma=1$ *activates* the penalty. Therefore, for DocVQA regex triggers (Tier 2), you are *forcing* visual intervention on numbers/dates. Acknowledge that broken OCR strings that fail the regex will default to $\gamma=0$, bypassing intervention precisely when it is needed most.

## RequiredRevisions
1. **Define the Threshold Fallback:** Add a mathematical or operational fallback rule for when the prefill-derived $\theta_{active}$ decays past a critical utility bound during long autoregression (e.g., reverting to a dynamic rolling window or a fallback MeanPool).
2. **Address Compositional InfoNCE:** Explicitly define how your hard-negative sampling prevents the destructive separation of naturally co-occurring compositional objects (e.g., clothing on a person).
3. **Refine Chain C Baseline:** Integrate the `Base + 5k LoRA` control into the dense reasoning tasks (Table 2) to ensure the OCR/Document improvements are strictly from decode-time spatial routing.

## SuggestedFiguresTablesExperiments
To ensure your execution meets the AC-level standard, enforce the following in your upcoming experiments:
- **Table 1 (Chain A - Hallucination):** The inclusion of FPR is excellent. Add a column for **Intervention ITL Overhead (ms/token)**. If your method requires $+40$ms per token to achieve a $1.5\%$ F1 gain over the `5k LoRA` baseline, the trade-off must be transparently debatable.
- **Figure 1 (Chain B - OOV Scatterplot):** Set a hard visual boundary line on this plot. If the Intervention Trigger Rate on MMMU drops below $10\%$, you must conclude in the text that the semantic mask is simply disabling the intervention to protect reasoning, rather than actively "preserving" it.
- **Figure 3 (Latency vs Window Size):** Do not just plot ITL against Candidate Window Size $M$. You must plot ITL against **Generation Step $t$** (e.g., $t=1$ to $t=500$) to prove that the $M \times N_v$ sorting operation does not geometrically explode as the KV-cache grows.
- **Appendix D (Failure Cases):** Your proposed qualitative analysis of Polysemy and OCR fragmentation is exactly what is expected. Add a third category: **Compositional Destruction**, where the intervention correctly suppressed a hallucination but inadvertently deleted a valid, highly overlapping contextual object due to the hard-negative training prior.
