# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Bounding Entity Verification via Geometrically Grounded Late-Fusion

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from entity hallucination when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during autoregressive decoding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations (e.g., DoLa, VCD) elegantly modulate general model confidence, they lack explicit, supervised spatial-to-lexical mappings. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a supervised late-fusion parametric head that projects post-connector visual features directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation, we explicitly downgrade our scope from general "hallucination suppression" to **visual entity existence verification** over an offline-curated physical noun dictionary ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). TLRA operates on three structural principles: (1) **Geometric Manifold Preservation**: TLRA employs a frozen calibration matrix strictly tied to the LLM's Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$), training only a lightweight Visual Alignment Adapter; (2) **Dynamic Activation Pooling** to resolve the spatial dilution inherent in static patch selection; and (3) **Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM)** via an Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$), mathematically guaranteeing that hallucination suppression does not force irreversible grammatical collapse. To resolve BPE fragmentation, we introduce **Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking**, isolating penalties to specific noun sequences without degrading homophonic prefixes. 

Crucially, this precise formulation forces us to confront and bound fundamental algorithmic fragilities. We pre-register a rigorous evaluation protocol to audit system-level vulnerabilities, including: the **Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) Shift** (where models bypass $V_{noun}$ penalties by hallucinating obscure synonyms); the **Distant Negation Trap** (where contextual kill-switches fail on long-range structural dependencies); and the **Batched Inference Latency Trap**. By separating Time-to-First-Token (TTFT) from Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) across varying batch sizes, we benchmark TLRA against prompt-injected object detection to define the strict operational limits of decode-time visual routing.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to entity hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD) are powerful training-free methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or distribution shifts. However, as purely zero-shot interventions, they lack explicit, supervised spatial-to-lexical mapping pathways trained specifically to verify spatial presence at the token level. 

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is a late-fusion parametric routing module requiring targeted supervision. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit existence-checking supervision.

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly downgrade our claim from universal "hallucination suppression" to **Entity Existence Verification**. Algorithmically, TLRA acts as an existence checker for an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). This precise formulation forces us to confront five hard theoretical boundaries:

First, **The Batched Inference Latency Trap & System Overheads**: Comparing late-fusion intervention against Prompt-Injected Object Detection (e.g., Grounding DINO) requires strict latency disaggregation. Prompt-injection incurs massive Time-to-First-Token (TTFT) delays but normal Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT). If TLRA's Deterministic Trie Masking introduces severe TPOT degradation during batched inference (e.g., batch size 32) due to CPU-GPU synchronization, its architectural complexity is practically unjustified.
Second, **The Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) Synonym Shift**: Because TLRA penalizes only a finite $V_{noun}$ dictionary, it inherently biases the LLM’s hallucination distribution. We must prove the base LLM does not simply bypass the penalty by dynamically shifting to hallucinate obscure OOV synonyms (e.g., substituting the penalized "sofa" with an unpenalized "chesterfield").
Third, **Relational Exacerbation**: Verifying that an object exists locally may inadvertently validate an incorrect spatial relation (e.g., "dog on the sofa") generated by the base LLM's deeper self-attention vulnerabilities.
Fourth, **The Negation Trap (Local vs. Distant)**: A context-blind visual checker will disastrously penalize correct negative constraints. While local explicit negations (e.g., "no dog") can be patched with heuristics, we must audit structural parsing failures on *distant* explicit negations and complex implicit negations (e.g., "omitted", "absent").
Fifth, **The BPE Collateral Damage Fallacy**: Naive subword suppression risks morphological destruction. Penalizing the subword `_re` because "refrigerator" is missing visually arbitrarily destroys non-noun vocabulary like `_really` or `_return`.

To resolve these threats, TLRA introduces **Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking** to safely route BPE probabilities without collateral damage, and **Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM)** equipped with an Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$) to gracefully concede a hallucination rather than forcing grammatical collapse. We prevent **Geometric Manifold Drift** by anchoring the projection to the LLM's frozen Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$), and replace static Top-$k$ pooling with **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**.

Our evaluation is a falsifiable protocol organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction, OOV Shifts, and the Negation Trap (POPE / CHAIR):** Evaluating entity grounding against zero-shot methods, tracking the OOV hallucination rate, and explicitly auditing True Negative Accuracy across *local*, *distant*, and *implicit* negations.
2. **Structure, Reasoning, and Hyperparameter Stability (MMBench / MME / DocVQA):** Auditing BPE collateral damage and the structural protection of VASM (`TLRA_no_VASM` vs Base) across varying $\tau$ thresholds on text-heavy tasks.
3. **Local Evidence Value and Batched System Profiling (FREAK / System Latency):** Comparing Dynamic Pooling against `TLRA_static_k` and Mean-Pooled bounds to prove local extraction, while rigorously benchmarking TTFT and TPOT across batched generation scales.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Connector Locus and the Manifold Preservation Principle

TLRA operates as a supervised parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage, strictly limited to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). 

**The Connector Locus Requirement:** To map visual features directly into the LLM's vocabulary space, the spatial features $X_v$ *must* be extracted **post-vision-language connector** (e.g., after the LLaVA MLP projector). Raw vision encoder features (e.g., CLIP) exist in an incompatible dimensional manifold.

**The Sparsity Trap and Manifold Preservation:** To enable zero-shot generalization to unseen tokens, the projection must inherit semantic structure. We utilize the base LLM's pre-trained **Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)** sliced for $V_{noun}$ as our anchor matrix $W_{anchor} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$. 
Crucially, if $W_{anchor}$ is updated during training, its weights will immediately drift away from the LLM's exact logit space. Therefore, **$W_{anchor}$ is strictly frozen**. We train only a lightweight, low-rank Visual Alignment Adapter $f_{adapt}(X_v)$ on a 50k Visual Genome subset. 

**Geometric Baselines:** To isolate spatial localization capacity, `TLRA_zero` computes cosine similarity directly between frozen post-connector features and $W_{anchor}$ without $f_{adapt}$. To prove that local extraction matters over global context, `TLRA_MeanPool` violently averages all $X_v$ tokens before projection.

### 3.2 Contrastive Calibration and Dynamic Activation Pooling

To calibrate $f_{adapt}$, we employ a **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** dynamically sampling hard negatives constrained by a **Semantic Margin $\phi_{sim}$** (via frozen CLIP text-text similarity) to prevent penalizing visual synonyms.

Visual patches are not bag-of-words. Static Top-$k$ pooling dilutes the existence signal of small objects and forces arbitrary spatial bounds. We utilize **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**. At decoding step $t$, for a candidate noun $c$, we identify the subset of active spatial patches $\mathcal{A}_c = \{j \mid (f_{adapt}(X_{v, j}) \cdot W_{anchor})_c > \theta_{pool}\}$. The spatial support is the mean of these dynamically selected patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{A}_c|} \sum_{j \in \mathcal{A}_c} (f_{adapt}(X_{v, j}) \cdot W_{anchor})_c \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
If $\mathcal{A}_c$ is empty, $S_{raw}(c) = 0$. The scaling factor $\beta$ is a fixed global constant derived from the adapter's training distribution variance; it requires no per-image or per-prompt tuning. We define `TLRA_static_k` as an ablation to rigorously prove that this dynamic $\mathcal{A}_c$ outperforms fixed top-k patch selection.

### 3.3 Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking (Solving BPE Collateral Damage)

Modern tokenizers shatter words ambiguously. The naive approach of penalizing a subword prefix (e.g., `_re` for `_refrigerator`) causes unacceptable BPE collateral damage by suppressing homophonic non-nouns (e.g., `_really`, `_return`). 

We replace probability-based subword lookahead with **Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking**. We construct a deterministic character-to-token Trie of $V_{noun}$. At step $t$, TLRA only applies a penalty to a subword logit if that subword deterministically routes *exclusively* to nodes within $V_{noun}$ that lack visual support $\hat S(c)$. If a subword branch contains *any* valid non-noun continuation (e.g., the path for `_re`), the visual penalty is deferred. 

*Computational Boundary:* While this mathematical guarantee prevents morphological collapse, dynamic per-sequence Trie traversal poses severe challenges for batched inference. Frequent CPU-GPU synchronizations to update batch-specific logit masks risk crippling Tokens/Second (TPOT). This forces an explicit evaluation of batched throughput to determine if the intervention is deployable.

### 3.4 Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM) and The Negation Trap

**The $\tau$-Abort Syntax Floor:** Let $L_{floor}$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the top-$K$ window. Forcing the argmax to fall to $L_{floor}$ is fatal if $L_{floor}$ represents a grammatically invalid token (e.g., a comma). The **Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance (VASM)** module enforces an Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$):
$$
L_{final}(c) = 
\begin{cases} 
L_{orig}(c) & \text{if } (L_{orig}(c) - L_{floor}) > \tau \\
\max \Big( L_{floor} - \epsilon, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big) & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
If the entropy gap exceeds $\tau$, VASM aborts the penalty. We formally concede that if the base LLM is overwhelmingly confident in a hallucination, TLRA must surrender to prevent irreversible structural collapse.

**The Explicit Negation Kill-Switch:** A context-blind checker mathematically destroys the LLM's ability to output accurate negative constraints. We introduce a lightweight look-behind heuristic: if an explicit negation token ("no", "not", "without") appears in the immediate local window $Y_{t-\delta:t}$, the penalty $\gamma(c)$ is forced to 0. We acknowledge this is a brittle regular-expression-style heuristic. It will systematically fail on **Distant Explicit Negations** (where the object is $> \delta$ tokens from the negator) and **Implicit Negations** (e.g., "absent"), defining a strict structural parsing boundary for late-fusion visual verification.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict linguistic, structural, and hardware boundaries. By reframing TLRA as a geometrically grounded entity existence verifier, we identify mathematically rigid limitations that define the method's frontier:

1. **The Batched Inference Latency Trap:** While Deterministic Vocabulary Trie Masking successfully prevents collateral damage to non-noun subwords, dynamically updating per-sequence subword masks across a batched dimension inherently conflicts with standard GPU tensor parallelism. If TPOT at batch size 32 (evaluated in Chain C) degrades significantly compared to Prompt-Injected baselines, decode-time subword routing may be fundamentally unscalable for high-throughput deployment.
2. **The Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) Synonym Shift:** Because TLRA bounds its verification strictly to $V_{noun} \approx 4,500$, it acts as an asymmetric penalty surface. The base LLM's autoregressive nature may learn to bypass spatial constraints by dynamically shifting its hallucination distribution toward obscure, unpenalized OOV synonyms, rendering the targeted dictionary an incomplete defense.
3. **The Distant and Implicit Negation Boundary:** The introduction of the Explicit Negation Kill-Switch mathematically limits the "Negation Trap" but remains a brittle heuristic. As hypothesized in Chain A, a context-blind existence checker fundamentally lacks structural parsing depth; it will systematically fail on distant explicit negations (where the negator exceeds the look-behind window $\delta$) and complex implicit negations (e.g., "The image lacks a dog"). 
4. **Relational Exacerbation vs. Global Existence:** Because TLRA pools visual patches independently of deep text history, it excels at global entity verification but actively risks *exacerbating* relational hallucinations. Validating an object's existence strictly via spatial features risks strengthening an incorrect positional or action-based claim made by the base LLM.
5. **The Grammatical Trade-off (VASM):** The Visual-Aware Syntax Maintenance threshold formally concedes that late-fusion logit manipulation cannot always elegantly override the language prior. If the sequence entropy is too low, the system must surrender and allow a hallucination to pass to prevent catastrophic grammatical destruction. The operational stability of the model therefore rests heavily on the resilience of the $\tau$ hyperparameter.

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head can safely navigate these morphological fragilities and latency bottlenecks without collapsing under standard deployment conditions.
