# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Sparse Decode-Time Visual Existence Checker for Hallucination Suppression

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during entity grounding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways to route fine-grained evidence. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens during autoregressive decoding. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual existence verifier** rather than a complex spatial reasoner. It reliably answers "Is this noun anywhere in the image?" to suppress pure hallucinations, establishing a strict theoretical boundary between global captioning success and targeted VQA vulnerabilities. To resolve parameter bloat, TLRA projects strictly to an offline-filtered physical noun subset ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$), reducing the structural footprint to $\sim$18M parameters. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A semantically coherent initialization tied to the LLM's **Layer 0 input embeddings** to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap, leveraging the residual stream's dot-product manifold alignment to Layer $N$; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring trained via a strict hard-negative contrastive objective, featuring an **in-window Dynamic Syntax Floor** to prevent structural collapse; and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) equipped with a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** mapped to GPU memory to survive BPE fragmentation—avoiding the catastrophic prefix collisions inherent in naive first-subword penalization. This work establishes a pre-registered, boundary-aware protocol that explicitly audits the intervention's structural integrity, tracking latency physics, adversarial context-blindness, and the semantic risks of grammar pivots.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), such as LLaVA-1.5 and Llama-3-Vision variants, exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, Image-Level Classifier-Free Guidance) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or distribution shifts. However, they do not introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways. Standard parametric approaches, conversely, operate entirely in the prefill phase, compressing spatial evidence into the global context window without explicitly routing it to specific token generation steps. This leaves a critical gap: **How can we inject fine-grained, token-specific visual evidence directly into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying morphological syntax, suffering geometric mismatch, or succumbing to the prefix-collision trap of modern BPE tokenizers?**

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit supervision that zero-shot methods lack. 

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly reject the framing of TLRA as a "context-aware spatial reasoner." Algorithmically, TLRA acts as a **Global Visual Existence Checker**. By pooling patch features independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. While this effectively suppresses pure hallucinations in captioning, it establishes a hard theoretical boundary: TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance queries or ignore irrelevant background objects. We embrace this "Context-Blindness" constraint, directly stress-testing it via adversarial VQA splits to map the exact threshold where global existence checking fails targeted instruction-following.

Furthermore, we structurally correct the bloat of vocabulary-space projections. Projecting to a full 128,000-token vocabulary incurs massive parameter costs ($\sim$524M parameters), the vast majority mapping to non-visual abstract tokens. TLRA utilizes a **Sparse Deterministic Projection** ($D \times V_{noun}$), reducing the trainable footprint to $\sim$18M parameters by strictly targeting an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems. 

Finally, we confront two existential threats to late-fusion routing: **Geometric Incoherence** and **BPE Subword Collisions**. Training a projection matrix from scratch on 50k captions functionally guarantees failure on unseen tokens. TLRA solves this by initializing its sparse projection head directly from the LLM's **Layer 0 input token embeddings**, relying on the residual stream's preservation of the embedding manifold to modulate Layer $N$ outputs. Simultaneously, modern tokenizers shatter common words (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`). Penalizing the first subword (`_re`) destroys valid non-nouns ("return"). We introduce a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** to defer penalties to unique semantic identifiers. However, this introduces the risk of the model executing a "Graceful Pivot" into fluent but semantically absurd text (e.g., "The man opened the *realize*"). 

Our evaluation is structured as a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Semantic Absurdity:** Evaluating entity grounding against zero-shot methods, while strictly auditing for Fluency (WikiText Perplexity) and Semantic Coherence (SPICE / GPT-4 Judge) to catch fluent but nonsensical pivots.
2. **Context-Blindness and Targeted VQA:** Explicitly splitting evaluation into Global Existence (POPE Random/Popular) and Targeted Relevance (Adversarial VQA) to map the exact failure boundary of history-independent visual routing.
3. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general capabilities (MMBench) and geometric initialization (Layer 0 vs. Random vs. Layer $N$) alongside a fully untrained `TLRA_zero` baseline.
4. **Hardware Reality and The Latency Bottleneck:** A formal evaluation of the Trie-Lookahead's computational overhead, mapping batched Tokens/Second throughput on GPU to prove the intervention does not induce a CPU-GPU synchronization death spiral.
5. **The Dynamic Syntax Floor Audit:** Quantifying the exact trigger rates and argmax-retention outcomes when the hallucination penalty clashes with weak non-noun distributions.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Sparse Architectural Scope and Geometrically Coherent Initialization

TLRA targets standard MLLM architectures where spatial provenance is preserved across visual tokens $X_v$. TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage:

**1. `TLRA_calib` (The Sparse Parametric Routing Head):** TLRA strictly limits its projection to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ maps cached spatial visual states to this subset. 
- **The Sparsity Trap and Geometric Coherence:** To enable zero-shot generalization to unseen tokens, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained Layer 0 input token embeddings** (omitting bias terms). Why does Layer 0 modulate Layer $N$ logits? The Transformer's residual stream heavily preserves token identity; the input embedding manifold and the output `lm_head` manifold remain strongly correlated (and are explicitly tied in many architectures). Aligning visual features to Layer 0 provides a geometrically robust proxy for Layer $N$ vocabulary projection.
- **`TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** A strict baseline that computes cosine similarity directly between frozen $X_v$ and the Layer 0 text embeddings without any parametric training. This lower-bound proves whether decode-time routing requires explicit contrastive supervision ($W_{calib}$), or if inherent feature alignment suffices.

### 3.2 Training Formulation and Hard Negative Sampling

If trained solely on positive occurrences, an existence checker suffers mode collapse, saturating outputs to 1.0. To calibrate $W_{calib}$, we employ a strict **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** ($\mathcal{L}_{calib}$). For a given image with ground-truth physical nouns $\mathcal{P}$, we dynamically sample **hard negative nouns** $\mathcal{N}$ from $V_{noun}$. Crucially, $\mathcal{N}$ is constructed using corpus co-occurrence matrices (objects frequently associated with $\mathcal{P}$ but explicitly absent from the current image), forcing the model to learn precise visual boundaries rather than dataset priors. The LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{P}} \log(\hat{S}(c)) - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{N}} \log(1 - \hat{S}(c))
$$

### 3.3 The Top-$k$ "Existence Checker" and the Dynamic Syntax Floor

At decoding step $t$, TLRA computes a spatial support score by pooling the **Top-$k$** matching patches for a candidate noun token $c$ from the candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_{c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
Because $S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$, TLRA functions as a context-blind *Global Existence Checker*. 

To apply this penalty without inducing catastrophic grammatical stuttering (e.g., forcing a loop of prepositions when a hallucinated noun is suppressed), we enforce a **Dynamic Syntax Floor**. Let $L_{floor} = \max_{v \in \mathcal{C}_t \setminus \mathcal{V}_{noun}} L_{orig}(v) + \epsilon$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the Top-$M$ window. The final logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = \max \Big( L_{floor}, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big)
$$
**The Argmax Retention Constraint:** We explicitly acknowledge a mathematical boundary in this formulation. If the model is 99% confident in a hallucinated noun, and the best non-noun alternative is a low-probability comma, $L_{floor}$ will be extremely low. Clamping $c$ to $L_{floor}$ may *still* leave $c$ as the argmax winner. We accept this as a deliberate fail-safe against syntax collapse, and pre-register "Floor Trigger Rate" and "Argmax Retention Rate" as explicitly tracked limitations in our evaluation.

### 3.4 Resolving BPE Fragmentation: Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification

VASM utilizes a deterministic binary mask ($\gamma \in \{0, 1\}^V$) targeting noun stems. However, modern tokenizers shatter words. A naive "First-Subword Penalty" introduces a fatal **Prefix Collision Threat**: if "refrigerator" tokenizes to `_re`, `friger`, `ator`, penalizing `_re` due to the lack of a refrigerator simultaneously penalizes valid non-nouns like "red" or "return".

TLRA implements a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** mapped directly via batched tensor masking to prevent CPU-GPU synchronization bottlenecks:
1. **Trie Construction:** $V_{noun}$ is compiled into a subword Trie offline.
2. **Deferred Penalty:** At step $t$, if a candidate matches an ambiguous prefix node (e.g., `_re`), $\gamma(c)$ is set to 0.
3. **Unique Resolution Pivot:** The penalty $\hat{S}$ is strictly deferred until the trajectory reaches a subword that *uniquely identifies* a noun stem (e.g., `friger` conditioned on `_re`). 

If the image lacks a refrigerator, TLRA drops the logit for `friger`. This forces the LLM to execute a **Graceful Pivot** (e.g., completing `_re` + `alize`). While this preserves low Perplexity (PPL), it introduces a severe risk of **Semantic Absurdity** ("The man opened the *realize*"). We explicitly audit this fluent-but-nonsensical failure mode in Section 4.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict linguistic, structural, and semantic boundaries. By reframing TLRA as an explicitly-trained, context-blind visual existence verifier, we identify mathematically rigid limitations that future research must confront:

1. **Context-Blindness in Targeted VQA:** The "Existence Checker" pools patches independently of text context ($Y_{<t}$). If a user asks, "What is the man holding?", and a car is parked in the background, TLRA validates the token "car." If language priors hallucinate "car", TLRA will not suppress it because the object physically exists. This establishes a hard boundary: TLRA excels at global captioning (POPE/AMBER) but introduces severe vulnerabilities in adversarial, targeted instruction-following.
2. **Semantic Absurdity vs. Graceful Pivots:** While the Subword-Trie Lookahead successfully prevents catastrophic prefix collisions (preserving valid tokens like "return" when suppressing "refrigerator"), it introduces the risk of fluent but nonsensical pivots. Forcing an autoregressive trajectory to abandon a noun mid-word preserves low Perplexity but can destroy sentence-level semantic meaning (measured via SPICE drops).
3. **The Dynamic Syntax Floor Flaw (Argmax Retention):** Clamping a heavily penalized noun to the highest non-noun logit ($L_{floor}$) prevents syntax loops (stuttering). However, if the model is extremely confident in the hallucination, the highest non-noun token (e.g., a comma) may have an extremely low logit. In these cases, the clamped noun logit remains the argmax winner, meaning the hallucination mathematically cannot be suppressed without destroying syntax.
4. **Hardware Latency and Trie Divergence:** Autoregressive decoding is memory-bandwidth bound. Traversing a Trie for candidate tokens introduces branching logic that can cause severe GPU divergence during batched generation. Claiming a "tiny 18M parameter footprint" is a red herring if the algorithmic complexity of the Trie search destroys tokens/second throughput.

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a sparse, geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities to provide scalable visual grounding, or if the tradeoff between syntax preservation and hallucination suppression represents an insurmountable theoretical ceiling.
