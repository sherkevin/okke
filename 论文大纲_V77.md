# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding, attention control, and layer-contrast methods—are strong, legitimate baselines, but they do not directly provide a framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is constructed on three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse visual contexts with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual states. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via an automated WordNet-based root-token lookup and deterministic BPE continuation inheritance. To enforce fair comparisons, we explicitly separate a strict zero-shot probe (`TLRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated variant (`TLRA_calib`). `TLRA_calib` is a base-model-frozen plug-in governed by an InfoNCE-trained visual-to-lexical projector, not a magically "training-free" method. 

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize this paper around a strictly pre-registered experimental contract. The protocol locks all intervention hyperparameters on a single validation set and introduces three explicit evidence chains: hallucination reduction (audited by Average Generation Length to prevent truncation artifacts), structure preservation (using OCR-heavy tasks mathematically as negative controls), and local-evidence parity (stratified by objects seen vs. unseen by the calibrator). All localized routing claims must pass a mandatory parity ablation against both `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` using the exact same calibrator weights. By auditing efficiency trade-offs and bounding video to a secondary exploratory pilot, this paper serves as a positive, falsifiable evaluation protocol for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model frequently generates text that is fluent and plausible but weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is acutely problematic in fine-grained physical grounding scenarios—such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting—where the necessary visual evidence is highly localized rather than global. 

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances in the field. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) contrast different input conditions to reshape distributions; attention-based control methods (`OPERA`) penalize abnormal generation patterns; and layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit differences between shallow and deep representations. These are all legitimate and competitive baselines. However, they leave a specific, complementary question unanswered:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit, automated structure-preserving safeguards. 

Executing this intervention is mathematically and systemically non-trivial due to four challenges. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and output logits do not naturally align in base models. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc logits intervention can only reweight candidates the base model keeps alive in its top-$M$ predictions. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression easily destroys function words, syntax, and complex entities. Fourth, **system cost**: token-local extraction scales poorly with high visual token counts.

To address these, TLRA is built upon three specific design commitments:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, avoiding base-model retraining.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance**, extracting spatial support while avoiding global homogenization.
3. **Automated VASM**, protecting linguistic structure through algorithmically derived WordNet masking and BPE inheritance.

To enforce scientific rigor, TLRA establishes strict boundary conditions. First, `TLRA_zero` serves strictly as a **viability probe**; if native architectural alignment is insufficient, the paper contracts to `TLRA_calib`, transparently defined as a base-model-frozen plug-in. Second, the framework actively embraces an **OCR concession**: because VASM algorithmically isolates physical entities and mathematically bypasses text-in-image tokens, document/OCR-heavy tasks are designated strictly as safety-oriented *negative controls*. Third, to prevent hyperparameter manipulation, all temperature and scaling variables ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M$) are strictly locked across all benchmarks based on a single validation split.

Ultimately, the validity of TLRA rests on passing a strict **parity ablation**. Any claim regarding "local evidence" must statistically outperform both a global pool (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and a random spatial subset (`TLRA_RandomK`) using the *exact same* calibrator weights, under an explicit audit separating objects seen vs. unseen during calibration. By restricting video to an exploratory appendix pilot, the primary scientific contract remains a highly controlled, image-centric evaluation of decode-time spatial routing.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary and the Calibrator Blackbox: `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib`

Acknowledging that visual and lexical states often do not natively align, we explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks to prevent "calibrator blackbox" ambiguity:

1. **`TLRA_zero`**: A strict decode-time probe where the base model's native language modeling head is applied directly to contextualized final-layer visual states to derive lexical support.
2. **`TLRA_calib`**: A lightweight calibrated variant utilizing a 2-layer MLP projection, $\Phi_{calib}$, to bridge the embedding asymmetry. Crucially, the base MLLM parameters remain strictly frozen.

**Transparency of $\Phi_{calib}$:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained exclusively to map final-layer visual patch representations to the base model's lexical embedding space. We train it via an InfoNCE contrastive loss utilizing in-batch negatives, executed on a pre-defined 50k subset of conceptual captions containing distinct physical objects. It updates no autoregressive pathways. Consequently, any routing superiority claimed by `TLRA_calib` must be validated against `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` using the exact same frozen $\Phi_{calib}$ weights, paired with a strict "Category Leakage Audit" separating objects seen vs. unseen in this 50k subset.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set derived from the original logits: $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ is a strictly locked candidate window (e.g., $M=50$). 

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the contextualized final-layer visual states, with visual token volume $N_v = H \times W$. For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support via an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule. To prevent low-resolution inputs from degenerating into global pooling, $k$ is determined by a fixed spatial ratio $\rho$:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big),
$$
followed by the raw support computation:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$
The patch-level score is obtained via `TLRA_zero` or $\Phi_{calib}$. The similarity temperature $\tau_{sim}$ dictates the sharpness of the spatial distribution and is locked globally prior to evaluation.

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a logits intervention without destroying the base model's native softmax dynamics, TLRA computes a relative evidence score over the bounded candidate set:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
To prevent out-of-bounds suppression, TLRA applies a dynamic scaling penalty anchored strictly to the candidate window's logit range ($\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t) - \min(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t)$):
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \Delta_L \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$
This ensures weakly supported candidates are penalized proportionally to the local logit scale.

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Automated VASM

Naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses critical syntax alongside hallucinations. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect structure, where $\gamma(c) = 1$ for visually groundable physical entities, and $\gamma(c) = 0$ for all other tokens.

Crucially, VASM is generated algorithmically, not via manual heuristics, operating in two stages:
1. **Automated Root-Token Lookup:** The vocabulary is processed via an automated pipeline using WordNet synsets (filtered for physical objects/nouns) and automated POS tagging. This mathematically isolates valid entity root tokens and explicitly rejects function words, verbs, and abstract syntax.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Because modern tokenizers (e.g., Llama's BPE) fragment words arbitrarily, any subword continuation token deterministicly inherits the mask value ($\gamma$) of its preceding root token.

**The OCR Concession:** By design, VASM's WordNet pipeline excludes arbitrary text strings. We explicitly concede that TLRA is mathematically blind to text-in-image tokens. Tasks like `DocVQA` are strictly negative controls—used only to prove that VASM safely bypasses text without degrading base model performance.

### 3.5 Methodological Boundaries

TLRA's limitations are explicitly mathematically bounded:
- **Out-of-Candidate Limit:** Post-hoc reweighting cannot recover a correct entity token if the base model has already ejected it from the initial Top-$M$ set.
- **Suffix-Collapse Risk:** Tokenizer edge-cases that violate logical linguistic boundaries can cause BPE inheritance to fail.
- **Single-Configuration Lock:** Parameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M$) are permanently locked after validation; per-dataset tuning is strictly prohibited.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because this paper is structured as a rigorous falsifiable proposal, the experimental methodology is organized into a sanity pilot, three mandatory evidence chains, and strict boundary audits. The following protocol dictates the criteria for claim validation. **Crucially, all hyperparameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M$) are locked uniformly across all tasks based on a single held-out validation set.**

### 4.1 Stage 0: Zero-Shot Semantic Validity Pilot

**Goal:** Determine whether `TLRA_zero` is a viable independent branch or mere noise.
- **Protocol:** Measure Top-1/5/10 overlap between patch readout words and annotated object labels on a 1k held-out image subset.
- **Decision Rule:** If overlap is near-random, the zero-shot claim is cleanly abandoned. The paper explicitly contracts to position `TLRA_calib` as the sole method, transparently defining it as a lightweight base-model-frozen plug-in requiring minimal alignment.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Truncation Audit

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, and strictly verify that this reduction is not an artifact of output truncation or calibrator memorization.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 1):** 
  - *Metrics:* POPE (Accuracy, F1), CHAIR (CHAIRi, CHAIRs), **Average Generation Length (AGL)**.
  - *Rows:* Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Validation Criteria:** If `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` reduces AGL by >10-15% compared to the Base model, the hallucination reduction claim must be downgraded, as the model is artificially inflating precision by remaining silent. 

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure Preservation and Negative Controls

**Goal:** Verify that VASM mathematically protects general multimodal understanding and that the OCR concession operates correctly as a negative control.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 2):**
  - *Metrics:* MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard), **DocVQA (including specific OCR-token overlap %)**.
  - *Rows:* Base, `TLRA_full` (with VASM), `TLRA_no_VASM`.
- **Validation Criteria:** 
  1. `TLRA_no_VASM` must demonstrate a catastrophic performance drop on MMMU, proving VASM's absolute necessity.
  2. `DocVQA` performance must remain strictly flat between Base and `TLRA_full` (supported by zero OCR-token masking), proving VASM safely bypasses out-of-scope textual entities without damage.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence Value and Category Leakage

**Goal:** Isolate and prove that spatial locality directly drives performance, rather than global calibration priors or randomized visual subset regularization.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 3):**
  - *Metrics:* FREAK (Spatial Relations, Object Existence, Counting). The dataset is strictly partitioned into **"Objects Seen in $\Phi_{calib}$"** vs. **"Objects Unseen in $\Phi_{calib}$"**.
  - *Rows:* Base, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK` (Mandatory), `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Validation Criteria:** All three TLRA variants must use the *exact same* $\Phi_{calib}$ weights. `AdaptiveTopK` must statistically outperform both `MeanPool` and `RandomK` on *both* the Seen and Unseen splits. If it only wins on the "Seen" split, the spatial routing hypothesis is invalidated as a data-leakage artifact, and the paper's claim will contract to "MeanPool + VASM."

### 4.5 Efficiency Pareto and Failure Audits

To audit systemic costs, a mandatory **Systemic Cost Pareto (Figure 1)** will plot Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT, X-axis) against POPE F1 (Y-axis), tracing the latency penalty of TLRA across $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$ alongside Base, VCD, and DoLa.

Furthermore, we will document two explicit failure mode visualizations:
1. **Out-of-Candidate Bound:** A visual sequence where the correct entity token ranked 51st in the base logits (outside $M=50$), mathematically proving the hard limit of post-hoc intervention.
2. **BPE Suffix-Collapse:** An instance where tokenizer fragmentation defies logical linguistic boundaries, causing VASM inheritance to fail.

### 4.6 Bounded Secondary Video Pilot

To explore preliminary multimedia transferability without contaminating the highly controlled image-centric spatial claims, a bounded video pilot (e.g., `VIDHALLUC`) is executed strictly in the appendix.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest iteration of TLRA is the one bound by the cleanest scientific contract. By algorithmically generating VASM, strictly separating zero-shot probes from calibrated adjustments, enforcing exact-calibrator parity against global and random baselines, and treating OCR tasks explicitly as negative controls, this benchmark prioritizes methodological transparency over unconditional SOTA claims. 

The integrity of this method is bound by several specific limitations:
1. **Calibrator Category Leakage:** External priors injected by $\Phi_{calib}$ present an ongoing risk. The mandatory Seen vs. Unseen audit and `TLRA_RandomK` ablation are the sole defenses ensuring gains stem from spatial routing, not data leakage.
2. **Hyperparameter Fragility:** The method relies on bounded parameters ($\tau_{sim}, \tau_{evidence}, \alpha, M$). If these are not strictly locked across datasets, the intervention collapses into an overfitted heuristic.
3. **Out-of-Candidate Bounds:** Decode-time reweighting cannot recover a true entity token that the base model has already discarded from its Top-$M$ candidate pool.
4. **Systemic Latency:** The adaptive Top-$k$ mechanism introduces a Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) overhead that scales dynamically with candidate window size and visual resolution.
5. **Modality Transferability:** The core mechanism is deeply optimized for spatial reasoning. Its transferability to spatio-temporal video settings remains strictly exploratory and bounded.

## 6. Revised Claims and Contract Execution

Pending empirical execution of the locked protocol, this paper restricts its claims strictly to:
- A structure-preserving masking mechanism (VASM) utilizing automated WordNet extraction and BPE inheritance.
- A controlled evaluation isolating token-local routing gains from global priors and random visual regularization.
- The establishment of explicit boundary conditions regarding inference latency, out-of-candidate failures, and text-in-image bypass.

We explicitly do not claim architecture-invariant zero-shot success, dense document-grounding competence, or unified image-video superiority.
