# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Bounded Hybrid Routing Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, including visual-noise contrastive decoding (VCD), attention-penalty control (OPERA), and layer-contrast methods (DoLa), are strong competitive baselines. However, as primarily language-side heuristics or global-noise interventions, they do not directly provide a framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, an intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from static prefill visual states.

TLRA is built on three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse visual contexts with candidate-wise support computed dynamically against the frozen visual KV-cache. To prevent memory bandwidth starvation, the visual projection is computed exactly once during the prefill phase, caching a $\approx 32$ MB tensor for lightweight $O(1)$ retrieval during decoding. Second, **Adaptive Top-k resonance** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via an offline, pre-computed WordNet-based boolean mask. Acknowledging the "BPE subword prefix trap"—where a subword like "car" belongs to both physical entities and abstract words like "cardinal"—VASM operates as a frequency-weighted statistical heuristic. It prevents the starvation of visual triggers at the accepted, measurable cost of minor collateral damage to abstract completions.

To enforce fair comparison, we explicitly separate a zero-shot architectural preliminary (`TLRA_zero`) from the main calibrated variant (`TLRA_calib`). Because `TLRA_calib` requires a lightweight projector mapping static visual states to the dynamic lexical space, it is explicitly classified as a **hybrid projection-routing method**. To isolate the decode-time routing claim from training-objective confounders, its evaluation contract mandates a direct, parameter-matched comparison against a LoRA fine-tuning of the base MLLM using the **exact same calibration data and identical cross-entropy alignment objective**.

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize the paper around a strictly pre-registered experimental contract. The protocol locks all intervention hyperparameters on a single validation set and evaluates three evidence chains: hallucination reduction audited against activation memory, structure preservation with OCR and text-only tasks treated as strict negative controls, and local-evidence parity. All localized-routing claims must pass parity ablations against `TLRA_MeanPool` and the objective-matched `Base+LoRA` control. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable proposal for structure-preserving hybrid visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible but weakly supported by the localized visual input. This failure is especially damaging in **fine-grained physical grounding** scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances in the field. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) inject visual noise to compare distributions under perturbed and clean inputs. Attention-based control methods (`OPERA`) penalize pathological autoregressive attention patterns. Layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep language-model representations. These are all legitimate baselines. However, because they rely primarily on language-side distribution shifts or global visual perturbations, they leave a specific, complementary question unanswered:

**How can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence into logits adjustment without damaging language structure, inflating autoregressive activation memory, or conflating routing gains with training-objective superiority?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a hybrid framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than global visual pooling, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards and bounds its claims against objective-matched baselines.

Executing this intervention requires overcoming four specific challenges. First, **the causal attention barrier**: in decoder-only MLLMs, visual tokens are processed during the prefill stage and remain causally isolated (frozen) from the dynamically unfolding text. Second, **manifold mismatch**: these static prefill visual states and the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical states do not natively share an embedding space without a bridging projection. Third, **computational and memory bandwidth integration**: computing similarity dynamically must not throttle the memory-bound autoregressive loop. We solve this by computing the visual projection exactly once during prefill, reducing the decode-time cost to a lightweight read of a cached $\approx 32$ MB tensor. Fourth, **the BPE Subword Prefix Trap**: naive intervention destroys syntax, exacerbated by subword bleeding where tokens (e.g., "car") are shared across physical entities ("automobile") and abstract language ("cardinal"). We introduce a frequency-weighted statistical heuristic to manage this trade-off transparently.

To address these issues, TLRA is built upon four explicit commitments:

1. **Hybrid projection-routing intervention** replacing purely training-free claims. Crucially, to prevent confounding the *routing mechanism* with the *training objective*, TLRA enforces a mandatory baseline: a LoRA model trained on the exact same data using the exact same alignment objective as the TLRA projector.
2. **Adaptive Top-k resonance** over static prefill visual states, implemented via batched operations and evaluated under strict Prefill/Decode latency accounting.
3. **Offline, pre-computed Frequency-Weighted VASM**, acknowledging its nature as a statistical compromise that prevents physical-entity recall collapse but necessitates formal auditing of collateral damage on abstract text.
4. **A strict zero/calibrated split** separating the architectural limitations of direct readout (`TLRA_zero`) from the evaluated hybrid intervention (`TLRA_calib`).

To keep the paper scientifically honest, TLRA enforces strict boundary rules. If `TLRA_calib` fails to outperform the objective-matched LoRA baseline, the decode-time routing claim is mathematically falsified. Furthermore, we accept explicit **OCR and Text-Only concessions**: because VASM is a statistical heuristic, tasks requiring arbitrary text-in-image or pure abstract reasoning serve as transparent negative controls.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: Isolating the Objective in `TLRA_calib`

We explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks to isolate architectural viability from calibration gains:

1. **`TLRA_zero` (Architectural Preliminary)**: A strict zero-shot probe where the model-native LM head is applied directly to prefill visual states. Due to the severe embedding asymmetry—where the LM head expects deeply processed text states, not early visual encodings—this stage anticipates near-random performance, confirming the absolute necessity of a bridging projection.
2. **`TLRA_calib` (Main Intervention)**: A hybrid projection-routing variant where a lightweight, 2-layer MLP projector $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained to map static visual states into the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical space. 

**Objective Matching for Fair Evaluation:**
A critical flaw in evaluating hybrid interventions is confounding the routing mechanism with the training objective (e.g., comparing an InfoNCE-trained projector against a Cross-Entropy-trained LoRA). To ensure mathematical fairness, $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained using a standard localized Cross-Entropy (Next-Token Prediction) objective over a conceptual-caption subset, aligning the visual states directly to the lexical target embeddings. 
Consequently, any claim about decode-time local routing must survive a mandatory parity ablation against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset **using the identical Cross-Entropy objective**, in addition to `TLRA_MeanPool` using the identical frozen $\Phi_{calib}$.

### 3.2 Bridging the Manifold Mismatch: $O(1)$ Prefill Caching and Local Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M$ is a globally locked candidate window (e.g., $M=50$). 

Crucially, in decoder-only MLLMs utilizing KV-caching, visual tokens are processed exclusively during the prefill stage. Let $h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}$ denote the static final-layer LLM hidden states at the visual token positions ($N_v \approx 4000$). 

**Hardware Lifecycle and Memory Bandwidth Protection:**
Recomputing the projection at every decoding step would cause severe sequential kernel dispatch delays and memory bandwidth starvation. Instead, $\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is executed **exactly once** at the conclusion of the prefill stage. The resulting $N_v \times D$ projected visual tensor (approximately $32.7$ MB in FP16) is stored in the KV-cache/SRAM. 

During the autoregressive loop, for each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA dynamically queries its support against the cached tensor:
$$
k = \max(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil)
$$
The raw local support is computed via a single batched dot-product:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}), c)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
This exacts $\approx 800$ MFLOPs per token—a negligible footprint that smoothly fuses with LM-head memory reads without fragmenting activation memory.

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw local support into a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
To avoid uncontrolled distortion when the model is extremely confident in a structural token, TLRA applies a scaled penalty anchored to the local logit range $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)) - \min(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t))$. This penalty is strictly bounded by a hyperparameter $\beta$ to ensure that even if a valid syntactic token is mistakenly flagged by the mask, the visual penalty cannot override a massive logit spread:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L, \beta) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 The BPE Subword Prefix Trap and Frequency-Weighted VASM

To prevent structural collapse without introducing inline parsing latency, TLRA uses an offline, pre-computed Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Mask (VASM), $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$, which maps physical entities to $1$ and protected syntax to $0$.

However, BPE tokenization introduces a critical theoretical vulnerability: **The Subword Prefix Trap**. Short physical entities frequently serve as subwords for abstract concepts (e.g., the physical token `[car]` is the prefix for `[car, dinal]` or `[car, diovascular]`). A strict conservative intersection would forcefully mask out `[car]`, mathematically starving the intervention of valid visual triggers and causing catastrophic physical-entity recall collapse. 

To resolve this, TLRA employs **Frequency-Weighted Subword Resolution**:
1. We query a standard text corpus (e.g., C4). 
2. If a BPE subword maps to a groundable root entity in $\ge \gamma$ (e.g., 85%) of its occurrences, it retains $M_{VASM}=1$. 

**Acknowledging Collateral Damage:** 
We explicitly frame VASM not as a perfect zero-overhead shield, but as a statistical heuristic. Frequency weighting prevents the starvation of visual triggers, but it dictates *which* semantic direction is sacrificed. If the model attempts to generate `[car, dinal]` and there is no automobile in the image, TLRA will heavily penalize the `[car]` candidate, potentially blocking the abstract word. We accept this collateral damage on non-physical vocabulary as an inherent trade-off, which we formally audit in our evaluation protocol using pure-text negative controls.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around one architectural preliminary, three main evidence chains, and strict efficiency/boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Architectural Preliminary (`TLRA_zero`)

**Goal:** Mathematically verify the embedding asymmetry between static prefill visual states and dynamic pre-LM-head representations.
- **Protocol:** Evaluate direct dot-product zero-shot routing on a miniature held-out subset.
- **Expected Outcome:** Near-random performance due to manifold mismatch, confirming the absolute necessity of $\Phi_{calib}$ and transitioning the evaluation to `TLRA_calib`.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Objective Fairness

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, strictly verifying that gains are derived from decode-time routing and not the calibration data or alignment objective.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Core Baselines:** Base, `VCD` (Visual Noise), `MM-DoLa` (Layer Contrast).
- **Mandatory Objective-Matched Baseline:** `Base + LoRA` using the exact same training subset and the exact same Cross-Entropy objective as $\Phi_{calib}$.
- **Metrics:** Hallucination score, `AGL`, `AGL StdDev` (to catch models cheating by collapsing generation diversity into terse, uniform answers).
- **Hard Rule:** If `TLRA_calib` loses to the objective-matched `LoRA` baseline, the hybrid routing claim is falsified.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure Preservation and Collateral Damage Audit

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention preserves general capability, while explicitly measuring the collateral damage caused by VASM's BPE Subword Prefix Trap.
- **General Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard)`.
- **Negative Controls (The Collateral Damage Audit):** 
  1. `DocVQA`: Arbitrary text strings receive zero mask coverage (OCR concession).
  2. `GSM8K` or Text-Only QA (passed through the MM interface): Measures how much the frequency-weighted VASM damages abstract linguistic reasoning when visual evidence is irrelevant.
- **Ablation Rows:** Base, `TLRA_full` (with VASM), `TLRA_no_VASM`.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence Value and Leakage Control

**Goal:** Isolate whether the local routing computation provides value over global pooling, given the exact same projector.
- **Benchmark:** `FREAK` (Spatial Relations, Object Existence, Counting).
- **Routing Parity Rows:** `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Strict Rule:** Both variants must share the identical frozen $\Phi_{calib}$. Leakage is monitored by splitting the results into Seen vs. Unseen categories based on the calibrator catalog.

### 4.5 Efficiency and Failure-Boundary Audits

**Goal:** Establish the actual deployment cost and formally document qualitative failure modes.
- **Hardware Metrics:** We track **Prefill Time (ms)** (which will slightly inflate due to the one-time $O(1)$ $\Phi_{calib}$ execution) and **Decode Time (ms/token)** (which must not throttle by more than 5% compared to Base). 
- **Failure Analysis 1: Out-of-Candidate Hijacking.** We implement a tracking hook for the true target token. A histogram will show the frequency at which the ground-truth token falls outside the Top-$M$ candidate window. In these cases, TLRA acts as a forced multiplier on incorrect candidates.
- **Failure Analysis 2: Candidate Window Ablation ($M$).** We will provide a line plot tracking POPE F1 and "Out-of-Candidate Hijacking %" as the window size $M$ scales from 10 to 100, empirically proving the boundary at which visual entities are pushed out of the LM's reach.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is the one with the cleanest, most unforgiving scientific contract:
- It explicitly frames itself as a hybrid projection-routing method, bounding its claims against an objective-matched `Base + LoRA` baseline to prevent conflating routing gains with alignment-objective superiority.
- It appropriately frames its computational footprint, demonstrating that computing the visual projection once during prefill and caching the 32MB tensor adds negligible overhead to the memory-bound decoding loop.
- It utilizes an offline, frequency-weighted VASM to preserve structure without introducing autoregressive parsing delays, while transparently auditing its own statistical flaws.

The primary unresolved risks and acknowledged limitations are:
1. **The Objective Matching Risk:** `TLRA_calib` may fail to significantly outperform `Base + LoRA (Matched Obj & Data)`, indicating that the cross-entropy alignment over the calibration data—not the decode-time routing—drives the performance.
2. **The Subword Prefix Trap (Collateral Damage):** As audited in our text-only negative controls, VASM is a statistical heuristic. By frequency-weighting BPE subwords to prevent the starvation of visual triggers, TLRA actively causes collateral damage to abstract language that shares physical subword prefixes (e.g., penalizing "cardinal" because "car" lacks visual support). 
3. **Out-of-Candidate Hijacking:** If strong language priors push the true visual entity entirely out of the LM's Top-$M$ candidate window, TLRA's dynamic routing cannot recover it. Instead, it anchors a hallucination to visual noise by acting as a forced multiplier on the remaining incorrect candidates.
