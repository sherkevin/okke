# Bounded Resonance Anchoring: Token-Local Logits Intervention for Multimodal Hallucination Mitigation

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are prone to hallucination when language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for grounded decoding. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention control) are highly competitive but typically solve different parts of the inference problem. They do not directly offer a general framework for injecting strictly **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support in the terminal logits space.

BRA operates on three core principles. First, **token-local logits intervention** evaluates candidate-wise support computed directly from contextualized visual states at the LLM's final layer. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance** extracts stable local evidence from high-resolution token fields—and optionally from video via temporal decay proximity—without disrupting the underlying decoding distribution. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via an $O(1)$ static dictionary and dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation inheritance. To rigorously address the input-output embedding asymmetry inherent in prefix-conditioned MLLMs, we explicitly separate a zero-shot variant (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated bound (`BRA_calib`), enforcing strict fairness by comparing the latter only to similarly fine-tuned baselines.

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize this paper around a falsifiable, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol spanning three evidence chains: hallucination reduction (measured strictly alongside Average Generated Length), structure and reasoning preservation, and the specific necessity of local evidence over coarse aggregation. Evaluated across `POPE`, `CHAIR`, `MMBench`, `MMMU`, `FREAK`, and `DocVQA`, this protocol presents a rigorously bounded architectural proposition for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual image or video input. This failure is especially damaging in dense grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is fundamentally token-local rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), attention-based pattern penalty (e.g., OPERA), or layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa). These families represent important, highly competitive baselines that effectively regularize the generation process. However, none of them natively serve as a direct mechanism for the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, defensible positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided that it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We term this framework **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**.

Executing this is nontrivial for three mathematical and structural reasons. First, the **input-output embedding asymmetry**: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, making direct cosine similarity with output vocabularies mathematically invalid unless properly projected. Second, the **post-hoc entanglement risk**: assuming the final-layer hidden states of visual patches retain spatial locality requires overcoming deep self-attention mixing. Third, the **structural collapse risk**: hallucination suppression easily overfires on Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities unless explicitly protected.

To address these challenges, BRA centers on three core mechanisms:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, mapping final-layer visual states to the terminal vocabulary space to evaluate localized patch support for text candidates.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance**, extracting localized support from bounded candidate subsets (incorporating temporal decay for video scenarios) without distribution collapse.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, a deterministic, structure-preserving protection layer utilizing an $O(1)$ static lookup and dynamic BPE continuation inheritance.

To ensure rigorous evaluation, we maintain a strict fairness boundary between a zero-shot probe (`BRA_zero`) and a calibrated fallback (`BRA_calib`). Following recent recommendations for reliable MLLM evaluation, we structure our experiments as a stringent protocol testing five lines of defense: verifying spatial locality and zero-shot validity, proving hallucination reduction against length collapse, confirming structural preservation, demonstrating local-evidence necessity, and mapping computational boundaries.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Overcoming Post-Hoc Entanglement and Embedding Asymmetry: `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`

A critical vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is the embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input embedding layer. To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* visual hidden states at the LLM's final transformer layer, $h_L^{(v_j)}$. 

We explicitly acknowledge the risk of **post-hoc entanglement**: because these states have passed through deep self-attention, they implicitly contain global context. We hypothesize that residual streams preserve enough of the original patch's spatial bias to allow local extraction, which we mandate proving empirically via representation similarity (Section 4.1). To handle the projection into lexical space cleanly, we split BRA into two distinct tracks:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute the local support by evaluating what vocabulary each visual patch would independently activate: 
   $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`BRA_calib`**: A calibrated bound utilizing a lightweight projection matrix $\Phi_{calib}$. To avoid test-set leakage, $\Phi_{calib}$ is a single linear layer trained on a small, frozen set of 5,000 COCO image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. The LLM remains entirely frozen; we optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss.

`BRA_zero` serves as the fair comparison against training-free baselines. However, for decoder-only MLLMs (e.g., LLaVA), visual tokens act as past prefixes and do not natively predict the next text token at their specific sequence positions. Projecting them through the native `lm_head` carries a severe theoretical risk of yielding pure noise. If this zero-shot alignment fails (tested in Defense Line 1), we explicitly pivot to `BRA_calib`. Crucially, to maintain strict baseline fairness, if `BRA_calib` is utilized, we mandate comparing it against baselines (e.g., Base, VCD) that have undergone Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT/LoRA) on the identical 5,000 COCO instances.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$

At decoding step $t$, BRA strictly bounds the intervention to a safe candidate set to prevent distribution collapse:
$$
\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})
$$
where $M \ll |V|$ is a candidate window (e.g., $M=100$). For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, BRA computes local visual support. To prevent spatial noise from washing out small objects, we apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule rather than a global average:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)
$$
where $k_{min}$ is an absolute integer floor (e.g., 4 patches). 

**Spatio-Temporal Extension (Video Formulation):** While strictly flattening video dimensions ($T \times H \times W$) causes temporal dilution, local visual evidence is still highly relevant for video reasoning if constrained by temporal proximity. For video inputs, the raw support integrates a temporal decay penalty $\lambda$ centered on the current inferred keyframe $t_{curr}$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c]}{\tau_{sim}} \cdot \exp(-\lambda |t_j - t_{curr}|)
$$
where $t_j$ is the frame index of patch $j$, penalizing visually resonant patches that are causally disconnected from the current event horizon.

### 3.3 Relative Penalty and VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)

The localized evidence is normalized into a relative distribution $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}(S_{raw}(c))$. The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens that lack local visual backing relative to their peers. Crucially, this penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$ to protect structural integrity:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

Hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing structural tokens or fragmenting multi-token entities. VASM deterministically gates the intervention strength via a strictly pre-computed dual-tier architecture:
1. **Root Token Expectation (Static $O(1)$ Lookup):** A static binary mask for the vocabulary $V$ assigns $\gamma = 1$ to visual nouns/adjectives and $\gamma = 0$ to functional syntax and stopwords. 
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance (Dynamic):** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the static dictionary and inherits the exact deterministic $\gamma$ weight of its most recent root token. For SentencePiece (e.g., LLaVA), root tokens are identified by `_`; for byte-level BPE (e.g., Qwen-VL), via `Ġ`. This exact inference-time inheritance completely prevents the mathematical collapse of multi-token entities (e.g., complex OCR strings) during decoding adjustment.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces a specific structural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence, we organize the evaluation plan into three falsifiable evidence chains supported by five lines of defense. The following outlines the hypothesis-driven protocol and the required benchmark execution parameters.

### 4.1 Defense Line 1: Locality Verification and Zero-Shot Semantic Validity

**Hypothesis:** The final-layer visual hidden states $h_L^{(v_j)}$ retain spatial locality despite post-hoc entanglement, and occupy a semantically meaningful subspace such that applying $W_{vocab}$ yields words aligned with the visual patch.
- **Protocol A (Spatial Locality Check):** We will compute the **Attention-Rollout / Representation Similarity Alignment** between $h_L^{(v_j)}$ and the ground-truth spatial bounding boxes on 1,000 COCO images to quantify how much spatial bias survives the deep self-attention layers.
- **Protocol B (Semantic Overlap Check):** We measure the **Token Overlap Rate** (Top-1, Top-5, Top-10) between the natively decoded words of $h_L^{(v_j)}$ and the ground-truth object labels.
- **Execution Rule (Fairness Fallback):** If the Token Overlap Rate is $<5\%$, it proves that for prefix-conditioned MLLMs, `BRA_zero` is mathematically invalid due to the absence of position-specific next-token supervision. In this scenario, the evaluation strictly pivots to `BRA_calib`. Consequently, all baselines in subsequent tables will be explicitly augmented with PEFT/LoRA fine-tuning on the identical 5,000 COCO instances to guarantee a strictly level playing field.

### 4.2 Defense Line 2 (Chain A): Hallucination Reduction vs. Length Collapse

**Hypothesis:** BRA mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting without causing pathological output truncation or length collapse.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE` (Acc, F1, Yes-Ratio), `CHAIR` (CHAIRs, CHAIRi).
- **Baselines:** Base Model, VCD, OPERA, DoLa (all LoRA-adjusted if the fallback is triggered), `BRA_zero` (if viable), `BRA_calib`.
- **Mandatory Metric:** `AGL` (Average Generated Length).
- **Execution Rule (Table 1):** We mandate reporting standard metrics alongside `AGL`. If BRA improves `CHAIR` metrics solely by generating significantly shorter responses (e.g., dodging hallucinations by truncating text, denoted by an AGL drop $> 15\%$), the core mitigation claim is falsified.

### 4.3 Defense Line 3 (Chain B): Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via local evidence does not degrade complex reasoning, provided that VASM's BPE inheritance actively protects structural and functional tokens.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard Subset)`.
- **Primary Ablation:** Base Model vs. `BRA_full` vs. `BRA_no_VASM`.
- **Execution Rule (Table 2):** `BRA_no_VASM` must demonstrate a catastrophic reasoning drop on `MMMU (Hard)`, while `BRA_full` preserves or improves upon the Base model's performance. This strictly isolates the BPE inheritance mechanism as an indispensable mathematical necessity for decode-time adjustment.

### 4.4 Defense Line 4 (Chain C): The Necessity of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting (Adaptive Top-$k$) is significantly more faithful than coarse global visual aggregation.
- **Benchmarks:** `FREAK` (OCR, Position, Counting), `DocVQA` (evaluated strictly via the ANLS metric).
- **Primary Ablation:** Base vs. `BRA_MeanPool` (replacing Top-$k$ with a global average of $h_L^{(v_j)}$) vs. `BRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Execution Rule (Table 3):** `BRA_AdaptiveTopK` must strictly outperform `BRA_MeanPool` by a statistically significant margin. If token-local logits intervention cannot beat the global average on dense document or spatial tasks, the core premise of "token-local necessity" is falsified.
- **Qualitative Audit (Figure 1):** We mandate spatial heatmaps tracking the Top-$k$ visual tokens selected during the generation of specific localized entities (e.g., decoding the word "mug" must highlight the specific patch geometry corresponding to the mug).

### 4.5 Defense Line 5: Efficiency Audits and Unrecoverability Limits

To ensure the bounded candidate filtering and $O(1)$ VASM lookup do not introduce prohibitive overhead at inference, the protocol establishes:
- **Scaling Curve (Figure 2):** Generation Latency (Tokens/Sec) plotted against Visual Token Count ($N_v$). The bounded Top-$M$ candidate mask must constrain the latency curve, keeping BRA computationally competitive with base autoregressive generation.
- **Failure Case Analysis (Appendix E - Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability):** Explicit step-by-step documentation of generation events where the ground truth visually grounded token falls entirely outside $\mathcal{C}_t$ (i.e., ground truth rank $> M$). Visualizing original vs. intervened logits will empirically map the absolute boundary limits of decode-time reweighting.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of BRA relies on a clean, verifiable scientific contract: it intervenes at decode-time, leverages token-local visual evidence, preserves structural integrity via a deterministic $O(1)$ VASM BPE inheritance, and establishes transparent performance boundaries. However, execution of this framework carries specific theoretical limitations:

1. **Embedding Alignment and Fairness Loops:** `BRA_zero` tests the absolute limits of the residual stream's ability to overcome post-hoc entanglement. If the native `lm_head` fails to project visual prefixes into meaningful semantic spaces, `BRA_calib` becomes a strict architectural requirement. Utilizing it forces the evaluation to adopt lightly fine-tuned baselines to maintain scientific integrity.
2. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** BRA fundamentally operates by reweighting the Top-$M$ language priors. It mathematically cannot generate a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$.
3. **Temporal Proximity Tuning:** While the inclusion of the temporal decay penalty $\lambda$ allows BRA to theoretically support video, tuning the temporal receptive field to balance immediate motion continuity against long-term memory retrieval requires dedicated domain adaptation beyond static image benchmarks.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines **Bounded Resonance Anchoring**, a positive, falsifiable framework for mitigating multimodal hallucinations through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By explicitly identifying the embedding asymmetry risk, proposing a testable protocol for post-hoc entanglement, and formalizing VASM with tokenizer-aware BPE inheritance, we establish a rigorous foundation for decode-time control. The claims herein are strictly bounded by the proposed five-line evaluation protocol spanning locality verification, hallucination reduction against length collapse, reasoning preservation, and the empirical necessity of localized visual evidence.
