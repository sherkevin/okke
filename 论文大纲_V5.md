# Breaking the Language Inertia: Zero-Pooling Resonance Anchoring for High-Resolution MLLM Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate high-resolution visual tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D-RoPE spatial coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., in Document VQA or GUI navigation). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a zero-pooling, decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space. 

To address critical topological constraints and hyper-parameter sensitivities, BRA incorporates two core mechanisms: (1) **Relative Resonance Penalty**, deploying a stabilized, single-parameter subtractive shift based on local visual evidence calibration; and (2) **Entropy-Guided Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)**, transitioning from brittle tokenizer heuristics to a universal, dynamic mechanism that protects continuous BPE fragments and functional syntax based on predictive entropy. We deliberately scope our framework to high-resolution static images, eliminating severe VRAM bottlenecks found in temporal tracking. Furthermore, we establish a rigorous 5-stage experimental protocol—anchored by a Cross-Domain Modality Alignment Probe and dense OCR tasks—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy, the domain-agnostic nature of latent alignment, and the Pareto-optimal system efficiency of BRA.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model hallucinates—producing text that is linguistically fluent but physically absent from the visual input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on contrastive decoding or backtracking (e.g., VCD, OPERA). While effective, they introduce significant latency overheads (Time-Per-Output-Token spikes) and VRAM bloat. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution MLLMs, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool or PCA) thousands of high-resolution visual tokens. This operation irreversibly destroys the fine-grained spatial coordinates (like 2D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects and dense text, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or artificial GUI navigation.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a zero-shot max-pooling match between candidate tokens and *un-pooled* visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial information. However, implementing this principle exposes mathematical and engineering vulnerabilities: the unproven assumption of modality alignment between visual features and the text unembedding matrix, the catastrophic fragmentation of continuous BPE sub-words, and hyper-parameter bloat in logits reshaping.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Based on stringent peer feedback, we have eliminated temporal video extensions to focus strictly on resolving high-resolution spatial reasoning. Our contributions are methodological and structural:
1. We formulate a mathematically simplified **Relative Resonance Penalty**, mitigating the hyper-sensitivity of logits scaling by reducing intervention to a single-parameter subtractive shift.
2. We design an **Entropy-Guided Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)**, replacing tokenizer-specific heuristic rules with a universal, dynamic mechanism to protect structural language components.
3. We define a stringent **5-stage Evaluation Protocol**, testing cross-domain modality alignment ($\Phi$), dense spatial preservation (DocVQA/TextVQA/ChartQA), and the true Time/VRAM Pareto frontier.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 The Modality Gap and Resonance Formulation

The core hypothesis of BRA is that intervention should occur in the logits space to avoid pooling high-resolution spatial tokens. Given a candidate token set $\mathcal{C}_t = \{c_1, \dots, c_K\}$ at decoding step $t$, we measure the visual support for each candidate. 

A critical vulnerability in naive logits-space intervention is the assumption of topological orthogonality—whether the last-layer visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ reside in the exact same semantic manifold as the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$. MLLMs typically map visual features into the *input* embedding space, whereas $W_{vocab}$ operates on the *output* latent space after dozens of non-linear transformer layers. 

We formalize the raw resonance score $S_{raw}(c)$ through an alignment mapping $\Phi$:

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \max_{j \in \{1 \dots N_v\}} \cos(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)}))
$$

To strictly preserve the "plug-and-play" inference-time nature of BRA, $\Phi$ is designed as an ultra-lightweight linear projector trained purely via contrastive loss on a general domain dataset (e.g., MSCOCO validation set) in under 1 hour. A central tenet of our methodology (to be proven in Protocol 0) is that $\Phi$ learns a *universal semantic mapping* rather than dataset-specific visual priors, enabling zero-shot transfer to artificial domains like Document VQA and GUI WebBench without retraining.

### 3.2 Relative Resonance Penalty (Simplified Shift)

Directly modifying logits with absolute resonance scores introduces extreme hyper-sensitivity. Previous iterations of logits intervention suffered from over-parameterization, utilizing simultaneous additive rewards and penalties. We simplify this framework based on the principle that suppressing hallucinations strictly requires penalizing ungrounded tokens, rather than artificially boosting grounded ones.

We compute the relative evidence position within the Top-K candidates:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}_{\tau}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$

The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
where $\alpha$ is the singular global penalty strength, and $\gamma(c)$ is a token-aware damping coefficient (defined in Sec 3.3). Tokens with high relative visual support ($\hat S(c) \approx 1$) receive near-zero penalty, while ungrounded tokens are linearly suppressed.

### 3.3 Dynamic Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)

Applying visual resonance blindly at the terminal token level causes semantic collapse for multi-token entities (e.g., splitting "play" and "ing") and destroys functional syntax ("therefore", "however"). Relying on hardcoded tokenizer prefixes (e.g., `Ġ` in BPE) is brittle, model-specific, and non-scalable. We upgrade SMI to an **Entropy-Guided Dynamic Weighting** mechanism.

Linguistic continuation tokens and functional syntax exhibit exceptionally low predictive entropy $H_t$ given the prior context, whereas new visual entities (nouns/verbs) exhibit higher entropy. We dynamically define the penalty weight $\gamma(c)$ as a function of the normalized candidate entropy:

$$
\gamma(c) = 1.0 - \exp(-\lambda \cdot H_t(c))
$$

1. **Visual Entities (High Entropy):** $\gamma \to 1.0$. Full resonance penalty is applied, enforcing strict visual grounding.
2. **BPE Fragments & Syntax (Low Entropy):** $\gamma \to 0.0$. Words like "ing" naturally drop to near-zero penalties ($L_{final} \approx L_{orig}$), ensuring universal, tokenizer-agnostic syntax preservation without requiring manual dictionaries.

### 3.4 Zero-Pooling Max-Match for Dense Spatial Preservation

Unlike hidden-state methods (e.g., DoLa) that compute a pooled visual vector $\bar{v} = \frac{1}{N_v} \sum h_L^{(v_j)}$ to establish an intervention direction, BRA's use of the $\max_{j}$ operator in $S_{raw}(c)$ explicitly bypasses pooling. 

By retaining the full sequence of $N_v$ unpooled tokens, BRA preserves the 2D-RoPE positional encodings embedded within each specific $h_L^{(v_j)}$. If a candidate token $c$ representing "small text" matches strongly with only a single visual patch $j$ representing a highly localized bounding box, the max-operator isolates this exact resonance, shielding dense, high-resolution spatial reasoning from the semantic blurring caused by MeanPool or PCA.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

This section outlines the rigorous, hypothesis-driven evaluation blueprint required to empirically validate BRA.

### 4.1 Protocol 0: The $\Phi$ Modality Alignment and Cross-Domain Generalization Probe (Crucial Prerequisite)
**Hypothesis:** The mapping $\Phi$ trained strictly on natural images (MSCOCO) learns a universal semantic alignment between the visual manifold and $W_{vocab}$, rather than memorizing domain-specific visual priors.
**Experiment:** 
- Train a lightweight linear $\Phi$ on MSCOCO-val ($<1$ hour, 1 GPU) to maximize cosine similarity between ground-truth text tokens and localized visual patches.
- **Evaluation:** Apply the *frozen* MSCOCO-trained $\Phi$ directly to VisualWebBench (GUI navigation) and DocVQA. 
- **Success Criteria:** A side-by-side t-SNE visualization showing that $\Phi$ correctly maps artificial domain patches (e.g., a web button or document text) to the correct semantic region in $W_{vocab}$. If cross-domain transfer holds, $\Phi$ is proven as a valid, universally applicable plug-and-play adapter rather than a fine-tuning hack.

### 4.2 Protocol 1: Static Hallucination Suppression
**Hypothesis:** BRA suppresses object hallucination in standard natural images.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: POPE, CHAIR, MME.
- Baselines: Base decoding, VCD, OPERA, DoLa.
- **Success Criteria:** BRA must achieve equivalent or superior F1 scores to DoLa and VCD while maintaining the Average Generation Length (AGL) within 5% of the Base model to prove it does not cause length collapse.

### 4.3 Protocol 2: The "Pooling Paradox" Proof via Dense Spatial Tasks
**Hypothesis:** Standard hidden-state interventions destroy 2D spatial features. BRA’s zero-pooling max-match preserves them.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: **DocVQA, ChartQA**, and VisualWebBench.
- **Mandatory Ablation Table:** Compare (1) Base Model, (2) DoLa, (3) `BRA-MeanPool` (where $S_{raw}$ uses pooled visual tokens), and (4) `BRA-ZeroPooling`.
- **Success Criteria:** While DoLa and `BRA-MeanPool` may perform well on POPE (Protocol 1), they must show significant degradation on DocVQA. `BRA-ZeroPooling` must dramatically outperform Rows 2 and 3 on DocVQA, definitively proving the Pooling Paradox.

### 4.4 Protocol 3: Entropy-Guided SMI Heatmap Validation
**Hypothesis:** SMI successfully protects continuous BPE words and functional syntax from unwarranted visual penalties.
**Experiment:**
- **Execution:** Extract a decoding trace for a complex generated sentence containing multi-token words (e.g., "play-ing") and syntax ("never-the-less").
- **Success Criteria:** A dual-layer heatmap visualization where the top layer shows Token Predictive Entropy $H_t$ and the bottom shows the calculated penalty weight $\gamma(c)$. The visualization must mathematically prove that $\gamma \to 0$ for structural fragments, allowing seamless decoding.

### 4.5 Protocol 4: The Latency/VRAM Pareto Frontier
**Hypothesis:** Operating strictly in the logits space via simple dot products is strictly Pareto-superior to multi-pass contrastive methods.
**Experiment:**
- Construct a 2D scatter plot: X-axis is Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT in ms), Y-axis is DocVQA Accuracy, and Bubble Size represents Peak VRAM overhead.
- **Success Criteria:** VCD and OPERA must sit on the far right (high latency/VRAM). BRA must occupy the top-left quadrant, delivering SOTA dense spatial accuracy with near-zero latency overhead over the Base model.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to zero-pooling logits-space resonance anchoring addresses a structural bottleneck in high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly exposing and resolving the "Pooling Paradox", BRA provides a pathway to intervene safely without destroying the fragile 2D spatial encodings established by Vision-RoPE. The evolution from heuristic tokenizer rules to Entropy-Guided SMI grounds the method in sound probability theory, enabling true tokenizer-agnostic deployment.

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Cluttered Scene Vulnerability:** While the zero-pooling $\max$ operator perfectly isolates small spatial features, extreme visual clutter (e.g., a highly disorganized room) could theoretically cause $S_{raw}$ to accidentally resonate with background noise. Future work will investigate localized attention-masking to bound the max-pooling search space.
2. **Modality Alignment Dependency:** Although Protocol 0 is designed to prove the cross-domain universality of $\Phi$, the absolute necessity of this projector means BRA is not *strictly* zero-parameter out of the box, requiring a one-time, 1-hour calibration for each new MLLM family (e.g., one adapter for Llama-3-Vision, one for Qwen-VL).

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until Protocols 0-4 are fully executed, we strictly limit our claims to the theoretical soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial bottlenecks of high-resolution MLLM interventions, offering a concrete mathematical blueprint to validate the supremacy of zero-pooling, logits-space decoding.
