# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Sparse Decode-Time Visual Entity Existence Checker

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from entity hallucination when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during autoregressive decoding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways to route fine-grained evidence. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual entity existence verifier** rather than a general hallucination solver or complex spatial reasoner. It reliably answers "Is this noun anywhere in the image?" establishing a strict theoretical boundary between global entity grounding and targeted relational VQA vulnerabilities. To resolve parameter bloat, TLRA projects strictly to an offline-filtered physical noun subset ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$), reducing the structural footprint to $\sim$18M parameters. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A semantically coherent initialization tied to the LLM's **Layer 0 input embeddings**, strictly contingent on the residual stream's Layer 0-to-Layer $N$ manifold alignment to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring trained via a hard-negative contrastive objective, featuring an **in-window Dynamic Syntax Floor** with explicit tie-breaking to prevent structural stuttering; and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) equipped with a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification**. Crucially, we reject the premise that forcing a subword pivot is inherently "graceful." Instead, we pre-register a boundary-aware evaluation protocol that explicitly audits the intervention's semantic destruction risks (the "Pivot Survival Rate"), relational hallucination exacerbation, and the true latency physics of batched GPU Trie-divergence.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), such as LLaVA-1.5 and Llama-3-Vision variants, exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to entity hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, OPERA) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding, attention-penalty heuristics, or distribution shifts. However, they do not introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways trained specifically to verify spatial presence at the token level. 

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit existence-checking supervision.

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly downgrade our scope from general "hallucination suppression" to **Entity Existence Verification**. Algorithmically, TLRA acts as a Global Visual Existence Checker for an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). By pooling patch features independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. This establishes a hard theoretical boundary: TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance queries, and more severely, it risks **exacerbating relational hallucinations**. If a prompt asks "Is the dog on the sofa?" and the image contains a dog on the floor, TLRA will validate the existence of "sofa" and may inadvertently reinforce the language prior's incorrect relational claim. We explicitly stress-test this via targeted spatial splits.

Furthermore, we confront two existential threats to late-fusion routing: **Geometric Incoherence** and **BPE Subword Collisions**. Training a projection matrix from scratch on 50k captions functionally guarantees failure on unseen tokens. TLRA initializes its sparse projection head directly from the LLM's **Layer 0 input token embeddings**, relying on the residual stream's preservation of the embedding manifold to modulate Layer $N$ outputs. 

Simultaneously, modern tokenizers shatter common words (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`). Penalizing the first subword (`_re`) destroys valid non-nouns ("return"). We introduce a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** to defer penalties to unique semantic identifiers. However, forcing an autoregressive trajectory to abandon a noun mid-word (e.g., after `_re`) is fundamentally a text-corruption risk, often leading to semantic absurdity ("The man opened the *realize*"). While a full "Lookahead and Rollback" beam-search mechanism is theoretically optimal, we audit the extreme boundary of greedy decoding by explicitly tracking the "Pivot Survival Rate" to quantify when hallucination suppression devolves into fluent nonsense.

Our evaluation is a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Semantic Absurdity:** Evaluating entity grounding (POPE/CHAIR) against zero-shot methods, while strictly auditing for Semantic Coherence via a "Pivot Survival Rate" LLM Judge to catch fluent but nonsensical subword pivots.
2. **Context-Blindness and Targeted VQA:** Explicitly mapping the exact failure boundaries of history-independent routing by splitting evaluation into Global Existence (POPE), Targeted Relevance (Adversarial VQA), and Relational Vulnerability (MMHal-Relations).
3. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general reasoning (MMBench) and geometric initialization (Layer 0 vs. Random) alongside a fully untrained `TLRA_zero` baseline, contingent on a Layer 0/N cosine alignment check.
4. **Hardware Reality and The Latency Bottleneck:** A formal evaluation of the Trie-Lookahead's computational overhead, mapping Tokens/Second throughput on GPU at Batch Size $\ge 8$ with diverse prompts to measure true warp divergence.
5. **The Dynamic Syntax Floor Audit:** Quantifying the exact trigger rates, tie-breaking mechanics, and argmax-retention outcomes when the hallucination penalty clashes with weak non-noun distributions.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Sparse Architectural Scope and Geometrically Coherent Initialization

TLRA targets standard MLLM architectures and operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage, strictly limited to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ maps cached spatial visual states to this entity subset. 

**The Sparsity Trap and Layer 0/N Manifold Alignment:** To enable zero-shot generalization to unseen tokens, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained Layer 0 input token embeddings**. The structural premise of this initialization rests on the assumption that the input embedding manifold and the output `lm_head` manifold remain sufficiently aligned through the residual stream. If the LLM's deep layers (e.g., heavily distorted by RMSNorms and Swish-GLU MLPs in certain LLaMA derivatives) destroy this alignment, zero-shot projection collapses. We mandate a preliminary **Cosine Similarity Alignment Metric** between Layer 0 and Layer $N$ for $V_{noun}$ to mathematically justify this structural premise before training.

**`TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** A strict baseline computing cosine similarity directly between frozen visual features $X_v$ and the Layer 0 text embeddings without any parametric training. This lower-bound isolates whether explicit contrastive supervision ($W_{calib}$) is necessary, or if inherent feature alignment suffices.

### 3.2 Training Formulation and Hard Negative Sampling

To calibrate $W_{calib}$ and prevent mode collapse (saturating outputs to 1.0), we employ a strict **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** ($\mathcal{L}_{calib}$). For a given image with ground-truth physical nouns $\mathcal{P}$, we dynamically sample **hard negative nouns** $\mathcal{N}$ from $V_{noun}$ using corpus co-occurrence matrices (objects frequently associated with $\mathcal{P}$ but explicitly absent from the current image). The LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{P}} \log(\hat{S}(c)) - \sum_{c \in \mathcal{N}} \log(1 - \hat{S}(c))
$$

### 3.3 The Top-$k$ "Existence Checker" and the Dynamic Syntax Floor

At decoding step $t$, TLRA computes a spatial support score by pooling the **Top-$k$** matching patches for a candidate noun token $c$ from the candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} (X_{v, j} \cdot W_{calib})_{c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
Because $S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$, TLRA functions strictly as a context-blind *Global Entity Existence Checker*. 

To apply this penalty without inducing catastrophic grammatical stuttering, we enforce a **Dynamic Syntax Floor**. Let $L_{floor} = \max_{v \in \mathcal{C}_t \setminus \mathcal{V}_{noun}} L_{orig}(v)$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the Top-$M$ window. The final logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = \max \Big( L_{floor} - \epsilon, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big)
$$
**Explicit Tie-Breaking & Argmax Retention:** We subtract a microscopic $\epsilon$ from the floor comparison. If a massive penalty drops $c$ exactly to the floor, $L_{floor}$ (the alternative syntax token) strictly wins the argmax tie, preventing stutter loops (e.g., "opened the the"). However, we explicitly acknowledge a severe mathematical limitation: if the model is 99% confident in a hallucinated noun ($L_{orig}$ is huge) and the best non-noun alternative is a low-probability comma ($L_{floor}$ is tiny), the clamped penalty may *still* leave $c$ as the argmax winner. We pre-register the "Argmax Retention Rate" to track this failure to suppress.

### 3.4 Resolving BPE Fragmentation: Subword-Trie Lookahead & The Pivot Risk

Modern tokenizers shatter words. A naive "First-Subword Penalty" introduces a fatal Prefix Collision Threat (penalizing `_re` due to the lack of a "refrigerator" simultaneously penalizes valid non-nouns like "return"). TLRA implements a **Subword-Trie Lookahead Verification** via batched tensor masking:
1. **Deferred Penalty:** If a candidate matches an ambiguous prefix node, the penalty mask $\gamma(c)$ is set to 0.
2. **Unique Resolution:** The penalty is strictly deferred until the trajectory reaches a subword that *uniquely identifies* a noun stem (e.g., `friger` conditioned on `_re`). 

**The Semantic Pivot Risk:** If the image lacks a refrigerator, TLRA drops the logit for `friger`. Under greedy decoding, this forces the LLM to execute a forward pivot (e.g., completing `_re` + `alize`). We explicitly reject the notion that this is inherently "graceful." It is a text-corruption risk. While a full "Lookahead and Rollback" mechanism (pruning the prefix from the beam) is technically sound, it incurs massive overhead. We deploy the greedy forward pivot but strictly mandate an LLM-as-a-judge **Pivot Survival Rate** audit to measure exactly how often this intervention destroys sentence-level meaning.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict linguistic, structural, and hardware boundaries. By reframing TLRA as an explicitly-trained, context-blind entity existence verifier, we identify mathematically rigid limitations that future research must confront:

1. **Relational Exacerbation and Context-Blindness:** The checker pools patches independently of text context ($Y_{<t}$). If an object physically exists in the background, TLRA validates it. This establishes a hard boundary: while TLRA excels at global entity captioning (POPE), it introduces severe vulnerabilities in targeted instruction-following and actively risks *exacerbating* relational hallucinations (validating the existence of "dog" and "sofa" even if the dog is not on the sofa).
2. **The "Graceful Pivot" is a Semantic Hazard:** While the Subword-Trie Lookahead successfully prevents catastrophic prefix collisions, forcing an autoregressive trajectory to abandon a noun mid-word is highly destructive. While it preserves low Perplexity, it frequently destroys sentence-level semantic meaning. Future systems must likely adopt computationally expensive "Lookahead and Rollback" beam adjustments rather than relying on greedy forward pivots.
3. **The Dynamic Syntax Floor Flaw (Argmax Retention):** Clamping a heavily penalized noun to the highest non-noun logit ($L_{floor}$) with an $\epsilon$ tie-breaker successfully prevents stuttering loops. However, if the model is extremely confident in the hallucination, the clamped noun logit may remain the argmax winner. The hallucination mathematically cannot be suppressed in these edge cases without breaking syntax.
4. **Hardware Latency and Warp Divergence:** Traversing a subword Trie for candidate tokens introduces branching logic. In diverse batched generation, sequences will hit ambiguous prefixes at completely different steps, causing severe GPU warp divergence that threatens Tokens/Second throughput despite the small 18M parameter footprint.

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a sparse, geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities to provide scalable visual grounding, or if the algorithmic limits of late-fusion vocabulary projection represent an insurmountable theoretical ceiling.
