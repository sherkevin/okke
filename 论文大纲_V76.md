# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding, attention control, and layer-contrast methods—are important competitive baselines, but they do not directly provide a framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual states. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window, making the method compatible with fine-grained physical grounding tasks such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via deterministic root-token lookup and BPE continuation inheritance. To keep comparisons honest, we explicitly separate a strict zero-shot probe (`TLRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated variant (`TLRA_calib`). `TLRA_calib` is a **base-model-frozen plug-in**, not a training-free method in the same sense as `VCD` or `DoLa`.

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize the paper around a strict, pre-registered experimental contract: a zero-shot semantic-validity pilot, three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction, structure and reasoning preservation, and local-evidence value, plus explicit efficiency and failure-boundary audits. The core empirical contract remains **image-centric**, specifically targeting physical entities while deliberately treating text-rich `DocVQA` as a negative control under an explicit OCR concession. Furthermore, all local-evidence claims must pass a strict **parity ablation** against both `TLRA_MeanPool` and a mandatory `TLRA_RandomK` baseline using the exact same calibrator. We retain only a **bounded secondary video pilot** as exploratory appendix evidence. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable evaluation protocol for structure-preserving decode-time visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained grounding scenarios involving physical entities—such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting—where the relevant evidence is often local rather than global. These are central multimodal settings in which visual grounding, language generation, and fine-grained perception interact directly.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate competitive baselines, but none of them directly answer the following question:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

That question is the center of this paper. We do not rely on macroscopic critiques of mainstream baselines. Instead, we advance a narrower, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided that it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards.

Executing this idea is nontrivial for four reasons. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and output logits do not naturally live in the same space. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc logits intervention can only reweight candidates that the base model already keeps alive. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression can damage function words, syntax, and long entities. Fourth, **system cost**: local support extraction can become expensive at high visual token counts.

TLRA therefore centers on three design commitments:

1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention** rather than model retraining.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** as the main local evidence extractor.
3. **VASM** as the structure-preserving protection layer via BPE inheritance.

At the same time, this framework fixes several boundaries to enforce scientific honesty. First, `TLRA_zero` is treated only as a **viability probe**. If it fails the semantic-validity pilot, the paper contracts toward `TLRA_calib`—explicitly framed as a **base-model-frozen plug-in** with a defined visual-to-lexical projector. Second, TLRA deliberately accepts an **OCR concession**: because VASM mathematically bypasses text-in-image tokens to remain structurally conservative, document/OCR-heavy tasks are strictly designated as safety-oriented negative controls, not capability claims. Our motivation is explicitly bounded to grounding *physical entities*, not rich-text environments. Third, to isolate the true value of spatial routing, we mandate that local-evidence claims pass parity ablations against both a global pool (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and a random visual subset (`TLRA_RandomK`) using the *exact same* calibrator weights.

To prevent scope drift, the primary scientific contract is **image-centric**, providing the cleanest environment for validating the token-local routing mechanism. A **secondary video pilot** is reserved strictly for the appendix to test exploratory spatio-temporal transferability, without overclaiming a full image-video unification.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary and the Calibrator Blackbox: `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib`

The first design principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. Acknowledging that visual and lexical states often do not natively align, we explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks:

1. **`TLRA_zero`**: a strict decode-time probe where the base model's native language modeling head is used directly on contextualized final-layer visual states to derive lexical support.
2. **`TLRA_calib`**: a lightweight calibrated variant where a small projection $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained to bridge the embedding asymmetry, while the base MLLM parameters remain strictly frozen.

To eliminate the "calibrator blackbox" ambiguity, we explicitly define $\Phi_{calib}$. It is implemented as a 2-layer MLP trained exclusively to map final-layer visual patch representations to the base model's lexical embedding space. It is trained via a contrastive loss on a bounded, pre-defined dataset (e.g., a 50k subset of conceptual captions containing distinct physical objects). **Crucially, $\Phi_{calib}$ only learns a static mapping between modalities; it does not update the MLLM's autoregressive pathways.** 

To avoid misleading comparisons:
- `TLRA_calib` is labeled as a **base-model-frozen plug-in**, not "training-free".
- The benchmark mandates a **Category Leakage Audit** to quantify the overlap between the objects seen in the $\Phi_{calib}$ training set and the test sets.
- Any local routing superiority claimed by `TLRA_calib` must be validated against `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` using the **exact same** frozen $\Phi_{calib}$ weights.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set derived from the original logits:
$$
\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig}),
$$
where $M \ll |V|$ is a small candidate window (e.g., $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$). 

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the contextualized visual states from the final layer, with visual token volume $N_v = H \times W$. For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes candidate-wise local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big),
$$
followed by:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$
Here, `score(v_j, c)` is the patch-level lexical support obtained via `TLRA_zero` or $\Phi_{calib}$. The temperature parameter $\tau_{sim}$ is critical: it requires empirical justification to ensure the resonance distribution remains sharp. If $\tau_{sim}$ is too high, the distribution flattens and Adaptive Top-$k$ degenerates mathematically into MeanPool.

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a logits intervention without destroying the base model's softmax temperature dynamics, TLRA computes a relative evidence score over the bounded candidate set:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
A pure absolute subtractive penalty risks pushing logits out of their active dynamic range. Therefore, TLRA applies a dynamic scaling penalty anchored to the candidate window's logit range ($\Delta_L$):
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \Delta_L \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)),
$$
where $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t) - \min(L_{orig} \in \mathcal{C}_t)$. This formulation ensures that weakly supported candidates are suppressed proportionally to the local logit scale, avoiding catastrophic shifts in the base model's probability distribution.

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

Naive token-level intervention can easily suppress useful structure together with hallucinations. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** as a deterministic protection mechanism:
- $\gamma(c) = 1$ for tokens eligible for visual grounding intervention (physical entities).
- $\gamma(c) = 0$ for structurally sensitive tokens.

VASM operates mechanically in two stages:
1. **Root-token lookup:** A precomputed lexical table identifies candidate root tokens associated with physical entities, strictly excluding function words, structural syntax, and arbitrary strings.
2. **BPE continuation inheritance:** Because modern tokenizers split entities arbitrarily, continuation subwords inherit the mask value ($\gamma$) of their preceding root token.

**The OCR Concession:** Because VASM's core design is structurally conservative, it mathematically bypasses arbitrary text-in-image strings. We therefore explicitly concede that TLRA is not an OCR/document-grounding method. Tasks like `DocVQA` are utilized strictly as **negative controls** to verify that VASM safely ignores text without degrading baseline performance. 

### 3.5 Methodological Boundaries

TLRA does not claim universal superiority. Specifically:
- It cannot recover a correct token if that token is absent from the initial Top-$M$ candidate set (out-of-candidate failure).
- It is vulnerable to suffix-collapse if BPE inheritance misaligns with linguistic boundaries.
- It does not claim an image-video unified theory; video is treated merely as a bounded secondary pilot.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because this paper is structured as a rigorous falsifiable proposal, the experimental story is organized into a sanity pilot, three mandatory evidence chains, and strict boundary audits. The following protocol dictates the criteria for claim validation or contraction.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Zero-Shot Semantic Validity Pilot

**Goal:** Determine whether `TLRA_zero` is a viable independent branch or just noise.
- **Protocol:** Measure Top-1/5/10 overlap between patch readout words and annotated object labels on a held-out image subset.
- **Decision Rule:** If overlap is near-random, the zero-shot claim is abandoned. The paper explicitly contracts to state: "Decode-time local intervention requires minimal calibration to bridge the embedding asymmetry," and `TLRA_calib` becomes the sole core method.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination (without just artificially truncating responses) and whether the gain is robust against the calibrator's inherent priors.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 1):** 
  - *Metrics:* POPE (Accuracy, F1), CHAIR (CHAIRi, CHAIRs), Average Generation Length (AGL), Intervention Coverage %.
  - *Rows:* Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Validation Criteria:** If `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` reduces AGL by >15% compared to the Base model, the hallucination reduction claim must be downgraded as confounded by response truncation. A Category Leakage Audit must also quantify performance on objects seen vs. unseen in $\Phi_{calib}$ training.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Goal:** Verify that VASM successfully protects general multimodal understanding and that the OCR concession behaves precisely as a negative control.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 2):**
  - *Metrics:* MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard), DocVQA, Exact-Match Suffix Completion %.
  - *Rows:* Base, `TLRA_full` (with VASM), `TLRA_no_VASM`.
- **Validation Criteria:** 
  1. `TLRA_no_VASM` must show a catastrophic drop in MMMU and Exact-Match Suffix Completion, proving VASM's necessity. 
  2. `DocVQA` performance must remain strictly flat between Base and `TLRA_full`, proving the negative control (VASM successfully bypassing OCR tokens).

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** Isolate and prove that *spatial locality* matters more than coarse visual aggregation or randomized visual subsets.
- **Protocol Matrix (Table 3):**
  - *Metrics:* FREAK (Spatial Relations, Object Existence, Counting splits).
  - *Rows:* Base, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK` (Mandatory), `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Validation Criteria:** All three TLRA variants must use the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$. To prove spatial routing works, `AdaptiveTopK` must statistically outperform both `MeanPool` (global prior) and `RandomK` (subset regularization prior). If it fails to separate, the local-evidence hypothesis is contracted.

### 4.5 Efficiency Pareto and Failure Audits

A mandatory **Figure 1 (Pareto Frontier)** will plot Throughput (Tokens/sec) or Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) on the X-axis against POPE F1 on the Y-axis, tracing the trajectory as the candidate window $M$ varies $\in \{10, 50, 100\}$.

Furthermore, the paper must explicitly document two specific failure modes:
1. **Out-of-Candidate Failures:** Visual examples where the correct entity token was absent from the base model's Top-$M$ logits, proving the fundamental mathematical limit of post-hoc reweighting.
2. **Suffix-Collapse Exampes:** Instances where BPE inheritance still fails due to edge-case tokenization defying logical linguistic boundaries.

### 4.6 Secondary Video Pilot

To satisfy broader multimedia relevance without contaminating the controlled image-centric claims, a bounded video pilot (e.g., `VIDHALLUC`) will be executed strictly in the appendix. Positive localization diagnostics will frame token-local intervention as "showing early spatio-temporal transferability," while negative results will cleanly bound the method to spatial (image) domains without invalidating the core paper.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is the one with the cleanest scientific contract. We do not claim to defeat all baselines unconditionally; rather, we propose a falsifiable framework establishing that decode-time intervention can leverage token-local visual evidence, provided structure is preserved through VASM. By isolating zero-shot probes from calibrated upper bounds, enforcing exact-calibrator parity ablations against global and random baselines, and treating OCR/document tasks strictly as negative controls, this benchmark prioritizes methodological honesty.

The main unresolved risks and limitations remain:
1. **Alignment Risk:** `TLRA_zero` relies heavily on native architectural alignment. If this fails, minimal calibration ($\Phi_{calib}$) becomes a hard dependency.
2. **Calibrator Leakage:** External priors injected by $\Phi_{calib}$ may confound routing gains. The mandatory `TLRA_RandomK` vs. `AdaptiveTopK` ablation is the sole line of defense against this.
3. **Out-of-Candidate Bounds:** No post-hoc reweighting can ground a token that the base model has already pruned from its Top-$M$ candidate set. 
4. **Efficiency Constraints:** The adaptive Top-$k$ mechanism introduces a Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT) overhead that scales with the visual token count ($N_v$).
5. **Modality Limits:** The mechanism is heavily optimized for spatial reasoning. Its transferability to spatio-temporal video settings remains strictly exploratory.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)

Based on the explicit evaluation protocol, this paper restricts its claims to:
- A structure-preserving masking mechanism (VASM) utilizing BPE inheritance for safe logits intervention.
- A controlled evaluation isolating token-local routing gains from global and randomized subset priors.
- A demonstration of specific failure boundaries, including out-of-candidate limitations and suffix-collapse risks.

It does **not** claim architecture-invariant zero-shot success, dense document-grounding competence, or a unified image-video capability.
