# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Bounded Hybrid Routing for MLLM Grounding via Mid-Layer Visual States

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, including visual-noise contrastive decoding (VCD), attention-penalty control (OPERA), and layer-contrast methods (DoLa), are strong competitive baselines. However, they do not directly provide a framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while strictly preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, an intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from static, mid-layer prefill visual states.

TLRA is built on three architectural corrections. First, to avoid the "local evidence illusion" where final-layer LLM states are irreversibly contaminated by global text-attention, TLRA extracts visual representations from intermediate LLM layers where spatial locality is preserved. Second, to prevent memory bandwidth starvation, the visual projection is computed exactly once during the prefill phase, caching a $\approx 32$ MB tensor for lightweight $O(1)$ retrieval. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via a pre-computed WordNet-based mask. Acknowledging the "BPE subword prefix trap"—where tokens like "car" overlap physical entities and abstract words ("cardinal")—VASM operates as a frequency-weighted statistical heuristic, actively audited across different tokenizer scales (e.g., 32k vs. 128k vocabularies). 

To enforce an unforgiving evaluation contract, we separate a zero-shot preliminary (`TLRA_zero`) from the calibrated hybrid variant (`TLRA_calib`). Because `TLRA_calib` maps mid-layer visual states to the dynamic lexical space, its evaluation strictly mandates parameter-matched parity against: (1) a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the identical cross-entropy alignment objective, proving the gain comes from decode-time routing rather than training data; and (2) a trivial continuous additive-fusion baseline, proving the necessity of discrete logit-space routing over simple hidden-state addition. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable protocol for structure-preserving visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model frequently generates text that is fluent but decoupled from localized visual evidence. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained physical grounding scenarios, such as spatial positioning and small-object recognition.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) inject visual noise to compare distributions; attention-based control (`OPERA`) penalizes pathological autoregressive patterns; layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep language representations. As these methods primarily rely on language-side distribution shifts or global visual perturbations, they leave a complementary question unanswered:

**How can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence into logits adjustment without conflating routing gains with training-objective superiority, and without falling victim to the globalization of final-layer LLM representations?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a hybrid framework scoring candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than global visual pooling or trivial continuous fusion, provided it extracts truly local states and incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards.

Executing this intervention requires overcoming four specific architectural barriers:
1. **The Local Evidence Illusion:** In decoder-only MLLMs, visual tokens processed during prefill undergo deep bidirectional self-attention. By the final layer, they are heavily contextualized and text-contaminated global states. TLRA solves this by extracting visual states from intermediate layers (e.g., $L/2$), preserving spatial locality.
2. **Manifold Mismatch:** Static mid-layer visual states and dynamic pre-LM-head lexical states do not natively share an embedding space without a trained bridging projection.
3. **Computational Throttling:** Computing dynamic visual similarity at every generation step starves memory bandwidth. We solve this by computing the projection exactly once during prefill, caching a lightweight $\approx 32$ MB tensor for $O(1)$ retrieval.
4. **The BPE Subword Prefix Trap:** Naive intervention destroys syntax, exacerbated by subword bleeding where tokens (e.g., "car") are shared across physical entities ("automobile") and abstract language ("cardinal"). We introduce a frequency-weighted statistical heuristic to manage this, explicitly acknowledging its brittleness across different tokenizer scales.

To address these issues, TLRA is built upon strict scientific commitments. We enforce a mandatory baseline: a LoRA model trained on the exact same dense region-caption data using the exact same alignment objective as the TLRA projector. We also enforce parity against a trivial "continuous additive fusion" baseline to justify the complexity of discrete logit routing. If `TLRA_calib` fails to outperform these objective-matched controls, its routing claim is mathematically falsified. Furthermore, we accept explicit OCR and Text-Only negative controls to transparently audit the collateral damage of our masking heuristics across modern tokenizers.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: Isolating the Objective in `TLRA_calib`

We separate TLRA into two tracks to isolate architectural viability from calibration gains:

1. **`TLRA_zero` (Architectural Preliminary)**: A strict zero-shot probe applying the LM head directly to prefill visual states. Due to severe embedding asymmetry, this anticipates near-random performance, confirming the absolute necessity of a bridging projection.
2. **`TLRA_calib` (Main Intervention)**: A hybrid projection-routing variant where a lightweight MLP projector $\Phi_{calib}$ maps static, mid-layer visual states into the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical space. 

**Objective Matching for Fair Evaluation:**
Confounding the routing mechanism with the training objective is a fatal flaw in hybrid interventions. To ensure mathematical fairness, $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained using a standard localized Next-Token Prediction objective over a dense region-caption subset. Consequently, any claim about decode-time local routing must survive a mandatory parity ablation against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset **using identical Cross-Entropy**, as well as a trivial continuous fusion baseline.

### 3.2 Bridging the Manifold Mismatch: Mid-Layer Extraction and $O(1)$ Caching

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, globally locked at $M=50$. Limiting routing to Top-$M$ mathematically bounds the intervention, purposefully acknowledging the "Hijacking Problem" where overwhelming language priors might push the correct visual entity beyond the candidate window.

**The Local Evidence Illusion and Mid-Layer Extraction:**
A common misconception is treating final-layer LLM hidden states as "visual." By the final layer, visual tokens have undergone dense self-attention with the text prompt, becoming text-contaminated global mixtures. To extract genuine token-local evidence, let $h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}$ denote the static hidden states at the visual token positions ($N_v \approx 4000$) extracted from an **intermediate LLM layer** (e.g., $L_{mid} = L/2$).

**Hardware Lifecycle Protection:**
$\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is executed **exactly once** at the conclusion of the prefill stage. The $N_v \times D$ projected tensor ($\approx 32.7$ MB in FP16) is stored in SRAM. During the autoregressive loop, raw local support for $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ is computed via a single batched dot-product:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}), c)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw support into a relative evidence score: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)$. To prevent overriding confident structural tokens, we anchor the penalty to the local logit range $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)) - \min(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t))$. Bounded by hyperparameter $\beta$, the adjustment is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L, \beta) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 The BPE Subword Prefix Trap and Frequency-Weighted VASM

To preserve syntax without inline parsing delays, TLRA uses a pre-computed Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Mask (VASM), $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$. 

**The Subword Prefix Trap:** Short physical entities serve as subwords for abstract concepts (e.g., `[car]` in `[car, dinal]`). A strict conservative mask causes catastrophic physical-entity recall collapse. We employ **Frequency-Weighted Subword Resolution**: if a BPE subword maps to a groundable root entity in $\ge \gamma$ (e.g., 85%) of its occurrences in a C4 corpus, it retains $M_{VASM}=1$. 

**Acknowledging Tokenizer Brittleness:** 
This statistical heuristic dictates *which* semantic direction is sacrificed. It is highly sensitive to the specific BPE merges of the base model (e.g., a 32k vocabulary vs. a 128k vocabulary). We accept this collateral damage on non-physical text and formally audit it across different tokenizer scales using pure-text negative controls.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around three evidence chains and strict boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Architectural Preliminary (`TLRA_zero`)

**Goal:** Mathematically verify the embedding asymmetry between static visual states and dynamic pre-LM-head representations.
- **Protocol:** Direct dot-product zero-shot routing on a held-out subset.
- **Expected Outcome:** Near-random performance, transitioning the evaluation to `TLRA_calib`.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Objective Fairness

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, verifying gains derive from discrete decode-time routing, not the calibration data or continuous hidden-state drift.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Mandatory Baselines:** Base, `VCD`, `MM-DoLa`.
- **Matched Objective Baseline 1:** `Base + LoRA` (trained on identical data/cross-entropy objective).
- **Matched Objective Baseline 2 ("Dumb" Fusion):** `TLRA_ContinuousAdd`, where $\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is attention-weighted and continuously added to the final hidden state before the LM head ($h_{t, final} + \sum \alpha_j \Phi(v_j)$) without VASM or discrete Top-$M$ routing. 
- **Hard Rule:** TLRA must outperform both matched baselines to justify the complexity of Top-$M$ logit-space routing.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure Preservation and Tokenizer Audit

**Goal:** Measure general capability preservation and explicitly quantify the collateral damage of VASM across different BPE structures.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench`, `MME`, `MMMU`.
- **Cross-Tokenizer Collateral Damage Audit:** Evaluated on two distinct MLLM families (e.g., LLaVA/32k vocab and LLaMA-3 based/128k vocab). 
  1. `DocVQA`: Arbitrary text strings receive zero mask coverage.
  2. `GSM8K`: Measures degradation of abstract reasoning.
- **Ablation Rows:** Base, `TLRA_full`, `TLRA_no_VASM`.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence and Representation Depth Ablation

**Goal:** Prove that mid-layer visual states retain significantly more token-local grounding than text-contaminated final layers.
- **Benchmark:** `FREAK` (Spatial Relations, Counting).
- **Depth Ablation:** Extracting $h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}$ from:
  1. Vision Encoder output / LLM Embedding Layer (Early)
  2. LLM Layer $L/2$ (Mid - Proposed)
  3. LLM Final Layer (Late - The "Illusion" baseline)
- **Routing Parity:** `TLRA_MeanPool` vs `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.

### 4.5 Efficiency and Failure-Boundary Audits

**Goal:** Document computational costs and topological failure modes.
- **Hardware Metrics:** Prefill Time (ms) and Decode Time (ms/token).
- **Failure Analysis 1 (The Hijacking Problem):** A histogram tracking the frequency at which the ground-truth token falls outside the Top-$M$ candidate window, mathematically bounding TLRA's upper intervention limit.
- **Failure Analysis 2 ($\Delta_L$ Restraint):** A scatter plot of $\Delta_L$ (logit spread) vs. visual penalty applied, empirically proving the bounded penalty prevents the model from overriding confident structural tokens.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is the one with the cleanest, most unforgiving scientific contract. It explicitly bounds its claims against an objective-matched `Base + LoRA` baseline and a trivial `Continuous Additive Fusion` baseline to prevent conflating routing gains with alignment superiority or continuous state drift. It frames its computational footprint precisely, computing the visual projection once during prefill. Crucially, it abandons the "local evidence illusion" of final-layer representations, shifting extraction to intermediate layers to preserve spatial locality before deep text-contamination occurs.

The primary unresolved risks and limitations are:
1. **The Representation Depth Hypothesis:** The claim that mid-layer visual states optimally balance spatial locality and semantic density remains to be fully verified in the planned depth ablation. If intermediate layers lack sufficient semantic alignment, the projection overhead may outweigh the localization benefits.
2. **Tokenizer Brittleness and the Subword Prefix Trap:** VASM is a brittle statistical heuristic. By frequency-weighting BPE subwords to prevent visual trigger starvation, TLRA actively accepts collateral damage to abstract language. As our planned Cross-Tokenizer Audit intends to show, this heuristic is highly sensitive to the specific vocabulary scale (e.g., 32k vs. 128k) and BPE merge rules of the base model.
3. **The Top-$M$ Ceiling (The Hijacking Problem):** By limiting routing to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ to guarantee $O(1)$ efficiency, TLRA is mathematically bounded. If the base LLM's language prior is so overwhelmingly wrong that the correct visual entity is completely expelled from the candidate window (e.g., rank 51), TLRA cannot recover it. It merely shuffles the remaining incorrect candidates, representing a hard ceiling for discrete inference-time interventions.
