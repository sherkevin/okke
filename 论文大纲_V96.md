# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Static Late-Fusion Visual Logit Bias for MLLM Object Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower localized visual evidence. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies operate globally via distribution shifts. In contrast, we present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a trained static, late-fusion visual logit bias. By projecting intermediate visual states directly into the LM's static vocabulary space, TLRA routes spatially local—yet explicitly context-agnostic—visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment.

To execute this intervention honestly, TLRA isolates its routing mechanism through three strict architectural boundaries. First, to bypass the text-contaminated global mixtures of final-layer visual tokens, TLRA extracts representations from intermediate LLM layers. Second, we map these states to the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical space using a lightweight projector. We prove the necessity of this discrete, late-fusion logit routing by strictly auditing it against a mathematically rigorous, zero-initialized intermediate gated cross-attention baseline, preventing the "continuous fusion strawman." Third, because this mapping constitutes an inherently context-blind "Bag-of-Words" noun booster, it guarantees severe interference with abstract grammatical syntax. To prevent catastrophic grammar collapse, we bound the intervention via a Top-$M$ candidate window and apply Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM).

We construct a highly unforgiving evaluation contract to test this fundamentally limited mechanism. Any claim about TLRA's routing superiority must survive a mandatory parameter-and-data-matched parity against a `Base + LoRA` model, evaluating across three random seeds to account for high variance. Furthermore, we explicitly audit the method's structural context-blindness by testing not just on object-existence (POPE, CHAIR), but on relational tasks (MMHal) and a novel "Distractor Prompt Test" (negative constraints), where static noun-boosting is theoretically expected to fail. We expose the true wall-clock latency and VRAM overhead of our Top-$K$ routing operations, mandate a `TLRA w/o VASM` ablation to document manifold bleeding, and acknowledge the multilingual breakdown induced by our hardcoded BPE mask. The resulting paper establishes a positive, highly falsifiable protocol for bounded, late-fusion visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding remains vulnerable to hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies—such as Contrastive decoding (`VCD`), Classifier-Free Guidance (`CFG`), and layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`)—are training-free, plug-and-play techniques relying on zero-shot distribution shifts. They leave a complementary architectural question unanswered: how can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence directly into logits adjustment using a trained projection, without conflating the routing gains with training-objective superiority?

This paper introduces Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA). Stripped of unnecessary jargon, TLRA is fundamentally a **static, late-fusion visual logit bias**. It advances a narrow, falsifiable hypothesis: directly scoring candidate tokens using spatially localized visual support can reduce object hallucination more faithfully than zero-shot global perturbations or continuous feature integration, provided the method extracts truly local states and explicitly bounds its own grammatical interference.

However, we explicitly define and bound the identity of TLRA: because it bypasses the LLM's autoregressive contextualization, it operates as an image-to-bag-of-words projector glued to the end of an LLM. It measures the static affinity between a visual patch and a word, structurally blind to the dynamic grammatical prefix. Executing this mechanism honestly requires overcoming specific architectural barriers and openly evaluating against the resulting blind spots:

1. **The Local Evidence Illusion:** Final-layer visual tokens are heavily contextualized, text-contaminated global mixtures. TLRA extracts visual states from intermediate layers, systematically profiling optimal layer depth across backbones.
2. **Manifold Mismatch & The Continuous Integration Baseline:** Static mid-layer visual states and dynamic pre-LM-head lexical states do not natively share an embedding space. We train a lightweight projector ($\Phi_{calib}$) to bridge this. To prove discrete logit routing is superior to continuous integration, we defeat a rigorous, zero-initialized gated cross-attention baseline injected at an intermediate layer, moving past weak final-layer addition strawmen.
3. **Hardware Latency, VRAM, and The Top-M Ceiling:** Retrieving local support during generation requires caching dense visual states and computing $O(M \cdot N_v)$ dot products dynamically at every autoregressive step. We bound this with a Top-$M$ window ($M=50$). We explicitly report the PyTorch kernel launch overhead and VRAM footprint. Furthermore, this bounded window introduces a hard mathematical ceiling (the "Hijacking Problem"): if the base LLM hallucination expels the ground truth token outside the Top-50, TLRA is structurally blind to it. 
4. **Context-Blindness and The VASM Fatal Heuristic:** Because $\Phi_{calib}$ is context-agnostic, it will indiscriminately boost matching vocabulary tokens regardless of relational constraints. To prevent catastrophic grammar collapse, we introduce Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)—a static, English-derived statistical dictionary. We acknowledge VASM is a severe structural bottleneck and a brittle heuristic that fails on multilingual prompts. We formally audit this bottleneck by ablating `TLRA w/o VASM` to expose raw manifold bleeding, and evaluating on relational tasks (MMHal) and "Distractor Prompts" where context-blind noun boosting inherently fails.

TLRA is a *trained* method. We therefore enforce a mandatory parity baseline: a LoRA model trained on the exact same dense region-caption data using the exact same alignment objective. If TLRA fails to outperform this objective-matched control, its routing claim is falsified.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: Isolating the Objective from the Architecture

TLRA utilizes a lightweight MLP projector $\Phi_{calib}$ to map static, mid-layer visual states into the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical space. During training, the base LLM and Vision Encoder are **strictly frozen**; only $\Phi_{calib}$ is updated using a localized Next-Token Prediction objective over a dense region-caption subset.

**Objective Matching for Fair Evaluation:**
Confounding the routing architecture with the fine-tuning data is a pervasive flaw in multimodal literature. Any claim about decode-time local routing must survive a mandatory parity ablation against an objective-matched `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset using identical Cross-Entropy. 

**Defeating the Continuous Integration Baseline:**
To justify discrete late-fusion logit routing, we must prove it outperforms continuous feature integration. Injecting raw visual vectors into the *final* syntax-heavy hidden state is mathematically destined to fail (a strawman). Instead, we enforce parity against `Continuous_Gated_XAttn`: a baseline that injects $\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ via a single, zero-initialized cross-attention layer inserted into the mid-to-late LLM blocks. We initialize the gating scalar $\gamma = 0$ to ensure pre-training preservation, but verify $\nabla \gamma \neq 0$ during training. If TLRA outperforms this fair continuous baseline on pure Textual Perplexity (WikiText PPL), we validate that bypassing the pre-trained autoregressive manifold shift is an architectural necessity for static visual states.

### 3.2 Bridging the Mismatch: Static Prefill and Context-Agnostic Scoring

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, locked at $M=50$ to constrain compute overhead.

**The Context-Agnostic Score Function:**
$\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is executed **exactly once** during prefill, caching an $N_v \times D$ projected tensor (requiring $\approx 32$MB VRAM per image in FP16). During autoregression, raw local support for a candidate token $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ is computed via:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}) \cdot W_{head}[c]}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
Crucially, $\tau_{sim}$ is a globally fixed scalar ($\tau_{sim} = \sqrt{D_{vocab}}$) during both training and inference; it does *not* require per-image tuning, ensuring zero-shot generalization. $W_{head}[c]$ represents the static final LM unembedding weight. Because this dot product relies entirely on static prefill visual states and static vocabulary embeddings, it is fundamentally **context-agnostic**. It measures the isolated affinity between a visual patch and a standalone word, completely blind to the dynamic grammatical prefix.

### 3.3 Temperature-Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw support into a relative evidence score: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)$. To prevent the visual head from overriding confident structural tokens, we anchor the penalty to the local logit spread $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)) - \min(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t))$. 

Because $\Delta_L$ is sensitive to generation temperature $T$, standard bounding ($\Delta_L / T$) explodes during greedy decoding ($T \to 0$). We stabilize this by clamping the temperature divisor: $\max(T, 1.0)$. During deterministic algorithms like Beam Search where temperature semantics are absent, the divisor defaults to $1.0$. The overall intervention is bounded by hyperparameter $\beta$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) + \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \min\left(\frac{\Delta_L}{\max(T, 1.0)}, \beta\right) \cdot \hat S(c)
$$

### 3.4 The VASM Fatal Heuristic and Multilingual Breakdown

Because $\Phi_{calib}$ maps to static embeddings without contextual awareness, continuous manifold bleeding inevitably causes it to spike on functional subwords. To protect syntax, TLRA applies Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM), a static $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$ mask derived from frequency-weighted BPE resolution in an English C4 corpus.

We explicitly define VASM not as a clever constraint, but as a **fatal heuristic**. A multimodal language model that relies on an English-derived static $\{0,1\}$ BPE mask to prevent catastrophic grammar collapse is fundamentally brittle. This approach mathematically guarantees breakdown in multilingual prompts or highly morphologically rich languages. We formally audit this structural flaw by mandating a `TLRA w/o VASM` ablation to expose the raw manifold bleeding, and we document the linguistic boundaries of the method.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around three evidence chains and strict boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper, deliberately designed to expose both the object-grounding strengths and the architectural blindness of the method. Due to the high variance of logit-adjustment methods, all trained baselines (`Base + LoRA`, `TLRA`) are evaluated across 3 random seeds, with standard deviations reported.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Continuous Integration Audit & VASM Ablation

**Goal:** Verify discrete logit-space routing outperforms continuous feature integration, while exposing the severe necessity of the VASM heuristic.
- **Protocol:** Compare TLRA against `Continuous_Gated_XAttn` (where visual states are injected into intermediate LLM blocks) and `TLRA w/o VASM`. Evaluate on standard Textual Perplexity (WikiText PPL) and a small-scale Non-English prompt set.
- **Hypothesis:** `Continuous_Gated_XAttn` will disrupt language modeling compared to discrete routing. However, `TLRA w/o VASM` will exhibit catastrophic PPL explosion, proving $\Phi_{calib}$ only learns to spike uncontextualized nouns. Furthermore, TLRA will exhibit a failure to boost target nouns (or severe grammar degradation) when prompts are shifted to non-English languages not covered by VASM.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Grounding vs. Relational and Negative Constraints

**Goal:** Test hallucination reduction while explicitly testing the context-agnostic "Bag-of-Words" limitation.
- **Benchmarks:** 
  - *Object Existence:* `POPE`, `CHAIR`. (Hypothesis: TLRA significantly reduces hallucination by indiscriminately boosting visible nouns).
  - *Relational/Spatial:* `MMHal` or `GQA` spatial subset. (Hypothesis: TLRA shows marginal or degraded performance as static noun-boosting cannot resolve spatial constraints).
  - ***The Distractor Prompt Test (Negative Constraints):*** Pass an image with two prominent objects (e.g., Dog and Cat). Prompt: *"Describe the Dog in detail. Do not mention the Cat."* (Hypothesis: Because TLRA computes static affinity, it will indiscriminately boost "Cat" logits, forcing the LLM to hallucinate the cat into the text despite negative instructions).
- **Hard Rule:** TLRA must significantly outperform the objective-matched `Base + LoRA` on existence benchmarks (POPE/CHAIR). The failure rates on MMHal and the Distractor Prompt Test will be honestly reported as the method's theoretical boundary.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: The Hijacking Problem and Ceilings

**Goal:** Transparently document the mathematical limits of the routing mechanism.
- **Figure B (The Hijacking CDF):** A Cumulative Distribution Function plot where X = Logit Rank of the Ground Truth Token in the Base model during CHAIR hallucinations. A hard vertical line at X=50 demonstrates TLRA's maximum mathematical salvage rate.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Hardware Latency and VRAM Overhead

**Goal:** Document computational overhead and prove whether $M=50$ is a viable threshold. Computing $O(M \cdot N_v)$ dot products dynamically at every autoregressive step in native PyTorch causes significant GPU idle time due to kernel launch overhead.
- **Table 2 (Latency, VRAM, & Top-M):** Evaluate Decode Time (ms/tok), VRAM overhead (MB/image), and POPE F1 simultaneously across $M \in \{10, 50, 100, \text{all}\}$. Decode time explicitly reports PyTorch native execution vs. theoretical/custom fused Triton kernel estimates.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA explicitly bounds its claims. By defining itself as a *static late-fusion visual logit bias*, it isolates its routing gains against a rigorous, objective-matched `Base + LoRA` control and a continuous cross-attention baseline. However, its architectural design reveals profound structural limits for multi-modal grounding.

The primary unresolved risks and limitations are:
1. **The Context-Agnostic "Bag-of-Words" Limitation:** Because TLRA computes dot products between static prefill visual states and static LM-head vocabulary embeddings, it is structurally context-blind. As proven by our "Distractor Prompt Test" and MMHal results, while highly effective for object-existence (POPE, CHAIR), it systematically fails to respect negative text constraints (e.g., "Do not mention the cat") or dynamic spatial relational phrasing.
2. **The VASM Fatal Heuristic and Multilingual Breakdown:** A perfectly calibrated objective should teach $\Phi_{calib}$ to ignore syntax natively. In reality, continuous manifold bleeding necessitates VASM. As proven by our `TLRA w/o VASM` ablation, without this English C4-derived $\{0,1\}$ dictionary, grammar collapses. This hardcoded structural crutch guarantees failure in multilingual or highly nuanced subword contexts.
3. **The Top-M Ceiling (The Hijacking Problem):** Bounded routing introduces a hard mathematical ceiling. If the base LLM's strong language prior completely expels the correct visual entity from the candidate window (Rank $> 50$), TLRA cannot recover it. 
4. **PyTorch Overhead and VRAM Footprint:** Caching $\sim 4000$ projected visual tokens requires $\approx 32$MB per image in FP16. More critically, computing dynamic dot products over a Top-$M$ subset at every autoregressive step using native PyTorch introduces massive GPU idle time due to CUDA kernel launch overhead, requiring custom fused Triton kernels for production viability.
