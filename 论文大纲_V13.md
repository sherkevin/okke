# Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring: Escaping the Pooling Paradox in High-Resolution Image and Video MLLMs

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual or temporal evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate thousands of high-resolution spatial or temporal tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D/3D positional coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., Document VQA) and specific frame localization (e.g., Video Action). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space via localized visual matching. 

To resolve the compute and memory-bandwidth bottlenecks inherent in zero-pooling logits interventions, BRA introduces three systemic upgrades: (1) **Dual-Track Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)**, establishing a strict zero-shot identity mapping ($\text{BRA}_{zero}$) for fair baseline comparisons, alongside an optional linear projector ($\text{BRA}_{calib}$) trained offline via Visual Genome bounding boxes; (2) **Bounded Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, isolating fine-grained $T \times H \times W$ features using dual $k_{min}/k_{max}$ bounds, while mathematically managing the severe $O(N_v)$ VRAM *read* bandwidth bottleneck via fused Triton kernels and pre-fill contiguous caching; and (3) **Stateful Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, replacing brittle predictive entropy with an offline syntactic mask equipped with strict BPE continuation rules. We establish a rigorous 5-tier "Defense Line" experimental protocol—spanning absolute A100 latency profiling, dense OCR heatmaps, temporal frame-hit rates, and BPE fragmentation tracking—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy of BRA against both image and video-adapted baselines.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model produces hallucinations—generating text that is linguistically fluent but physically or temporally absent from the multimedia input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on multi-pass contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD). While effective, these methods introduce severe latency overheads and VRAM bloat, rendering them computationally hostile to long-context generation. The second route employs hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution images or long videos, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool, MaxPool, or PCA) thousands of visual tokens. This irreversible aggregation destroys the fine-grained spatial-temporal coordinates that the model relies on to ground microscopic dense text or fleeting temporal actions, leading to catastrophic failures in high-resolution visual question answering or specific video frame localization.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a localized match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial and temporal information. However, escaping the pooling paradox inherently introduces severe systemic trade-offs: comparing vast spatio-temporal volumes ($N_v \to 50,000$) against the vocabulary at every step not only explodes FLOPs but hits a massive memory bandwidth wall (reading $\approx 800$ MB/step from global VRAM). Furthermore, it exposes the sub-word fragmentation vulnerability where multi-token visual entities escape penalization.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA, bounded by strict memory bandwidth controls and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Our methodology features:
1. **Dual-Track Calibration**: To ensure fair structural comparisons against training-free baselines, we explicitly define $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ (pure zero-shot identity mapping) alongside $\text{BRA}_{calib}$ (an offline-trained localized projector).
2. **Bandwidth-Aware Adaptive Resonance**: Transitioning from dangerous absolute max-pooling to a dynamic Top-$k$ formulation bounded by both $k_{min}$ and $k_{max}$ over a sharply truncated candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$, optimized via custom fused CUDA/Triton kernels to bypass the $O(N_v)$ memory read bottleneck.
3. **Stateful Probabilistic VASM**: Replacing flawed predictive entropy with an offline, polysemy-aware masking mechanism structurally upgraded with a BPE inheritance heuristic to protect multi-token entities across diverse tokenizers.
4. **The 5-Line Defense Protocol**: Expanding the experimental blueprint into a strict five-stage defense, explicitly tracking absolute latency (ms/token), Average Generated Length (AGL) to prevent `<EOS>` hacking, and Temporal Histogram visualizations to prove continuous Image+Video viability.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Bounded Candidate Filtering & Offline Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)

Comparing $N_v$ visual tokens against the entire vocabulary $|V|$ at every decoding step $t$ results in an intractable $O(|V| \times N_v \times d)$ complexity and catastrophic memory read overhead. To resolve this, we define a sharply bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=50$). We utilize the post-LLM contextualized hidden states corresponding to the visual token positions, denoted as $h_L^{(v_j)}$. To compute resonance with the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$, we formalize an alignment mapping $\Phi$:

1. **Zero-Shot BRA ($\text{BRA}_{zero}$)**: $\Phi$ is the identity function. This strict zero-shot formulation relies purely on the LLM's intrinsic representation alignment and serves as the primary fair baseline against methods like DoLa and VCD.
2. **Calibrated BRA ($\text{BRA}_{calib}$)**: A lightweight single-layer linear projector trained entirely offline to bridge disjointed visual and textual spaces (e.g., in early Qwen-VL variants). To prevent contextual bleed, we extract visual states ($h_{bbox}$) using strict RoIAlign cropping over ground-truth bounding boxes from the Visual Genome (VG) dataset, ensuring the background is fully masked. Let $c^+$ be the ground-truth text entity, and $\{c^-\}$ be *hard negatives* (co-occurring but incorrect objects):
$$
\mathcal{L}_{calib} = -\log \frac{\exp\left(\cos(W_{vocab}[c^+], \Phi_{calib}(h_{bbox})) / \tau\right)}{\sum_{c \in \{c^+\} \cup \{c^-\}} \exp\left(\cos(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi_{calib}(h_{bbox})) / \tau\right)}
$$
*Note on Temporal Generalization:* While $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained on 2D spatial data, it explicitly aligns *object semantics* rather than temporal dynamics. Therefore, it generalizes seamlessly to isolating objects within specific video frames (localization/grounding), though it inherently cannot reason about causal temporal motion.

### 3.2 Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance & Overcoming the Memory Bandwidth Wall

For continuous resolution models and videos, $N_v = T \times H \times W$ fluctuates drastically. Relying on global mean-pooling destroys spatial-temporal localization. We formulate **Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, defining an adaptive $k$ bounded by both a lower threshold (to avoid noise) and an upper threshold (to prevent re-introducing the pooling paradox in ultra-long videos):
$$
k = \min\Big(k_{max}, \max\big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot (T \times H \times W) \rceil\big)\Big)
$$
where $\rho$ is a small fractional threshold (e.g., $0.005$). The raw resonance score for a candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ is:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{\cos\left(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)})\right)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
**Overcoming the $O(N_v)$ Memory Read Bottleneck:** 
While the compute overhead is $\approx 10$ GFLOPs/step, the true hardware limit is the memory bandwidth wall: reading $N_v \approx 50,000$ tokens of dimension $d=4096$ at every autoregressive step requires streaming $\approx 800$ MB from global VRAM, which would cripple generation speed even on an A100. 

BRA resolves this via **Pre-fill Contiguous Caching and Fused Kernels**. Inspired by PagedAttention, $h_L^{(v_j)}$ are extracted during the pre-fill stage and cloned into a strictly contiguous C++ tensor block. During decoding, the Top-$k$ resonance is computed via a custom fused Triton kernel that performs batched GEMV and Top-$k$ reduction within SRAM, avoiding intermediate global memory roundtrips. This decouples the theoretical complexity from systemic memory read bottlenecks.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty 

We map raw resonance scores into stable logits modifications by computing the relative evidence position within the Top-$M$ candidates via a Softmax operation, bounding the score $\hat S(c) \in [0,1]$:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_t)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 Escaping the Entropy Trap: Stateful Probabilistic VASM

Previous iterations used predictive entropy to scale penalties, creating an **"Entropy Trap"**: strong language priors inherently produce low-entropy predictions for hallucinated objects. We guarantee mathematical stability via **Stateful Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**. 

We pre-compute a continuous syntactic weight based on POS probability distributions: $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = \sum P(pos \mid c) \cdot \gamma_{pos}$. However, naive token-level masking introduces a **Sub-word Fragmentation Vulnerability**: if a tokenizer fractures a visual entity (e.g., "refrigerator" $\to$ "re", "friger", "ator"), the fragments lack corpus POS tags and default to $0.0$, escaping the penalty.

VASM solves this via a **BPE Continuation Heuristic**:
If a token $c_t$ is morphologically identified as a sub-word continuation (e.g., lacking the `_` (U+2581) prefix in SentencePiece, or matching continuation byte-patterns in Tiktoken), it inherits the expected semantic weight of its root word:
$$
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_t)] = \begin{cases} 
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c_{t-1})], & \text{if } c_t \text{ is a BPE continuation} \\ 
\sum P(pos \mid c_t) \cdot \gamma_{pos}, & \text{otherwise} 
\end{cases}
$$
This stateful tracking structurally protects functional syntax while ensuring long, multi-token visual entities are rigorously penalized if unsupported by visual resonance.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

To definitively validate the structural advantages of BRA—and explicitly address the computational and fairness assumptions inherent in logits-space interventions—we define a rigorous, hypothesis-driven Evaluation Protocol. All planned experiments will be conducted across distinct model families (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT, Qwen-VL), isolating $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ vs. $\text{BRA}_{calib}$.

### 4.1 Preliminary Ablations & Systems Complexity Profiling
**Objective:** Prove memory bandwidth optimization claims, isolate the candidate set parameter ($M$), and establish absolute latency bounds.
**Protocol:**
- **Absolute Latency Profiling:** Report exact Time Per Output Token (ms/token) and End-to-End Generation Time (sec) on a single A100 80G for $N_v \in \{1k, 10k, 50k\}$.
- **Candidate Set Ablation:** Sweep $M \in \{10, 50, 100, 500\}$ to map the Pareto frontier between hallucination interception and compute overhead.
- **Hypothesis:** Despite the massive $O(N_v)$ memory read requirement, BRA's fused Triton kernels and contiguous caching will yield a manageable absolute TPOT (e.g., $<50$ ms/token at $N_v=50k$) that scales sub-linearly relative to naive PyTorch implementations, maintaining superiority over multi-pass methods (VCD).

### 4.2 Defense Line 1: Core Hallucination Suppression & BPE Tracking
**Objective:** Prove $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ structurally mitigates object hallucinations without relying on offline data, `<EOS>` hacking, or sub-word fragmentation.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** POPE, CHAIR (MSCOCO).
- **Baselines:** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa. MUST include both $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ and $\text{BRA}_{calib}$.
- **Key Metric Requirements:** F1 (POPE), CHAIR$_S$, CHAIR$_I$, strictly **Average Generated Length (AGL)**, and **% of BPE Continuations Penalized**.
- **Hypothesis:** $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ will independently outperform DoLa and VCD while maintaining an AGL within 5% of the Base model (refuting `<EOS>` hacking). The BPE tracking metric will prove that standard methods miss up to 30% of fragmented hallucinated entities, which VASM successfully intercepts.

### 4.3 Defense Line 2: General Intelligence & Language Prior Integrity
**Objective:** Ensure visual logit penalization does not lobotomize instructional compliance.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME.
- **Reporting:** Total score, explicitly isolating "Perception" vs. "Cognition/Reasoning".
- **Hypothesis:** Thanks to VASM shielding functional syntax ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma] \approx 0$), pure $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ will improve Perception without degrading Cognition, bypassing the catastrophic reasoning degradation typical of contrastive decoding.

### 4.4 Defense Line 3: "Zero-Pooling" Supremacy on Dense Spatial Tasks
**Objective:** Qualitatively and quantitatively expose the "Pooling Paradox" on high-resolution targets.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR / Position subset) and DocVQA.
- **Spatial Heatmap Mandate:** Render a side-by-side visualization on a dense receipt/document: (1) Original dense image, (2) DoLa MeanPool spatial activations (expected: blurry global blob), and (3) BRA Top-$k$ activations.
- **Hypothesis:** BRA's Adaptive Top-$k$ will dynamically produce sharp, isolated activation points precisely over the microscopic text patches being decoded, visually proving that mean-pooling destroys fine-grained coordinates.

### 4.5 Defense Line 4: Spatio-Temporal Grounding in Video Narrative
**Objective:** Validate that Adaptive Top-$k$ scales across $T \times H \times W$ for exact frame localization, while acknowledging causal limitations.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** VIDHALLUC and Video-MME (Localization subsets).
- **Baselines:** Must include explicitly adapted temporal variants (e.g., Temporal-DoLa utilizing temporal mean-pooling).
- **Temporal Histogram Mandate:** Plot Top-$k$ Activation Count (Y) against Video Frames (X). Overlay Temporal-DoLa's activation curve on the same plot.
- **Hypothesis:** Temporal-DoLa will display a flat, uniform activation curve due to temporal averaging. BRA's Top-$k$ tokens will autonomously spike into a sharp Gaussian peak strictly within the ground-truth temporal window, yielding a $>85\%$ Frame Hit Rate. *(Note: This evaluates localization/grounding, not sequential motion logic).*

### 4.6 Defense Line 5: VASM Ablation & Boundary Failure Cases
**Objective:** Test limits where Language Inertia is beneficial and analyze algorithmic edge-case failures.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMMU (Hard Subset) for VASM ablation; custom tiny-object dataset (objects $< 16 \times 16$ pixels) for failure analysis.
- **Hypothesis (VASM):** The "No-VASM" ablation ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma] = 1.0$) will crash MMMU reasoning scores, mathematically proving the "Entropy Trap" and validating VASM.
- **Hypothesis (Failure Case):** For objects smaller than $16 \times 16$ pixels where the true token count $< k_{min}$, BRA is forced to absorb background noise, diluting the Top-$k$ resonance signal. This will explicitly document the operational limits of $k_{min}$.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to localized logits-space resonance anchoring resolves a critical structural bottleneck in high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly addressing the memory bandwidth wall via fused Triton kernels, resolving sub-word fragmentation via Stateful VASM, and ensuring fair zero-shot evaluations, BRA provides a mathematically robust pathway to intervene safely without destroying fragile 2D/3D encodings. 

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Temporal Naivety (Bag-of-Frames):** While treating $T \times H \times W$ as a unified volume is highly effective for spatio-temporal *grounding* (e.g., localizing which frame contains an action), it inherently ignores causal temporal order. BRA cannot currently reason about sequential motion logic (e.g., "A man opens the door, *then* walks out"), limiting its efficacy on causal video reasoning tasks.
2. **The $k_{min}$ Dilution Vulnerability:** As mapped out in Defense Line 5, if an object is microscopically small (occupying fewer tokens than $k_{min}$), the Top-$k$ selection is forced to include background tokens, diluting the localized signal. Future dynamic $k_{min}$ bounds based on attention-head entropy could address this.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until the 5-Tier Defense Protocol is fully executed, we limit our claims to the theoretical and algorithmic soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial-temporal bottlenecks of MLLM interventions, offering a mathematically bounded, memory-aware, and BPE-safe blueprint to validate zero-pooling decoding across static and dynamic multimedia architectures.
