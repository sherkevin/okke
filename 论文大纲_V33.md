# Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention: Token-Local Visual Evidence for Structure-Preserving MLLM Decoding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to hallucination when strong language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense grounding. While existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalties) serve as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers for the language or attention generation dynamics, they lack a mechanism to inject strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present **Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention (TSLI)**, a decode-time method that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

TSLI operates on three core mechanisms. First, **token-local logits intervention** maps final-layer visual states into the LLM's latent dimension to evaluate candidate-wise spatial support. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence while mathematically preventing background patches from casting forced votes, explicitly avoiding the spatial dilution of global average pooling. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via a WordNet-lemmatized dictionary and dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation inheritance. To rigorously handle input-output embedding asymmetry and spatial washout, we establish a strict evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot probe (`TSLI_zero`) from a lightweight, strictly linear calibrated bound (`TSLI_calib`). Furthermore, to prevent catastrophic latency overheads during generation, we introduce entropy-gated triggers and candidate-window bounds.

To validate TSLI, we propose a pre-registered, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol restricted to 2D spatial grids, guaranteeing zero data leakage. Evaluated across three definitive evidence chains—(A) Hallucination Reduction against parameter-matched LoRA baselines, including a strict Object vs. Attribute gap analysis, (B) Structure Preservation with exact Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) tracking, and (C) Local Evidence Value via geometric attention mapping—alongside rigorous transparent profiling of latency versus candidate size, this framework establishes a scientifically sound, structurally protected proposition for decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable zero-shot capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to clarify that these methods do not rely on flawed global visual pooling; rather, they serve as state-of-the-art orthogonal regularizers that effectively discipline the language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. However, because they operate on overarching generation dynamics rather than explicit spatial grounding, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, defensible hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination more faithfully than pure language/attention heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We conceptualize this intervention as **Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention (TSLI)**. In this work, we explicitly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, deliberately avoiding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims.

Executing TSLI requires acknowledging and overcoming three structural barriers. First, **input-output embedding asymmetry**: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, rendering direct dot-product similarity with the terminal output vocabulary matrix invalid. Second, the **post-hoc entanglement gamble**: leveraging the final-layer hidden states of visual patches assumes localized support survives deep attention mixing, which risks spatial washout in 1D-sequence architectures. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities.

TSLI resolves these barriers via three core mechanisms, supported by a critical latency optimization:
1. **Token-local logits intervention**, mapping final-layer visual states to the LLM's pre-vocabulary dimension (`TSLI_zero` vs. `TSLI_calib`), trained under strict semantic disjoint constraints to prevent contrastive poisoning.
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations to avoid background dilution.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, utilizing dynamic BPE continuation inheritance to shield structural language, explicitly confining intervention to visual nouns.
4. **Entropy-Gated Execution**, restricting the $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ latency trap by confining intervention strictly to high-uncertainty decoding steps and bounded candidate windows.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, we deploy our experimental design under a stringent pre-registered protocol encompassing three primary evidence chains: 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction & Attribute Gap):** Validating mitigation on POPE/CHAIR against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline to isolate the true advantage of the decode-time mechanism over pure data exposure, while explicitly measuring the unmitigated "attribute/relation gap."
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving the necessity of VASM and BPE Inheritance on MMMU(Hard) while transparently reporting benchmark Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rates.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the geometric superiority of localized evidence via 3-way side-by-side attention mapping against natively diffuse and internal mean-pooled baselines.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Overcoming Post-Hoc Entanglement: `TSLI_zero` vs `TSLI_calib`

A fundamental vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input layer. To construct a valid local support metric, TSLI evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states of the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$. 

We explicitly acknowledge the gamble of **post-hoc entanglement**: deep self-attention implicitly mixes visual states with prefix context. To handle vocabulary projection cleanly without falsely claiming zero-shot alignment if the architecture implicitly washes out spatial fidelity, we define two distinct tracks:

1. **`TSLI_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support via $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`TSLI_calib`**: If `TSLI_zero` fails due to spatial washout, we pivot to a lightweight calibrated decoding method. $\Phi_{calib}: \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{D_{llm}}$ is strictly defined as a **single linear layer (zero hidden layers)** projecting the visual state to the LLM's final hidden dimension *prior* to $W_{vocab}$. This mathematically prevents $\Phi_{calib}$ from acting as a deep memory bank. It is trained on a frozen set of 5,000 COCO image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. The base LLM remains entirely frozen, and these 5,000 images are mathematically verified to be disjoint from all downstream evaluation sets.

**Strict Semantic Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. The positive target $W_{vocab}[c^+]$ is the contextualized hidden state of the final subword of the ground-truth label. To prevent contrastive poisoning in densely packed images (where low-IoU patches might still contain fragments of the target object), negative samples ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are not merely drawn based on spatial IoU $< 0.1$. We enforce a **strict semantic disjoint constraint**: negative patches are cross-referenced with panoptic/instance annotations to ensure they definitively belong to a different semantic class than the target, ensuring a pure gradient signal for spatial localization.

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling and Latency Optimization

At decoding step $t$, TSLI evaluates spatial support. A naive Softmax over all visual patches would force background patches to sum to 1.0, artificially inflating noise. We apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation, allowing irrelevant patches to mathematically abstain:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{noise}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{noise}\Big)
$$
To prevent $\theta_{noise}$ from becoming a brittle hyperparameter that overfits to specific datasets, it is dynamically defined per-step as the moving median of the activation distribution, ensuring generalization across domains without manual tuning. We then apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$, aggregating only the top-$k$ most supportive activated patches for candidate $c$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c)
$$

**Evading the Latency Trap:** Computing similarities across $V$ tokens and $N_v$ patches at every single decoding step inherently introduces an $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ latency trap, threatening to reduce throughput by $>80\%$ and degrade TSLI to a strictly offline analytical tool. To salvage real-time viability, we introduce two optimizations:
1. **Candidate Restriction:** We bound intervention strictly to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=100$), reducing the computation surface from $V$ to $M$.
2. **Entropy-Gated Execution:** TSLI is entirely bypassed if the base LLM's native confidence (probability of the Top-1 token) exceeds $0.90$. Visual intervention is only triggered during high-entropy, uncertain decoding steps where hallucination risk peaks.

### 3.3 VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing. This penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

VASM prevents structural collapse via a dual-tier architecture:
1. **Root Token Expectation (WordNet-Augmented Visual Nouns):** $\gamma=1$ *only* for an unambiguous dictionary of visual object nouns. Out-of-dictionary terms default to $\gamma=0$, guaranteeing structural syntax safety. We explicitly acknowledge the capability ceiling here: by ignoring visual verbs and attributes, VASM relies entirely on the base model's priors for relational accuracy.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance (Dynamic):** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the dictionary and inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its root token. Designed to be robust across tokenizers (e.g., Llama's SentencePiece `_`, Qwen's Tiktoken), this ensures subwords like `_rhi`, `no`, `cer`, `os` receive synchronized logit adjustment, mathematically preventing multi-token fragmentation.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because TSLI introduces a specific structural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence and latency optimization, we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains. The protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector, 1D sequence) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). All experiments below outline our planned, pre-registered execution.

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction vs. Attribute Gap

**Hypothesis:** TSLI mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting. These gains derive from the decode-time intervention mechanism, not mere parameter exposure, and restricting VASM strictly to nouns does not trigger cascading failures in attribute/relation generation.
- **Zero Data Leakage Guarantee:** The 5,000 COCO images used to train $\Phi_{calib}$ are strictly verified via MD5 hashes to have zero intersection with evaluation sets.
- **Planned Execution (Table 1):**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Acc/F1) | CHAIR (Object vs Attribute/Relation breakdown) | **AGL (± std)**
  - **Zero-Shot Baselines:** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `TSLI_zero`.
  - **Calibrated Baselines:** `Base + 5k LoRA` (fine-tuned on the exact same 5k pairs, targeting LLM Q/V projections), `TSLI_calib`.
- **Success Criteria:** `TSLI_calib` must outperform the zero-shot heuristics and the parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline on Object-CHAIR and POPE. Average Generated Length (AGL) must remain stable. Crucially, the "Attribute/Relation Gap" in CHAIR must prove that leaving non-nouns unprotected by VASM does not degrade base performance.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via visual suppression does not degrade complex reasoning, provided VASM actively protects structural tokens and BPE Inheritance protects multi-token entities.
- **Planned Execution (Table 2):**
  - **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1 | MME | MMMU (Hard Subset) | **Average OOV Rate**
  - **Ablations:** Base | `TSLI_no_VASM` (applies penalty to all tokens) | `TSLI_no_BPE_Inherit` | `TSLI_full`
- **OOV Tracking Requirement:** We will explicitly report the Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rate—the exact percentage of benchmark ground-truth targets where VASM defaulted to $\gamma=0$ due to dictionary exhaustion.
- **Success Criteria:** Unconstrained suppression (`TSLI_no_VASM`) must severely degrade MMMU(Hard) reasoning. `TSLI_full` must closely preserve Base model performance. If OOV exceeds 10%, VASM will be analyzed as fundamentally insufficient.

### 4.3 Chain C: The Necessity of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting geometrically isolates evidence that diffuse pooling natively washes out.
- **Planned Execution (Table 3):**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR/Position) | DocVQA (ANLS)
  - **Ablations:** Base | `TSLI_MeanPool` | `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK`
- **Execution Rule:** `TSLI_MeanPool` serves as our internal ablation. It *must* use the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$ and VASM mask as `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK`. 
- **Qualitative Protocol (Figure 1 - 3-Way Heatmaps):** We mandate a 3-way side-by-side failure analysis plotting: (A) Base model's native attention map, (B) The `TSLI_MeanPool` 2D grid (diffuse), and (C) The `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK` 2D grid (sharp localization). 
- **Crucial Limitation Analysis:** We mandate the inclusion of **one explicit semantic distractor failure case** where `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK` locks onto the *wrong* local patch (e.g., an adjacent object of the same semantic class) forcing a hallucination, proving the mechanistic limits of spatial projection.

### 4.4 Latency Profiling and Boundary Limits

**Hypothesis:** The $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ latency penalty can be effectively managed via entropy-gating and bounded candidate windows without sacrificing hallucination mitigation.
- **Planned Execution (Figure 2 & Figure 3):**
  - **Latency vs. M Chart:** A line chart plotting **A100 Tokens/Sec** on the y-axis against the **Candidate Window Size ($M$)** on the x-axis, mapping the threshold at which overhead becomes catastrophic. We will report absolute throughput for Base, VCD, DoLa, and TSLI.
  - **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability (Logit Rank Plot):** We will explicitly document the absolute structural ceiling of TSLI. Because TSLI mathematically reweights the initial top-$M$ distribution, it is fundamentally helpless to rescue tokens the base model completely suppresses.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of TSLI lies in its falsifiable, structurally bounded framework: intervening at decode-time using strictly token-local visual evidence, and mathematically protecting language integrity via VASM. However, its execution carries severe theoretical and operational constraints:

1. **The Post-Hoc Entanglement Gamble:** The core assumption that localized visual support survives deep self-attention in the final layers of an LLM is precarious. The text prefix heavily contaminates visual hidden states. If `TSLI_zero` completely fails and `TSLI_calib` yields only marginal gains over LoRA baselines, the premise of extracting clean "local states" from final layers may be fundamentally capped by architecture.
2. **The Latency Trap:** Despite entropy-gating and bounding the candidate window to $M$, the dynamic BPE mask lookups and Top-$k$ spatial pooling still introduce substantial per-step overhead. If our empirical profiling reveals that throughput drops by over 50% on A100s, TSLI must concede its status as a real-time production inference strategy and serve strictly as a high-precision, offline analytical decoding tool.
3. **VASM’s Noun-Centric Blindspot:** By restricting $\gamma=1$ exclusively to nouns to guarantee structural safety, VASM renders the intervention entirely blind to visual verbs (actions) and attribute hallucinations (e.g., "red" vs. "blue"). While our protocol explicitly measures this capability ceiling, it remains a severe limitation compared to full-sequence fine-tuning.
4. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** TSLI mathematically reweights the LLM's initial candidate distribution. It cannot synthesize a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window (e.g., rank $>M$), establishing a hard theoretical ceiling on all post-hoc logits adjustment strategies.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention (TSLI), a rigorously bounded framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By mapping embedding asymmetry risks, utilizing threshold-gated Top-$k$ pooling to avoid background dilution, engineering robust language protection via VASM BPE inheritance, and addressing severe latency bottlenecks via entropy-gating, we establish a technically precise foundation for decode-time visual control. The claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol spanning Hallucination, Structure, and Local Evidence chains, awaiting full empirical execution.
