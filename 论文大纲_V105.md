# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Budget-Matched Decode-Time Routing Head for Visual Existence Verification in MLLMs

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during entity grounding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate internal model confidence, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into BPE vocabulary logits to penalize unsupported entity tokens during autoregressive decoding. 

Recognizing the mathematical limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual existence verifier** rather than a complex spatial reasoner. Trained as an isolated projection over a frozen LLM, it reliably answers "Is this noun in the image?" to suppress pure hallucinations. To justify its $\sim$131M parameter footprint, TLRA is subjected to a strict "budget-matched" evaluation against a high-rank prefill-time LoRA adaptation, enforcing identical 50k masked-caption supervision. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A calibrated projection head navigating a static vs. dynamic memory footprint tradeoff; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring over bounded candidate windows, explicitly audited against Generic Collapse (noun-suppression); and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM). VASM restricts interventions to physical nouns via an offline BPE dictionary, employing an **Unambiguous Stem Restriction** to handle whitespace tokenization and semantic collisions (e.g., `_cat` vs. `catastrophe`). This work establishes a pre-registered, boundary-aware protocol for parameter-efficient decode-time visual routing, explicitly mapping its structural, morphological, and relational failure boundaries.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination. In fine-grained physical grounding scenarios, global image representations often fail to provide the highly localized evidence required to anchor specific generated tokens.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts. However, they cannot introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways. Conversely, standard parametric approaches (like LoRA) operate entirely in the prefill phase, compressing spatial evidence into the global context window without explicitly routing it to specific token generation steps. This leaves a critical gap: **How can we inject fine-grained, token-specific visual evidence directly into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying morphological syntax or hitting severe memory bandwidth walls?**

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. TLRA extracts local spatial support to bias decode-time logits, strictly limited to entity-centric grounding. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a 50k masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit supervision that zero-shot methods lack. 

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly reject the framing of TLRA as a "context-aware spatial reasoner." Mathematically, TLRA acts as a **Global Visual Existence Checker**. By projecting patch features into the vocabulary space independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. While this effectively suppresses pure hallucinations, it establishes a hard theoretical boundary: TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. We embrace this constraint, demonstrating that context-blind existence checking is highly effective for core hallucination reduction if properly bounded.

Furthermore, we dispel the "lightweight" illusion of vocabulary-space projections. For a standard architecture ($D=4096$, $V=32000$), TLRA introduces $\sim$131M parameters. To justify this architectural complexity, TLRA must survive a "do-or-die" evaluation against a data-matched, mathematically budget-matched (high-rank) prefill LoRA adaptation. While a context-aware LoRA may outperform TLRA on pure grounding, TLRA's structural advantage lies in its **Zero-Interference Guarantee**: unlike LoRA, which risks catastrophic forgetting of general instruction-following when overfitted to 50k captions, TLRA operates entirely post-hoc, theoretically perfectly preserving base reasoning capabilities.

Finally, we confront the **Vocabulary Sparsity Trap** and **BPE Collisions**. A $4096 \times 32000$ matrix trained on 50k captions leaves vast regions of the vocabulary un-updated. To prevent arbitrary noise on unseen tokens and morphological corruption (e.g., penalizing `_cat` and inadvertently breaking the word "catastrophe"), TLRA introduces an upgraded Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) mechanism with strict Unambiguous Stem Restrictions. 

Our evaluation is structured as a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction & Recall Integrity:** Evaluating standard entity grounding (POPE, CHAIR) while auditing for Morphological Error Rate (MER) and Object Recall to prove TLRA is not merely silencing noun generation (Generic Collapse).
2. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general capabilities (MMBench) against the high-rank LoRA, and explicitly testing TLRA on a held-out vocabulary split.
3. **Local Evidence Value & Relational Boundaries:** Isolating spatial advantage on FREAK against a matched-parameter `Global_Visual_State` control, while explicitly documenting TLRA's failure boundary on a Multi-Instance/Relational stress test.
4. **Semantic Boundaries and OOD Fallback:** Bounding the method via an Action/Verb stress test and verifying graceful degradation on Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOD) jargon.
5. **Hardware Bandwidth Physics:** A formal evaluation of the static vs. dynamic memory footprint, mapping the tradeoff between Time-to-First-Token (TTFT) and Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT), including scaling projections for $128k$ vocabularies.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Architectural Scope, Parameter Accounting, and the Late-Fusion Head

TLRA targets standard ViT + MLP projector architectures where spatial provenance is preserved across visual tokens $X_v$. TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage:

**1. `TLRA_calib` (The Parametric Routing Head):** A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V}$ maps cached spatial visual states of dimension $D$ directly to the lexical vocabulary size $V$. For a standard model (e.g., Llama-2 with $D=4096$, $V=32000$), $W_{calib}$ constitutes $\sim$131 million parameters. 
- **Training Formulation:** $W_{calib}$ is trained on a bounded subset of 50k conceptual captions. **The base LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen; gradients flow *only* into $W_{calib}$.** The objective is standard next-token prediction cross-entropy, but the loss is strictly masked to only backpropagate on tokens where VASM $\gamma(y_t) = 1$ (physical entities). $\mathcal{L}_{calib} = -\sum_{t} \gamma(y_t) \log P(y_t \mid X_v, Y_{<t})$. 
- **The Parametric Baseline Contract:** Because 131M parameters significantly exceeds standard LoRA configurations, TLRA *must* be compared against a high-rank (e.g., $r \ge 256$) budget-matched LoRA adapter trained on the exact same masked objective.
- **The Vocabulary Scaling Wall:** We explicitly acknowledge that $W_{calib}$ scales linearly with $V$. For next-generation tokenizers (e.g., Llama-3 with $V=128k$), this projection balloons to $\sim$524M parameters, marking a strict structural limitation of direct decode-time routing.

**2. `TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** Computes cosine similarity directly between frozen post-projector visual features ($X_v$) and the LLM's discrete text embeddings. Because the ViT/MLP features are optimized to align with the LLM's *input* layers rather than its output vocabulary space, this mapping is geometrically incoherent, mathematically proving that decode-time spatial routing requires the learned $W_{calib}$ projection.

### 3.2 The Hardware Bandwidth Tradeoff: Static vs. Dynamic Memory Footprint

At each decoding step $t$, TLRA maps spatial features $X_v$ to lexical biases for a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$. Computing this routing introduces memory bandwidth challenges:

**A. Static Precomputation (Optimizing TPOT over TTFT):** During prefill, cached visual states are mapped to the full vocabulary, yielding a static bias matrix $B = X_v \cdot W_{calib}$. This incurs a one-time FLOP penalty degrading TTFT, but gathering arbitrary token columns during generation limits step computation to uncoalesced memory reads from High Bandwidth Memory (HBM).
**B. Dynamic Sliced-Matmul (Optimizing static footprint over FLOPs):** We dynamically compute the projection *only* for the Top-$M$ candidates at each step: $B_{step} = X_v \cdot W_{calib}[:, \mathcal{C}_t]$. For $N_v \approx 3000$, the true bottleneck is the HBM bandwidth saturated by uncoalesced gather operations. Dynamic sliced-matmul trades FLOPs to avoid these uncoalesced reads.

### 3.3 The Context-Blind "Existence Checker" and Logit Clipping

Mathematically, TLRA acts as a **Global Existence Checker**. It computes a raw spatial support score by pooling the top-$k$ matching patches for a candidate token $c$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} B_{j, c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
Crucially, $S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$ outside of the LLM's candidate proposal window. It verifies if visual evidence for candidate $c$ exists *anywhere* in the image. This theoretically guarantees failure on multi-instance/relational queries. 

To apply this evidence without forcing structural collapse, we compute a clipped penalty based on the margin between the base model's top-1 and top-2 logits ($\Delta_L$). The final adjusted logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L, \delta_{max}) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
**Noun-Only Fallback Guarantee:** By bounding the penalty by $\Delta_L$, we ensure that when the base model is confident in syntax, TLRA cannot arbitrarily flip the argmax. More importantly, penalties are only applied *relative* to other physical nouns; we enforce a floor margin to prevent a penalized hallucinated noun from falling below the probability of a verb or syntactic token, thereby avoiding morphological breaks.

### 3.4 VASM, BPE Collisions, and the Unambiguous Stem Restriction

To preserve syntax, TLRA utilizes VASM, an offline deterministic binary mask ($\gamma \in \{0, 1\}^V$) extracted from English WordNet physical entity synsets. 

**Addressing BPE Tokenizer Leakiness:** BPE tokenization is exquisitely sensitive to preceding whitespace (e.g., `_apple` vs. `apple`) and semantic stem collisions (e.g., `_cat` acting as the prefix for "cat", "catastrophe", or "catalyst"). If VASM blindly adjusts `_cat`, it corrupts non-visual abstract text generation.
To resolve this, VASM enforces an **Unambiguous Stem Restriction**: 
1. **Whitespace Inclusivity:** Both whitespace-prefixed and raw tokens are mapped.
2. **Abstract Collision Filtering:** A subword stem is explicitly excluded from the VASM dictionary ($\gamma = 0$) if its top 10 most frequent completions in standard English corpora are abstract non-physical concepts. Interventions are restricted purely to unambiguous initial stems of groundable nouns, safeguarding general abstract generation capabilities.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The evaluation is organized into three evidence chains and a hardware bandwidth audit. The tables below serve as the pre-registered execution contract. Results are currently marked as "To Be Filled" (TBF) pending hardware execution.

**Data Isolation Guarantee & Baseline Protocol:** The 50k training split for $W_{calib}$ is drawn from a filtered subset of Visual Genome, strictly verified to have zero image-level overlap with the COCO validation sets used in POPE/CHAIR. The `Base + LoRA` control is trained on the exact same *VASM-masked* loss, ensuring identical optimization targets.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction vs. Generic Collapse

**Goal:** Determine if TLRA suppresses hallucinations without simply silencing noun generation (The Penalty-Only Fallacy).
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Metrics:** F1, CHAIR-s, Morphological Error Rate (MER), and **Object Recall** / Average Nouns per Generation. If Object Recall drops significantly, TLRA is failing via generic collapse (e.g., replacing "dog" with "thing").
- **Parametric Controls (50k strict isolation):** `Base + LoRA` ($\sim$131M params via high rank), `Global_Visual_State`, `TLRA_calib`.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict computational, structural, and relational boundaries. By explicitly reframing TLRA as a targeted, context-independent visual existence verifier, we identify the following mathematically rigid limitations:

1. **The Vocabulary Sparsity Trap and BPE Fragility:** Training a $D \times V$ matrix on bounded data leaves massive portions of the vocabulary un-updated. Furthermore, relying on an offline WordNet dictionary mapped to specific BPE stems inherently limits scalability. TLRA is structurally vulnerable to multilingual tasks, semantic prefix collisions, and migrations to newer, larger tokenizers. As shown in Section 4.4, transitioning from a 32k to 128k vocabulary inflates the routing head to over half a billion parameters.
2. **The "Existence Checker" Fallacy (Relational Blindness):** The mathematical formulation of $S_{raw}$ pools patches independently of text context. TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. It trades complex reasoning for robust, lightweight decode-time hallucination suppression.
3. **The Penalty-Only Fallacy (Recall Degradation):** Because TLRA relies strictly on penalizing logits, it runs the risk of "Generic Collapse"—suppressing hallucinations by simply silencing all specific noun generation in favor of generic pronouns. We explicitly mandate Object Recall metrics to police this boundary.
4. **The Parametric Baseline Mandate vs. Zero-Interference:** At $\sim$131M parameters, TLRA must be evaluated against a budget-matched LoRA. While a context-aware LoRA may achieve superior raw grounding metrics, TLRA's existential justification lies in its *Zero-Interference Guarantee*—operating as a purely post-hoc adjustment that physically cannot induce catastrophic forgetting in the base LLM weights.
5. **Top-M Unrecoverability:** The intervention is mathematically constrained by the base model's candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$. If strong language priors marginalize the ground-truth visual token below the Top-$M$ cutoff, absolute evidence penalties cannot rescue it.

Future execution of this pre-registered protocol will definitively confirm whether a trained decode-time routing head can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities, sparsity traps, and relational blindness to provide scalable, targeted visual grounding.
