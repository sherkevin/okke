# Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring: Escaping the Pooling Paradox in High-Resolution Image and Video MLLMs

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual or temporal evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate thousands of high-resolution spatial or temporal tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D/3D-RoPE coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., Document VQA) and specific frame localization (e.g., Video Action). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space via localized visual matching. 

To resolve critical mathematical and computational flaws in prior logits-based interventions, BRA introduces three structural upgrades: (1) **Dual-Track Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)**, offering a strictly zero-shot identity mapping to preserve fair comparisons, alongside a lightweight contrastive variant for unaligned architectures; (2) **Bounded Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, which isolates fine-grained spatial and temporal features ($T \times H \times W$) while mathematically bounding the computational overhead via pre-filtered candidate sets ($O(M \times N_v)$); and (3) **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, replacing brittle predictive entropy with an offline, tokenizer-aware syntactic mask that handles polysemy. We establish a rigorous 5-tier "Defense Line" experimental protocol—spanning dense OCR, temporal hallucination clustering, and cross-architecture hardware profiling (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT vs. Qwen-VL)—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy and inference efficiency of BRA across continuous and dynamic-resolution MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model produces hallucinations—generating text that is linguistically fluent but physically or temporally absent from the multimedia input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on multi-pass contrastive decoding or backtracking (e.g., VCD, OPERA). While effective, these methods introduce severe latency overheads (massive drops in Output Tokens Per Second) and VRAM bloat. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution images or long videos, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool, MaxPool, or PCA) thousands of visual tokens. This irreversible aggregation destroys the fine-grained spatial-temporal coordinates (like 2D/3D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects, dense text, or fleeting temporal actions, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or Video Action Recognition.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a localized match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial and temporal information. However, implementing this exposes severe mathematical and systemic vulnerabilities: the computational explosion of matching vast spatio-temporal volumes against the entire vocabulary $|V|$, the unverified assumption of zero-shot modality alignment, and the "Entropy Trap" where contextually predictable hallucinations evade penalization.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA, bounded by strict computational complexity controls and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Our methodology features:
1. **Bounded Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**: Transitioning from a statistically dangerous absolute max-pooling to a resolution-aware Top-$k$ mean formulation. By restricting resonance checks to a truncated candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$, BRA handles dynamic-resolution images and video frames ($N_v = T \times H \times W$) without bottlenecking generation latency.
2. **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**: Replacing flawed predictive entropy with an offline, polysemy-aware masking mechanism based on corpus-level Part-of-Speech (POS) distributions, structurally protecting functional syntax across diverse tokenizers (SentencePiece, Tiktoken).
3. **The 5-Line Defense Protocol**: Expanding the experimental blueprint into a strict five-stage defense. Crucially, this protocol mandates cross-architecture validation (proving intrinsic zero-shot alignment is not an isolated artifact) and necessitates the explicit reporting of Output Tokens/s, Average Generated Length (AGL), and temporal clustering visualizations to prove true Image+Video viability.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Bounded Candidate Filtering & Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)

The core hypothesis of BRA is that intervention should occur in the logits space to avoid pooling high-resolution spatial-temporal tokens. However, comparing $N_v$ visual tokens against the entire vocabulary $|V|$ at every decoding step $t$ would result in an intractable $O(|V| \times N_v \times d)$ complexity, destroying Time-Per-Output-Token (TPOT). 

To resolve this compute explosion, we define a sharply bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=50$). We strictly utilize the **post-LLM contextualized hidden states** corresponding to the visual token positions, denoted as $h_L^{(v_j)}$. To compute resonance with the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$, we formalize an alignment mapping $\Phi$, decoupled into two variants to maintain strict baseline fairness:
1. **Zero-Shot BRA ($\text{BRA}_{zero}$)**: $\Phi$ is defined as the identity function (or the base model's default layernorm projection). This relies on the LLM's intrinsic representation alignment and serves as the primary fair baseline against other inference-time interventions.
2. **Calibrated BRA ($\text{BRA}_{calib}$)**: A lightweight linear projector trained via a localized InfoNCE loss using visual grounding datasets. This addresses architectures (e.g., potentially some Qwen-VL variants) where visual tokens do not organically cluster near lexical counterparts in the unembedding space without explicit bridging.

### 3.2 Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

Unlike hidden-state methods that compute a globally pooled visual vector $\bar{v} = \frac{1}{N_v} \sum h_L^{(v_j)}$ (causing the Pooling Paradox), BRA explicitly isolates fine-grained features. Operating over high-resolution videos produces massive spatio-temporal token volumes defined by $N_v = T \times H \times W$. Relying on pure $\max_{j}$ creates extreme statistical vulnerability to background noise, while mean-pooling destroys temporal localization.

We formulate **Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**. Modern MLLMs utilize dynamic cropping and varying frame counts, meaning $N_v$ fluctuates drastically. We define an adaptive $k$ scaling proportionally to the total spatio-temporal sequence length:
$$
k = \max(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot (T \times H \times W) \rceil)
$$
where $\rho$ is a fractional threshold (e.g., $\rho = 0.01$). For a candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, we compute the temperature-scaled mean resonance of these highly localized patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{\cos\left(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)})\right)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
**Complexity Analysis:** By bounding $\mathcal{C}_t$, the complexity per decoding step drops from $O(|V| \times N_v \times d)$ to $O(M \times N_v \times d)$. Because $M$ is a tiny constant, this ensures BRA is Pareto-superior in latency compared to multi-pass methods like VCD. Crucially for video, if an action occurs in a single frame, the Top-$k$ tokens naturally cluster around that specific $T_i$, bypassing explicit temporal decay without signal dilution.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty 

To map the raw resonance scores into stable logits modifications, we compute the relative evidence position within the Top-$M$ candidates:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
where $\alpha$ is a global penalty strength. Because high-resolution images and long videos produce different cosine similarity density distributions, the Softmax normalization in $\hat S(c)$ intrinsically stabilizes cross-modality scaling, reducing the need for aggressive per-task tuning of $\alpha$ and $\tau_{sim}$.

### 3.4 Escaping the Entropy Trap: Probabilistic VASM

Applying visual resonance blindly at the terminal token level causes semantic collapse for multi-token entities. Previous iterations used predictive entropy $H_t$ to scale the penalty, creating an **"Entropy Trap"**: strong language priors inherently produce low-entropy predictions for predictable *hallucinated* objects, effectively turning off the visual penalty.

We guarantee mathematical stability via **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**. Recognizing that static binary assignments fail for polysemous words (e.g., "lead" as a noun vs. verb), we pre-compute a continuous syntactic weight $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \in [0, 1]$ based on POS probability distributions in a large corpus (e.g., C4):
$$
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = \sum_{pos} P(pos \mid c) \cdot \gamma_{pos}
$$
1. **Semantic Entities ($\gamma_{pos} = 1.0$):** Nouns, Verbs, Adjectives. 
2. **Functional Syntax ($\gamma_{pos} = 0.0$):** Prepositions, conjunctions.
3. **Tokenizer-Specific BPE Fallback:** Sub-words that cannot be reliably POS-tagged are explicitly forced to $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = 0.0$. We formulate distinct fallback rules for **SentencePiece** (LLaMA-based, protecting tokens lacking a leading `_`) and **Tiktoken** (Qwen-based), preventing the fracturing of continuous BPE fragments.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

To definitively validate the structural advantages of BRA—and explicitly address the computational and architectural assumptions inherent in logits-space interventions—we define a rigorous, hypothesis-driven Evaluation Protocol. All experiments must be conducted across at least two fundamentally distinct model families (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT [Vicuna/LLaMA] and Qwen-VL [Qwen]) to prove that intrinsic zero-shot alignment is not an isolated artifact.

### 4.1 Preliminary Ablations & Efficiency Profiling
**Objective:** Isolate the individual mechanisms of BRA before full-scale evaluation.
**Protocol:**
- **Pooling Paradox Ablation:** Evaluate Base vs. Top-$k$ vs. MeanPool vs. MaxPool vs. PCA on the FREAK (Position) subset. 
- **Masking Ablation:** Compare Probabilistic VASM vs. Predictive Entropy vs. No Mask on POPE.
- **Hardware Metrics:** Alongside task performance, report **Output Tokens/s** and **Peak VRAM** against VCD and DoLa to empirically validate the $O(M \times N_v \times d)$ Big-O complexity claims.
- **Hypothesis:** BRA will strictly outperform MeanPool/PCA on positional metrics, and the bounded $\mathcal{C}_t$ extraction will yield a generation latency nearly identical to Base decoding, decisively beating multi-pass methods.

### 4.2 Defense Line 1: Core Hallucination Suppression & Length-Bias Control
**Objective:** Prove that BRA mitigates object hallucinations without merely truncating outputs.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** POPE, CHAIR (MSCOCO).
- **Baselines:** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa. 
- **Key Metric:** Alongside F1 and CHAIR$_S$, mandate reporting the **Average Generated Length (AGL)**. 
- **Hypothesis:** Hidden-state methods (e.g., DoLa) often inflate CHAIR scores by forcing premature `<EOS>` generation. $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ will lower hallucination rates while maintaining an AGL comparable to the Base model, proving true visual grounding over length collapse.

### 4.3 Defense Line 2: General Intelligence & Language Prior Integrity
**Objective:** Ensure visual logit penalization does not lobotomize instructional compliance.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME.
- **Hypothesis:** Because Probabilistic VASM heavily shields functional syntax and non-visual logic tokens ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \approx 0$), $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ will maintain or slightly improve overall multimodal reasoning scores without the catastrophic degradation often seen in aggressive contrastive decoding.

### 4.4 Defense Line 3: "Zero-Pooling" Supremacy on Dense Spatial Tasks
**Objective:** Qualitatively and quantitatively expose the "Pooling Paradox" on high-resolution targets.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR, Counting) and DocVQA.
- **Qualitative Requirement:** Provide a spatial heat map on a Document VQA sample, comparing the global attention/hidden-state extraction of DoLa versus the specific Top-$k$ tokens selected by BRA.
- **Hypothesis:** DoLa will show severe degradation and blurry global heatmaps due to spatial pooling. $\text{BRA}_{zero}$'s Adaptive Top-$k$ will dynamically produce sharp activation points over exact text patches, dramatically outperforming DoLa on dense 2D tasks.

### 4.5 Defense Line 4: Spatio-Temporal Grounding in Video Narrative
**Objective:** Validate that Adaptive Top-$k$ seamlessly extends across $T \times H \times W$ without destroying temporal action localization.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** VIDHALLUC and Video-MME.
- **Quantitative Visualization Requirement:** Provide a temporal histogram plot (X-axis: Frame Index $T_i$, Y-axis: Number of Top-$k$ selected tokens per decoding step) for specific action queries (e.g., "When does the man jump?").
- **Hypothesis:** Traditional temporal MeanPool averages out single-frame actions. $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ will successfully suppress action hallucinations, and the temporal histogram will definitively prove that Top-$k$ dynamically clusters at the exact specific $T_i$ where the action occurs.

### 4.6 Defense Line 5 (The Boundary Test): Complex Reasoning Verification
**Objective:** Test limits where Language Inertia is highly beneficial (e.g., reciting external knowledge).
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMMU (Hard Subset).
- **Hypothesis:** On tasks demanding parametric knowledge rather than direct pointing, resonance scores might be inherently low. VASM's protection of functional logic will prevent catastrophic failure, ensuring no "mis-kill" of valid reasoning pathways.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to localized logits-space resonance anchoring resolves a structural bottleneck in high-resolution image and video MLLMs. By explicitly addressing the computational explosion via bounded candidate sets ($O(M \times N_v)$) and exposing the "Pooling Paradox," BRA provides a pathway to intervene safely without destroying fragile 2D/3D encodings. 

**Limitations & Failure Cases:**
1. **Context-Blindness of Offline VASM:** While Probabilistic VASM mathematically hedges corpus-level polysemy via expected values, it relies on static offline distributions. A critical failure case occurs under highly unusual, contextually skewed prompts where a functional word acts as a visual noun (or vice-versa). Because VASM ignores immediate prompt context, it may inappropriately shield or penalize these edge-case tokens.
2. **Zero-Shot Alignment Limits:** While $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ relies on intrinsic embedding alignment, certain proprietary or highly compressed MLLM architectures may exhibit disjointed visual and textual spaces at the final layer, necessitating a fallback to the $\text{BRA}_{calib}$ projector.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until the 5-Tier Defense Protocol is fully executed, we limit our claims to the theoretical and algorithmic soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial-temporal bottlenecks of MLLM interventions, offering a computationally tractable mathematical blueprint to validate adaptive Top-$k$ decoding across both static and dynamic multimedia architectures.
