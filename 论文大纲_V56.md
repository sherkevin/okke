# Dictionary-Gated Token-Local Visual Intervention: An Offline-Calibrated Plug-in Framework for Hallucination Reduction

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to object hallucination when overarching language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense spatial grounding. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, OPERA) successfully regularize global token probabilities or attention distributions. However, as highly effective but purely zero-shot, training-free regularizers, they operate orthogonally to the explicit routing of spatial evidence. This paper investigates how to explicitly inject **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space at decode-time without damaging functional language structure. We propose **Token-Local Visual Intervention**—a dictionary-gated logits adjustment framework utilizing localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

Strictly scoped to 2D Image-LLMs to ensure defensible claims regarding spatial locality, this framework addresses three structural barriers. First, acknowledging the near-universal spatial washout inherent to deep self-attention, we utilize a zero-shot diagnostic (`BRA_zero`). Upon expected failure, the framework deploys a lightweight **offline-calibrated plug-in adapter** (`BRA_calib`) applied per-visual-token. This explicitly positions our method as a *Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)* intervention. Second, to combat autoregressive threshold drift and the threat of lagging indicators, we extract localized support via an Entropy-Scaled Adaptive Top-$k$ mechanism governed by a Sliding Window Exponential Moving Average (EMA) with a proactive momentum reset. Third, to protect functional syntax from subword tokenization splits, we implement a **Visual-Aware Semantic Masking (VASM)** module—a dual-tier shield utilizing an ~85k WordNet dictionary via a greedy prefix-trie bitmask. 

Because this framework introduces distinct operational risks—including CUDA fragmentation, out-of-vocabulary (OOV) bypasses, and the fundamental incompatibility with arbitrary OCR strings—we propose a pre-registered execution protocol. This blueprint organizes empirical validation across three definitive evidence chains: (A) Hallucination Reduction, (B) Structure and Reasoning Preservation, and (C) Local Evidence Value (benchmarked on RefCOCO and VSR). Backed by explicit defense lines tracking everything from stacked ms/token latency breakdowns to exact-subword BPE failure trajectories, this protocol establishes a rigorous, falsifiable proposition for spatially-aware, plug-in decode-time intervention.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable visual-linguistic capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as small-object recognition, multi-entity relationship mapping, and complex visual spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to establish the precise operational category of these methods: they serve as highly successful, state-of-the-art *zero-shot* regularizers that effectively discipline overarching language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. Because they are training-free, they operate on global generation dynamics. Consequently, they are not natively designed to address the following orthogonal structural question:

**How can we explicitly inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying structural language, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning?**

This paper advances a direct, functional hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination faithfully, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We functionalize this as **Token-Local Visual Intervention**. In this work, we strictly bound our scope to 2D Image-LLMs, deliberately discarding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims regarding spatial locality.

Executing this intervention requires mathematically overcoming three structural barriers. First, **spatial washout** in embedding spaces. Deep self-attention almost universally diffuses localized patch coordinates by the final layer. We formalize this via a zero-shot Bounding Box Retrieval Assessment (`BRA_zero`). Acknowledging this limitation, our framework explicitly operates as an *offline-calibrated plug-in adapter* (`BRA_calib`) applied per-visual-token. We do not claim zero-shot parity with DoLa or VCD; rather, we evaluate whether our PEFT-based spatial routing justifies its minimal training footprint. Second, the computational drift of **dynamic spatial pooling**. We replace static thresholds with an entropy-scaled dynamic activation governed by a continuous Sliding Window EMA, reinforced by a proactive recovery mechanism to prevent the threshold from flatlining during abstract reasoning. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments or complex entities. We resolve this via a Visual-Aware Semantic Masking (VASM) module, engineered with a greedy prefix-trie to openly track subword mappings. Crucially, we formally decouple our motivation from arbitrary OCR tasks, acknowledging that non-dictionary text inherently bypasses our semantic mask.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, our experimental design is organized into a pre-registered execution protocol defining three primary evidence chains and six explicit defense lines:
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mechanism value on POPE, CHAIR, and AGL against a strictly parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline and a Random Spatial Control.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving VASM dynamics via MMBench, MME, and MMMU(Hard), mandating an "Intervention Coverage Rate" and scatterplots isolating degradation on out-of-vocabulary jargon.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the definitive superiority of localized evidence on standard-English spatial tasks (RefCOCO and VSR) by contrasting `BRA_zero`, `BRA_MeanPool`, and `BRA_calib`.

Through these bounded chains and corresponding defense lines quantifying stacked Inter-Token Latency (ITL), proactive EMA resets, and multi-token BPE failure trajectories, this protocol establishes a verifiable and highly falsifiable framework.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Embedding Asymmetry, Spatial Washout, and Compositional InfoNCE

To construct a valid local support metric, we evaluate the *contextualized* final-layer LLM hidden states corresponding to the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{model}}$. Deep self-attention demonstrably diffuses spatial coordinates by the final layer. We formalize this via an operational boundary:

1. **`BRA_zero` (Zero-Shot Diagnostic)**: We evaluate zero-shot bounding box retrieval using the model's native `lm_head` as the projection $\Phi_{zero}$. If this fails to achieve an average bounding box $\text{IoU} > 0.15$ on a 500-sample validation set, spatial fidelity is mathematically declared washed out.
2. **`BRA_calib` (Offline-Calibrated Plug-in Adapter)**: Upon triggering washout, the framework deploys $\Phi_{calib}$, a 2-layer MLP mapping $\mathbb{R}^{D_{model}} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{D_{model}}$, applied *per-visual-token*. This explicitly positions our method as a lightweight PEFT module. It is optimized on 5,000 Visual Genome (VG) pairs. The vocabulary-dimension logit distribution for a specific visual token $v_j$ is defined as projecting the calibrated hidden state through the frozen LLM language head:
$$
logit^{(v_j)}[c] = \Phi_{calib}\left(h_L^{(v_j)}\right) \cdot W_{vocab}^T
$$

**Compositional-Aware Hard-Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. A naive spatial exclusion zone ($\text{IoU} < 0.1$) fails to disentangle overlapping objects (e.g., repelling a "rider" from a "horse"). We introduce a dual-layered *Compositional Coherence Margin* (Graph Veto + Semantic Veto via frozen CLIP). 

*Strict Ablation Commitment:* We explicitly frame this dual-veto as a heavily engineered algorithmic stabilizer. In our protocol (Defense Line 3), if empirical ablations demonstrate that this dual-veto fails to yield a statistically significant $>2\text{-}3\%$ absolute reduction in CHAIR error over a standard hard-negative InfoNCE, it will be aggressively stripped to reduce offline training complexity.

### 3.2 Entropy-Scaled Top-$k$ Pooling & Proactive EMA Reset

At decoding step $t$, we apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{active}^{(t)}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{active}^{(t)}\Big)
$$
We derive an **entropy-scaled dynamic threshold base** during the prefill stage: $\theta_{base} = \mu_A + \lambda\left(\frac{\mathcal{H}_{vis}}{\log(N_v)}\right) \cdot \sigma_A$.

**The Sliding Window EMA and Proactive Reset:** Deriving $\theta_{base}$ strictly at prefill risks threshold obsolescence. We implement a **Sliding Window Exponential Moving Average (EMA)** tracking the Valid Activation Rate $R_t$. 
$$
\theta_{active}^{(t)} = \theta_{base} \cdot \min\left(1.0, \frac{\text{EMA}(R_{t-W:t}) + \epsilon}{\tau_{target}}\right)
$$
*The Lagging Indicator Threat:* If the model naturally transitions to a long span of abstract reasoning (where spatial grounding is unnecessary), $R_t$ drops and the EMA flatlines to zero. To prevent this "kill-switch" from permanently disabling the intervention when the text shifts back to concrete spatial descriptions, we introduce a **Proactive Momentum Reset**: If a generated token triggers the VASM dictionary ($\gamma=1$) but the EMA is below a minimum functional floor $\theta_{floor}$, the EMA window is instantly forcefully reset to $\theta_{base}$. This guarantees immediate reactivation of visual routing.

### 3.3 Visual-Aware Semantic Masking (VASM)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing, gated by a deterministic VASM module $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
We utilize an **Offline Root Dictionary**: The WordNet *physical entity* subtree ($\approx 85,000$ synsets), filtering interventions strictly to concrete nouns.

**Formalizing the BPE-to-WordNet Mapping:** Subword tokenization creates structural brittleness. We resolve this via a **Greedy Prefix-Trie Bitmask**. During generation step $t$, if the generated subword `c` forms a valid prefix path within the WordNet trie, it inherits $\gamma=1$ conditionally. If step $t+1$ breaks the prefix path, the token is retrospectively unmasked in the contextual cache. 

**Logical Limitations and the Arbitrary OCR Paradox:** We explicitly abandon arbitrary OCR as a target domain for this method. Specialized non-dictionary terms (e.g., lowercase garbled text like "199$", or domain-specific jargon) will natively fail the Prefix-Trie, forcing VASM to default to $\gamma=0$. This is mathematically necessary to prevent destroying functional language, but it means the intervention *bypasses itself* on dense OCR strings. Our evaluation protocol explicitly tracks this Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) inaction boundary.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because Token-Local Visual Intervention operates as a plug-in adapter with specific operational risks (latency fragmentation, algorithmic overhead, OOV bypasses), we organize the evaluation plan into three pre-registered falsifiable evidence chains and six distinct defense lines. The planned protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector) and **Qwen-VL-Chat**. All subsequent sections outline our hypothesis-driven execution blueprint.

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction

**Hypothesis:** The intervention mitigates object hallucination specifically through localized decode-time routing, not merely through the parametric knowledge exposed during the 5k-sample offline calibration or arbitrary visual noise injection.
- **Evaluation Plan:**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Random, Popular, Adversarial), CHAIR (Object), and AGL (Accuracy).
  - **Planned Output (Table 1):** Performance comparison explicitly separated into two operational blocks: (1) *Zero-Shot Regularizers* (Base, DoLa, VCD, OPERA) and (2) *Trained/Plug-in Adapters* (`Base + 5k LoRA`, `BRA_calib`). Crucially, a mandatory **"Intervention Coverage Rate (%)"** column (percentage of generated tokens where $\gamma=1$) will be included to prove the method is actively firing rather than drifting alongside the baseline.
  - **Defense Line 1 (Strict Parity & Spatial Controls):** To isolate "routing" from "parametric injection," the `Base + 5k LoRA` control will be trained on the exact 5,000 VG samples used for $\Phi_{calib}$, utilizing identical hyperparameters. Furthermore, we will include a `BRA_random` ablation, injecting visual evidence from a *randomly selected* visual token $v_{random}$. If the LoRA control or `BRA_random` closes $>95\%$ of the performance gap, the spatial routing hypothesis is redundant and will be reported as falsified.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** The VASM module protects structural language, but reliance on standard English dictionaries introduces a vulnerability where Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) jargon bypasses the intervention entirely, conflating "preservation" with mere "inaction."
- **Evaluation Plan:**
  - **Metrics:** MMBench, MME, and MMMU(Hard).
  - **Planned Output (Table 2 & Scatterplot):** Accuracy across benchmarks. We will plot an Intervention Trigger Rate scatterplot: OOV Rate (%) vs. Accuracy Drop relative to Base across three settings: `Base`, `BRA_calib` (Full VASM), and a strict `BRA_no_VASM` ablation. 
  - **Defense Line 2 (The MMMU Jargon Boundary):** By plotting performance directly against the OOV rate of specialized subsets (e.g., MMMU Medicine/Physics), this will explicitly reveal if VASM actively protects reasoning, or if it simply shuts off ($\gamma=0$) during highly technical jargon due to dictionary misses, defaulting to an algorithmic placebo.

### 4.3 Chain C: Local Evidence Value

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks utilizing standard English vocabularies, token-local visual reweighting isolates specific evidence that diffuse global pooling natively washes out, mapping exact localized patch coordinates to specific token logits.
- **Evaluation Plan:**
  - **Benchmarks:** RefCOCO (Comprehension/Grounding subsets) and Visual Spatial Reasoning (VSR). *Note: DocVQA is intentionally excluded here to prevent logical contradiction with the VASM OCR bypass.*
  - **Planned Output (Figure 1 - Exact Subword Visual Proof):** A side-by-side heatmap overlay comparing `BRA_zero`, `BRA_MeanPool`, and `BRA_calib`. These heatmaps will be extracted directly from the routing weights at the *exact autoregressive subword step* where hallucination typically begins, demonstrating that the prefix-trie properly deferred the masking and routed the correct local patch.
  - **Defense Line 3 (Strict Compositional Coherence Ablation):** Evaluated directly on CHAIR, we will ablate $\Phi_{calib}$ trained *with* vs *without* the dual-layered Compositional Coherence Margin. As pre-registered, if the dual-veto fails to yield a $>2\text{-}3\%$ absolute reduction over naive InfoNCE, it will be discarded from the final architecture as over-engineered.

### 4.4 Defense Lines 4, 5, & 6: Algorithmic Bottlenecks and Execution Limits

We will monitor execution feasibility through the following empirical defense lines, benchmarked on a single A100 80GB GPU (Batch Size = 1):

- **Defense Line 4 (The Latency Fragmentation Test):** Logits manipulation across the full vocabulary using spatial routing and Prefix-Trie dictionary lookups introduces severe CUDA kernel fragmentation. 
  - **Planned Output (Figure 2a):** A stacked bar chart explicitly isolating the ms/token breakdown across four stages: (1) LLM Forward Pass, (2) Prefix-Trie Lookup, (3) Per-Token $\Phi_{calib}$ Projection, and (4) Final Logits Adjustment. We must transparently report where the computational bottleneck lies.
- **Defense Line 5 (The BPE Failure Trajectory):** 
  - **Planned Output (Figure 3):** A step-by-step logits trajectory mapping the exact top-5 logits of a fragmented multi-token entity (e.g., "San Francisco" splitting into `["San", " Francisco"]`). It will explicitly show how a casing or spacing mismatch causes the Prefix-Trie to fail, dropping $\gamma$ to 0, and allowing the model to hallucinate on the immediately subsequent token. This proves we have analyzed the granular boundaries of our subword logic.
- **Defense Line 6 (The EMA Proactive Recovery Test):** 
  - **Planned Output (Figure 2b - Long Context Trajectory):** We will plot the token-by-token trajectory of the Sliding Window EMA ($\theta_{active}^{(t)}$) over a continuous >500 token reasoning chain. We must demonstrate the Proactive Momentum Reset successfully catching the threshold when it flatlines to zero during abstract reasoning and forcing it back to $\theta_{base}$ when concrete entities reappear.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The theoretical strength of Token-Local Visual Intervention lies in its structurally bounded proposition: injecting localized visual evidence at decode-time while protecting language integrity via Dictionary-Gated VASM. However, as established in our proposed evaluation protocol, its practical execution is governed by severe operational constraints:

1. **Category Error Avoidance:** Our method (`BRA_calib`) is a Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) strategy applied as an offline-calibrated plug-in adapter. It is mathematically not a zero-shot method. It cannot claim zero-shot parity with training-free methods like DoLa, VCD, or OPERA. Its validity rests entirely on surpassing the parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` boundary.
2. **Throughput Bottlenecks:** As formalized in our stacked latency analysis (Defense Line 4), performing final-layer MLP projections and Prefix-Trie lookups at *every autoregressive decoding step* incurs CUDA kernel fragmentation, potentially restricting this framework to a high-precision analytical diagnostic tool rather than a high-throughput serving API.
3. **The OCR Paradox and Jargon Degradation:** WordNet inherently bounds the method's reliability to standard English. Arbitrary OCR strings or highly specialized domains (e.g., MMMU Medicine) yield high Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rates, forcing VASM to default to $\gamma=0$. This is a fundamental limitation: the method protects functional language by explicitly bypassing itself on non-dictionary text.
4. **EMA Recovery Reliance:** The framework is highly dependent on the Proactive Momentum Reset to survive long-context generation. Without it, the EMA functions as a detrimental lagging indicator.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines a functionally precise framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through VASM-gated, token-local logits intervention. By formalizing spatial washout (`BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`), instituting a strict ablation threshold for InfoNCE stabilizers, and establishing greedy prefix-trie semantic masking, we construct a technical foundation for decode-time visual routing via a plug-in adapter. Acknowledging the inherent risks of latency bottlenecks, subword dictionary brittleness, and OCR incompatibilities, the claims herein are strictly governed by a pre-registered evaluation protocol spanning three evidence chains and six distinct defense lines. The immediate future execution of this protocol will definitively validate or falsify the operational viability of token-local visual intervention.
