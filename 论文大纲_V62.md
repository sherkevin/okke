# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Logits Intervention for Multimodal Hallucination Mitigation

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for the next decoding step. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa), are highly competitive baselines, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual patches. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with background pruning** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window, ensuring computational viability even at high visual resolutions ($N_v > 2000$). Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities. Because strict zero-shot intervention often collapses due to visual-lexical embedding asymmetry, TLRA utilizes a lightweight, domain-agnostic calibrator (`TLRA_calib`) trained purely on generic image-text pairs, leaving the base MLLM entirely frozen to ensure fair comparison against training-free baselines. 

We organize the paper around a strict experimental contract: three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction (measured alongside average generated length to prevent length-collapse artifacts), structure and reasoning preservation (validating VASM against perplexity drops), and the specific value of local over global evidence. To guarantee practical viability, we incorporate explicit efficiency limits up to 4096 visual tokens and out-of-candidate failure audits. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the relevant evidence is distinctly local.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate, highly competitive baselines, but none of them directly answer the following question:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

We advance a direct positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided it includes explicit structure-preserving safeguards. 

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to four foundational challenges. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and output logits do not naturally live in the same space, making strict zero-shot lookup noisy. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc logits intervention can only reweight candidates that the base model already keeps alive. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression of visually unsupported tokens easily damages function words and subword (BPE) syntax. Fourth, **system cost at high resolutions**: computing patch-level support at every generation step scales poorly when modern vision encoders use dynamic patching ($N_v > 2000$).

TLRA therefore centers on three design commitments:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention** utilizing a highly lightweight calibrator to bridge embedding asymmetry without altering the base MLLM.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Pre-decoding Pruning** as the local evidence extractor over a strictly bounded candidate set, ensuring $O(M \times N_{active})$ scaling.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)** as the structure-preserving protection layer. To mitigate the inherent brittleness of static subword POS-tagging, VASM employs a highly conservative masking rule that strictly defaults to bypassing intervention for any structurally ambiguous BPE token. We explicitly acknowledge the *OCR paradox*: by relying on dictionaries to protect syntax, VASM natively masks out out-of-vocabulary (OOV) arbitrary OCR strings. Consequently, TLRA scopes its core claim strictly to spatial object grounding and counting.

To maintain scientific rigor, this paper avoids "fishing expeditions" and commits to a definitive image-centric evaluation protocol establishing three distinct chains of evidence: hallucination reduction, structural preservation, and local-evidence validation against coarse pooling. A spatio-temporal video application is explicitly relegated to an exploratory appendix probe to maintain focus on the core mathematical formulation.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. In our preliminary viability probes, a strict zero-shot readout (`TLRA_zero`) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yielded near-random overlap. The embedding asymmetry is too large. 

Therefore, the definitive intervention is **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain a fair comparison against zero-shot inference baselines, the calibration protocol is strictly constrained:
1. **Lightweight & Low-Cost:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib}$, to map final-layer visual states to the LM's embedding space. The parameter footprint is negligible (~few MBs) and trains in under 2 hours on a single A100 GPU.
2. **Generic Data via InfoNCE:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained solely on a generic image-caption dataset (e.g., CC3M subset) using a token-to-patch InfoNCE contrastive loss. It aligns primitive visual features (colors, shapes) with corresponding noun embeddings. It is *never* trained on downstream task data (e.g., POPE, MMBench).
3. **Frozen Base Model:** The vision encoder and the LLM remain 100% frozen. During inference, $\Phi_{calib}$ acts purely as a plug-in state-translator.

### 3.2 High-Resolution Efficiency and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA must avoid the massive cost of evaluating the entire vocabulary against all visual patches, especially in modern MLLMs where visual tokens $N_v$ can exceed 4096. We introduce two efficiency boundaries:

**1. Candidate Bounding:** We restrict intervention to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=10$).

**2. Pre-decoding Background Pruning:** Before step-wise generation begins, we use the vision encoder's global average attention map to prune uninformative background patches, retaining only an active subset $N_{active}$ (e.g., top 30% of $N_v$). 

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes candidate-wise local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule over the active patches:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_{active} \rceil\Big),
$$
followed by:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$
Here, `score(v_j, c)` is the cosine similarity between the projected patch representation and the embedding of candidate $c$. Calculating this exclusively for $M$ candidates over $N_{active}$ bounds the decode-time overhead to $O(M \times N_{active})$.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a stable logits intervention, TLRA computes a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted as:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$
This relative formulation emphasizes the suppression of weakly supported candidates relative to stronger local competitors within the Top-$M$ window.

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Conservative VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses useful language structure. Subword tokenizers (BPE) complicate this, as isolated subwords lack context (a token might be a noun in one context and syntax in another). TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect these tokens via a binary mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0, 1\}$.

To mitigate subword brittleness, VASM relies on a **conservative fail-safe principle**:
1. **Strict Unambiguous Whitelist:** We construct an intervention whitelist identifying strictly unambiguous physical visual nouns and colors. If a BPE token is contextually ambiguous, represents functional syntax, or is a stopword, VASM conservatively assigns $\gamma = 0$ (intervention bypassed, $L_{final} = L_{orig}$).
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Continuation fragments (e.g., `##board`) automatically inherit the $\gamma$ mask value of their immediate root token to prevent partial-word corruption.

**The OCR Concession:** Because VASM explicitly defaults to $\gamma=0$ for out-of-vocabulary and non-whitelisted text to protect grammar, it inherently bypasses arbitrary OCR strings. We consider this a necessary trade-off: ensuring structural integrity is prioritized over intervening on in-the-wild text.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Rather than presenting cherry-picked subsets, our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The evaluation is structured into three interlocking evidence chains, followed by a strict efficiency scaling audit. All reported results represent the execution of this pre-registered protocol.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length-Collapse Check)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces object hallucination and that this reduction is not a deceptive artifact of the model generating shorter, truncated responses.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE` (Accuracy / F1), `CHAIR` ($CHAIR_s$, $CHAIR_i$).
- **Baselines:** Base MLLM, `VCD`, `OPERA`, `DoLa`.
- **Mandatory Control Metric:** Average Generated Length (`AGL`).
- **Hypothesis:** TLRA will yield a lower `CHAIR` score and higher `POPE` F1 than purely linguistic baselines (DoLa), while maintaining an `AGL` within 5% of the Base model. Any mitigation method achieving SOTA CHAIR scores purely by collapsing AGL is flagged as a false positive.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The VASM Ablation)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not irreparably degrade general multimodal cognition, and prove that VASM effectively protects subword grammar.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1` (Overall), `MMMU` (Hard), and a **Grammar/Fluency Score** (Language-only Perplexity).
- **Ablation Setup:** Base model vs. `TLRA_no_VASM` vs. `TLRA_full`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_no_VASM` will suffer catastrophic performance drops on complex reasoning tasks and exhibit severe perplexity spikes due to the penalization of functional syntax. `TLRA_full` must recover these scores to equal or slightly exceed the Base model, empirically proving that the conservative dictionary approach successfully protects language structure despite the theoretical brittleness of isolated BPEs.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate the core thesis that *token-local adaptive selection* is superior to coarse visual aggregation for fine-grained spatial grounding tasks.
- **Benchmarks:** `FREAK` (Position / Counting subsets), `Object HalBench`. (Document tasks are excluded due to the OCR paradox).
- **Ablation Setup:** Base vs. `DoLa` vs. `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool` ($k = N_{active}$) vs. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Hypothesis:** For localized phenomena like counting, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will demonstrate a statistically significant margin over global visual aggregation (`TLRA_MeanPool`), and `TLRA_zero` will perform poorly, justifying the absolute necessity of both the Top-$k$ patch matching and the $\Phi_{calib}$ module.

### 4.4 Efficiency Hard Bounds and Failure Analysis

To prove practical viability at modern MLLM resolutions, we enforce an explicit efficiency audit.
- **Figure 1 (Efficiency Scaling):** A line chart plotting `Tokens/Second` against increasing `Visual Token Volume` ($N_v$ from 256 up to 4096). Plotting Base, VCD, and TLRA.
- **Efficiency Constraint:** By utilizing Pre-decoding Background Pruning to yield $N_{active}$, we hypothesize TLRA will maintain inference speeds within a 50% drop compared to base decoding, even at $N_v = 4096$.

**Qualitative Failure Audits:** We mandate the inclusion of highly visual attention/similarity heatmaps showcasing two specific failure modes:
1. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** An instance where the correct visual token was ranked $> M$ by the base model.
2. **VASM Masking Error:** An instance where VASM conservatively masked a valid visual concept, causing TLRA to bypass intervention and fail to prevent a hallucination.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its actual empirical execution. The core scientific contribution is a positive, decode-time framework that leverages token-local visual evidence while deploying VASM to prevent the structural collapse of generated language. However, several hard limitations define the boundaries of this method:

1. **The OCR / OOV Paradox:** To guarantee the safety of functional syntax and multi-token entities, VASM relies on conservative lexical whitelists. This explicitly sacrifices intervention on out-of-vocabulary strings, making TLRA inherently ineffective for arbitrary OCR transcription tasks.
2. **Calibrator Domain Shift:** While $\Phi_{calib}$ is lightweight and requires no downstream task data, it is trained purely on generic image-caption pairs (e.g., CC3M). It excels at aligning primitive objects and colors but may fail to generalize to highly abstract geometric layouts or scientific diagrams (e.g., MMMU Hard subsets).
3. **Candidate-Bound Ceiling:** Like all post-hoc logits interventions, TLRA operates strictly on the Top-$M$ tokens proposed by the base model. It cannot resurrect a correct token if the base model's internal probability assigns it a rank lower than $M$.
4. **Temporal Generalization:** The primary formulation of TLRA is spatial. While extending Adaptive Top-$k$ across the spatio-temporal volume ($T \times H \times W$) is an intuitive next step, handling the exponential explosion of $N_v$ in video generation requires formal temporal decay mechanisms not covered in this work. We relegate preliminary spatio-temporal probes to future work / appendices.

By explicitly declaring these limitations, we establish TLRA not as a panacea for all multimodal errors, but as a scientifically principled, mathematically bounded mechanism for correcting spatial and object-level visual grounding during generation.
