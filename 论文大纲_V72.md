# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for Physical Entity Grounding

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa)—are highly competitive baselines for modulating global generation trajectories, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework specifically designed for physical entity grounding in natural scenes. TLRA reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states via a base-model-frozen calibrator.

To execute this safely and efficiently, TLRA operates on three commitments. First, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and abstract reasoning via a deterministic WordNet whitelist. We explicitly accept an "OCR Concession"—trading off intervention capability on arbitrary text to guarantee 0% structural degradation on document-reading tasks. Second, we manage modern BPE fragmentation through a **Tokenizer-Aware Subword Boundary Heuristic**, intervening only on valid prefixes while relying on the base LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete suffixes. Third, **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance** with relaxed candidate bounding ($M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$) and entropy-driven fallback balances latency with high-resolution visual evaluation.

We organize our evaluation protocol around a strict experimental contract consisting of three evidence chains: Hallucination Reduction (mandating a rigorous Vocabulary and Domain Leakage Audit), Structure Preservation (quantifying suffix survival via a formalized BPE Completion Success Rate utilizing character-level edit distance), and Local Evidence Value (isolating our Top-$k$ routing against global pooling baselines like `TLRA_MeanPool`). This blueprint offers a positive, falsifiable, and tightly bounded proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and attribute binding, where the required evidence is distinctly local.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate, highly competitive baselines that effectively modulate global generation trajectories. However, they do not directly answer the following positive hypothesis:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment for physical entities without damaging language structure, corrupting subword KV-cache momentum, or degrading downstream reasoning ability?**

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to embedding asymmetry, candidate limitation at the decoding head, the structural collapse risk of penalizing functional syntax, and the prohibitive system cost of computing patch-level support at high resolutions ($N_v > 4096$). 

We explicitly restrict the scope of our motivation: TLRA is designed exclusively for grounding physical entities in natural scenes. It actively trades off intervention capability on text-rich images (OCR) and abstract reasoning to ensure absolute grammatical safety. TLRA centers on three design commitments:

1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention via a Base-Model-Frozen Plug-in.** We bridge embedding asymmetry using a lightweight calibrator (`TLRA_calib`). We clarify our taxonomy upfront: while the base MLLM remains 100% frozen, TLRA introduces generic external distribution data. Through strict parity ablations against global pooling (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and random selection (`TLRA_RandomK`) using the *exact same calibrator*, we mathematically isolate the value of token-local decoding from this external prior.
2. **Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking).** Built on a strict WordNet whitelist, VASM deterministically bypasses intervention for structural and abstract tokens. We formalize two critical operational concessions: the *OCR paradox* (VASM natively ignores arbitrary text strings, serving as a zero-trigger negative control on document tasks) and *Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum*. VASM triggers on valid subword prefixes but relies on the base LLM to complete suffixes, a dynamic we rigorously audit via a formalized BPE Completion Success Rate (BPE-CSR).
3. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Relaxed Candidate Bounding and Entropy-Driven Fallback.** To control latency, TLRA evaluates only the Top-$M$ logits (exploring $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$ to mitigate the risk of strong language priors hiding valid tokens). To counter the "prefill pruning blindspot"—where visually grounded objects absent from the prompt emerge late in open-ended generation—we incorporate a dynamic fallback mechanism that reverts to dense visual evaluation when attention entropy spikes.

We commit to a definitive evaluation protocol mapped to 2D image-centric benchmarks (relegating temporal video extensions strictly to exploratory appendices). We establish three distinct chains of evidence: Hallucination Reduction (audited for calibrator data/domain leakage), Structure Preservation (proving VASM's safety via an OCR negative control), and Local-Evidence Validation (proving Top-$k$ anchoring statistically outperforms global pooling).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. A strict zero-shot readout (`TLRA_zero`) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yields near-random overlap due to massive embedding asymmetry. 

Therefore, our intervention relies on **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain fair comparisons, the calibration protocol is constrained and transparent about its external priors:
1. **Plug-in Objective & Precomputation Cost:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_v \times |V|}$, mapping the MLLM's visual tokens into the LM head's pre-softmax vocabulary space. The training objective is a standard **Cross-Entropy loss** over the vocabulary, using pseudo-labels from spaCy noun chunks aligned to GroundingDINO bounding boxes and encoded with a frozen CLIP-ViT-L/14 text encoder.
2. **Generic Data with Strict Dual Deduplication:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained solely on a 100K generic subset of CC3M/Visual Genome. To prevent evaluation corruption, we enforce a strict **Dual Leakage Audit**: we mathematically guarantee zero vocabulary overlap *and* zero spatial/domain image overlap with the evaluation sets of POPE, CHAIR, or MMBench. $\Phi_{calib}$ is *never* trained on downstream instruction-tuning data.
3. **Frozen Base Model:** The vision encoder, vision-language connector, and LLM remain 100% frozen.

### 3.2 High-Resolution Efficiency: Bounding and Fallback

At decoding step $t$, evaluating the entire vocabulary against $>4096$ visual patches is computationally prohibitive. 

**1. Relaxed Candidate Bounding:** We restrict intervention strictly to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$. Recognizing that strong language priors can push valid visual tokens down the ranking, we actively relax and evaluate bounds across $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$ to map the Pareto frontier of latency versus hallucination rescue.

**2. Prompt-Conditioned Pruning with Dynamic Fallback:** We aggregate cross-attention from prefill prompt tokens to the visual sequence, pruning low-attention patches to an active subset $N_{active}$. We acknowledge a critical **Prefill Pruning Blindspot**: open-ended generation may require visual evidence for objects never mentioned in the prompt. TLRA enforces an **Entropy-Driven Dynamic Fallback**: we measure the normalized Shannon entropy $H_{attn}$ of the sink-excluded attention weights. If $H_{attn} > \theta_{fallback}$, pruning is bypassed entirely ($N_{active} = N_v$). We recognize that entropy is a hypersensitive proxy; $\theta_{fallback}$ is calibrated strictly on a held-out VisDial split.

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule over the active patches:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_{active} \rceil\Big), \quad S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{sim(\Phi_{calib}(v_j), c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw local support into a stable relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted as:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses functional syntax. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect these tokens via a binary mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0, 1\}$.

1. **Deterministic Whitelist Generation:** A token receives $\gamma=1$ *if and only if* its string maps to a lemma under structural WordNet synsets like `physical_entity.n.01`.
2. **Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum:** Modern tokenizers fragment words. VASM triggers $\gamma=1$ only on the valid initial prefix (filtered via a tokenizer-aware heuristic, e.g., $L_{char} \ge 4$ or lacking continuation metacharacters), relying on the base LLM's unmodified autoregressive momentum to complete suffix tokens ($\gamma=0$).
3. **The OCR Concession:** VASM explicitly defaults to $\gamma=0$ for out-of-vocabulary text. TLRA inherently bypasses arbitrary OCR strings, prioritizing absolute grammatical safety and fluency over hallucination intervention on out-of-dictionary text.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The scientific validity of TLRA hinges entirely on the explicit verification of three interlocking evidence chains, designed to isolate local evidence from external priors and ensure structural safety.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length & Leakage Checks)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces physical object hallucination, that the improvement is not an artifact of shorter responses, and that the gain stems from decode-time routing rather than calibrator memorization.
- **Table 1 Target (The Core Claim):** `POPE` and `CHAIR` metrics. Columns will explicitly report: Model, Method, F1/Accuracy, Average Generated Length (AGL), AGL StdDev, and VASM Trigger Rate (%).
- **Baselines:** `Base`, `VCD`, `DoLa`, `OPERA`, **`TLRA_MeanPool`**, **`TLRA_RandomK`**, and **`TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`**.
- **The Asterisk Rule:** Any configuration achieving SOTA CHAIR scores by collapsing AGL variance by $> 5\%$ compared to the base model will be flagged (*) as an invalid artifact.
- **The Dual Leakage Audit:** We mandate a stratification of POPE F1 scores into `Seen in Calibrator` vs. `Unseen in Calibrator`, mathematically guaranteeing no spatial or vocabulary overlap between the CC3M/VG calibrator subset and the evaluation sets.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will yield lower `CHAIR` and higher `POPE` F1 than all baselines, maintaining consistent performance across both `Seen` and `Unseen` entities without collapsing AGL.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The Safety Audit)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not degrade general cognition, explicitly validate the "OCR Concession" via a negative control, and quantify BPE momentum survival.
- **Table 2 Target:** `MMBench`, `MMMU` (Hard), **Language Perplexity (PPL)**, `DocVQA`, and `BPE-CSR`.
- **The Strict Negative Control:** Because VASM explicitly trades off OCR intervention for structural safety, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must demonstrate a `VASM Trigger Rate` statistically indistinguishable from $0\%$ on `DocVQA`.
- **Formalized BPE Completion Success Rate (BPE-CSR):** To move beyond brittle synonym matching, BPE-CSR is procedurally defined using a normalized character-level Levenshtein distance. We track instances where a VASM-triggered prefix ($\gamma=1$) forces an intervention. A success is recorded if the completed generated subword sequence achieves a character edit similarity $\ge 0.8$ against the target physical entity, without decaying into repetitious stuttering.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must recover `MMBench`/`MMMU` scores comparable to the base model, keep PPL strictly $\le +5\%$, and maintain a high BPE-CSR, proving that prefix-only intervention preserves suffix generation.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Parity Ablation for Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate that *token-local adaptive selection* is statistically superior to coarse visual aggregation, controlling perfectly for external priors.
- **Figure 1 Target (The Parity Ablation):** A line chart plotting *Object Existence Accuracy* (`FREAK` / `Object HalBench`) on the Y-axis against *Intervention Strength ($\alpha$)* on the X-axis.
- **Critical Parity Constraint:** `TLRA_MeanPool` ($k = N_{active}$) and `TLRA_RandomK` utilize the *exact same* $\Phi_{calib}$ weights as `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`. 
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must demonstrate a statistically significant gap ($p < 0.05$) above `TLRA_MeanPool`. If MeanPool achieves parity with Top-$k$, the core scientific premise of "token-local resonance" is falsified. (Note: Temporal localization validations are strictly relegated to exploratory tests on `Video-MME` in the Appendix, requiring proof of exclusive frame-specific activation to be considered valid).

### 4.4 Efficiency Audits, Sensitivity, and Failure Analysis

To map operational boundaries, the execution plan mandates the following diagnostic visualizations:
- **Figure 2 (BPE Survival Curve):** Plotting `BPE-CSR` (Y-axis) vs. $\alpha$ (X-axis) to visually identify the precise threshold where KV-cache momentum breaks down and suffix generation stutters.
- **Figure 3 (Candidate Bounding Pareto):** A Pareto frontier plotting `POPE F1` vs. `Decode Latency` across varying candidate bounds $M \in \{10, 50, 100, \text{All}\}$.
- **Qualitative Failure Analysis:** Visualizing the actual top-5 token probabilities from the LM head at step $t$ (prefix forced by TLRA) and step $t+1$ (suffix collapse), demonstrating how uncalibrated interventions mathematically destroy autoregressive continuation.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its empirical execution. The scientific contribution is a positive, decode-time framework leveraging token-local visual evidence alongside VASM to prevent structural collapse. However, severe rigidities define its operational boundaries:

1. **The OCR Paradox and Negative Controls:** VASM's reliance on a dictionary whitelist ensures grammatical safety but explicitly abandons the ability to intervene on text-rich images. TLRA is strictly a mitigation strategy for *physical entities in natural scenes*, not a general-purpose multimodal hallucination cure. Its zero-trigger behavior on document tasks is an intentional limitation.
2. **Calibrator External Priors and Parity Risks:** $\Phi_{calib}$ injects an external object-detection prior. The scientific validity of TLRA rests on a knife-edge, depending entirely on the `TLRA_MeanPool` parity ablation (Figure 1). If local routing does not statistically outperform global pooling using the same calibrator, the method acts merely as a stealth object detector.
3. **WordNet Brittleness:** VASM's deterministic protection mechanism is strictly bound to the English WordNet database. It is profoundly brittle to novel compounds, modern slang, and non-English reasoning, fundamentally capping its multilingual scalability.
4. **Candidate Bounding ($M$) Vulnerability:** While evaluating $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$ maps the latency Pareto frontier, bounding introduces a hard failure mode. If the base LLM's language prior is overwhelmingly strong and pushes the visually grounded token to rank $>M$, TLRA will fail to inspect or promote it.
5. **Entropy Fallback Hypersensitivity:** The Shannon entropy fallback ($H_{attn}$) is a hypersensitive proxy. If attention is inherently peaky, entropy will remain low even when the model looks at the "wrong" patches, meaning the prefill pruning blindspot may persist in long-horizon generation.
6. **BPE Stuttering and KV-Cache Alteration:** Artificially suppressing competing logits at step $t$ alters the hidden states added to the KV-cache. If the prefix intervention forces an unnatural selection, suffix generation mathematically collapses, a limitation actively audited by our BPE-CSR tracking.
