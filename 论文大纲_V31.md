# Bounded Resonance Anchoring: Token-Local Visual Evidence for Structure-Preserving MLLM Decoding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to hallucination when strong language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense grounding. While existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalties) serve as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers, they lack a mechanism to inject strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, conceptually operationalized as Thresholded Spatial Logit Intervention with Noun Masking. BRA reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

BRA operates on three core mechanisms. First, **token-local logits intervention** maps final-layer visual states into the LLM's latent dimension to evaluate candidate-wise spatial support. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence while mathematically preventing background patches from casting forced votes. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via a WordNet-lemmatized dictionary and dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation inheritance. To rigorously handle input-output embedding asymmetry and spatial washout, we establish a strict evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot probe (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated bound (`BRA_calib`). 

To validate BRA, we propose a pre-registered, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol restricted to 2D spatial grids, explicitly guaranteeing zero data leakage. Evaluated across three definitive evidence chains—(A) Hallucination Reduction against parameter-matched LoRA baselines, (B) Structure Preservation with exact Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) tracking, and (C) Local Evidence Value via geometric attention mapping—alongside rigorous transparent profiling of the inherent $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ latency overhead, this framework establishes a scientifically sound, structurally protected proposition for decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable zero-shot capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), attention-based pattern penalty (e.g., OPERA), or layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa). These families represent distinct, orthogonal regularizers that effectively discipline the language-side or attention-side generation process. However, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, defensible hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than pure language/attention heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We conceptualize this intervention as **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**. In this work, we explicitly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, avoiding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims.

Executing BRA requires acknowledging and overcoming three structural barriers. First, **input-output embedding asymmetry**: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, rendering direct dot-product similarity with the terminal output vocabulary matrix invalid. Second, the **post-hoc entanglement gamble**: leveraging the final-layer hidden states of visual patches assumes localized support survives deep attention mixing, which risks spatial washout in 1D-sequence architectures. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities.

BRA resolves these barriers via three mechanisms:
1. **Token-local logits intervention**, mapping final-layer visual states to the LLM's pre-vocabulary dimension to evaluate localized patch support (`BRA_zero` vs. `BRA_calib`).
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations to prevent the spatial dilution inherent in global averaging.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, a deterministic, tokenizer-agnostic protection layer utilizing dynamic BPE continuation inheritance to shield structural language.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, we deploy our experimental design under a stringent protocol encompassing three primary evidence chains: 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mitigation on POPE/CHAIR alongside Average Generated Length (AGL) variance. Crucially, this includes comparisons against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline to isolate the true advantage of the decode-time mechanism.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving the necessity of VASM and BPE Inheritance on MMMU(Hard) while transparently reporting benchmark Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rates.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the geometric superiority of localized evidence via 3-way side-by-side attention mapping against natively flawed and globally pooled baselines.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Overcoming Post-Hoc Entanglement: `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`

A fundamental vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input layer. To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states of the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$. 

We explicitly acknowledge the gamble of **post-hoc entanglement**: deep self-attention implicitly mixes visual states with prefix context. To handle vocabulary projection cleanly without falsely claiming zero-shot alignment if the architecture (e.g., 1D-sequence models) implicitly washes out spatial fidelity, we define two distinct tracks:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support via $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`BRA_calib`**: If `BRA_zero` fails due to spatial washout, we explicitly pivot to a lightweight calibrated decoding method. $\Phi_{calib}: \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{D_{llm}}$ is a linear layer projecting the visual state to the LLM's final hidden dimension *prior* to $W_{vocab}$. It is trained on a frozen set of 5,000 COCO image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. **Crucially, the base LLM remains entirely frozen, and these 5,000 images are guaranteed strictly disjoint from all downstream evaluation sets.**

We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. To resolve multi-token label ambiguity and subword anisotropy, we define the positive target $W_{vocab}[c^+]$ by extracting the **contextualized hidden state of the final subword** after passing the full ground-truth word $T$ through the frozen LLM. (Prior attempts using L2-normalized mean subword embeddings proved too mathematically risky due to semantic collapse).

Negative samples ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are drawn from intra-image patches with an Intersection over Union (IoU) $< 0.1$ with the target bounding box. To prevent penalizing valid overlapping objects, we enforce a **semantic negative constraint**: negative patches are strictly filtered to ensure their ground-truth labels do not share the exact semantic class as the target. 

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling

At decoding step $t$, BRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=100$). A naive Softmax over $\mathcal{C}_t$ would force every visual patch to sum to 1.0, artificially inflating the voting power of irrelevant background patches. 

To prevent noise injection, we apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation, allowing patches with low semantic overlap to mathematically abstain:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{noise}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{noise}\Big)
$$
To prevent small objects from being washed out by background noise, we apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$. The raw localized support for candidate $c$ aggregates only its top-$k$ most supportive activated patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c)
$$
This is then normalized across the candidate set: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}_{c \in \mathcal{C}_t}(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{norm})$. The sensitivity of $\theta_{noise}$ across varying image resolutions will be explicitly ablated.

### 3.3 VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing. Crucially, this penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
Where $\alpha = \alpha_0 \cdot \mathcal{H}(\text{Softmax}(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)))$.

VASM deterministically prevents structural collapse via a dual-tier architecture:
1. **Root Token Expectation (WordNet-Augmented Visual Nouns):** $\gamma=1$ *only* for an unambiguous dictionary of visual object nouns. Out-of-dictionary terms default to $\gamma=0$, guaranteeing structural safety. By strictly limiting this to *nouns*, VASM consciously cedes attribute (e.g., "red") and relational (e.g., "running") hallucination mitigation to the base model's priors. This is a deliberate design limitation optimized for absolute object-level structural safety.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance (Dynamic):** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the dictionary and inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its root token. Designed to be robust across tokenizers (e.g., Llama's SentencePiece `_`, Qwen's Tiktoken), this ensures words like "rhinoceros" (`_rhi`, `no`, `cer`, `os`) receive synchronized logit adjustment, mathematically preventing multi-token fragmentation.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces a specific structural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence, we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains. The protocol mandates explicit architecture specification, utilizing **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector, 1D sequence) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). 

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction vs. Length Collapse

**Hypothesis:** BRA mitigates hallucination through localized evidence reweighting without causing pathological output truncation, and these gains are derived from decode-time intervention, not merely parameter exposure.
- **Zero Data Leakage Guarantee:** The 5,000 COCO images used to train $\Phi_{calib}$ are strictly verified via MD5/ID hashes to have zero intersection with POPE, CHAIR, and downstream sets.
- **Planned Execution (Table 1):**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Acc/F1) | CHAIRi | CHAIRs | **AGL (± std)**
  - **Zero-Shot Baselines:** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `BRA_zero`.
  - **Calibrated Baselines:** `Base + 5k LoRA` (fine-tuned on the exact same 5k pairs, explicitly targeting the LLM's Q/V projections with a parameter budget $\ge \Phi_{calib}$, trained to convergence), `BRA_calib`.
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA_calib` must outperform both the zero-shot baselines and the `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline. This ensures the performance fundamentally stems from the *decode-time local mechanism*. AGL must not drop significantly, and the standard deviation must prove generation isn't collapsing into repetitive fragments.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via visual suppression does not degrade complex reasoning, provided VASM actively protects structural tokens and BPE Inheritance protects multi-token entities.
- **Planned Execution (Table 2):**
  - **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1 | MME | MMMU (Hard Subset) | **Average OOV Rate**
  - **Ablations:** Base | `BRA_no_VASM` (applies penalty to all tokens) | `BRA_no_BPE_Inherit` | `BRA_full`
- **OOV Tracking Requirement:** We will explicitly report the Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rate—the exact percentage of benchmark ground-truth targets where VASM defaulted to $\gamma=0$ due to dictionary exhaustion. If VASM "turns off" for 80% of reasoning vocabulary, the method's limits must be quantified.
- **Success Criteria:** Unconstrained suppression (`BRA_no_VASM`) must severely degrade MMMU(Hard) reasoning. `BRA_full` must match or strictly preserve Base model performance.

### 4.3 Chain C: The Necessity of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting geometrically isolates evidence that global pooling washes out.
- **Planned Execution (Table 3):**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR/Position) | DocVQA (ANLS)
  - **Ablations:** Base | `BRA_MeanPool` | `BRA_AdaptiveTopK`
- **Execution Rule:** `BRA_MeanPool` *must* use the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$ and VASM mask as `BRA_AdaptiveTopK`. The *only* difference is pooling logic.
- **Qualitative Protocol (Figure 1 - 3-Way Heatmaps):** We mandate a 3-way side-by-side DocVQA failure analysis plotting: (A) The base model's *native cross/self-attention map* for the target token (failing), (B) The `BRA_MeanPool` 2D grid (diffuse/washed out), and (C) The `BRA_AdaptiveTopK` 2D grid (sharp localization). Crucially, we will also include **one failure case** where `BRA_AdaptiveTopK` highlights the *wrong* object due to semantic ambiguity, proving the intervention's mechanistic limits.

### 4.4 Throughput Profiling and Latency Penalties

**Hypothesis:** Decode-time intervention imposes measurable latency that must be transparently profiled for system viability.
- **Planned Execution:** Operating at *every single decoding step* to calculate spatial Top-$k$ pooling, apply threshold gates, and perform dynamic BPE dictionary lookups introduces an inherent $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ overhead. We will benchmark and report exact **A100 Tokens/Sec** throughput for Base, VCD, and BRA directly in the main text. If BRA reduces generation speed by $>40\%$, it will be stated explicitly as a severe operational constraint.

### 4.5 Boundary Profiling: Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability

- **Out-of-Candidate Limit (Figure 2):** We will explicitly document the absolute structural ceiling of BRA. We mandate a qualitative visual example showing an "Out-of-Candidate" failure: visualizing the logits distribution where the correct visual token ranks at position $>M$ (e.g., rank 150 outside an $M=100$ window). This geometrically proves that because BRA mathematically reweights the initial distribution, it cannot rescue tokens that the base model completely suppresses.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of BRA lies in its falsifiable, structurally bounded framework: intervening at decode-time using strictly token-local visual evidence, avoiding global pooling dilution, and mathematically protecting language integrity via VASM. However, its execution carries severe theoretical and operational constraints that restrict its scope:

1. **The Post-Hoc Entanglement Gamble:** The core assumption that localized visual support survives deep self-attention in the final layers of an LLM is precarious. Even with $\Phi_{calib}$, the context prefix heavily contaminates visual hidden states. If future experiments reveal `BRA_zero` completely fails and `BRA_calib` yields only marginal gains over LoRA baselines, the premise of extracting clean "local states" from final layers may be fundamentally capped by architecture.
2. **Severe Latency Bottlenecks:** The dynamic BPE mask lookups, thresholding, and Top-$k$ spatial pooling introduce an $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ overhead per step. This heavily restricts BRA's applicability for real-time inference without heavily parallelized CUDA-level optimizations. 
3. **VASM’s Noun-Centric Blindspot:** By restricting $\gamma=1$ exclusively to nouns to guarantee structural safety, VASM renders the intervention entirely blind to attribute hallucinations (e.g., claiming a car is "red" when it is "blue") and action/relational hallucinations. This is a conscious tradeoff, but it establishes a severe capability ceiling. Future work must quantify the exact percentage of unaddressed errors on action-specific splits of CHAIR.
4. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** BRA mathematically reweights the LLM's initial candidate distribution (Top-$M$). It cannot synthesize a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window (e.g., rank $>M$), establishing a hard theoretical ceiling on all post-hoc logits adjustment strategies.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines Bounded Resonance Anchoring, a rigorously bounded framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By mapping embedding asymmetry risks, replacing failure-prone global pooling with threshold-gated Top-$k$ pooling, and engineering robust language protection via VASM BPE inheritance, we establish a technically precise foundation for decode-time visual control. The claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol spanning Hallucination, Structure, and Local Evidence chains, awaiting full empirical execution.
