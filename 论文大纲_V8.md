# Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring: Escaping the Pooling Paradox in High-Resolution Image and Video MLLMs

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual or temporal evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate thousands of high-resolution spatial or temporal tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D/3D-RoPE coordinates necessary for dense grounding (e.g., Document VQA) and specific frame localization (e.g., Video Action Recognition). In this paper, we propose the mathematical framework and evaluation blueprint for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention operating directly in the terminal logits space via localized visual matching. 

To resolve critical mathematical flaws in prior logits-based interventions and maintain strict baseline fairness, BRA introduces three structural upgrades: (1) **Dual-Track Modality Calibration ($\Phi$)**, offering both a strictly zero-shot identity mapping to preserve fair comparisons with existing training-free methods, and a lightweight contrastive variant for calibrated domains; (2) **Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**, which scales logarithmically with dynamic visual resolutions ($T \times H \times W$) to isolate fine-grained spatial and temporal features without the statistical vulnerability of pure max-pooling; and (3) **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, replacing brittle predictive entropy with an offline, frequency-weighted syntactic mask that robustly handles polysemy and continuous BPE fragments. We establish a rigorous 5-tier "Defense Line" experimental protocol—spanning dense OCR, temporal hallucination, and complex reasoning—designed to systematically validate the zero-pooling supremacy of BRA across continuous and dynamic-resolution MLLM architectures.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model hallucinates—producing text that is linguistically fluent but physically or temporally absent from the multimedia input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on contrastive decoding or backtracking (e.g., VCD, OPERA). While effective, they introduce significant latency overheads (Time-Per-Output-Token spikes) and VRAM bloat. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection (e.g., DoLa). However, when applied to high-resolution images or long videos, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool or PCA) thousands of visual tokens. This operation irreversibly destroys the fine-grained spatial-temporal coordinates (like 2D/3D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects, dense text, or fleeting temporal actions, leading to catastrophic failures in Document VQA or Video QA.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a localized match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial and temporal information. However, implementing this principle exposes several mathematical vulnerabilities: the topological gap between contextualized visual tokens and the text vocabulary, the extreme noise sensitivity of pure max-pooling over vast spatio-temporal volumes, and the "Entropy Trap" where contextually predictable hallucinations evade penalization.

Crucially, previous iterations of logits-space methods blurred the line between zero-shot inference and supervised fine-tuning. This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA alongside a strict fairness boundary, introducing a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Our methodology features:
1. **Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**: Transitioning from a statistically dangerous absolute max-pooling to a resolution-aware Top-$k$ mean formulation. This seamlessly handles dynamic-resolution images and video frames ($N_v = T \times H \times W$) by isolating specific regions or frames of interest without global pooling.
2. **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**: Replacing flawed predictive entropy with an offline, polysemy-aware masking mechanism based on corpus-level Part-of-Speech (POS) distributions, guaranteeing the structural protection of functional syntax and unidentifiable BPE fragments.
3. **The 5-Line Defense Protocol**: Expanding the experimental blueprint into a strict five-stage defense, explicitly testing core hallucination suppression (with length-bias controls), general capability preservation, zero-pooling supremacy on dense spatial tasks, video temporal grounding, and complex reasoning boundaries.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Post-LLM Modality Calibration ($\Phi$): The Fairness Boundary

The core hypothesis of BRA is that intervention should occur in the logits space to avoid pooling high-resolution spatial-temporal tokens. Given a candidate token set $\mathcal{C}_t = \{c_1, \dots, c_K\}$ at decoding step $t$, we measure the visual support for each candidate. 

A critical mathematical ambiguity in prior work is the exact positioning of the visual tokens. In BRA, we strictly utilize the **post-LLM contextualized hidden states** corresponding to the visual token positions, denoted as $h_L^{(v_j)}$. Because these tokens have passed through the LLM's self-attention layers alongside the text prefix, they encode both the localized visual feature (anchored by 2D/3D RoPE) and its contextual relevance.

To compute resonance with the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$, we formalize an alignment mapping $\Phi$. To maintain strict baseline fairness against training-free methods (e.g., DoLa, VCD), we explicitly decouple BRA into two variants:
1. **Zero-Shot BRA ($\text{BRA}_{zero}$)**: $\Phi$ is defined as the identity function (or the base model's default layernorm projection). This strictly relies on the LLM's intrinsic representation alignment and serves as the primary fair baseline against other inference-time interventions.
2. **Calibrated BRA ($\text{BRA}_{calib}$)**: A lightweight linear projector trained via a localized InfoNCE contrastive loss using visual grounding datasets (e.g., RefCOCO). While breaking the "training-free" constraint, this variant serves to probe the upper bound of logits-space intervention when the topological gap is explicitly bridged.

### 3.2 Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

Unlike hidden-state methods that compute a globally pooled visual vector $\bar{v} = \frac{1}{N_v} \sum h_L^{(v_j)}$ to establish an intervention direction, BRA explicitly isolates fine-grained features. Operating over high-resolution images or videos produces massive spatio-temporal token volumes defined by $N_v = T \times H \times W$. Relying on a pure $\max_{j}$ operator creates extreme statistical vulnerability, where a hallucinated text token is highly likely to find spurious high cosine similarity with at least one background noise patch across hundreds of frames. Conversely, mean-pooling destroys temporal action localization.

To resolve this, we formulate **Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance**. Modern MLLMs (e.g., InternVL, LLaVA-NeXT-Video) utilize dynamic cropping and varying frame counts, meaning $N_v$ fluctuates drastically. We define an adaptive $k$ that scales proportionally to the total spatio-temporal sequence length:
$$
k = \max(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot (T \times H \times W) \rceil)
$$
where $\rho$ is a fractional threshold (e.g., $\rho = 0.01$) and $k_{min} \ge 2$. We then compute the temperature-scaled mean resonance of these highly localized patches:

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{\cos\left(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)})\right)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$

For video inputs, if an action occurs in a single frame, the Top-$k$ tokens will naturally cluster around that specific $T_i$, bypassing the need for explicit temporal decay while avoiding the catastrophic signal dilution of temporal mean-pooling.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty 

To map the raw resonance scores into stable logits modifications, we compute the relative evidence position within the Top-$K$ candidates:

$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$

The final logits are reshaped via a unified **Relative Resonance Penalty**:

$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

where $\alpha$ is a global penalty strength, and $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)]$ is the probabilistically-weighted semantic mask. Tokens with high relative visual support ($\hat S(c) \approx 1$) receive near-zero penalty, while ungrounded tokens are suppressed.

### 3.4 Escaping the Entropy Trap: Probabilistic VASM

Applying visual resonance blindly at the terminal token level causes semantic collapse for multi-token entities and destroys functional syntax. Previous iterations attempted to use predictive entropy $H_t$ to scale the penalty. This creates an **"Entropy Trap"**: strong language priors inherently produce low-entropy predictions for predictable *hallucinated* objects, turning off the visual penalty for the most stubborn hallucinations.

We abandon runtime statistical heuristics in favor of mathematical guarantees via **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**. Recognizing that static binary $\{0, 1\}$ assignments fail for polysemous words (e.g., "lead" as a noun vs. verb), we pre-compute a continuous syntactic weight $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \in [0, 1]$ based on the probability distribution of POS tags in a large corpus (e.g., the C4 dataset):

$$
\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = \sum_{pos} P(pos \mid c) \cdot \gamma_{pos}
$$

1. **Semantic Entities ($\gamma_{pos} = 1.0$):** Nouns, Verbs, and Adjectives. 
2. **Functional Syntax ($\gamma_{pos} = 0.0$):** Prepositions, conjunctions, punctuation.
3. **BPE Fallback Protection:** Sub-words that cannot be reliably POS-tagged in isolation (e.g., LLaMA/Qwen tokens lacking a leading space like `##ing`) are explicitly forced to $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] = 0.0$. This prevents the fracturing of continuous BPE fragments.

This guarantees structural preservation of the tokenizer's syntax with $O(1)$ runtime overhead, offering a mathematically rigorous, albeit probabilistically hedged, solution to contextual polysemy.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

To empirically validate the structural advantages of BRA and address the implicit baseline fairness contract, we outline a rigorous, hypothesis-driven 5-Tier Defense Line Evaluation Protocol. This protocol systematically isolates hallucination suppression, spatial-temporal preservation, and general capability integrity across continuous and dynamic-resolution MLLMs.

### 4.1 Defense Line 1: Core Hallucination Suppression & Length-Bias Control
**Objective:** Prove that BRA mitigates object hallucinations without merely truncating outputs—a common artifact in hidden-state methods.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** POPE, CHAIR (MSCOCO).
- **Baselines:** Base Decoding, VCD, OPERA, DoLa. 
- **Key Metric:** Alongside standard Accuracy/F1 and CHAIR$_I$/CHAIR$_S$, we mandate reporting the **Average Generated Length (AGL)**. 
- **Hypothesis:** Hidden-state methods (e.g., DoLa) often inflate CHAIR scores by forcing premature `<EOS>` generation. We expect $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ to significantly lower hallucination rates while maintaining an AGL comparable to the Base model, proving true visual grounding rather than length collapse.

### 4.2 Defense Line 2: General Intelligence & Language Prior Integrity
**Objective:** Ensure that penalizing logits based on visual matching does not lobotomize the model's general conversational ability or instructional compliance.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME.
- **Hypothesis:** By employing Probabilistic VASM, functional syntax and non-visual reasoning tokens (e.g., logic connectors) are shielded ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \approx 0$). Consequently, $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ is expected to maintain or slightly improve overall MMBench/MME scores, avoiding the general capability degradation often seen in aggressive contrastive decoding methods.

### 4.3 Defense Line 3: The "Zero-Pooling" Supremacy on Dense Spatial Tasks
**Objective:** Definitively expose the "Pooling Paradox" and prove the necessity of localized Top-$k$ resonance for high-resolution 2D-RoPE retention.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** FREAK (specifically the OCR, Position, and Counting subsets) and DocVQA.
- **Ablation Matrix:** Compare Base, DoLa, $\text{BRA}_{MeanPool}$, and $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ (with Adaptive Top-$k$).
- **Hypothesis:** While DoLa performs adequately on natural images (Defense Line 1), it will show severe degradation on FREAK's dense OCR and Position tasks due to spatial pooling. $\text{BRA}_{zero}$'s Adaptive Top-$k$ is expected to dramatically outperform DoLa and $\text{BRA}_{MeanPool}$, theoretically preserving the exact spatial coordinates required to read tiny text or count multiple small objects.

### 4.4 Defense Line 4: Spatio-Temporal Grounding in Video Narrative
**Objective:** Validate that the Adaptive Top-$k$ framework seamlessly extends across the temporal dimension ($T \times H \times W$) without destroying temporal action localization.
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** VIDHALLUC and Video-MME.
- **Setup:** Evaluate a video-native MLLM (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT-Video) using $\text{BRA}_{zero}$.
- **Hypothesis:** Traditional temporal pooling averages out rapid, single-frame actions, causing temporal hallucinations. Because Adaptive Top-$k$ dynamically clusters around high-resonance tokens regardless of their $t$-index, $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ will successfully suppress action hallucinations in VIDHALLUC, proving its viability as a unified multimedia (Image+Video) intervention.

### 4.5 Defense Line 5 (The Boundary Test): Complex Reasoning Verification
**Objective:** Test the absolute limits of logits-space intervention where Language Inertia is actually *beneficial* (e.g., reciting complex external knowledge triggered by an image).
**Protocol:**
- **Benchmarks:** MMMU (Hard Subset).
- **Hypothesis:** On deep reasoning tasks where the answer relies heavily on internalized parametric knowledge rather than direct visual pointing, visual resonance scores might be inherently low. We hypothesize that Probabilistic VASM's protection of functional logic tokens will prevent catastrophic failure, keeping $\text{BRA}_{zero}$'s performance on par with the Base model, ensuring no "mis-kill" of valid reasoning pathways.

## 5. Discussion & Limitations

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to localized logits-space resonance anchoring addresses a structural bottleneck in high-resolution image and video MLLMs. By explicitly exposing the "Pooling Paradox", BRA provides a pathway to intervene safely without destroying the fragile 2D/3D spatial-temporal encodings. Furthermore, the decoupling of $\text{BRA}_{zero}$ and $\text{BRA}_{calib}$ establishes a rigorous fairness boundary for decoding-time evaluations.

**Limitations & Future Work:**
1. **Extreme Polysemy Edge Cases:** While Probabilistic VASM mathematically hedges corpus-level polysemy via expected values ($\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)]$), highly contextual deviations remain a challenge. For instance, the token "like" (verb vs. preposition) relies on statistical averaging; if a specific prompt severely skews against the corpus norm, VASM may over- or under-penalize. 
2. **Computational Overhead of Large $K$:** For extremely long videos, even with adaptive $k$, computing localized cosine similarity against a massive candidate vocabulary $\mathcal{C}_t$ introduces overhead compared to pure Base decoding, though it remains strictly Pareto-superior to multi-pass contrastive methods like VCD.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until the 5-Tier Defense Protocol is fully executed, we strictly limit our claims to the theoretical soundness of the framework. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose the specific spatial-temporal bottlenecks of MLLM interventions, offering a concrete mathematical blueprint to validate the supremacy of adaptive Top-$k$ decoding across both static and dynamic multimedia.
