# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies such as contrastive decoding, attention control, and layer-contrast methods are important competitive baselines, but they do not directly provide a general framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual states. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a bounded candidate window, making the method compatible with fine-grained natural-scene grounding tasks such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via deterministic root-token lookup and BPE continuation inheritance. To keep comparisons honest, we explicitly separate a strict zero-shot probe (`TLRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated variant (`TLRA_calib`). `TLRA_calib` is a **base-model-frozen plug-in**, not a training-free method in the same sense as `VCD`, `DoLa`, or `OPERA`.

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize the paper around a strict experimental contract: a zero-shot semantic-validity pilot, three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction, structure and reasoning preservation, and local-evidence value, plus explicit efficiency and failure-boundary audits. The core empirical contract remains **image-centric**, which lets us validate the decode-time mechanism under the most controllable multimodal setting. We also enforce two crucial honesty constraints: `DocVQA` is treated as a **negative control** under the OCR concession, and all local-evidence claims must pass a strict **parity ablation** against `TLRA_MeanPool` using the exact same calibrator. To better align with ACM Multimedia without overstating scope, we retain only a **bounded secondary video pilot** as exploratory appendix evidence. The resulting paper is a positive, falsifiable proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the relevant evidence is often local rather than global. These are central multimodal settings in which visual grounding, language generation, and fine-grained perception interact directly.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate competitive baselines, but none of them by themselves define a direct answer to the following question:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

That question is the center of this paper. We no longer rely on claiming that mainstream baselines share one mistaken mechanism. Instead, we advance a narrower and more defensible positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided that it also includes explicit structure-preserving safeguards.

Executing this idea is nontrivial for four reasons. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and output logits do not naturally live in the same space. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc logits intervention can only reweight candidates that the base model already keeps alive. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression can damage function words, syntax, and long entities. Fourth, **system cost**: local support extraction can become expensive at high visual token counts.

TLRA therefore centers on three design commitments:

1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention** rather than model retraining.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance** as the main local evidence extractor.
3. **VASM** as the structure-preserving protection layer.

At the same time, this benchmark also fixes several boundaries that became necessary after repeated reviewer/scientist iterations. First, `TLRA_zero` is treated only as a **viability probe**. If it fails the semantic-validity pilot, the paper contracts toward `TLRA_calib` rather than inventing excuses. Second, `TLRA_calib` is explicitly framed as a **base-model-frozen plug-in** that introduces external generic visual-text priors; therefore, the paper must isolate the value of token-local routing through strict parity ablations against global pooling variants that use the exact same calibrator. Third, TLRA deliberately accepts an **OCR concession**: because VASM must remain structurally conservative, document/OCR-heavy tasks are not part of the main capability claim. They appear only as safety-oriented negative controls.

To prevent future drift, the benchmark constrains scope in a disciplined but MM-compatible way. The **primary scientific contract** is image-centric, because image-language MLLMs already constitute a legitimate multimodal setting and provide the cleanest environment for validating a new decode-time mechanism. At the same time, the benchmark does not ignore broader multimedia settings: it reserves a **secondary video pilot** to test whether the same token-local idea shows early transferability to spatio-temporal inputs. This lets the paper stay scientifically tight without presenting itself as a single-modality study or, conversely, overclaiming a full image-video unification that the evidence cannot yet support.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib`

The first design principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. If the method requires a learned projector to align visual states with lexical representations, it should not be reported as though it were purely zero-shot. We therefore explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks:

1. **`TLRA_zero`**: a strict decode-time probe where the model-native readout is used directly on contextualized final-layer visual states.
2. **`TLRA_calib`**: a lightweight calibrated variant where a small projection `Phi_calib` is trained to improve visual-to-lexical alignment while the base MLLM remains frozen.

This distinction is central to the paper's credibility. `TLRA_zero` is the fair comparison against zero-shot decoding baselines; `TLRA_calib` is the calibrated upper bound. If the semantic-validity pilot shows that `TLRA_zero` is effectively noise, the main paper should pivot to the scientifically useful conclusion that lightweight calibration is the minimum requirement for bridging embedding asymmetry in this family of models.

To avoid misleading fairness claims, the benchmark also locks three additional rules:

- `TLRA_calib` must be labeled as **base-model-frozen**, not as training-free.
- Any claim that the calibrator does not leak external priors must be audited through a **Category Leakage Audit** rather than impossible statements like "zero domain overlap."
- Any local-evidence claim attributed to `TLRA_calib` must be tested against `TLRA_MeanPool` using the **exact same** `Phi_calib` weights.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, TLRA first restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set:

$$
\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig}),
$$

where $M \ll |V|$ is a small candidate window derived from the original logits.

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the contextualized visual states from the final layer, and let the visual token volume be

$$
N_v = H \times W.
$$

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes candidate-wise local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule:

$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big),
$$

followed by

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

Here, `score(v_j, c)` is the patch-level lexical support for candidate `c`, obtained either directly from the model-native readout in `TLRA_zero` or from the calibrated projection in `TLRA_calib`. `tau_sim` is a temperature parameter controlling resonance sharpness.

This design preserves locality while reducing the noise sensitivity of pure max matching. It also makes the paper's claim narrower and cleaner: the primary goal is to test whether candidate-wise localized evidence is more faithful than coarse aggregation on dense grounding tasks, while a secondary video pilot can later examine whether this locality principle survives the shift from spatial to spatio-temporal inputs.

The benchmark further requires that the system story be reported as a **Pareto frontier** rather than a hand-wavy efficiency claim. Candidate-window sensitivity over `M ∈ {10, 50, 100}` should therefore be read as part of the scientific contract, not a post-hoc engineering appendix.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a stable logits intervention, TLRA computes a relative evidence score over the bounded candidate set:

$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$

The final candidate logits are adjusted as

$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

This formulation emphasizes suppression of weakly supported candidates relative to stronger local competitors, rather than introducing a separate reward branch that can destabilize decoding.

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention can easily suppress useful structure together with hallucinations. TLRA therefore introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** as a deterministic protection mechanism:

- `gamma(c) = 1` for tokens eligible for visual grounding intervention.
- `gamma(c) = 0` for tokens that should remain structurally protected.

At benchmark level, VASM operates in two stages:

1. **Root-token lookup:** a precomputed lexical table assigns intervention eligibility to visually grounded token groups while excluding function words and other structurally sensitive tokens.
2. **BPE continuation inheritance:** continuation fragments inherit the mask value of their root token instead of being treated independently.

This is important for long entity names and tokenizer-dependent subword splits. The key point is not that VASM solves every ambiguity problem; it is that TLRA without VASM should be measurably worse on reasoning- and structure-sensitive benchmarks.

At the same time, the benchmark now makes two additional concessions explicit:

- **OCR concession:** because VASM must remain structurally conservative, arbitrary OCR strings are expected to receive near-zero intervention coverage. `DocVQA` is therefore a negative control for safety, not a main positive-evidence dataset.
- **Suffix-collapse risk:** if a prefix token is intervened on and the base model fails to continue the intended entity coherently, that failure must be measured explicitly rather than waved away. The paper should therefore audit suffix completion via an exact-match style BPE completion metric and targeted failure cases.

### 3.5 What TLRA Does Not Claim

This benchmark deliberately avoids several overclaims:

- `TLRA_zero` is not assumed to work equally well on all architectures.
- `TLRA_calib` is not presented as "still zero-shot."
- VASM is not claimed to solve all polysemy or context sensitivity.
- TLRA is not presented as a universal replacement for `VCD`, `OPERA`, or `DoLa`.
- The main contribution is **not** claimed as a full image-video unified theory.
- TLRA cannot recover a correct token if that token is absent from the initial Top-$M$ candidate set.
- TLRA is **not** claimed as an OCR/document-grounding method under the current VASM contract.

These boundaries are part of the paper's honesty, not a weakness to hide.

## 4. Experimental Design

The experimental story is organized around one sanity pilot, three main evidence chains, one efficiency-and-boundary audit, and one bounded secondary video pilot.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Zero-Shot Semantic Validity Pilot

**Goal:** determine whether `TLRA_zero` is a meaningful zero-shot branch or merely a noisy probe.

- Pilot benchmark: patch-level lexical overlap on a held-out image subset with object annotations.
- Output: Top-1 / Top-5 / Top-10 overlap between patch readout words and annotated object labels.
- Decision rule:
  - If overlap is clearly above random, `TLRA_zero` remains in the main comparison.
  - If overlap is near-random, the benchmark contracts toward `TLRA_calib`, and the paper explicitly states that lightweight calibration is the minimum viable bridge across the embedding asymmetry.

This pilot is not a cosmetic add-on. It determines the admissible scope of the paper's main claim.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction

**Goal:** test whether TLRA reduces hallucination without merely shortening responses, and whether the gain comes from token-local routing rather than just from calibrator priors.

- Benchmarks: `POPE`, `CHAIR`
- Baselines: Base, `VCD`, `OPERA`, `DoLa`, `TLRA_zero` (if viable), `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`
- Optional stronger internal control: `TLRA_RandomK`
- Mandatory metrics: `AGL`, `AGL StdDev`
- Mandatory audit: **Category Leakage Audit** on entities seen vs. unseen in calibrator pseudo-labels
- Table 1 must report hallucination metrics side by side with output length and intervention coverage.

This line establishes that any gain is not merely a length-collapse artifact. If `CHAIR` improves only because `AGL` collapses, the claim must be weakened accordingly. If `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` fails to separate from `TLRA_MeanPool`, the contribution should also be weakened accordingly.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Goal:** verify that local-evidence intervention does not degrade general multimodal understanding, that VASM is genuinely necessary, and that the OCR concession really behaves like a negative control rather than a hidden capability claim.

- Benchmarks: `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard)`, `DocVQA` (negative control)
- Main ablation: Base vs. `TLRA_full` vs. `TLRA_no_VASM`
- Mandatory diagnostics:
  - `DocVQA` intervention coverage should remain near zero under the OCR concession
  - BPE continuation / suffix completion success should be tracked through an exact-match style metric
- Table 2 should emphasize both overall capability preservation and the specific drop caused by removing VASM.

This evidence chain turns VASM from a side mechanism into a central scientific contribution. If `TLRA_no_VASM` does not clearly degrade reasoning or structure-sensitive behavior, then the current VASM claim is too strong and should be contracted. If `DocVQA` shows significant trigger coverage, the OCR concession must be reconsidered or rewritten more carefully.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** test whether token-local evidence outperforms coarse internal aggregation on dense grounding tasks.

- Benchmarks: `FREAK`
- Preferred breakdown: spatial relations / object existence / counting
- Primary ablation: `TLRA_MeanPool` vs. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`
- Optional stronger internal control: `TLRA_RandomK`
- Secondary reference baseline: `DoLa`
- Critical parity rule: `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must use the **same** `Phi_calib`
- Table 3 and Figure 1 are mandatory.

The key claim here is narrow but central: on tasks that depend on local visual evidence, adaptive local reweighting should be more faithful than coarse aggregation. If `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` does not clearly beat `TLRA_MeanPool`, the local-evidence thesis should be weakened.

### 4.5 Efficiency and Failure-Boundary Audits

Before any strong practicality claim, TLRA must also report:

- `TTFT`
- `TPOT` / `ITL`
- `tokens_per_second`
- `peak_vram`
- latency scaling against increasing $N_v$
- sensitivity over `alpha`, `rho`, `k_min`, and `M`
- fallback-activation behavior under different prompt families
- explicit out-of-candidate failure cases
- explicit suffix-collapse cases

These audits define whether the method is merely conceptually interesting or actually usable. Failure-case analysis is especially important: the paper should show examples where the correct visually grounded token lies outside the initial Top-$M$ window and is therefore unrecoverable by any post-hoc reweighting strategy of this kind.

### 4.6 Secondary Video Pilot

**Goal:** test whether the token-local intervention principle shows early transferability to spatio-temporal multimodal decoding, without elevating video to a co-equal main claim.

- Candidate benchmarks: `VIDHALLUC`, `Video-MME`
- Status: exploratory appendix evidence only; does not define the core acceptance contract
- Required outputs if run:
  - a small pilot table
  - temporal concentration or frame-localization diagnostics where feasible
  - a clear go / no-go judgment on whether video should stay only in appendix or future work

Interpretation rule:

- Positive pilot evidence strengthens the paper's multimedia relevance.
- Negative pilot evidence contracts only the extension claim, not the main image-centric contribution.

This is the intended ACM MM compromise: keep the paper's central scientific contract tight, while still showing that the proposed decode-time idea is not artificially blind to broader multimedia settings.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is not the one with the biggest narrative, but the one with the cleanest scientific contract:

- it is a decode-time method
- it uses token-local visual evidence
- it preserves structure through VASM
- it separates zero-shot probing from calibrated upper bounds
- it isolates routing gains from calibrator gains through parity ablations
- it treats OCR/document tasks as negative controls under the current safety contract
- it is judged through effectiveness, side effects, local-evidence value, and efficiency
- it includes only a bounded secondary video pilot rather than an unsupported image-video unified theory

The main unresolved risks remain:

1. **Alignment risk:** `TLRA_zero` may fail if final-layer visual states do not align meaningfully with lexical readout.
2. **Calibrator-risk:** external priors injected by `Phi_calib` may dominate the story unless parity ablations clearly separate them from token-local routing gains.
3. **Structure risk:** VASM may still miss difficult contextual or tokenizer-specific edge cases.
4. **Suffix risk:** token-level intervention on prefixes may perturb autoregressive continuation and create suffix-collapse failure cases.
5. **Efficiency risk:** Adaptive Top-$k$ must prove itself under realistic high-resolution token loads.
6. **Transfer risk:** the image-centric mechanism may not transfer cleanly to spatio-temporal video settings; this is why video remains a secondary appendix pilot rather than a main claim.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)

Until the protocol is fully executed, the paper should claim only:

- a positive decode-time framework for token-local visual evidence reweighting
- a structure-preserving masking mechanism for safe logits intervention
- a fair `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib` evaluation split
- a strict parity-based test of whether local routing beats coarse aggregation under the same calibrator
- a falsifiable image-centric evaluation protocol covering effectiveness, side effects, local-evidence value, and efficiency
- a bounded secondary video pilot for exploratory multimodal transfer assessment

It should **not yet** claim universal superiority, architecture-invariant zero-shot success, OCR/document-grounding competence, or a full image-video unified theory.
