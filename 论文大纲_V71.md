# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for Physical Entity Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa)—are highly competitive baselines for modulating global generation trajectories, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework specifically designed for physical entity grounding in natural scenes, which reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three operational commitments. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual patches via a lightweight, base-model-frozen calibrator. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Prompt-Conditioned Pruning** bounds the decode-time overhead, supplemented by an entropy-driven fallback mechanism to preserve dense visual evaluation during long-horizon generation. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via a deterministic, WordNet-backed whitelist. To address modern BPE fragmentation, VASM employs a tokenizer-aware subword boundary heuristic, intervening on valid physical entity prefixes while relying on the base LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete subword suffixes. Crucially, VASM explicitly bypasses arbitrary text strings, intentionally trading off performance on text-rich OCR tasks to guarantee grammatical safety.

We organize our evaluation protocol around a strict experimental contract: three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction (controlling for length collapse and mandating a rigorous Vocabulary Leakage Audit), structure preservation (quantifying suffix survival via a formalized BPE Completion Success Rate across intervention strengths, utilizing document-understanding as an exact negative control), and isolating the distinct value of local adaptive selection against both global pooling (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and random patch selection (`TLRA_RandomK`). The resulting blueprint offers a positive, falsifiable, and rigorously bounded proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and attribute binding, where the required evidence is distinctly local.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate, highly competitive baselines that effectively modulate global generation trajectories. However, they do not directly answer the following positive hypothesis:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment for physical entities without damaging language structure, corrupting subword KV-cache momentum, or degrading downstream reasoning ability?**

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to embedding asymmetry, candidate limitation at the decoding head, the structural collapse risk of penalizing functional syntax, and the prohibitive system cost of computing patch-level support at high resolutions ($N_v > 4096$). 

We explicitly restrict the scope of our motivation: TLRA is designed for grounding physical entities in natural scenes. It actively trades off intervention capability on text-rich images (OCR) and abstract reasoning to ensure absolute grammatical safety. TLRA centers on three design commitments to address the core challenges:

1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention via a Base-Model-Frozen Plug-in.** We bridge embedding asymmetry using a calibrator (`TLRA_calib`) trained on generic noun chunks. We clarify our taxonomy upfront: unlike purely training-free methods, TLRA introduces generic external distribution data, though the base MLLM remains 100% frozen. Through strict parity ablations against global pooling (`TLRA_MeanPool`) using the exact same calibrator, we mathematically isolate the value of token-local decoding from this external prior.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Entropy-Driven Fallback.** By filtering patches based on prefill-stage prompt attention and bounding evaluations to the Top-$M$ logits, TLRA controls decode-time latency. Acknowledging the "prefill pruning blindspot"—where visually grounded objects absent from the prompt emerge late in open-ended generation—we incorporate a dynamic fallback mechanism that reverts to dense visual evaluation when attention entropy spikes.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking).** Built on a strict WordNet whitelist, VASM deterministically bypasses intervention for structural and abstract tokens. We formalize two critical operational concessions: the *OCR paradox* (VASM natively ignores arbitrary text strings, serving as a zero-trigger negative control on document tasks) and *Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum*. VASM triggers on valid subword prefixes but relies on the base LLM to complete suffixes. To evaluate the systemic risk of "BPE stuttering," we explicitly quantify suffix survival rates.

To maintain scientific rigor, we commit to a definitive evaluation protocol strictly mapped to 2D image-centric benchmarks, supplemented by a bounded video pilot. We establish three distinct chains of evidence: hallucination reduction (audited for calibrator data leakage), structural preservation (proving VASM's safety via an OCR negative control and BPE survival metric), and local-evidence validation (isolating Top-$k$ anchoring against global pooling).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. A strict zero-shot readout (`TLRA_zero`) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yields near-random overlap due to massive embedding asymmetry. 

Therefore, our intervention relies on **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain fair comparisons against inference baselines, the calibration protocol is constrained and transparent about its external priors:
1. **Plug-in Location & Precomputation Cost:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_v \times |V|}$, mapping the MLLM's visual tokens into the LM head's pre-softmax vocabulary space. While $\Phi_{calib}$ acts as a lightweight plug-in during inference, its training pipeline is resource-intensive. It requires extracting spaCy noun chunks aligned to pseudo-bounding boxes via GroundingDINO, encoded with a frozen CLIP-ViT-L/14 text encoder. 
2. **Generic Data with Strict Deduplication:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained solely on a strictly limited generic dataset (a 100K subset of CC3M/Visual Genome). To prevent evaluation corruption, we enforce a strict deduplication protocol, mathematically guaranteeing no overlap with the evaluation images in POPE, CHAIR, or MMBench. $\Phi_{calib}$ is *never* trained on downstream instruction-tuning data.
3. **Frozen Base Model:** The vision encoder, vision-language connector, and LLM remain 100% frozen.

### 3.2 High-Resolution Efficiency: Prompt-Conditioned Pruning and Fallback

At decoding step $t$, evaluating the entire vocabulary against $>4096$ visual patches is computationally prohibitive. 

**1. Candidate Bounding:** We restrict intervention strictly to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=10$). We acknowledge the inherent risk here: if the base LLM suffers from a severe language prior pushing the correct visually grounded token to rank $>M$, TLRA will fail to rescue it.

**2. Prompt-Conditioned Pruning with Dynamic Fallback:** We compute aggregated cross-attention from the prefill prompt tokens to the visual sequence, pruning low-attention patches to retain an active subset $N_{active}$. 
Crucially, we explicitly acknowledge a **Prefill Pruning Blindspot**: an autoregressive model executing long-horizon generation may require visual evidence for objects never mentioned in the static prompt. Relying solely on prefill cross-attention is architecturally perilous. TLRA enforces an **Entropy-Driven Dynamic Fallback**: we measure the normalized Shannon entropy $H_{attn}$ of the sink-excluded attention weights. If $H_{attn} > \theta_{fallback}$, pruning is bypassed entirely ($N_{active} = N_v$). We calibrate $\theta_{fallback}$ strictly on a held-out VisDial split to avoid test-set data leakage.

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule over the active patches:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_{active} \rceil\Big), \quad S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{sim(\Phi_{calib}(v_j), c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a stable logits intervention, TLRA computes a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted as:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses functional syntax. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect these tokens via a binary mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0, 1\}$.

VASM is algorithmically defined using a strict WordNet fail-safe:
1. **Deterministic Whitelist Generation:** A token receives $\gamma=1$ *if and only if* its string maps to a lemma under structural synsets like `physical_entity.n.01`.
2. **Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum & Collision Avoidance:** Modern tokenizers fragment words. VASM triggers $\gamma=1$ only on the valid initial prefix, relying on the base LLM's unmodified autoregressive momentum to complete suffix tokens ($\gamma=0$). We utilize a **Tokenizer-Aware Subword Boundary Heuristic**, applying a length filter ($L_{char} \ge 4$) if the token acts as a subword prefix (e.g., lacking a start-of-word metacharacter). We acknowledge this is a heuristic calibrated to SentencePiece; boundary behaviors must be empirically validated across different tokenizer architectures (e.g., LLaMA vs. Qwen).
3. **The OCR Concession:** VASM explicitly defaults to $\gamma=0$ for out-of-vocabulary text. TLRA inherently bypasses arbitrary OCR strings, prioritizing absolute grammatical safety and fluency over hallucination intervention on out-of-dictionary text.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Rather than presenting cherry-picked subsets, our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The success of this paper hinges on the explicit validation of the following three interlocking evidence chains and sensitivity audits.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length & Leakage Checks)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces physical object hallucination, that the improvement is not an artifact of shorter responses, and that the gain stems from *local extraction* rather than calibrator memorization.
- **Table 1 Target:** `POPE` and `CHAIR` across two decoding temperatures ($T=0.2, 0.7$).
- **Baselines:** Base MLLM, `DoLa`, `VCD`, `OPERA`, **`TLRA_MeanPool`**, and **`TLRA_RandomK`**.
- **Mandatory Control Metrics:** Average Generated Length (`AGL`), **AGL Standard Deviation**, and **VASM Trigger Rate (%)**.
- **The Asterisk Rule:** Any configuration achieving SOTA CHAIR scores by collapsing AGL variance by $> 5\%$ compared to the base model will be flagged (*) as an invalid artifact.
- **The Vocabulary Leakage Audit (Table 1b):** To definitively rule out $\Phi_{calib}$ distribution leakage, we enforce a mandatory audit reporting the exact noun-chunk vocabulary overlap between the calibrator training data and POPE benchmark targets. We will explicitly stratify POPE F1 scores into `Seen in Calibrator` vs. `Unseen in Calibrator`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will yield lower `CHAIR` scores and higher `POPE` F1 than all baselines, with consistent gains across both `Seen` and `Unseen` entities.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The Negative Control)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not degrade general cognition, quantify the survival of BPE autoregressive momentum, and validate the "OCR Concession" via an explicit negative control.
- **Table 2 Target:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MMMU` (Hard), **Language Perplexity (PPL)**, and `DocVQA`.
- **Quantitative BPE Metric:** **BPE Completion Success Rate (BPE-CSR)**. BPE-CSR is procedurally defined as the percentage of instances where forcing a VASM-triggered prefix ($\gamma=1$) successfully results in the base LLM generating a valid semantic continuation (exact match or WordNet synonym) for the remaining fragments without decaying into stuttering.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_no_VASM` will exhibit severe PPL spikes and catastrophic BPE-CSR drops. `TLRA_full` must recover these scores, keeping PPL strictly $\le +5\%$ of the Base model. 
- **The Strict Negative Control:** Because VASM explicitly trades off OCR intervention for structural safety, `TLRA_full` must demonstrate a `VASM Trigger Rate` statistically indistinguishable from $0\%$ on `DocVQA`, guaranteeing zero degradation of the Base model's native reading capabilities at the cost of zero hallucination improvement on that task.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate that *token-local adaptive selection* is statistically superior to coarse visual aggregation.
- **Table 3 Target:** `FREAK` (broken down into **Spatial Relations** vs. **Object Existence**) and `Object HalBench`. 
- **Critical Parity Constraint:** `TLRA_MeanPool` ($k = N_{active}$) and `TLRA_RandomK` utilize the identical $\Phi_{calib}$ weights as `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_zero` will perform near-randomly. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must demonstrate a statistically significant margin ($p < 0.05$) over both `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` on *Object Existence*. If `TLRA_MeanPool` achieves within 1-2% of the Top-$k$ method, the core premise of "token-local resonance" is falsified.
- **Bounded Secondary Video Pilot:** To prove token-local evidence extends to temporal localization, we will execute a strictly bounded evaluation on `Video-MME` (short-clip subset). The core requirement is proving that intervention peaks exclusively during the frames where the physical entity appears.

### 4.4 Efficiency Audits, Sensitivity, and Failure Analysis

To map operational boundaries, the execution plan mandates the following diagnostic visualizations:
- **Figure 1 (BPE-CSR Distribution):** A distribution chart plotting `BPE-CSR` against different intervention strengths ($\alpha$). We will identify the precise $\alpha$ threshold where suffix survival collapses.
- **Figure 2 (Efficiency Pareto & Fallback Audit):** A Pareto frontier plotting `POPE F1` vs. `Decode Latency` across varying candidate bounds $M \in \{5, 10, 50, \text{All}\}$. We will also graph `Fallback Activation Rate` vs. sequence length to prove the system accurately reverts to dense evaluation to mitigate the prefill blindspot.
- **Figure 3 (Qualitative Failure Analysis):** Mandated visualizations of intervention limits:
  1. **BPE Stuttering Logits:** The actual top-5 token probabilities from the LM head at step $t$ (prefix) and $t+1$ (suffix), showing how KV-cache momentum fails after an unnaturally forced prefix selection (e.g., "straw-straw-berry").
  2. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** An instance where the correct physical token was ranked $> M$ by the base model's strong language prior, rendering it entirely invisible to TLRA.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its empirical execution. The scientific contribution is a positive, decode-time framework leveraging token-local visual evidence alongside VASM to prevent structural collapse. However, severe rigidities define its operational boundaries:

1. **The OCR Paradox and Claim Scoping:** VASM's strict reliance on a dictionary whitelist ensures grammatical safety but explicitly sacrifices the ability to intervene on text-rich images, diagrams, or arbitrary OCR strings. TLRA is strictly a mitigation strategy for *physical entities in natural scenes*, not a general-purpose multimodal hallucination cure.
2. **The Calibrator Asymmetry and Leakage Risk:** While TLRA operates as a base-model-frozen plug-in, the $\Phi_{calib}$ projector relies on generic external data. The scientific validity of TLRA relies entirely on the successful execution of the Vocabulary Leakage Audit and the `TLRA_MeanPool` parity ablations to isolate local extraction mechanics from generic dataset memorization.
3. **WordNet Brittleness:** VASM's deterministic protection mechanism is strictly bound to the English WordNet database (`physical_entity.n.01`). It is profoundly brittle to novel compounds, modern slang, and non-English reasoning, fundamentally capping its multilingual scalability.
4. **Prefill Pruning Blindspot and Entropy Fallback Hypersensitivity:** The assumption that prefill cross-attention reliably indicates late-generation visual relevance is inherently flawed. For long-horizon multimodal reasoning, our entropy-driven fallback acts as a necessary band-aid. If $\theta_{fallback}$ is tuned too low, TLRA degrades into computationally prohibitive dense evaluation; if too high, latent evidence is permanently deleted.
5. **The KV-Cache Alteration and BPE Stuttering:** TLRA relies on the base LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete BPE fragments. Artificially suppressing competing logits at step $t$ alters the hidden states added to the KV-cache. If the prefix intervention forces an unnatural selection, suffix generation mathematically collapses, leading to observable "BPE stuttering".
6. **Candidate Bounding Risks ($M=10$):** Restricting evaluation to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ bounds latency but introduces a hard failure mode. If the base LLM's language prior is overwhelmingly strong and pushes the visually grounded token to rank 50, TLRA will fail to inspect or promote it.
