# Breaking the Language Inertia: Mitigating MLLM Hallucinations via Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from hallucinations, generating text disconnected from visual evidence due to overwhelming language priors. Existing hidden-state interventions often fall into the "Pooling Paradox": to apply unified directional corrections, they aggregate high-resolution visual tokens, inadvertently destroying the fine-grained 2D-RoPE spatial coordinates necessary for dense grounding. In this paper, we propose a formalized methodology and comprehensive evaluation protocol for **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a zero-pooling, decode-time intervention operating directly in the logits space. 

To address critical topological and linguistic constraints, BRA incorporates three core mechanisms: (1) **Relative Resonance Reshaping**, which shifts from absolute logit scaling to an additive shift based on local evidence calibration; (2) **Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)**, an explicit damping mechanism to protect BPE fragments and functional syntax from semantic collapse; and (3) **Camera-Motion-Suppressed Temporal Differencing**, substituting naive frame subtraction with localized cross-frame feature representations to isolate true action semantics. Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art results, this manuscript rigorously outlines the mathematical formulations and a 6-stage experimental protocol—including a foundational Modality Alignment Probe—designed to empirically validate the feasibility, stability, and system overhead of zero-pooling interventions in high-resolution MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

A persistent challenge in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) is the competitive imbalance between language priors and visual evidence. During autoregressive decoding, when the statistical inertia of the language model overpowers the conditioned visual context, the model hallucinates—producing text that is linguistically fluent but physically absent from the visual input.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies generally bifurcate into two routes. The first relies on contrastive decoding, backtracking, or multi-path sampling (e.g., VCD, OPERA, DoLa). While effective, they introduce significant latency overheads and VRAM spikes, making them prohibitive for real-time AgentOS applications. The second route employs representation engineering or hidden-space projection. However, when applied to high-resolution MLLMs, these methods consistently encounter what we term the **Pooling Paradox**: to compute a unified visual direction for hidden-state subtraction, they forcefully pool (e.g., MeanPool or PCA) thousands of high-resolution visual tokens. This operation irreversibly destroys the fine-grained spatial coordinates (like 2D-RoPE) that the model relies on to ground small objects and dense text.

The fundamental premise of Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA) is to bypass the hidden space entirely and intervene at the terminal logits space. By computing a zero-shot max-pooling match between candidate tokens and un-pooled visual tokens, BRA preserves exact spatial information. However, implementing this principle exposes several critical mathematical and engineering vulnerabilities: the unproven assumption of modality alignment in the unembedding space, the catastrophic fragmentation of continuous BPE sub-words, and the susceptibility of naive temporal differencing to camera motion in video tasks.

This paper establishes the rigorous mathematical framework of BRA-V2 and a comprehensive, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol to systematically test its validity. Our contributions are strictly methodological and structural:
1. We formulate **Relative Resonance Reshaping**, mitigating the hyper-sensitivity of logits scaling by introducing a conservative, normalized additive shift.
2. We design **Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI)** to structurally protect BPE continuation tokens and abstract logic words from unjust visual penalization.
3. We refine the video extension into a **Camera-Motion-Suppressed Temporal Difference** module, utilizing localized relative features rather than absolute linear subtraction.
4. We define a stringent **6-stage Evaluation Protocol**, anchored by a Modality Alignment Probe, to explicitly prove the mathematical assumptions and benchmark the zero-pooling supremacy before final deployment.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Inference-Time Intervention for MLLMs

针对 MLLM 幻觉的推理期方法大致可分为三类。第一类是对比解码，如 VCD 通过干净图像和噪声图像两路前向的 logits 差异进行校正。第二类是注意力级回溯方法，如 OPERA 通过监控注意力聚集模式，对过度信任的生成路径施加惩罚。第三类是层间对比方法，如 DoLa 通过成熟层与未成熟层的分布差异来进行对数空间干预。

这些方法的共同问题在于：它们往往以更高的推理代价换取更保守的输出。在高吞吐或实时系统中，这种代价很容易超过可部署边界。对于网页代理、GUI grounding 或长视频推理场景，几十毫秒到数秒级的额外延迟会直接破坏 OODA 闭环。

### 2.2 Representation Engineering and the Pooling Paradox

与解码搜索不同，隐藏状态干预强调从表征几何中找到“真实性方向”或“偏见方向”，并在推理期做最小幅度修正。这类方法在纯文本上富有启发性，但在高分辨率 MLLM 上遭遇新的结构性问题：视觉分支不再是一个紧凑的全局向量，而是保留大规模空间排列的 token 网格。如果为了做单向量干预而先进行均值池化或 token 压缩，那么最需要保留的局部位置与细粒度 OCR 线索就会在干预前被破坏。

我们将这种问题称为 pooling paradox：**为了修正 hallucination，先破坏 grounding。** BRA 的“zero-pooling logits-side intervention”正是对这一悖论的回应。

### 2.3 Temporal Hallucination in Video MLLMs

视频场景中的幻觉不只是“看见了不存在的物体”，还包括“看见了不存在的动作”。近年的工作已经开始强调动作-场景混淆、时间顺序错误以及长程事件因果失配。一个常见但危险的简化，是将时间维度扁平化为静态特征平均，或将相邻帧在绝对坐标上直接相减。这样的设计在存在 camera motion、zoom、pan 或主体位移时，很容易把背景变化误当成动作证据。

因此，视频版 BRA 的目标不应是简单叠加一条 temporal branch，而应是构造一个尽量保守、尽量抑制公共运动噪声的 temporal resonance。

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 The Modality Gap and Resonance Formulation

The core hypothesis of BRA is that intervention should occur in the logits space to avoid pooling high-resolution spatial tokens. Given a candidate token set $\mathcal{C}_t = \{c_1, \dots, c_K\}$ at decoding step $t$, we aim to measure the visual support for each candidate without altering the underlying hidden states. 

A critical vulnerability in naive logits-space intervention is the assumption of topological orthogonality—specifically, whether the last-layer visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ reside in the exact same semantic manifold as the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$. MLLMs align modalities at the input projector, but visual tokens are not explicitly unembedded during training. To formalize this rigorously, we define the raw resonance score $S_{raw}(c)$ through an alignment mapping $\Phi$:

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \max_{j \in \{1 \dots N_v\}} \cos(W_{vocab}[c], \Phi(h_L^{(v_j)}))
$$

where $\Phi$ can either be the identity mapping $I$ (under the hypothesis that late-layer self-attention naturally projects visual keys into a shared vocabulary-compatible space, to be verified by our Protocol 0 Probe) or a lightweight, frozen cross-attention projection mimicking the LLM's final query-key interaction.

### 3.2 Relative Resonance Reshaping (Additive Shift)

Directly multiplying the logits by absolute resonance scores introduces extreme hyper-sensitivity, especially since original logits $L_{orig}$ can vary drastically in magnitude, and $S_{raw}$ is bounded by $[-1, 1]$. To stabilize the intervention, BRA shifts from multiplicative scaling to a normalized **Additive Shift**.

We first compute the relative evidence position within the Top-K candidates:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}_{\tau}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final logits are then reshaped via additive penalties and rewards:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) + \alpha \cdot \eta(c) \cdot \hat S(c) - \beta \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
where $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are controllable additive constants (e.g., tuned within $[0.5, 2.0]$), and $\eta(c), \gamma(c)$ are token-aware damping coefficients defined by the SMI module. This formulation ensures that intervention acts as a local ranking calibrator rather than a global magnitude disruptor.

### 3.3 Sub-word Momentum Integration (SMI) for BPE

Applying visual resonance strictly at the isolated token level causes semantic collapse for multi-token entities (BPE fragmentation) and destroys functional syntax. SMI categorizes the vocabulary into functional types and applies targeted damping coefficients $\gamma(c)$ (penalty weight) and $\eta(c)$ (reward weight):

1. **Visual Entities/Action Words:** $\gamma = 1.0, \eta = 1.0$. Full resonance mapping.
2. **Continuation Sub-words (BPE fragments):** Recognized via tokenizer prefix rules (e.g., missing space prefix `Ġ`). $\gamma \to 0.1, \eta \to 0.5$. This prevents the model from heavily penalizing the middle of a valid entity just because the fragment lacks independent visual meaning.
3. **Function & Abstract Logic Words:** $\gamma \to 0.0, \eta \to 0.0$. Words like "therefore" or "not" are frozen ($L_{final} \approx L_{orig}$), ensuring absolute syntax preservation.

### 3.4 Camera-Motion-Suppressed Temporal Differencing

For video hallucination, a temporal pool $Z_\Delta$ is required. However, naive linear subtraction $h_L^{(t)} - h_L^{(t-1)}$ in a highly non-linear space captures camera pan and background shifts as "actions". BRA mitigates this by applying a localized relative difference. 

We redefine the temporal feature of patch $j$ at time $t$ by subtracting the local spatial neighborhood mean of the difference, effectively acting as a spatial Difference-of-Gaussians (DoG) filter in the feature space:
$$
\Delta_{raw}^{(t, j)} = h_L^{(t, j)} - h_L^{(t-1, j)}
$$
$$
\tilde Z_\Delta^{(t, j)} = \Delta_{raw}^{(t, j)} - \text{MeanPool}_{N(j)}\big(\Delta_{raw}^{(t, \cdot)}\big)
$$
The temporal resonance $S_{video}(c)$ is then computed against $\tilde Z_\Delta$. If the empirical delta proves unstable, our protocol dictates falling back to local cross-frame attention rather than absolute subtraction.

### 3.5 Triggering Strategy

To avoid the unreliability of Shannon entropy—which fails when models hallucinate with extreme overconfidence (low entropy)—BRA defaults to a **Threshold-Free Continuous Application**. The conservative nature of the additive shift and SMI ensures that syntax is protected implicitly without needing a brittle $\Delta E_t > \epsilon$ trigger. We relegate entropy-based triggering strictly to an ablation study to empirically prove its inferiority.

## 4. Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol

This section outlines the strict, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol required to empirically validate BRA. No claims of superiority are made a priori; rather, we define the exact experiments to be executed.

### 4.1 Protocol 0: The Modality Alignment Probe (Crucial Prerequisite)
**Hypothesis:** The final-layer visual tokens $h_L^{(v_j)}$ in decoder-only MLLMs (like Qwen-VL) naturally align with the text unembedding matrix $W_{vocab}$ due to the shared self-attention pathways.
**Experiment:** 
- Extract $h_L^{(v_j)}$ for 100 random MSCOCO images.
- Compute $\max \cos(W_{vocab}, h_L^{(v_j)})$ and extract the Top-5 text tokens for distinct visual patches (e.g., "dog", "grass").
- **Success Criteria:** A t-SNE plot or heatmap demonstrating that the highest-scoring words semantically match the physical patch. If this fails, the alignment projector $\Phi$ (Section 3.1) must be trained and applied.

### 4.2 Protocol 1: Static Hallucination and Length-Bias Avoidance
**Hypothesis:** BRA effectively suppresses object hallucination without causing the severe length collapse often seen in attention-manipulation methods.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: POPE, CHAIR, MME.
- Baselines: Base decoding, VCD, OPERA, DoLa.
- **Key Metric:** F1 Score / Accuracy, alongside **Average Generation Length (AGL)**. The target is to improve F1 while maintaining an AGL within 5% of the Base model.

### 4.3 Protocol 2: The "Pooling Paradox" Proof via High-Res GUI Navigation
**Hypothesis:** Standard hidden-state interventions destroy 2D spatial features, failing on tasks requiring dense spatial coordinates, whereas BRA's zero-pooling max-match preserves them.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmark: VisualWebBench (requires pinpointing small text/icons on a 4K screen).
- **Mandatory Ablation:** Compare `BRA (Zero-Pooling Max-Match)` against `BRA-MeanPool` (where visual tokens are averaged before resonance calculation).
- **Success Criteria:** `BRA (Zero-Pooling)` must demonstrate a statistically significant margin over the `MeanPool` variant on action-prediction accuracy.

### 4.4 Protocol 3: Video Temporal Robustness Validation
**Hypothesis:** Camera-motion-suppressed temporal differencing isolates true actions better than naive linear subtraction.
**Experiment:**
- Benchmarks: MVBench, VidHalluc.
- **Mandatory Ablation:** Base vs. `BRA (Spatial Only)` vs. `BRA (Raw Subtraction)` vs. `BRA (Camera-Suppressed)`.
- **Criteria:** If `Raw Subtraction` provides $\leq 2\%$ gain, or degrades under camera-pan subsets, it confirms the reviewer's critique of non-linear space instability, validating the necessity of the localized suppressed version.

### 4.5 Protocol 4: The Latency/Overhead Pareto Frontier
**Hypothesis:** Operating strictly in the logits space via simple dot products is strictly Pareto-superior to multi-pass methods in terms of system overhead.
**Experiment:**
- Construct a 2D scatter plot: X-axis is Latency overhead (ms/token), Y-axis is POPE F1 Score, and Bubble Size represents Peak VRAM overhead.
- Compare BRA against VCD (requires batched image repeats) and OPERA (requires attention graph backtracking).

### 4.6 Protocol 5: Token-Class Audit for SMI
**Hypothesis:** The SMI mechanism successfully isolates functional words and continuation tokens from arbitrary penalization.
**Experiment:**
- Generate a KDE density plot of the reshaped resonance scores $\hat S(c)$ during a subset of evaluations.
- **Success Criteria:** Explicit separation showing that Function Words and BPE continuations receive concentrated scores near neutral (protecting syntax), while Nouns/Verbs show a polarized distribution (enforcing visual grounding).

## 5. Discussion

The architectural shift from hidden-state subtraction to logits-space resonance anchoring is not merely an algorithmic trick; it is a structural necessity for AgentOS and high-resolution MLLMs. By explicitly addressing the "Pooling Paradox", BRA provides a pathway to intervene safely without destroying the fragile 2D spatial encodings established by Vision-RoPE. However, this theoretical elegance is entirely contingent upon solving the engineering realities of BPE fragmentation and modality manifold alignment. The introduction of Additive Shift, SMI, and localized temporal differencing bridges this gap from theory to deployable methodology.

## 6. Limitations and Future Work

1. **Modality Alignment Dependency:** If the Protocol 0 Probe reveals that $h_L^{(v_j)}$ and $W_{vocab}$ remain topologically distant in certain model families (e.g., strictly late-fusion architectures), BRA cannot function zero-shot and will require an adapter $\Phi$, negating its "training-free" appeal.
2. **BPE Fragmentation Depth:** While SMI aggressively mitigates immediate sub-word penalization, it is still a heuristic damping strategy. It does not perfectly reconstruct the holistic semantic vector of a multi-token entity before computing visual resonance.
3. **Temporal Differencing Constraints:** Even with spatial mean-suppression, pseudo-DoG filtering in the final LLM layer may fail to capture complex causal actions over long temporal windows, remaining vulnerable to severe phase shifts.

## 7. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)
Until Protocol 1-6 are fully executed, we strictly limit our claims to the theoretical soundness of the framework and the necessity of zero-pooling interventions. We do not claim arbitrary SOTA performance, nor do we assert that Shannon entropy is a universally reliable hallucination trigger. Our contribution stands as a rigorously formulated protocol designed to expose and solve the specific bottlenecks of high-resolution MLLM interventions.
