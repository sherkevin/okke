# Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Token-Local Logits Intervention for Multimodal Hallucination Mitigation

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the local visual evidence needed for the next decoding step. Existing mitigation strategies have shown that hallucination can be reduced through contrastive decoding, attention control, or inter-layer comparison. However, they do not directly provide a general framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that operates in the terminal logits space by reweighting candidate tokens using localized visual support.

BRA is built around three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse global correction with candidate-wise support computed directly from the contextualized visual states at the LLM's final layer. Second, **Bounded Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance** extracts stable local evidence from high-resolution token fields without disrupting the underlying decoding distribution, strictly operating within a safe candidate window. Third, **Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation inheritance, preventing hallucination suppression from degrading grammar or long lexical units. To maintain methodological transparency, we explicitly separate a zero-shot variant (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated upper-bound (`BRA_calib`).

Rather than claiming premature state-of-the-art performance, we organize this paper around a falsifiable, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol spanning three evidence chains: hallucination reduction (measured alongside Average Generated Length), structure and reasoning preservation, and the specific value of local evidence over coarse aggregation. Evaluated across `POPE`, `CHAIR`, `MMBench`, `MMMU`, `FREAK`, and `DocVQA`, this protocol presents a rigorously bounded proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual evidence control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure is especially damaging in dense grounding scenarios such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex GUI understanding, where the relevant visual evidence is fundamentally local rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods (e.g., VCD) compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods (e.g., DoLa) exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These families represent important, highly competitive baselines, but none of them by themselves define a general answer to the following structural question:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This question is the center of BRA. This paper does not attempt to frame mainstream baselines as sharing a single flawed mechanism. Instead, it advances a direct, defensible positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided that it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards.

Executing this is nontrivial for three mathematical and structural reasons. First, the input-output embedding asymmetry: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, making direct cosine similarity with output vocabularies mathematically invalid unless properly projected via the model's native `lm_head`. Second, the candidate generation limit: decode-time intervention can only reweight existing language priors; it cannot resurrect a completely suppressed visual token. Third, the structural collapse risk: hallucination suppression easily overfires on Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities unless explicitly protected. 

To address these challenges, BRA centers on three core mechanisms:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, utilizing final-layer visual states to bridge the embedding asymmetry.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance**, extracting localized support from bounded candidate subsets without distribution collapse.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, a structure-preserving protection layer utilizing dynamic BPE continuation inheritance.

To ensure rigorous evaluation, we preserve a strict fairness boundary between `BRA_zero` and `BRA_calib`. Following recent recommendations for reliable MLLM evaluation, we structure our experiments as a stringent protocol testing five lines of defense: zero-shot semantic validity, hallucination reduction (controlling for length), structural preservation, local-evidence necessity, and computational efficiency.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary and the Embedding Asymmetry: `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`

A critical vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is the embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens typically enter at the input embedding layer. Directly comparing input-aligned visual features to output lexical unembeddings is mathematically invalid.

To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* visual hidden states at the LLM's final transformer layer, $h_L^{(v_j)}$. Because these states have passed through the entire residual stream alongside text tokens, they occupy the same manifold as the pre-unembedding text states. We split BRA into two distinct tracks:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute the local support by evaluating what vocabulary each visual patch would independently activate: 
   $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`BRA_calib`**: A calibrated upper-bound variant where a lightweight matrix $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained on a small alignment set to correct any semantic drift between the final visual states and the lexical space.

`BRA_zero` serves as the fair comparison against training-free baseline decoding methods. Its success hinges on a critical assumption: that $W_{vocab} \cdot h_L^{(v_j)}$ yields a semantically meaningful distribution rather than high-entropy noise. We formalize the empirical proof of this assumption as "Defense Line 1" in our evaluation protocol (Section 4.1).

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance

At decoding step $t$, BRA acknowledges a fundamental limitation: decode-time logits intervention cannot rescue a target token if the base model assigns it near-zero probability. We therefore strictly bound the intervention to a safe candidate set:
$$
\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})
$$
where $M \ll |V|$ is a candidate window (e.g., $M=100$).

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, BRA computes local visual support from the visual token volume $N_v = H \times W$. To prevent spatial noise from washing out small objects, we apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule rather than a global average:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)
$$
The raw support is the average activation of candidate $c$ across its top-$k$ most resonant visual patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c]}{\tau_{sim}}
$$

*Note on Spatial Scope:* BRA strictly operates on 2D spatial layouts. Naively extending this to video by flattening spatial and temporal dimensions ($T \times H \times W$) risks severe spatio-temporal dilution, scattering attention across causally disconnected frames. Consequently, video reasoning is explicitly scoped out of the main claims and reserved for future architectures enforcing temporal proximity.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into stable logits adjustment, BRA normalizes the evidence over the bounded candidate set:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens that lack local visual backing relative to their peers:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)] \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Probabilistic VASM and BPE Inheritance

Hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing structural tokens (which lack visual grounding) or fragmenting multi-token entities. We introduce Probabilistic Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) to modulate the intervention strength $\mathbb{E}[\gamma(c)]$.

Because BPE subwords are highly context-dependent, a static dictionary prior is fragile and computationally bottlenecked. VASM therefore relies on a dual-tier mechanism prioritizing runtime structural inheritance:
1. **Root Token Expectation (Static):** For tokens initiating a word (e.g., identified by SentencePiece `_` prefix), we query a static prior dictionary to assign high $\gamma$ to visual nouns/adjectives and low $\gamma$ to functional syntax.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance (Dynamic):** This is the core structural defense. Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the dictionary and inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its most recent root token. 

This strict inference-time inheritance prevents the mathematical collapse of multi-token entities (e.g., complex OCR strings, hyphenated object names) during decoding, ensuring that hallucination mitigation does not degrade the LLM's native reasoning syntax.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces a specific architectural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence, we organize the evaluation plan into three falsifiable evidence chains supported by five lines of defense. The following outlines the hypothesis-driven protocol and the required benchmark execution.

### 4.1 Defense Line 1: Zero-Shot Semantic Validity Pilot

**Hypothesis:** The final-layer visual hidden states $h_L^{(v_j)}$ occupy a semantically meaningful subspace such that applying the native $W_{vocab}$ yields words aligned with the visual patch.
- **Protocol:** Before conducting full benchmarks, we measure the **Token Overlap Rate** between the top-10 decoded words of $h_L^{(v_j)}$ and the ground-truth object labels from COCO bounding boxes corresponding to patch $j$.
- **Execution Rule:** If the Token Overlap Rate is near-random, `BRA_zero` is mathematically invalid, and the framework must strictly fall back to `BRA_calib`. This sanity check proves the feasibility of Section 3.1.

### 4.2 Defense Line 2 (Chain A): Hallucination Reduction vs. Length Collapse

**Hypothesis:** `BRA_zero` mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting without causing pathological output truncation or length collapse.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR` (Image).
- **Baselines:** Base Model, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `BRA_zero`, `BRA_calib`.
- **Mandatory Metric:** `AGL` (Average Generated Length).
- **Execution Rule (Table 1):** We must report Accuracy/F1 alongside `AGL`. If BRA improves `CHAIR` metrics solely by generating significantly shorter responses (e.g., dodging hallucinations by saying less), the mitigation claim is falsified.

### 4.3 Defense Line 3 (Chain B): Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via local evidence does not degrade complex reasoning, provided that VASM's BPE inheritance protects structural and functional tokens.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard Subset)`.
- **Primary Ablation:** `BRA_zero` (with full VASM) vs. `BRA_zero_no_VASM`.
- **Execution Rule (Table 2):** `BRA_zero_no_VASM` must demonstrate a severe reasoning drop on `MMMU (Hard)`, while `BRA_zero` with VASM preserves or improves upon the Base model's performance. This isolates the BPE inheritance mechanism as a strictly necessary component.

### 4.4 Defense Line 4 (Chain C): The Value of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting (Adaptive Top-$k$) is significantly more faithful than coarse global visual aggregation.
- **Benchmarks:** `FREAK` (OCR, Position, Counting), `DocVQA`.
- **Primary Ablation:** Base vs. `BRA_MeanPool` vs. `BRA_zero`. 
  *(Note: `BRA_MeanPool` replaces the Top-$k$ extraction with a global average of $h_L^{(v_j)}$).*
- **Execution Rule (Table 3):** `BRA_zero` must outperform `BRA_MeanPool`. If `BRA_zero` cannot beat the global average on dense document or spatial tasks, the core premise of "token-local necessity" is falsified.
- **Qualitative Audit (Figure 1):** We mandate spatial heatmaps tracking the Top-$k$ visual tokens selected during the generation of localized entities (e.g., small objects on a cluttered desk, specific OCR text lines) to geometrically prove token-to-patch alignment.

### 4.5 Defense Line 5: Efficiency Audits

To ensure the bounded candidate filtering and Top-$k$ search do not introduce prohibitive overhead at inference, the protocol includes:
- **Scaling Curve (Figure 2):** Decoding Latency (Tokens/Sec) plotted against Visual Token Count ($N_v$). 
- **Execution Rule:** The bounded Top-$M$ candidate mask must constrain the latency curve, keeping `BRA_zero` computationally competitive with base autoregressive generation even at high resolutions.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of BRA relies on a clean scientific contract: it intervenes at decode-time, leverages token-local visual evidence, preserves structural integrity via BPE-aware VASM, and maintains a transparent zero-shot baseline. However, execution of this framework carries specific theoretical limitations:

1. **Embedding Alignment Dependency:** `BRA_zero` assumes that applying the LLM's native `lm_head` to final-layer visual hidden states produces a meaningful semantic distribution. While our pilot protocol (Defense Line 1) tests this, architectures where visual states remain heavily abstract or geometrically entangled at the final layer will yield noisy local support, necessitating the `BRA_calib` variant.
2. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** BRA fundamentally operates by reweighting the Top-$M$ language priors. It cannot generate a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$.
3. **Static Dictionary Fragility in VASM:** While BPE continuation inheritance effectively mitigates subword fragmentation, VASM's reliance on root-token prior dictionaries can become a bottleneck at inference and may misclassify highly contextual homographs.
4. **Spatio-Temporal Dilution (Video):** BRA is strictly formulated for 2D spatial layouts. Flattening video sequences to $T \times H \times W$ for Top-$k$ search is mathematically naive and ignores motion continuity, risking severe temporal dilution. Extending token-local logits intervention to video requires future work incorporating explicit temporal proximity penalties.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines **Bi-directional Resonance Anchoring**, a positive, falsifiable framework for mitigating multimodal hallucinations through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By explicitly identifying the embedding asymmetry risk, formalizing VASM with BPE inheritance, and separating `BRA_zero` from calibrated bounds, we establish a rigorous foundation for decode-time control. The claims herein are strictly bounded by the proposed five-line evaluation protocol spanning zero-shot validity, hallucination reduction, reasoning preservation, and the empirical necessity of local over coarse visual evidence.
