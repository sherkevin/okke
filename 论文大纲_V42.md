# Bounded Region Alignment: Token-Local Visual Evidence for Structure-Preserving MLLM Decoding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to object hallucination when strong language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense spatial grounding. While existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalties) serve as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers for overarching language or attention dynamics, they lack a mechanism to inject strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bounded Region Alignment (BRA)**, a decode-time intervention that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

Strictly scoped to 2D Image-LLMs to secure defensible claims regarding spatial locality, BRA operates on three core mechanisms designed to balance intervention with computational honesty. First, to confront the "zero-shot illusion" of post-hoc spatial washout, we establish a strict evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot projection (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight, dynamically calibrated bound (`BRA_calib`), governed by an offline spatial retrieval threshold. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence bounded by a once-per-prompt prefix limit to prevent signal collapse in dense images. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via an explicitly defined WordNet spatial dictionary. To avoid conflating "structure preservation" with mere "intervention bypass" in out-of-vocabulary domains, VASM is instrumented to transparently log exact intervention trigger rates, operating alongside an **Arrogance-Triggered Entropy Gate** subject to ruthless step-by-step ablation.

To validate BRA and strictly prevent heuristic pipeline bloat, we propose a pre-registered, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol. Evaluated across three definitive evidence chains—(A) Hallucination Reduction isolating mechanisms against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` control, (B) Structure Preservation explicitly profiling polysemy false positives and intervention bypass rates, and (C) Local Evidence Value comparing `BRA_zero/calib` against global pooling (`BRA_MeanPool`). Alongside transparent defense lines logging CUDA kernel overheads and batched Out-of-Memory (OOM) failures, this framework establishes a scientifically rigorous proposition for test-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable zero-shot capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to clarify that these methods serve as state-of-the-art orthogonal regularizers that effectively discipline the overarching language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. However, because they operate on broader generation dynamics rather than explicit spatial grounding, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, defensible hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination more faithfully than pure language/attention heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We conceptualize this intervention as **Bounded Region Alignment (BRA)**. In this work, we strictly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, deliberately discarding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims surrounding spatial locality.

Executing BRA requires acknowledging and mathematically overcoming three structural barriers. First, **input-output embedding asymmetry** and the **zero-shot illusion**. We explicitly hypothesize that for architectures like LLaVA-1.5, deep self-attention entirely washes out localized patch fidelity by the final layer. Second, the heavy computational burden of **dynamic spatial pooling** at every decoding step. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities.

BRA resolves these barriers via a structured, rigorously ablated framework:
1. **Bi-Level Logits Intervention (`BRA_zero` / `BRA_calib`)**: A strict evaluation boundary testing whether final-layer visual states natively align with the terminal vocabulary. If a mathematically formalized spatial washout threshold is crossed, we immediately pivot to `BRA_calib`—acknowledging the method as a hybrid test-time alignment requiring a lightweight calibrator.
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations bounded by a once-per-prompt prefix limit.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, utilizing dynamic BPE continuation inheritance and an explicit ~85k WordNet physical entity dictionary to shield structural language. Crucially, we directly confront VASM's conflation of "preservation" and "inaction" by transparently tracking explicit Intervention Trigger Rates (%).
4. **Arrogance-Triggered Entropy Gate**: An optional heuristic targeting instances where language priors are extremely arrogant (high confidence on visual nouns). To avoid pipeline bloat, this module is subject to a strict pruning threshold: if it fails to yield $\ge 2\%$ absolute gain, it is discarded.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, our experimental design is organized into an execution protocol defining three primary evidence chains. 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating true mechanism value on POPE, CHAIR, and AGL against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline, isolating inference-time gains from parameter exposure. 
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving VASM dynamics via MMBench and MMMU(Hard), openly profiling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) intervention bypass rates and manually analyzing polysemy false positives.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the definitive superiority of localized evidence on dense tasks (FREAK, DocVQA) by strictly comparing against a global average baseline (`BRA_MeanPool`).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Overcoming the Zero-Shot Illusion: The Washout Threshold

A fundamental vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input layer. To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states corresponding to the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$.

We explicitly confront the "zero-shot illusion": the precarious assumption that deep self-attention preserves localized visual support in final layers. To prevent falsely claiming zero-shot alignment if an architecture natively destroys spatial fidelity, we define a strict operational boundary governed by a feature-level **Washout Threshold**, evaluated offline once per architecture:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support via $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$. 
2. **The Washout Threshold**: We test zero-shot bounding box retrieval on a held-out 500-sample spatial validation set. A predicted bounding box is extracted by binarizing a Gaussian-smoothed heatmap of patch logits using Otsu's thresholding. If the native projection fails to achieve an average bounding box $\text{IoU} > 0.15$ against ground truth, native spatial fidelity is mathematically declared lost. We anticipate that architectures like LLaVA-1.5 will immediately trigger this threshold.
3. **`BRA_calib`**: Upon triggering the threshold, we pivot to a lightweight calibrated alignment, acknowledging the method as a hybrid test-time intervention. $\Phi_{calib}$ is a 2-layer MLP projection ($\approx 4.2$M parameters, requiring $\approx$ 1 A100 GPU hour to train) optimized on 5,000 COCO image-patch to bounding-box label pairs, with the base LLM entirely frozen.

**Strict Semantic Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. Negative patches ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are cross-referenced to ensure an $\text{IoU} < 0.1$ with the target *and* membership in a differing COCO semantic super-category. While this prevents the most severe latent poisoning, we acknowledge that dense, crowded patches (e.g., a person holding a cup) remain a fundamental limitation of patch-level contrastive alignment.

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling

At decoding step $t$, a naive Softmax over all visual patches artificially inflates noise. We apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{noise}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{noise}\Big)
$$
To prevent $\theta_{noise}$ from becoming brittle, it is dynamically defined per-step as the moving **median** of the activation distribution across the $N_v$ patches. To address dense images where the median natively spikes, wiping out valid evidence, we apply a historical upper-bound clamp $\theta_{max}$, derived **strictly once per prompt** as the 90th percentile of visual activations from the text prefix.

We then apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$, aggregating only the top-$k$ most supportive activated patches. Crucially, the mathematical sorting operation operates on a tensor of size $M \times N_v$ (where $M$ is the candidate vocabulary window). While executed via GPU-native approximations (`torch.kthvalue`), performing this sort per-token at every decoding step incurs undeniable CUDA kernel launch overheads, necessitating strict latency tracking in our evaluation protocol.

### 3.3 VASM and the Arrogance-Triggered Entropy Gate

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing. This penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

VASM prevents structural collapse via a dual-tier protection architecture:
1. **Explicit Root Dictionary Expectation:** We define the visual noun dictionary offline using the WordNet *physical entity* subtree ($\approx 85,000$ synsets). $\gamma=1$ *only* for tokens matching this dictionary. 
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance:** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its root token, implemented as a pre-compiled GPU-side bitmask.

**The Polysemy Concession:** We explicitly recognize that a deterministic dictionary cannot resolve polysemy natively. Non-visual usages of physical nouns (e.g., the "bank" of a river vs. a financial "bank") risk triggering false positive suppression.

**The Arrogance-Triggered Entropy Gate:** 
Intervening across all confidence bands introduces pipeline bloat. We define an optional heuristic strictly targeting extreme confidence ($Entropy < H_{low}$) on visual nouns ($\gamma=1$). High confidence on a visual noun *invites* visual scrutiny. Candidate sets are explicitly bounded to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$. To maintain rigorous scientific hygiene, this gate is governed by a strict pruning mandate: if empirical ablations fail to demonstrate $\ge 2\%$ absolute gain from this specific module, it will be excised to prioritize throughput.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces specific structural hypotheses—and severe risks of computational bloat and intervention bypass if unverified—we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains and strict defense lines. The protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector, 1D sequence) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). All subsequent sections outline our planned, hypothesis-driven execution. 

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction via Step-by-Step Attribution

**Hypothesis:** BRA mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting. These gains derive from the decode-time mechanism, not parameter exposure, and each pipeline component justifies its distinct latency cost.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Acc/F1), CHAIR (Object vs. Attribute), and AGL (Attention-Grounded Localization, IoU). 
  - **Planned Output (Table 1 - Strict Format):** 
    - *Columns:* Model | POPE (F1) | CHAIR (Obj) | AGL (IoU) | **Tok/Sec** | **% Tokens Intervened**
    - *Rows:* Base | `Base + 5k LoRA` | DoLa | VCD | OPERA | `BRA_MeanPool` | `BRA_calib (Base)` | `BRA_calib (+VASM)` | `BRA_calib (Full)`
  - **The 5k LoRA Control & Ruthless Pruning:** The `Base + 5k LoRA` control is fine-tuned on the identical 5k SAM-filtered pairs to definitively isolate representation learning from inference-time adjustment. Furthermore, if the progression to `BRA_calib (Full)` via the Entropy Gate fails to yield $\ge 2\%$ absolute gain over `+VASM`, the gate will be formally pruned from the recommended methodology.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure Preservation and the Inaction Conflation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via visual suppression does not degrade complex reasoning if VASM protects structural tokens. However, reliance on an explicit WordNet dictionary introduces a critical vulnerability where Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) terms bypass intervention entirely, conflating "preservation" with mere "inaction."
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1, MME, MMMU (Hard Subset).
  - **Intervention Trigger Rate Profiling (Figure 1):** To enforce honesty, we construct a scatterplot. **X-axis = OOV Rate (%)** (percentage of ground truth visual nouns missing from VASM), and **Y-axis = Accuracy Drop relative to Base**. Point sizes will explicitly represent the **Intervention Frequency (%)**. If MMMU performance is "preserved" simply because BRA never triggers, this limitation will be explicitly documented.
  - **Qualitative Polysemy Failure Case:** We will isolate and manually visualize a **Polysemy False Positive**—e.g., an instance where a linguistic metaphor (such as "running a *process*") triggered $\gamma=1$, forced local visual evidence, found nothing in the image, and erroneously suppressed the correct token.

### 4.3 Chain C: Local Evidence Value & Domain Shift Crucible

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting geometrically isolates evidence that diffuse pooling natively washes out.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR/Position) and DocVQA (ANLS).
  - **The Ultimate Crucible:** Strictly comparing `Base + 5k LoRA`, `BRA_MeanPool` (naive global averaging), and `BRA_calib`. If `BRA_MeanPool` outperforms the Adaptive Top-$k$ variant in dense document contexts, the core hypothesis of localized evidence is falsified.

### 4.4 Defense Lines: Latency Reality and OOM Transparency

**Hypothesis:** Executing moving medians and $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorts at every decoding step incurs massive CUDA kernel launch overheads, fundamentally bottlenecking generation throughput.
- **Evaluation Protocol (Figure 3):** Executed strictly on a standardized single A100 (80GB) GPU. We plot **Tokens/Sec** (left y-axis) and **Peak VRAM Usage** (right y-axis) against an expanding Candidate Window Size **$M \in \{10, 50, 100, 200\}$**, across **Batch Sizes = 1, 4, and 8**.
- **Separation of Latency and Explicit OOM Markers:** We rigorously separate **Prefill Latency** from **Decode Latency** to prevent blending from masking the true $\mathcal{O}(N_v \log k)$ computational cost. We will explicitly plot Batched Out-of-Memory (OOM) failures with a red 'X', acknowledging BRA as a high-precision, low-throughput analytical tool rather than a real-time API.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of Bounded Region Alignment (BRA) lies in its structurally bounded proposition: intervening at decode-time using token-local visual evidence while protecting language integrity via VASM. However, its execution is governed by severe theoretical and operational constraints:

1. **The Zero-Shot Illusion and Hybrid Shift:** Deep self-attention likely destroys localized patch fidelity by final layers. For architectures triggering the offline Washout Threshold (IoU < 0.15), the premise of zero-shot alignment is invalid, forcing reliance on the $\Phi_{calib}$ track. This explicitly shifts the method from pure inference to hybrid test-time alignment.
2. **VASM Conflation: Preservation vs. Inaction:** An explicitly defined ~85k WordNet superset is brittle for domain shifts. In specialized tasks (e.g., MMMU), if critical visual nouns are OOV, VASM defaults to $\gamma=0$. "Structure preservation" in niche domains intrinsically conflates with "intervention bypass." Furthermore, polysemy false positives (suppressing abstract usages of physical nouns) remain an unresolvable vulnerability of the deterministic dictionary.
3. **Pipeline Bloat vs. Attribution:** Deploying an MLP calibrator, moving-median Top-$k$ pooling, VASM, and an optional Entropy Gate introduces significant complexity. Our protocol legally binds us to ruthless step-by-step pruning: components failing strict absolute gain thresholds ($\ge 2\%$) will be discarded to protect methodological elegance.
4. **Latency Crater from CUDA Overheads:** Executing spatial pooling and $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorting at every single decoding step triggers massive kernel launch overheads. Isolating decode-time latency scaling reveals severe bottlenecks, and testing large batch sizes risks hard VRAM OOM failures, confining BRA to high-precision offline applications rather than high-throughput serving.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines Bounded Region Alignment (BRA), a rigorously defined framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By formalizing embedding asymmetry (`BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`) via offline spatial washout thresholds, utilizing Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ pooling bounded by prefix limits, and engineering robust language protection via VASM, we establish a technically precise foundation for decode-time visual control. To prevent conflating inaction with preservation and to penalize pipeline bloat, the claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol—mandating exact Intervention Trigger Rate logging, polysemy failure analysis, step-by-step ablations, and aggressive latency transparency—establishing a verifiable roadmap for empirical execution.
