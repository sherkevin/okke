# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Sparse Decode-Time Routing Head for Visual Existence Verification

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during entity grounding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate general model confidence, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens during autoregressive decoding. 

Recognizing the mathematical limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual existence verifier** rather than a complex spatial reasoner. It reliably answers "Is this noun in the image?" to suppress pure hallucinations. To resolve geometric incoherence and parameter bloat, TLRA projects strictly to an offline-filtered physical noun subset ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$), reducing the structural footprint to $\sim$18M parameters. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A semantically coherent initialization tied to the LLM's **Layer 0 input token embeddings**—matching the geometric space of the visual projector—to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring over bounded candidate windows, featuring a strict syntactic logit floor to prevent False Negative behavioral interference and model timidity; and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) with an **Unambiguous Stem Restriction** to handle semantic collisions (e.g., `_cat` vs. `catastrophe`), while explicitly quantifying the upper bound of un-penalizable BPE fragments. This work establishes a pre-registered, boundary-aware protocol for parameter-efficient decode-time visual routing, strictly mapping its structural, morphological, and relational failure boundaries.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts. However, they cannot introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways. Conversely, standard parametric approaches (like LoRA) operate entirely in the prefill phase, compressing spatial evidence into the global context window without explicitly routing it to specific token generation steps. This leaves a critical gap: **How can we inject fine-grained, token-specific visual evidence directly into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying morphological syntax, suffering geometric mismatch, or hitting severe memory bandwidth walls?**

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. TLRA extracts local spatial support to bias decode-time logits, strictly limited to entity-centric grounding. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a 50k masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit supervision that zero-shot methods lack. 

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly reject the framing of TLRA as a "context-aware spatial reasoner." Mathematically, TLRA acts as a **Global Visual Existence Checker**. By projecting patch features into the vocabulary space independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. While this effectively suppresses pure hallucinations, it establishes a hard theoretical boundary: TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. We embrace this constraint, demonstrating that context-blind existence checking is highly effective for core hallucination reduction if properly bounded.

Furthermore, we fundamentally correct the structural bloat of vocabulary-space projections. Projecting to a full 32,000-token vocabulary incurs a $\sim$131M parameter cost, the vast majority of which maps to non-visual abstract tokens. TLRA instead utilizes a **Sparse Deterministic Projection** ($D \times V_{noun}$), reducing the trainable footprint to $\sim$18M parameters by strictly targeting an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems. TLRA's structural advantage lies in its **Zero-Interference Guarantee on Weights**: unlike restricted LoRAs, which risk catastrophic forgetting of general instruction-following when overfitted to 50k captions, TLRA operates entirely post-hoc, theoretically perfectly preserving base reasoning capabilities. However, to ensure this weight preservation translates to *behavioral preservation*, we explicitly audit for False Negative suppression and generic model timidity (e.g., generating shorter, safer sentences).

Finally, we confront the **Vocabulary Sparsity Trap** and **Geometric Incoherence**. Training a projection matrix from scratch on 50k captions mathematically guarantees failure on unseen tokens due to random initialization. Previous attempts to tie this to the output `lm_head` (Layer $N$) ignored the geometric reality that post-projector visual features align with the LLM's *input* space (Layer 0). TLRA solves this by initializing its sparse projection head directly from the LLM's pre-trained **input token embeddings**, grounding the projection in a geometrically coherent semantic space. To prevent morphological corruption (e.g., penalizing `_cat` and breaking "catastrophe"), TLRA enforces Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) with strict Unambiguous Stem Restrictions, while openly quantifying the un-penalizable limits of fragmented BPE tokens (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`).

Our evaluation is structured as a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Generic Collapse:** Evaluating entity grounding (POPE, AMBER, MMHAL-Bench) while auditing for Morphological Error Rate (MER), Object Recall, False Negative Rate (FNR), and **Response Length Timidity** to prove TLRA is not merely silencing noun generation.
2. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general capabilities (MMBench) against the catastrophic forgetting of restricted LoRAs. We explicitly test geometric initialization (Layer 0 vs. Random vs. Layer $N$) on a held-out "Unseen Objects" split, and validate the Dense vs. Sparse projection tradeoff.
3. **Local Evidence Value & Relational Boundaries:** Isolating spatial advantage on FREAK, explicitly documenting TLRA's failure boundary on a Multi-Instance/Relational stress test (measuring true Degradation Rate), and Top-M Unrecoverability limits.
4. **Semantic Boundaries and Fragment Leakage:** Bounding the method via VASM Coverage Drop audits (calculating the exact percentage of mathematically un-penalizable BPE fragments) and Out-Of-Vocabulary jargon fallbacks.
5. **Hardware Bandwidth Physics:** A formal evaluation of the static vs. dynamic memory footprint, mapping real-world latency (TTFT and TPOT in ms), optimized by the new sparse $V_{noun}$ architecture.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Sparse Architectural Scope and Geometrically Coherent Initialization

TLRA targets standard ViT + MLP projector architectures where spatial provenance is preserved across visual tokens $X_v$. TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage:

**1. `TLRA_calib` (The Sparse Parametric Routing Head):** Instead of a full $D \times V$ matrix ($\sim$131M parameters), TLRA strictly limits its projection to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$ maps cached spatial visual states of dimension $D$ to this subset. For a Llama-2 based model ($D=4096$), $W_{calib}$ constitutes an ultra-lightweight $\sim$18 million parameters. The restricted output is then deterministically scattered back into the full $V$-dimensional logit space using our VASM dictionary indices.
- **The Sparsity Trap and Geometric Coherence:** If $W_{calib}$ is randomly initialized, training on a restricted 50k caption subset leaves unseen physical tokens mathematically un-updated (yielding pure noise). Crucially, the visual features $X_v$ (post-MLP projector) are geometrically aligned to the LLM's **input embedding space** (Layer 0), *not* the output `lm_head` (Layer $N$). To enable zero-shot generalization without forcing a single linear layer to simulate 32 transformer blocks, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained Layer 0 input token embeddings** (specifically the rows corresponding to $V_{noun}$). 
- **Training Formulation:** $W_{calib}$ is trained on a bounded subset of 50k conceptual captions. **The base LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen; gradients flow *only* into $W_{calib}$.** The loss is strictly masked to backpropagate only on tokens where VASM $\gamma(y_t) = 1$. 

**2. `TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** Computes cosine similarity directly between frozen post-projector visual features ($X_v$) and the LLM's discrete text embeddings. Because geometric gaps between modalities can still persist even at Layer 0, this mapping proves whether decode-time spatial routing requires the explicitly supervised $\mathcal{L}_{calib}$ alignment.

### 3.2 Hardware Bandwidth Tradeoff: Optimized by Sparsity

At each decoding step $t$, TLRA maps spatial features $X_v$ to lexical biases for a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$. 

**A. Static Precomputation (Optimizing TPOT over TTFT):** During prefill, cached visual states are mapped to the sparse vocabulary, yielding a static bias matrix $B = X_v \cdot W_{calib}$. Because $W_{calib}$ is only $4096 \times 4500$, this incurs a nominal one-time FLOP penalty (radically reducing TTFT overhead compared to a full 32k projection), limiting step computation to rapid uncoalesced memory reads from High Bandwidth Memory (HBM).
**B. Dynamic Sliced-Matmul (Optimizing static footprint over FLOPs):** We dynamically compute the projection *only* for the valid VASM candidates within the Top-$M$ at each step. We mandate real-world ms latency measurements to map this sparse tradeoff.

### 3.3 The Context-Blind "Existence Checker" and the Absolute Logit Floor

Mathematically, TLRA acts as a **Global Existence Checker**. It computes a raw spatial support score by pooling the top-$k$ matching patches for a candidate token $c$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} B_{j, c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
Here, $\beta$ is a learned scalar calibration parameter. *During training, we explicitly monitor the CDF of $S_{raw}$ prior to the sigmoid to guarantee that uncalibrated visual features do not blindly saturate $\hat S(c)$ to 1.0 for all patches.* $S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$ outside of the candidate proposal window. It verifies if visual evidence for candidate $c$ exists *anywhere* in the image.

To apply this evidence without forcing behavioral collapse or extreme timidity (where the model generates unnaturally short sentences to avoid hallucination risks), we enforce an **Absolute Syntax Floor**. Let $L_{floor} = \max_{v \notin \mathcal{V}_{noun}} L_{orig}(v) + \epsilon$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token. The final adjusted logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = \max \Big( L_{floor}, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L, \delta_{max}) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big)
$$
**Noun-Only Fallback Guarantee:** By bounding the penalty to never drop below $L_{floor}$, we mathematically guarantee that TLRA cannot alter the grammatical structure of the sentence (e.g., replacing a noun with an adjective), isolating the intervention strictly to physical grounding.

### 3.4 VASM, BPE Fragmentation, and Coverage Drop

To preserve syntax, TLRA utilizes VASM, an offline deterministic binary mask ($\gamma \in \{0, 1\}^V$) extracting $\sim$4,500 viable physical noun tokens from WordNet.

**Addressing BPE Tokenizer Leakiness:** BPE tokenization is exquisitely sensitive to preceding whitespace and semantic collisions (e.g., `_cat` acting as the prefix for "catastrophe"). VASM enforces an **Unambiguous Stem Restriction**, explicitly zeroing out ($\gamma = 0$) any subword stem whose top 10 most frequent completions in standard English corpora are abstract concepts.
**The Fragmentation Cost (VASM Coverage Drop):** While the Unambiguous Stem Restriction ensures high precision (near-zero MER), it exposes TLRA to BPE fragmentation mathematically. Complex nouns shattered into non-semantic fragments (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`) cannot be matched to WordNet stems. Consequently, they become mathematically un-penalizable. We explicitly quantify this "VASM Coverage Drop" over the Visual Genome evaluation splits to define the hard ceiling of dictionary-gated decode-time interventions.

**Data Isolation Guarantee & Baseline Protocol:** The 50k training split for $W_{calib}$ is drawn from a filtered subset of Visual Genome, verified to have zero image-level overlap with validation sets. To avoid the strawman trap, we utilize parametric baselines: `Standard Vision LoRA` (trained purely on the vision projector to ensure parameter fairness) and `Restricted Vision LoRA` (trained solely on the 50k VASM-masked loss to explicitly test catastrophic forgetting vs. Zero-Interference).

**Goal:** Determine if TLRA suppresses hallucinations without silencing noun generation, exhibiting a high False Negative Rate (FNR), or causing generic model timidity.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict computational, structural, and relational boundaries. By explicitly reframing TLRA as a targeted, context-independent visual existence verifier operating in a sparse lexical space, we identify the following mathematically rigid limitations:

1. **VASM Coverage Drop and BPE Fragmentation:** Because VASM relies on an Unambiguous Stem Restriction to prevent morphological collapse, any word shattered by BPE into non-semantic fragments (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`) mathematically escapes TLRA's jurisdiction. As tokenizers become more granular or multilingual, the percentage of un-penalizable fragments increases, establishing a hard ceiling on dictionary-gated decode interventions.
2. **Geometric Coherence vs. Multimodal Depth:** While initializing $W_{calib}$ from Layer 0 input embeddings respects the geometric space of the visual projector, it forces a single linear layer to execute a cross-modal alignment that deeply-fused architectures handle over multiple transformer blocks. If the visual features lack linearly separable existence boundaries at Layer 0, TLRA will inherently underperform deep-fusion LoRAs.
3. **The "Existence Checker" Fallacy (Relational Blindness):** The mathematical formulation of $S_{raw}$ pools patches independently of text context. TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. It explicitly trades complex relational reasoning for robust, lightweight decode-time hallucination suppression.
4. **Behavioral Timidity (The False Negative Risk):** While TLRA guarantees *structural* Zero-Interference (no base weights are altered), aggressive scalar penalties ($\alpha$) risk inducing behavioral timidity, where the model outputs overly short, non-descriptive sentences to avoid triggering penalties. We enforce the Absolute Syntax Floor to combat grammatical collapse, but calibration remains fragile.
5. **Top-M Unrecoverability:** The intervention is mathematically constrained by the base LLM's candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$. If strong language priors marginalize the ground-truth visual token below the Top-$M$ cutoff, absolute evidence penalties cannot rescue it, leaving the model blind to correct objects that the language model heavily discounted.

Future execution of this pre-registered protocol will definitively confirm whether a sparse, geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities, fragment limits, and relational blindness to provide scalable, targeted visual grounding.
