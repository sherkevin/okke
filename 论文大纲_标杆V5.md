# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for MLLM Grounding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) still hallucinate when fluent language priors overpower the small image regions that actually determine the next grounded entity. Existing inference-time defenses are effective, but they largely operate through global distribution shifts, attention penalties, or layer contrasts. They do not directly address a sharper question that matters for grounded generation: can decode-time intervention be driven by **candidate-specific local visual evidence** while preserving syntax, multi-token entities, and general multimodal reasoning? We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time framework that injects localized visual support into candidate logits while keeping the base MLLM frozen.

TLRA is built around three design ideas. First, it replaces coarse visual conditioning with **candidate-wise local evidence reweighting** over a bounded candidate window, enabling visual intervention at the level of individual lexical alternatives rather than a single global correction signal. Second, it uses **Adaptive Top-k resonance** to stabilize local evidence extraction under fixed decoding-time budgets. Third, it introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)**, an offline precomputed vocabulary mask with deterministic BPE continuation inheritance that protects structural tokens from indiscriminate visual suppression. To keep the method scientifically well-posed, we explicitly separate a zero-shot probe (`TLRA_zero`) from a calibrated branch (`TLRA_calib`), and we treat `TLRA_calib` as what it is: a lightweight base-model-frozen plug-in with an auxiliary visual-to-lexical projector, rather than a training-free baseline.

Beyond the mechanism itself, TLRA contributes a rigorous evaluation paradigm for local visual control in MLLMs. We pair the method with matched internal controls (`TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK`), a matched-budget `Base + LoRA` baseline, OCR negative controls, and explicit failure-boundary audits such as Top-M hijacking and system-cost analysis. This framing makes the paper both ambitious and disciplined: it advances a concrete, novel route for structure-preserving decode-time grounding, while forcing that route to justify itself against coarse aggregation, calibration-data gains, and known boundary failures. The main paper focuses on image-centric grounded generation, with video retained only as a bounded appendix pilot.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible but weakly supported by the visual input. This failure is especially damaging in **fine-grained physical grounding** scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the relevant evidence is often localized rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances in the field. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) compare distributions under perturbed and clean inputs. Attention-based control methods (`OPERA`) penalize pathological autoregressive behavior. Layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep representations. These are all legitimate and competitive baselines. However, they leave a specific, complementary question unanswered:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a narrow, falsifiable positive hypothesis: a decode-time framework that scores candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. The proposal is intentionally bounded. It is not framed as a universal replacement for existing baselines, and it is not framed as a claim about dense OCR/document reasoning. Instead, it targets physical-entity grounding under a controlled image-centric scientific contract.

Executing this intervention is nontrivial for four reasons. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and lexical logits do not naturally align in base models. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc intervention can only reweight candidates retained in the base model's Top-M set. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression can destroy syntax and long entities. Fourth, **system cost**: candidate-wise local evidence extraction introduces nontrivial latency and engineering overhead even when implemented with cached visual states.

To address these issues, TLRA is built upon four explicit commitments:

1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention** rather than base-model retraining.
2. **Adaptive Top-k resonance** as the primary local evidence extractor.
3. **Automated VASM** as the structure-preserving protection layer.
4. **A strict zero/calibrated split** between `TLRA_zero` and `TLRA_calib`, plus a matched-budget baseline for the calibrated branch.

To keep the paper scientifically honest, TLRA enforces three boundary rules. First, `TLRA_zero` is only a **viability probe**; if Stage 0 fails, the paper contracts toward `TLRA_calib` rather than inventing excuses. Second, `TLRA_calib` is explicitly a **base-model-frozen plug-in** and must be evaluated not only against matched-calibrator internal controls, but also against a `Base + LoRA` baseline trained with the same calibration budget. Third, TLRA accepts an **OCR concession**: because VASM intentionally protects arbitrary text strings, OCR-heavy tasks are not part of the positive capability claim and are used only as negative controls.

The paper's acceptance does not depend on forcing a grand image-video story. The primary scientific contract is image-centric. A bounded video pilot is retained only as appendix evidence for exploratory transferability and is not allowed to dominate the main narrative.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib`

We explicitly separate TLRA into two tracks to avoid the calibrator blackbox problem:

1. **`TLRA_zero`**: a strict decode-time probe in which the model-native readout is applied directly to cached visual states.
2. **`TLRA_calib`**: a calibrated variant in which a lightweight projection `Phi_calib` maps cached visual states into the lexical space while the base MLLM remains frozen.

`Phi_calib` is trained on a bounded conceptual-caption subset. This external supervision is precisely why `TLRA_calib` is not treated as training-free. Any claim about local routing must therefore survive two fairness checks:

- **Internal parity:** `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK`, and `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` must share the exact same frozen `Phi_calib`.
- **Matched-budget fairness:** `TLRA_calib` must be compared against `Base + LoRA`, trained on the same calibration subset and budget.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering, Visual State Provenance, and Adaptive Top-k Resonance

At decoding step `t`, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set:

$$
\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig}),
$$

where `M` is a small, globally locked candidate window.

Let $h_L^{(v_j)}$ denote the cached visual states and let the visual token volume be $N_v = H \times W$. In the formal benchmark, these states must be accompanied by explicit provenance in the experiment output, including at least:

- source module / capture path
- layer role or extraction depth
- whether the state is cached during prefill

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support by selecting:

$$
k = \max(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil),
$$

where `rho`, `k_min`, `tau_sim`, `tau_evidence`, `alpha`, and `M` are all locked from a single validation split. The spatial ratio `rho` is part of the lock; it must not be re-tuned per dataset.

The raw local support is:

$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw local support into a relative evidence score:

$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$

To avoid uncontrolled softmax distortion, TLRA applies a scaled penalty anchored to the local logit range:

$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \Delta_L \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)),
$$

where $\Delta_L$ is the candidate-window logit spread.

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Automated VASM

TLRA uses **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to protect structure:

- `gamma(c) = 1` for visually groundable physical-entity tokens.
- `gamma(c) = 0` for structurally protected tokens.

VASM is generated algorithmically rather than manually:

1. **Offline precomputed vocab lookup:** root-token candidates are compiled before inference.
2. **BPE continuation inheritance:** subword continuations inherit the mask state of the active root token.

The benchmark explicitly assumes **no online POS tagger in the loop**. Polysemy, subword collision, suffix-collapse, and related edge cases are tracked as limitations and appendix audits rather than hidden under method prose.

This design deliberately yields an **OCR concession**: arbitrary text-in-image strings receive near-zero intervention coverage, so `DocVQA` is treated as a strict negative control rather than a positive-evidence dataset.

### 3.5 Methodological Boundaries and Fallback Claims

TLRA explicitly does not claim:

- architecture-invariant zero-shot success
- OCR/document-grounding competence under the current VASM contract
- guaranteed superiority of `AdaptiveTopK` over all coarse alternatives
- a unified image-video theory

In addition, TLRA has an explicit **Top-M ceiling**: if the correct entity token falls outside the candidate window, the method is mathematically unable to recover it. This failure boundary must be quantified rather than mentioned only qualitatively.

If `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` is statistically tied with `TLRA_MeanPool`, the paper must contract its claim to a **MeanPool + VASM** result rather than force a false locality story.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around one sanity pilot, three main evidence chains, one efficiency-and-boundary audit, and one appendix-only video pilot. This section is the paper's executable contract.

### 4.1 Stage 0: Zero-Shot Semantic Validity Pilot

**Goal:** determine whether `TLRA_zero` is a viable branch or merely noise.

- Benchmark: patch-level lexical overlap on a held-out image subset with object annotations
- Output: Top-1 / Top-5 / Top-10 overlap and candidate-window overlap
- Decision rule:
  - if patch-level overlap is weak, `TLRA_zero` is downgraded to a viability probe / appendix item
  - if `TLRA_zero` fails broadly, the paper contracts toward `TLRA_calib`

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Truncation Audit

**Goal:** test whether TLRA reduces hallucination without merely shortening outputs.

- Benchmarks: `POPE`, `CHAIR`
- Zero-/low-training comparison rows: Base, `VCD`, `DoLa`, `TLRA_zero`
- Calibrated fairness rows: `Base + LoRA`, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`
- Mandatory metrics: hallucination score, `AGL`, `AGL StdDev`, intervention coverage, `ITL/TPOT`, `tokens_per_second`, `peak_vram`
- Hard rule: if gains come with major AGL collapse, the claim contracts

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Goal:** verify that local-evidence intervention preserves general capability and that VASM is necessary.

- Benchmarks: `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard)`, `DocVQA` (negative control)
- Main rows: Base, `TLRA_full`, `TLRA_no_VASM`
- Mandatory diagnostics:
  - `DocVQA` trigger coverage near zero
  - `normalized_exact_match`
  - `anls`
  - suffix / continuation metric
  - structure-sensitive degradation under `TLRA_no_VASM`

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: Local Evidence Value and Leakage Control

**Goal:** isolate whether local routing itself matters beyond global priors.

- Benchmark: `FREAK`
- Splits: Spatial Relations / Object Existence / Counting
- Main rows: Base, `TLRA_MeanPool`, `TLRA_RandomK`, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`
- Mandatory parity rule: the three TLRA variants must share the exact same `Phi_calib`
- Mandatory leakage rule: results must be broken down by Seen vs. Unseen categories under the calibrator catalog

### 4.5 Efficiency and Failure-Boundary Audits

**Goal:** establish whether the method is usable and where it fails.

Mandatory audits:

- `TTFT`
- `TPOT` / `ITL`
- `tokens_per_second`
- `peak_vram`
- candidate-window sensitivity over `M in {10, 50, 100}`
- sensitivity over `alpha`, `rho`, `k_min`
- out-of-candidate failure cases
- `Hijacking CDF` or equivalent Top-M boundary quantification
- suffix-collapse failure cases
- cost split for routing vs. VASM where available

### 4.6 Bounded Secondary Video Pilot

**Goal:** test exploratory transferability to video without changing the main claim.

- Candidate benchmarks: `VIDHALLUC`, `Video-MME`
- Status: appendix-only exploratory evidence
- Interpretation rule:
  - positive results strengthen MM relevance
  - negative results only weaken the extension claim, not the main image-centric paper

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is the one with the cleanest scientific contract:

- it is a decode-time method
- it uses token-local visual evidence
- it preserves structure through VASM
- it separates zero-shot probing from calibrated upper bounds
- it audits calibrated gains against both internal matched-calibrator controls and `Base + LoRA`
- it treats OCR/document tasks as negative controls
- it reports cost and failure boundaries explicitly
- it keeps video bounded to appendix-only exploratory evidence

The main unresolved risks are:

1. `TLRA_zero` may remain a weak probe.
2. `Phi_calib` may inject category priors unless leakage is audited rigorously.
3. `AdaptiveTopK` may fail to separate from `MeanPool`.
4. suffix-collapse or subword collision may expose tokenizer-dependent brittleness.
5. some failures may simply be unrecoverable because the correct token is outside the Top-M window.
6. video transfer may remain inconclusive or empty.

## 6. Revised Claims (Pending Empirical Execution)

Until the protocol is fully executed, the paper should claim only:

- a positive decode-time framework for token-local visual evidence reweighting
- a structure-preserving masking mechanism for safe logits intervention
- a fair `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_calib` split
- a strict parity test of local routing vs. coarse matched-calibrator alternatives
- a calibrated fairness test against `Base + LoRA`
- a falsifiable image-centric evaluation contract with explicit efficiency and failure audits
- a bounded appendix-only video pilot

The paper should not claim:

- architecture-invariant zero-shot success
- OCR/document-grounding competence
- guaranteed locality superiority before Table 3 is populated
- a unified image-video theory
