# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Hybrid Auxiliary Routing Head for MLLM Grounding via Intermediate Visual States

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for grounding physical entities. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as visual-noise contrastive decoding (VCD) or layer-contrast methods (DoLa)—are training-free and operate globally. In contrast, we present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a hybrid trained auxiliary routing head that directly injects token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment while strictly preserving language structure.

TLRA is built on three architectural corrections. First, to avoid the "local evidence illusion" where final-layer LLM visual tokens are irreversibly contaminated by global text-attention, TLRA extracts visual representations from intermediate LLM layers dynamically profiled across architectures. Second, to bridge the manifold mismatch between static mid-layer states and the dynamic lexical space, we train a lightweight MLP projector ($\Phi_{calib}$). Crucially, we enforce discrete logit-space routing rather than continuous hidden-state addition, mathematically isolating the routing gain against a rigorous, zero-initialized gated continuous-fusion baseline to prevent manifold collapse. Third, we address the GPU kernel latency bottleneck of dynamic autoregressive Top-K sorting by capping the intervention window, and we manage the "BPE subword prefix trap" through Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM). 

To enforce an unforgiving evaluation contract, we separate our hybrid architectural mechanism from the training objective. Any claim about TLRA's routing superiority must survive a mandatory parameter-and-data-matched parity against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the identical cross-entropy alignment objective. Furthermore, we explicitly audit the true wall-clock latency of our Top-K routing kernel and the collateral fragility of the VASM heuristic. The resulting paper establishes a positive, falsifiable protocol for structure-preserving, hybrid visual control in MLLMs.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model frequently generates text that is fluent but decoupled from localized visual evidence. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained physical grounding scenarios, such as spatial positioning and small-object recognition.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies represent major advances. Contrastive decoding methods (`VCD`) inject visual noise to compare distributions; attention-based control (`OPERA`) penalizes pathological autoregressive patterns; layer-contrast methods (`DoLa`) exploit discrepancies between shallow and deep language representations. Crucially, these are **training-free, plug-and-play techniques** relying on zero-shot distribution shifts. They leave a complementary architectural question unanswered:

**How can we dynamically route token-local visual evidence directly into logits adjustment using a trained auxiliary head, without conflating the routing gains with training-objective superiority, and without falling victim to the globalization of final-layer representations?**

This paper introduces a hybrid auxiliary routing head, Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA), advancing a narrow, falsifiable hypothesis: a hybrid framework scoring candidate tokens using localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than zero-shot global visual perturbations or trivial continuous fusion, provided it extracts truly local states and incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards.

Executing this intervention requires overcoming four specific architectural and hardware barriers:
1. **The Local Evidence Illusion:** In decoder-only MLLMs, visual tokens processed during prefill undergo deep bidirectional self-attention. By the final layer, they are heavily contextualized, text-contaminated global mixtures. TLRA extracts visual states from intermediate layers. Rather than hardcoding a naïve $L/2$ assumption, we systematically profile the layer depth across backbones (e.g., LLaMA-2 vs. LLaMA-3) to pinpoint where spatial resolution degrades into textual semantics.
2. **Manifold Mismatch & The Continuous Fusion Strawman:** Static mid-layer visual states and dynamic pre-LM-head lexical states do not natively share an embedding space. We train a lightweight projector ($\Phi_{calib}$) to bridge this. However, naïvely adding continuous vectors to the LLM's final hidden state often causes grammar collapse. To prove discrete logit routing is fundamentally superior, we must defeat a rigorous, zero-initialized gated continuous fusion baseline, ensuring the failure of continuous addition is not merely a scaling artifact.
3. **Hardware Latency and Top-K Bottlenecks:** Computing dynamic visual similarity inside the autoregressive loop threatens GPU memory bandwidth. While $\Phi_{calib}$ is executed exactly once during prefill (caching a $\approx 32$ MB tensor), retrieving local support during generation requires $O(M \cdot N_v)$ dot products and a dynamic Top-K sort at every step. We explicitly isolate and audit this kernel latency to prove it does not cripple standard decoding speeds.
4. **The BPE Subword Trap vs. Objective Regularization:** A perfect Next-Token Prediction objective should natively teach $\Phi_{calib}$ to ignore grammatical syntax and fire only on visual entities. In practice, manifold bleeding occurs. We introduce Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) as an explicit engineering fallback to protect syntax. We formally audit whether $\Phi_{calib}$ learns semantic separation natively, and quantify the collateral damage of the VASM heuristic across multilingual and out-of-domain boundaries.

TLRA is a *trained hybrid method*. It is inherently more expensive to deploy than VCD or DoLa. We therefore enforce a mandatory parity baseline: a LoRA model trained on the exact same dense region-caption data using the exact same alignment objective. If TLRA fails to outperform this objective-matched control, its routing claim is mathematically falsified.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Fairness Boundary: Isolating the Objective in Hybrid Routing

TLRA is a hybrid method utilizing a lightweight MLP projector $\Phi_{calib}$ to map static, mid-layer visual states into the dynamic pre-LM-head lexical space. During training, the base LLM and Vision Encoder are **strictly frozen**; only $\Phi_{calib}$ is updated using a standard localized Next-Token Prediction objective over a dense region-caption subset.

**Objective Matching for Fair Evaluation:**
Confounding the routing mechanism with the training objective is a fatal flaw. Any claim about decode-time local routing must survive a mandatory parity ablation against a `Base + LoRA` model fine-tuned on the exact same calibration subset **using identical Cross-Entropy**. 

**Defeating the Continuous Fusion Strawman:**
To justify discrete logit-space routing, we enforce parity against a robust `ContinuousAdd_Gated` baseline. Rather than a naïve addition (which easily collapses), this baseline projects $\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ and continuously adds it to the final hidden state via a learned, zero-initialized gating scalar $\gamma$: $h_{t, final} + \gamma \sum \alpha_j \Phi_{calib}(v_j)$. If TLRA's discrete logit routing outperforms this optimally initialized continuous baseline, we prove that avoiding the pre-trained LM-head manifold shift is an architectural necessity, not an implementation artifact.

### 3.2 Bridging the Mismatch: Layer Profiling and Kernel Latency

At decoding step $t$, TLRA restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, globally locked at $M=50$. 

**The Local Evidence Illusion:**
By the final layer, visual tokens ($N_v \approx 4000$) have become text-contaminated global mixtures. Instead of hardcoding depth, TLRA identifies the optimal extraction layer $L_{mid}$ through empirical layer-sweep profiling to capture representations before spatial resolution is irreversibly diluted.

**Hardware Lifecycle and Top-K Kernel Bottlenecks:**
$\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)})$ is executed **exactly once** during prefill, storing an $N_v \times D$ projected tensor ($\approx 32.7$ MB in FP16) in SRAM. During autoregression, raw local support for $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$ is computed via:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(\Phi_{calib}(h_{prefill}^{(v_j)}), c)}{\tau_{sim}}
$$
We explicitly acknowledge the hidden hardware cost: performing a Top-$k$ sort over $N_v=4000$ elements for $M=50$ candidates *at every decoding step* is hostile to CUDA thread synchronization. We isolate this specific kernel latency in our evaluation protocol to transparently document the true ms/token overhead.

### 3.3 Stabilized Relative Resonance Penalty

We convert raw support into a relative evidence score: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{evidence} \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big)$. To prevent the visual head from blowing up the logits and overriding confident structural tokens, we anchor the penalty to the local logit spread $\Delta_L = \max(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t)) - \min(L_{orig}(\mathcal{C}_t))$. Bounded by hyperparameter $\beta$, the adjustment is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot M_{VASM}(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L, \beta) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
(An ablation on $\beta$ is required, as a too-strict bound may artificially squash visual signals when model uncertainty is high and $\Delta_L$ is low).

### 3.4 BPE Subword Prefix Trap and the VASM Heuristic

Ideally, the localized Next-Token Prediction objective should natively teach $\Phi_{calib}$ to output low visual resonance scores for abstract syntax. In practice, continuous manifold bleeding causes the projector to occasionally spike on functional subwords (e.g., `[car]` in `[car, dinal]`), leading to syntax collapse. 

To protect syntax without inline parsing, TLRA introduces Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM), a static $M_{VASM} \in \{0, 1\}^V$ mask. We employ **Frequency-Weighted Subword Resolution**: if a BPE subword maps to a groundable root entity in $\ge \gamma$ of its occurrences in a C4 corpus, it retains $M_{VASM}=1$. 

We acknowledge VASM is an English-centric engineering fallback masking a limitation in the training objective. We formally audit this by comparing $\Phi_{calib}$'s raw entity vs. syntax score distributions *before* masking, and by measuring VASM's collateral fragility across multilingual domains.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The paper is organized around three evidence chains and strict boundary audits. This section represents the executable contract of the paper.

### 4.1 Stage 0: The Gated Continuous Fusion Validation

**Goal:** Mathematically verify that discrete logit-space routing is structurally superior to continuous hidden-state addition, even when optimized.
- **Protocol:** Compare TLRA against `TLRA_ContinuousAdd_Gated` (using a zero-initialized gating parameter $\gamma$).
- **Hypothesis:** Despite optimal initialization, continuous addition will still exhibit grammar degradation or fail to adequately boost visual entities compared to discrete logit routing, proving the necessity of avoiding the LM-head manifold shift.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and Objective Fairness

**Goal:** Test whether TLRA reduces hallucination, verifying gains derive from routing rather than calibration data.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE`, `CHAIR`.
- **Partitioned Baselines:** 
  - *Training-Free (Zero-Shot):* Base, `VCD`, `MM-DoLa` (0 extra parameters, 0 extra data).
  - *Matched Objective (Hybrid):* `Base + LoRA` (trained on identical data/cross-entropy).
- **Hard Rule:** TLRA *must* significantly outperform both the zero-shot methods (to justify setup cost) and the objective-matched `Base + LoRA` (to justify the architectural routing mechanism).

### 4.3 Evidence Chain B: Native Separation and Tokenizer/Domain Audit

**Goal:** Measure capability preservation, audit $\Phi_{calib}$'s native learning, and quantify VASM's collateral damage.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench`, `MME`, `MMMU`.
- **Native Separation Audit:** A scatter plot of $\Phi_{calib}$ output scores for grammatical tokens vs. visual entity tokens *before* VASM is applied. (Hypothesis: $\Phi_{calib}$ learns partial separation, but VASM is required to clip the overlapping distribution tails).
- **Cross-Tokenizer & OOD Collateral Damage Audit:** `DocVQA`, `GSM8K` (English Text/Math), and Multilingual Queries. Tests the catastrophic failure boundaries of the C4-English-derived VASM heuristic.

### 4.4 Evidence Chain C: The Layer Sweep and Visual Resolution

**Goal:** Prove intermediate visual states retain more token-local grounding than final layers, and test spatial resolution limits.
- **Protocol:** Systematic layer-sweep ($L_1$ to $L_N$) across LLaMA-2-7B and LLaMA-3-8B measuring `POPE F1`.
- **Failure Analysis (Visual Resolution):** Stratify performance by Ground Truth bounding box size. (Hypothesis: $L_{mid}$ retains enough spatial resolution to ground large objects, but extremely small <10x10 pixel objects may still be lost in the intermediate attention layers).

### 4.5 Efficiency and Hardware Latency Audits

**Goal:** Transparently document the computational wall-clock overhead and topological failure modes.
- **Latency Breakdown (Micro-benchmark):** Decode Time (ms/tok) explicitly split into: [Standard LLM Forward Pass] + [Dot Product Time] + [Top-$k$ Sort Time]. 
- **The "Hijacking" Bounding Box:** A histogram tracking the generation-step rank of the GT physical entity token. This proves TLRA's upper intervention limits: if the GT token falls outside the Top-50 window, TLRA cannot recover it.
- **The $\beta$ Restraint Ablation:** Demonstrates how tuning $\beta$ prevents the $\min(\Delta_L, \beta)$ term from artificially squashing the visual signal during high model uncertainty.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA explicitly bounds its claims. By defining itself as a *hybrid trained routing head*, it acknowledges its parameter and data costs relative to pure zero-shot methods like VCD. It isolates its routing gains against an unforgiving objective-matched `Base + LoRA` baseline and an optimized `ContinuousAdd_Gated` baseline to prevent conflating architectural superiority with alignment data or trivial scaling artifacts. Crucially, it abandons the "local evidence illusion" of final-layer representations, shifting extraction to dynamically profiled intermediate layers.

The primary unresolved risks and limitations are:
1. **The VASM Crutch and Objective Shortcomings:** A perfectly calibrated objective should teach $\Phi_{calib}$ to ignore syntax entirely. In reality, manifold bleeding necessitates VASM. By frequency-weighting BPE subwords using an English C4 corpus, TLRA actively accepts collateral damage to abstract and non-English language. This statistical heuristic fails ungracefully when BPE statistics shift in multilingual contexts.
2. **GPU Kernel Latency of Dynamic Top-K:** As our micro-benchmark shows, sorting over $N_v \approx 4000$ elements for $M=50$ candidates at every decoding step creates a CUDA thread synchronization bottleneck. While it prevents global VRAM explosion, it introduces a non-trivial wall-clock latency hit per token.
3. **The Top-$M$ Ceiling (The Hijacking Problem):** By limiting routing to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, TLRA is mathematically bounded. If the base LLM's language prior is so overwhelmingly wrong that the correct visual entity is completely expelled from the candidate window, TLRA cannot recover it. This represents a hard ceiling for discrete, bounded late-fusion interventions.
