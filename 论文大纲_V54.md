# Dictionary-Gated Token-Local Visual Intervention: A Hybrid Decode-Time Framework for Hallucination Reduction

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to object hallucination when overarching language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense spatial grounding. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, OPERA) successfully regularize global token probabilities or attention distributions. However, as highly effective but purely zero-shot, training-free regularizers, they operate orthogonally to the explicit routing of spatial evidence. This paper investigates how to explicitly inject **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space at decode-time without damaging functional language structure. We propose **Token-Local Visual Intervention**—a dictionary-gated logits adjustment framework utilizing localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

Strictly scoped to 2D Image-LLMs to ensure defensible claims regarding spatial locality, this framework addresses three structural barriers. First, acknowledging the near-universal spatial washout inherent to deep self-attention, we utilize a zero-shot diagnostic (`BRA_zero`). Upon expected failure, the framework shifts to a lightweight, dynamically calibrated test-time adapter (`BRA_calib`), mathematically fortified by a dual-veto Compositional Coherence Margin. This explicitly positions our method as a *hybrid test-time adaptation* rather than a purely zero-shot algorithm. Second, to combat autoregressive threshold drift, we extract localized support via an Entropy-Scaled Adaptive Top-$k$ mechanism governed by a Rolling Decay activation threshold. Third, to protect functional syntax from subword tokenization splits, we implement a **Visual-Aware Semantic Masking (VASM)** module—a dual-tier shield utilizing an ~85k WordNet dictionary via a greedy prefix-trie bitmask, and deterministic regex triggers.

Because this framework introduces distinct operational risks—including CUDA fragmentation, algorithmic over-engineering, and Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) bypasses—we propose a pre-registered execution protocol. This blueprint organizes empirical validation across three definitive evidence chains: (A) Hallucination Reduction, (B) Structure and Reasoning Preservation, and (C) Local Evidence Value. Backed by five explicit defense lines tracking everything from exact-step attention heatmaps to BPE bypass trajectories, this protocol establishes a rigorous, falsifiable proposition for spatially-aware, hybrid decode-time intervention.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable visual-linguistic capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to establish the precise operational category of these methods: they serve as highly successful, state-of-the-art *zero-shot* regularizers that effectively discipline overarching language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. Because they are training-free, they operate on global generation dynamics. Consequently, they are not natively designed to address the following orthogonal structural question:

**How can we explicitly inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying structural language, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning?**

This paper advances a direct, functional hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination faithfully, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We functionalize this as **Token-Local Visual Intervention**. In this work, we strictly bound our scope to 2D Image-LLMs, deliberately discarding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims regarding spatial locality.

Executing this intervention requires mathematically overcoming three structural barriers. First, **spatial washout** in embedding spaces. Deep self-attention almost universally diffuses localized patch coordinates by the final layer. We formalize this via a zero-shot Bounding Box Retrieval Assessment (`BRA_zero`). Acknowledging this limitation, our framework explicitly operates as a *hybrid test-time adaptation*. Upon washout detection, it shifts to an offline-trained lightweight calibration (`BRA_calib`) optimized via a Compositional-Aware InfoNCE loss, ensuring naturally co-occurring objects are not repelled. We do not claim zero-shot parity with DoLa or VCD; rather, we evaluate whether our parameter-efficient spatial routing justifies its training footprint. Second, the computational drift of **dynamic spatial pooling**. We replace static thresholds with an entropy-scaled dynamic activation governed by a continuous Sliding Window Exponential Moving Average (EMA). Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments or OCR-critical entities. We resolve this via a Visual-Aware Semantic Masking (VASM) module, engineered with a greedy prefix-trie to openly track subword mappings and definitively determine when the mask actively preserves structure versus when it defaults to algorithmic inaction.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, our experimental design is organized into a pre-registered execution protocol defining three primary evidence chains and five explicit defense lines:
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mechanism value on POPE, CHAIR, and AGL against a strictly parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline, logically separating zero-shot baselines from trained hybrid interventions to isolate the utility of decode-time routing.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving VASM dynamics via MMBench, MME, and MMMU(Hard). We mandate the reporting of a "VASM Intervention Rate" to explicitly quantify the inaction boundary and prevent conflating "preservation" with "bypassing the intervention entirely."
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the definitive superiority of localized evidence on FREAK and DocVQA by contrasting `BRA_zero`, `BRA_MeanPool`, and `BRA_calib`, backed by exact-autoregressive-step visual proofs of spatial routing.

Through these bounded chains and corresponding defense lines quantifying single-GPU Inter-Token Latency (ITL), EMA decay validity, and BPE exact-match trajectories, this protocol establishes a verifiable and highly falsifiable framework.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Embedding Asymmetry, Spatial Washout, and Compositional InfoNCE

To construct a valid local support metric, we evaluate the *contextualized* final-layer LLM hidden states corresponding to the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{model}}$. However, deep self-attention demonstrably diffuses spatial coordinates by the final layer. We formalize this via an operational boundary:

1. **`BRA_zero` (Zero-Shot Diagnostic)**: We evaluate zero-shot bounding box retrieval using the model's native `lm_head` as the projection $\Phi_{zero}$. If this fails to achieve an average bounding box $\text{IoU} > 0.15$ on a 500-sample spatial validation set, spatial fidelity is mathematically declared washed out.
2. **`BRA_calib` (Hybrid Test-Time Adaptation)**: Upon triggering the washout threshold, the framework shifts to $\Phi_{calib}$, a 2-layer MLP mapping $\mathbb{R}^{D_{model}} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{D_{model}}$. This explicitly transitions our method from zero-shot to a lightweight hybrid adapter. It is optimized on 5,000 size-stratified Visual Genome (VG) pairs disjoint from all test sets.

**Compositional-Aware Hard-Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. A naive spatial exclusion zone ($\text{IoU} < 0.1$) fails to disentangle overlapping objects. Therefore, valid negative patches include *Hard Negatives* ($\text{IoU} > 0.5$, heavily overlapping with the anchor but differing in semantic category). To prevent **Compositional Destruction** (e.g., repelling a "rider" from a "horse"), we enforce a dual-layered *Compositional Coherence Margin*. 

Let $z_a = \Phi_{calib}(h_L^{(v_{anchor})})$ and $z_c$ be a candidate patch embedding. The optimized loss is formulated as:
$$
\mathcal{L}_{InfoNCE} = - \log \frac{\exp(sim(z_a, z^+)/\tau)}{\exp(sim(z_a, z^+)/\tau) + \sum_{k=1}^{K} \mathbb{I}_{comp}^{(k)} \exp(sim(z_a, z_k^-)/\tau)}
$$
The compositional validity indicator $\mathbb{I}_{comp}^{(k)} \in \{0,1\}$ determines if a hard negative $z_k^-$ is mathematically permissible. It is defined via two vetos:
1. *Graph Veto ($\mathbb{I}_{graph}$):* $1$ if the anchor and negative share a parent-child relationship in the VG scene graph, $0$ otherwise.
2. *Semantic Veto ($\mathbb{I}_{sem}$):* Using frozen CLIP text embeddings, if $\cos(c_{anchor}, c_{candidate}) > \tau_{comp}$, $\mathbb{I}_{sem} = 1$. 

*Implementation Risk:* We acknowledge this dual veto represents significant algorithmic overhead. If empirical ablations demonstrate that a simpler naive InfoNCE achieves $>95\%$ of the performance, this module will be formally downgraded to an implementation detail rather than a core contribution.

### 3.2 Entropy-Scaled Top-$k$ Pooling & Rolling Decay Activation

At decoding step $t$, we apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{active}^{(t)}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{active}^{(t)}\Big)
$$
We derive an **entropy-scaled dynamic threshold base** during the prefill stage: $\theta_{base} = \mu_A + \lambda\left(\frac{\mathcal{H}_{vis}}{\log(N_v)}\right) \cdot \sigma_A$.

**The Rolling Decay Fallback Mechanism:** Deriving $\theta_{base}$ strictly at prefill risks threshold obsolescence during long context generation. Replacing a brittle step-function fallback, we implement a **Sliding Window Exponential Moving Average (EMA)**. We monitor the *Valid Activation Rate* $R_t$. The active threshold dynamically decays:
$$
\theta_{active}^{(t)} = \theta_{base} \cdot \min\left(1.0, \frac{\text{EMA}(R_{t-W:t}) + \epsilon}{\tau_{target}}\right)
$$
*Parameter Sensitivity:* The window size $W$ dictates decay severity. If $W$ requires manual tuning per dataset, the method is unacceptably brittle. We must explicitly test whether the EMA acts as a legitimate dynamic scaler or merely as a slow "kill-switch" that turns the intervention off before the reasoning chain concludes.

### 3.3 Visual-Aware Semantic Masking (VASM)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing, gated by a deterministic VASM module $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
We utilize a **Dual-Tier Protection Architecture**:
1. **Tier 1 (Root Dictionary):** Offline WordNet *physical entity* subtree ($\approx 85,000$ synsets).
2. **Tier 2 (Deterministic Format Triggers):** For DocVQA and dense reasoning, numerical strings and capitalized entities are assigned $\gamma=1$ via deterministic regex logic.

**Formalizing the BPE-to-WordNet Mapping:** Subword tokenization (e.g., `[" base", "ball"]`) creates structural brittleness against dictionary matching. We resolve this via a **Greedy Prefix-Trie Bitmask**. During generation step $t$, if the generated subword `c` forms a valid prefix path within the WordNet trie, it inherits $\gamma=1$ conditionally. If the subsequent step $t+1$ breaks the prefix path, the token is retrospectively unmasked in the contextual cache. 

**Regex and Broken OCR Limitations:** We explicitly acknowledge severe boundaries: (1) Broken OCR strings containing erroneous characters (e.g., "199$") will fail the Tier 2 regex and break the Prefix-Trie. (2) Reliance on capitalization regex is fundamentally dangerous in non-English languages or poorly formatted prompts. Consequently, the intervention defaults to $\gamma=0$ precisely when the generation is non-standard. Our evaluation protocol explicitly tracks this "inaction boundary."

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because Token-Local Visual Intervention operates as a hybrid test-time adaptation with specific operational risks (latency fragmentation, algorithmic overhead, OOV bypasses), we organize the evaluation plan into three pre-registered falsifiable evidence chains and five distinct defense lines. The planned protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector) and **Qwen-VL-Chat**. All subsequent sections outline our hypothesis-driven execution blueprint.

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction

**Hypothesis:** The intervention mitigates object hallucination specifically through localized decode-time routing, not merely through the parametric knowledge exposed during the 5k-sample test-time calibration.
- **Evaluation Plan:**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Random, Popular, Adversarial), CHAIR (Object), and AGL (Accuracy).
  - **Planned Output (Table 1):** Performance comparison explicitly separated into two operational blocks: (1) *Zero-Shot Regularizers* (Base, DoLa, VCD, OPERA) and (2) *Trained/Hybrid Adapters* (`Base + 5k LoRA`, `BRA_calib`). This logically isolates zero-shot baselines from our method's training advantage. A mandatory **"VASM Trigger Rate (%)"** column will be included for all datasets.
  - **Defense Line 1 (Strict Parity Control):** To isolate "routing" from "parametric injection," the `Base + 5k LoRA` control will be trained on the exact 5,000 VG samples used for $\Phi_{calib}$, utilizing identical optimizer states, batch sizes, and learning rate schedules. If this LoRA control alone closes $>95\%$ of the performance gap between Base and full `BRA_calib`, the decode-time routing hypothesis is mathematically redundant and will be reported as falsified.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** The VASM module protects structural language, but reliance on explicit dictionaries introduces a vulnerability where Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) and specialized terms bypass intervention entirely, conflating "preservation" with mere "inaction."
- **Evaluation Plan:**
  - **Metrics:** MMBench, MME, MMMU(Hard).
  - **Planned Output (Table 2 & Scatterplot):** Accuracy across benchmarks. Crucially, we will plot an Intervention Trigger Rate scatterplot: OOV Rate (%) vs. Accuracy Drop relative to Base across three settings: `Base`, `BRA_calib` (Full VASM), and a strict `BRA_no_VASM` ablation. 
  - **Defense Line 2 (The Inaction Boundary):** We establish a hard empirical boundary. If `BRA_no_VASM` catastrophically degrades MME, it proves VASM's necessity. However, if `BRA_calib` matches `Base` accuracy on highly specialized subsets (e.g., MMMU Biology) but exhibits a VASM trigger rate below 10%, we will explicitly concede that the mask preserves reasoning primarily by defaulting to inaction—functioning as an algorithmic placebo in specialized domains.

### 4.3 Chain C: Local Evidence Value

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting isolates specific evidence that diffuse global pooling natively washes out, mapping exact localized patch coordinates to specific token logits.
- **Evaluation Plan:**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (Accuracy/ANLS) and DocVQA (ANLS).
  - **Planned Output (Figure 1 - Visual Proof of Locality):** We will provide a side-by-side heatmap overlay on a complex text-dense image comparing `BRA_zero`, `BRA_MeanPool`, and `BRA_calib`. Crucially, these heatmaps will be extracted directly from the routing weights at the *exact autoregressive step* of the hallucinated/correct token, rather than aggregated post-hoc visualizations.
  - **Defense Line 3 (Compositional Coherence Ablation):** Evaluated strictly on CHAIR for highly overlapping objects, we will ablate $\Phi_{calib}$ trained *with* vs *without* the dual-layered Compositional Coherence Margin to prove the necessity of the Graph and Semantic veto math. If the naive $\text{IoU}$ exclusion zone performs adequately, the dual-veto will be stripped from the core claims.

### 4.4 Defense Lines 4 & 5: Algorithmic Bottlenecks and OCR Bypasses

We will monitor execution feasibility through the following empirical defense lines, explicitly benchmarked on a single A100 80GB GPU with Batch Size = 1:
- **Defense Line 4 (Latency Stress Test & EMA Tuning Proof):** Logits manipulation across the full vocabulary using spatial routing and Prefix-Trie dictionary lookups introduces severe CUDA kernel fragmentation. 
  - **Planned Output (Figure 2):** A line chart plotting Inter-Token Latency (ms/token) against Sequence Length for Base, DoLa, and `BRA_calib`. 
  - **Planned Output (EMA Curve):** A token-by-token trajectory curve showing the Sliding Window EMA ($\theta_{active}^{(t)}$) alongside token hallucination probability. This is required to prove the rolling decay window $W$ is effectively tuned and does not simply act as an algorithmic "kill-switch."
- **Defense Line 5 (The BPE/OCR Failure Trajectory):** To transparently document BPE/Regex brittleness (Section 3.3).
  - **Planned Output (Figure 3):** A step-by-step logits trajectory chart mapping the exact top-5 logits of a fragmented subword passing through the model. It will explicitly show where the correct localized token was suppressed because $\gamma=0$ bypassed the visual intervention due to non-standard formatting, demonstrating the real-world magnitude of the missed analytical opportunity.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The theoretical strength of Token-Local Visual Intervention lies in its structurally bounded proposition: injecting token-local visual evidence at decode-time while protecting language integrity via Dictionary-Gated VASM. However, as established in our proposed evaluation protocol, its practical execution is governed by severe operational constraints:

1. **Category Error Avoidance:** Our method (`BRA_calib`) is a parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) strategy applied as a test-time adapter. It cannot and should not claim zero-shot superiority over purely training-free methods like DoLa, VCD, or OPERA. Its validity rests entirely on surpassing the parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` boundary.
2. **Latency and Throughput Bottlenecks:** Performing final-layer MLP projections, compositional InfoNCE routing, and Prefix-Trie dictionary lookups at *every autoregressive decoding step* incurs non-trivial CUDA kernel fragmentation. This inevitably increases ms/token latency (benchmarked on A100), potentially restricting this framework to a high-precision analytical diagnostic tool rather than a high-throughput serving API.
3. **The "Inaction" Vulnerability of VASM:** WordNet and regex triggers are inherently limited (especially in non-English contexts). Highly specialized domains (e.g., MMMU Medicine) yield high Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rates, forcing VASM to default to $\gamma=0$. This risks conflating "preservation of reasoning" with "the method doing absolutely nothing." Transparent reporting of the VASM Intervention Rate is mandatory to police this boundary.
4. **Algorithmic Over-Engineering:** Components such as the Sliding Window EMA and the Dual-Veto InfoNCE are heavy. If the EMA window $W$ requires manual per-dataset tuning, or if the semantic veto $\tau_{comp}$ proves practically untunable without marginal gains, these modules will be formally downgraded, simplifying the pipeline to ensure robustness.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines a functionally precise framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through VASM-gated, token-local logits intervention. By formalizing spatial washout (`BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`), integrating explicit InfoNCE margins, and establishing greedy prefix-trie semantic masking, we construct a technical foundation for decode-time visual routing. Acknowledging the inherent risks of latency bottlenecks, algorithmic bloat, and dictionary brittleness, the claims herein are strictly governed by a pre-registered evaluation protocol spanning three evidence chains and five distinct defense lines. The immediate future execution of this protocol will definitively validate or falsify the operational viability of token-local visual intervention.
