# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for Multimodal Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for the next decoding step. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa), are highly competitive baselines, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three principles. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual patches. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with prompt-conditioned pruning** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a strictly bounded candidate window, ensuring computational viability even at high visual resolutions ($N_v > 4096$). Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via a deterministic, WordNet-backed whitelist. Because strict zero-shot intervention often collapses due to visual-lexical embedding asymmetry, TLRA utilizes a lightweight calibrator (`TLRA_calib`) trained on generic image-text pairs. While this introduces external data distribution knowledge, TLRA operates as a strictly **base-model-frozen plug-in**, leaving the underlying MLLM entirely unmodified. 

We organize the paper around a strict experimental contract: three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction (measured alongside Average Generated Length and variance to prevent length-collapse artifacts), structure and reasoning preservation (validating VASM against perplexity spikes), and the specific value of local over global evidence. To guarantee practical viability, we incorporate explicit efficiency bounds and hyperparameter sensitivity audits. The resulting paper offers a positive, falsifiable, and rigorously bounded proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the required evidence is distinctly local.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate, highly competitive baselines, but none of them directly answer the following positive hypothesis:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to four foundational challenges. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and output logits do not naturally live in the same space, making strict zero-shot lookup noisy. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc logits intervention can only reweight candidates that the base model already keeps alive. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression of visually unsupported tokens easily damages function words and subword (BPE) syntax. Fourth, **system cost at high resolutions**: computing patch-level support at every generation step scales poorly when modern vision encoders use dynamic patching ($N_v > 4096$).

TLRA therefore centers on three design commitments:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention via a Base-Model-Frozen Plug-in.** We utilize a highly lightweight calibrator to bridge embedding asymmetry. While this introduces generic external data (creating a slight asymmetry compared to purely unsupervised methods like DoLa), it leaves the base MLLM 100% frozen.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Prompt-Conditioned Pruning** as the local evidence extractor. By filtering patches based on prefill-stage prompt attention and evaluating only the Top-$M$ logits, TLRA guarantees a strictly bounded decode-time overhead of $O(M \times N_{active})$.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)** as a deterministic structure-preserving layer. Built on a strict WordNet lexical whitelist, VASM defaults to bypassing intervention for structural and abstract tokens. We explicitly acknowledge the *OCR paradox*: by protecting syntax, VASM natively ignores out-of-vocabulary (OOV) text and arbitrary OCR strings. TLRA accepts this trade-off, strictly confining its motivation and claims to spatial object grounding, relations, and counting.

To maintain scientific rigor, this paper avoids exploratory "fishing expeditions" and commits to a definitive evaluation protocol. We establish three distinct chains of evidence: hallucination reduction (controlled for length collapse), structural preservation (proving VASM's necessity via perplexity), and local-evidence validation (isolating the value of Top-$k$ anchoring against global pooling). Spatio-temporal video extensions are relegated to exploratory appendix probes, keeping the main narrative focused on the core mathematical spatial formulation.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. In our preliminary viability probes, a strict zero-shot readout (`TLRA_zero`) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yielded near-random overlap. The embedding asymmetry is too large. 

Therefore, the definitive intervention relies on **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain a fair comparison against inference baselines, the calibration protocol is strictly constrained:
1. **Plug-in Location & Low Cost:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib}$, to map the MLLM's visual tokens—*after* they have passed through the native vision-language connector and reside in the LLM's hidden space—directly into the LM head's pre-softmax vocabulary space. 
2. **Generic Data via Dense InfoNCE:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained solely on a generic image-caption dataset (e.g., CC3M subset). To prevent the projector from collapsing into a bag-of-words representation, we use an off-the-shelf parser (e.g., spaCy) to extract noun phrases from captions, align them to pseudo-bounding boxes via a frozen GroundingDINO/CLIP dense feature matcher, and train $\Phi_{calib}$ using a dense token-to-patch InfoNCE contrastive loss. It is *never* trained on downstream task data (e.g., POPE, MMBench).
3. **Frozen Base Model:** The vision encoder, vision-language connector, and the LLM remain 100% frozen. During inference, $\Phi_{calib}$ acts purely as a transient state-translator. We acknowledge that leveraging CC3M introduces external distribution knowledge, making TLRA a "base-model-frozen plug-in" rather than a strictly "training-free" method like DoLa.

### 3.2 High-Resolution Efficiency: Prompt-Conditioned Pruning and Adaptive Top-$k$

At decoding step $t$, TLRA must avoid evaluating the entire vocabulary against all visual patches, especially when visual tokens $N_v$ exceed 4096. We enforce efficiency via two mechanisms:

**1. Candidate Bounding:** We restrict intervention strictly to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=10$).

**2. Prompt-Conditioned Pre-decoding Pruning:** Unconditional ViT attention is notoriously noisy. Instead, we compute the aggregated cross-attention (or self-attention) from the *text prompt tokens* to the visual sequence during the initial prefill stage. We prune visual patches that receive negligible prompt attention, retaining an active subset $N_{active}$. 

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes candidate-wise local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule over the active patches:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_{active} \rceil\Big),
$$
followed by:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$
Here, `score(v_j, c)` is the cosine similarity between the projected patch representation (via $\Phi_{calib}$) and the embedding of candidate $c$. Calculating this exclusively for $M$ candidates over $N_{active}$ strictly bounds the overhead to $O(M \times N_{active})$.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a stable logits intervention, TLRA computes a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted as:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$
This relative formulation suppresses weakly supported candidates relative to stronger local competitors within the Top-$M$ window.

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses functional syntax, as isolated subwords lack visual grounding. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect these tokens via a binary mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0, 1\}$.

VASM is algorithmically defined using a **strict WordNet fail-safe**:
1. **Deterministic Whitelist Generation:** We query WordNet to extract all lemmas subsumed under the structural synsets `physical_entity.n.01` and `color.n.01`. A BPE token is assigned $\gamma=1$ *if and only if* it directly maps to a lemma in this set.
2. **Polysemy Delegation:** A context-free mapping is vulnerable to polysemy (e.g., "crane" the bird vs. "crane" the verb). VASM adopts a conservative safety-first rule: if a BPE token matches *any* physical sense in WordNet, it is flagged as $\gamma=1$. We rely on the base LLM's dominant original logits ($L_{orig}$) to naturally suppress grammatically incorrect verb usages, effectively delegating Word Sense Disambiguation back to the LLM. All function words and structurally ambiguous tokens default to $\gamma = 0$ ($L_{final} = L_{orig}$).
3. **The OCR Concession:** Because VASM explicitly defaults to $\gamma=0$ for out-of-vocabulary text to guarantee grammatical safety, it inherently bypasses arbitrary OCR strings. Preserving LLM fluency takes absolute precedence over hallucination intervention on out-of-dictionary text.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Rather than presenting cherry-picked subsets, our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The evaluation is structured into three interlocking evidence chains, followed by efficiency and sensitivity audits. All planned results represent the execution of this pre-registered protocol.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length-Collapse Check)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces object hallucination and that this reduction is not a deceptive artifact of the model generating shorter, truncated responses.
- **Table 1 Target:** `POPE` (Accuracy / F1) and `CHAIR` ($CHAIR_s$, $CHAIR_i$).
- **Baselines:** Base MLLM, `DoLa`, `VCD`, `OPERA`.
- **Mandatory Control Metric:** Average Generated Length (`AGL`) and **AGL Standard Deviation**.
- **Hypothesis:** TLRA will yield a lower `CHAIR` score and higher `POPE` F1 than baselines, while maintaining an `AGL` and variance within 5% of the Base model. Any mitigation configuration achieving SOTA CHAIR scores purely by collapsing AGL will be flagged as invalid.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The VASM Ablation)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not irreparably degrade general multimodal cognition, and prove that VASM effectively protects subword grammar.
- **Table 2 Target:** `MMBench V1.1` (Overall), `MME`, `MMMU` (Hard), and **Language Perplexity (PPL)** measured on WikiText.
- **Ablation Setup:** Base model vs. `TLRA_no_VASM` vs. `TLRA_full`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_no_VASM` will exhibit severe perplexity spikes (indicating grammar destruction) and crash on complex reasoning tasks (MMMU). `TLRA_full` must recover these scores to baseline levels, empirically proving that the deterministic WordNet dictionary approach successfully protects language structure.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate the core thesis that *token-local adaptive selection* is superior to coarse visual aggregation, specifically for spatial and existence grounding.
- **Table 3 Target:** `FREAK` (broken down into **Spatial Relations** vs. **Object Existence**) and `Object HalBench`. (Document/OCR tasks are strictly excluded per the VASM boundary).
- **Ablation Setup:** Base vs. `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool` ($k = N_{active}$) vs. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`. 
- **Critical Parity Constraint:** To ensure valid ablation, `TLRA_MeanPool` utilizes the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$ weights as `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_zero` will perform poorly, justifying the necessity of $\Phi_{calib}$. For spatial relations, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will demonstrate a statistically significant margin over global aggregation (`TLRA_MeanPool`), proving that $M \times N_{active}$ local matching is mathematically necessary.

### 4.4 Efficiency Audits, Sensitivity, and Failure Analysis

To map the operational boundaries of TLRA, the execution plan mandates the following diagnostic plots:

- **Figure 1 (Efficiency Scaling):** A line chart plotting `Tokens/Second` against Visual Token Volume ($N_v$ scaled up to 4096) for Base, VCD, and TLRA. We hypothesize TLRA's prompt-conditioned pruning will cap inference drops to $<50\%$ of the base speed, even at max resolution.
- **Figure 2 (Hyperparameter Sensitivity):** A $1 \times 3$ grid of line plots showing `POPE F1` and `Perplexity` as functions of varying $M$ (candidate window, e.g., 5, 10, 50), varying $\alpha$ (intervention strength), and varying $k_{min}$.
- **Figure 3 (Qualitative Failure Analysis):** We mandate heatmaps explicitly showcasing boundaries:
  1. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** An instance where TLRA fails because the correct token was ranked $> M$ by the base model.
  2. **VASM Masking Error:** An instance where the WordNet fallback masked a valid visual concept, causing TLRA to bypass intervention. Crucially, this visualization will explicitly display the raw $S_{raw}(c)$ score that *would* have been applied if VASM had not erroneously zeroed out the $\gamma$ mask.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its actual empirical execution. The core scientific contribution is a positive, decode-time framework that leverages token-local visual evidence while deploying VASM to prevent the structural collapse of generated language. However, several hard limitations define the boundaries of this method:

1. **The "Training-Free" Distinction:** While TLRA operates as a base-model-frozen plug-in during inference, the $\Phi_{calib}$ projector is trained on generic external data (CC3M). This creates a slight baseline asymmetry compared to purely unsupervised, zero-shot methods like DoLa.
2. **The OCR / OOV Paradox:** To guarantee the safety of functional syntax and multi-token entities, VASM's deterministic WordNet whitelist explicitly sacrifices intervention on out-of-vocabulary strings. TLRA is inherently ineffective for arbitrary OCR transcription, prioritizing absolute grammatical safety over in-the-wild text intervention.
3. **Polysemy and Context-Free Mapping:** VASM's reliance on isolated BPE-to-lemma mapping makes it vulnerable to polysemy. By defaulting to $\gamma=1$ if any physical sense exists, VASM acts conservatively, delegating Word Sense Disambiguation entirely to the base LLM's unmodified logits.
4. **Temporal Generalization:** The formulation of TLRA is purely spatial. Extending Adaptive Top-$k$ across a spatio-temporal volume ($T \times H \times W$) for video applications requires formal temporal decay mechanisms to handle sequence length explosion. We relegate preliminary spatio-temporal probes strictly to the Appendix, keeping the main paper focused on spatial benchmarks.

By explicitly declaring these limitations and mathematically formalizing the trade-offs, we establish TLRA not as an unconstrained panacea, but as a scientifically principled mechanism for correcting spatial and object-level visual grounding during generation.
