# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for Multimodal Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for the next decoding step. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies—such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa)—are highly competitive baselines, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three mathematical commitments. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual patches via a lightweight, base-model-frozen calibrator. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Prompt-Conditioned Pruning** bounds the decode-time overhead, supplemented by an entropy-driven fallback mechanism to preserve dense visual evaluation during long-horizon generation. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax via a deterministic, WordNet-backed whitelist. To address modern BPE fragmentation, VASM employs a tokenizer-aware subword boundary check, intervening on valid physical entity prefixes while relying on the base LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete subword suffixes. 

To ensure fair comparison against purely training-free methods, we organize our evaluation protocol around a strict experimental contract: three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction (controlling for length collapse and calibrator data leakage), structure preservation (quantifying suffix survival via a formalized BPE Completion Success Rate and utilizing document-understanding as an exact negative control), and isolating the distinct value of local adaptive selection against both global pooling (`TLRA_MeanPool`) and random patch selection (`TLRA_RandomK`). The resulting blueprint offers a positive, falsifiable, and rigorously bounded proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and attribute binding, where the required evidence is distinctly local.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate, highly competitive baselines that effectively modulate global generation trajectories. However, they do not directly answer the following positive hypothesis:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, corrupting subword KV-cache momentum, or degrading downstream reasoning ability?**

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to embedding asymmetry, candidate limitation at the decoding head, the structural collapse risk of penalizing functional syntax, and the prohibitive system cost of computing patch-level support at high resolutions ($N_v > 4096$).

TLRA centers on three design commitments to address these challenges:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention via a Base-Model-Frozen Plug-in.** We bridge embedding asymmetry using a lightweight calibrator (`TLRA_calib`) trained on generic noun chunks and scene graphs. We explicitly clarify our taxonomy upfront: unlike purely training-free methods, TLRA introduces generic external distribution data. However, it leaves the base MLLM 100% frozen. Through strict parity ablations against global pooling and random patch selection using the exact same calibrator, we mathematically isolate the value of token-local decoding from this external prior.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Entropy-Driven Fallback.** By filtering patches based on prefill-stage prompt attention and bounding evaluations to the Top-$M$ logits, TLRA controls decode-time latency. Acknowledging the "prefill pruning blindspot"—where visually grounded objects absent from the prompt emerge late in open-ended generation—we incorporate a dynamic fallback mechanism. Tuned strictly on a held-out split, this fallback defaults to dense visual evaluation when attention entropy spikes during long-horizon reasoning.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking).** Built on a strict WordNet whitelist, VASM deterministically bypasses intervention for structural and abstract tokens. We formalize two critical operational concessions: the *OCR paradox* (VASM natively ignores arbitrary text strings, serving as a zero-trigger negative control on document tasks) and *Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum*. VASM triggers on valid subword prefixes but relies on the base LLM to complete suffixes. To evaluate the systemic risk of "BPE stuttering," we explicitly quantify suffix survival rates.

To maintain scientific rigor, we commit to a definitive evaluation protocol strictly mapped to 2D image-centric benchmarks. We establish three distinct chains of evidence: hallucination reduction (audited for calibrator data leakage), structural preservation (proving VASM's safety via an OCR negative control and BPE survival metric), and local-evidence validation (isolating Top-$k$ anchoring against global pooling on spatial and object existence tasks).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. A strict zero-shot readout (`TLRA_zero`) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yields near-random overlap due to massive embedding asymmetry. 

Therefore, our intervention relies on **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain fair comparisons against inference baselines, the calibration protocol is constrained:
1. **Plug-in Location & Low Cost:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_v \times |V|}$, mapping the MLLM's visual tokens—*after* the vision-language connector—into the LM head's pre-softmax vocabulary space. 
2. **Generic Data via Dense InfoNCE:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained solely on a strictly limited generic dataset (e.g., a 100K subset of CC3M/Visual Genome). We extract spaCy noun chunks and scene-graph relational triplets, aligning them to pseudo-bounding boxes via GroundingDINO. The text chunks are encoded using a frozen CLIP-ViT-L/14 text encoder to match standard MLLM alignment practices. We train $\Phi_{calib}$ using dense token-to-patch InfoNCE. $\Phi_{calib}$ is *never* trained on downstream instruction-tuning data.
3. **Frozen Base Model:** The vision encoder, vision-language connector, and LLM remain 100% frozen. 

### 3.2 High-Resolution Efficiency: Prompt-Conditioned Pruning and Fallback

At decoding step $t$, evaluating the entire vocabulary against $>4096$ visual patches is computationally prohibitive. 

**1. Candidate Bounding:** We restrict intervention strictly to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=10$).

**2. Prompt-Conditioned Pruning with Dynamic Fallback:** We compute the aggregated cross-attention from the prefill text prompt tokens to the visual sequence, pruning patches with negligible attention to retain an active subset $N_{active}$. 

Crucially, we explicitly acknowledge a **Prefill Pruning Blindspot**: an autoregressive model executing long-horizon generation may require visual evidence for objects never mentioned in the static prompt. To prevent catastrophic deletion of latent evidence, TLRA enforces an **Entropy-Driven Dynamic Fallback**: we measure the normalized Shannon entropy $H_{attn}$ of the sink-excluded attention weights. If $H_{attn} > \theta_{fallback}$, pruning is bypassed entirely ($N_{active} = N_v$). To prevent test-set data leakage, $\theta_{fallback}$ is calibrated strictly on a held-out split of 1,000 samples from the VisDial validation set. We will report the exact derived $\theta_{fallback}$ value in the final manuscript to ensure exact reproducibility without test-set tuning.

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule over the active patches:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_{active} \rceil\Big), \quad S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{sim(\Phi_{calib}(v_j), c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a stable logits intervention, TLRA computes a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted as:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses functional syntax. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect these tokens via a binary mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0, 1\}$.

VASM is algorithmically defined using a strict WordNet fail-safe:
1. **Deterministic Whitelist Generation:** A token receives $\gamma=1$ *if and only if* its string maps to a lemma under structural synsets like `physical_entity.n.01`.
2. **Polysemy Delegation:** If a token matches *any* physical sense, it receives $\gamma=1$. The base LLM's dominant original logits ($L_{orig}$) naturally suppress grammatically incorrect usages.
3. **Tokenizer-Aware BPE Momentum & Collision Avoidance:** Modern tokenizers fragment words. VASM triggers $\gamma=1$ only on the valid initial prefix, relying on the base LLM's unmodified autoregressive momentum to complete suffix tokens ($\gamma=0$). To mitigate false-prefix collisions, we utilize a **Tokenizer-Aware Subword Boundary Check**. A length filter ($L_{char} \ge 4$) is applied if the token acts as a subword prefix (e.g., lacking a start-of-word metacharacter). This threshold is theoretically grounded in the statistical distribution of SentencePiece subwords, where prefixes shorter than 4 characters disproportionately represent common functional fragments rather than distinct physical entities. Whole words (e.g., "cat") bypass this filter.
4. **The OCR Concession:** VASM explicitly defaults to $\gamma=0$ for out-of-vocabulary text to guarantee grammatical safety, inherently bypassing arbitrary OCR strings. Preserving LLM fluency takes absolute precedence over hallucination intervention on out-of-dictionary text.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Rather than presenting cherry-picked subsets, our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The evaluation is structured into three interlocking evidence chains, followed by efficiency and sensitivity audits on 2D image-centric benchmarks.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length & Leakage Checks)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces object hallucination, that the improvement is not an artifact of shorter responses, and that the gain stems from *local extraction* rather than calibrator memorization.
- **Table 1 Target:** `POPE` and `CHAIR` across two decoding temperatures ($T=0.2, 0.7$).
- **Baselines:** Base MLLM, `DoLa`, `VCD`, `OPERA`, **`TLRA_MeanPool`**, and **`TLRA_RandomK`**.
- **Mandatory Control Metrics:** Average Generated Length (`AGL`), **AGL Standard Deviation**, and **VASM Trigger Rate (%)**.
- **The Vocabulary Leakage Audit:** To definitively rule out calibrator information leakage, we enforce an audit reporting the exact noun-chunk vocabulary overlap between the $\Phi_{calib}$ training data and benchmark targets (e.g., POPE objects), explicitly stratifying performance on *seen* vs. *unseen* entities.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will yield lower `CHAIR` scores and higher `POPE` F1 than all baselines. Crucially, it must significantly outperform `TLRA_MeanPool` (global aggregation) and `TLRA_RandomK` (uniform random patch selection using the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$).
- **The Asterisk Rule:** Any configuration achieving SOTA CHAIR scores by collapsing AGL variance by $> 5\%$ compared to the base model will be flagged (*) as an invalid artifact.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The Negative Control)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not degrade general multimodal cognition, quantify the survival of BPE autoregressive momentum, and validate the "OCR Concession" via an explicit negative control.
- **Table 2 Target:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MMMU` (Hard), **Language Perplexity (PPL)**, and `DocVQA`.
- **Quantitative BPE Metric:** **BPE Completion Success Rate (BPE-CSR)**. BPE-CSR is procedurally defined as the percentage of instances (measured over a curated list of 500 multi-token physical entities) where forcing a VASM-triggered prefix ($\gamma=1$) successfully results in the base LLM generating a valid semantic continuation (exact string match or valid WordNet synonym) for the remaining fragments, without decaying into ungrammatical stuttering.
- **Ablation Setup:** Base model vs. `TLRA_no_VASM` vs. `TLRA_full`. 
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_no_VASM` will exhibit severe perplexity spikes, catastrophic BPE-CSR drop, and crash on `MMMU`. `TLRA_full` must recover these scores to baseline levels, keeping PPL strictly $\le +5\%$ of the Base model. 
- **The Strict Negative Control:** Because VASM masks out OCR tokens, `TLRA_full` must demonstrate a `VASM Trigger Rate` statistically indistinguishable from $0\%$ on `DocVQA`, guaranteeing zero degradation of the Base model's native reading capabilities at the cost of zero hallucination improvement on that task.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate that *token-local adaptive selection* is superior to coarse visual aggregation across both spatial reasoning and pure object existence.
- **Table 3 Target:** `FREAK` (explicitly broken down into **Spatial Relations** vs. **Object Existence**) and `Object HalBench`. 
- **Ablation Setup:** Base vs. `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool` ($k = N_{active}$) vs. `TLRA_RandomK` vs. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`. 
- **Critical Parity Constraint:** `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` utilize the identical $\Phi_{calib}$ weights as `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_zero` will perform near-randomly. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` is hypothesized to demonstrate a massive, statistically significant margin ($p < 0.05$) over both `TLRA_MeanPool` and `TLRA_RandomK` on *Object Existence*. On *Spatial Relations*, the gap may be narrower due to the inherent difficulty of capturing complex spatial prepositions via generic InfoNCE calibrators.

### 4.4 Efficiency Audits, Sensitivity, and Failure Analysis

To map operational boundaries, the execution plan mandates the following diagnostic visualizations:

- **Figure 1 (Efficiency Pareto & Fallback Audit):** A Pareto frontier plotting `POPE F1` vs. `Decode Latency (ms/token)` across varying candidate bounds $M \in \{5, 10, 50, \text{All}\}$. Additionally, a scatter plot correlating the **Fallback Activation Rate** with average sequence length, mathematically proving the system correctly reverts to dense evaluation to bypass the prefill blindspot on long tasks.
- **Figure 2 (Hyperparameter Causal Analysis):** A scatter plot showing `VASM Trigger Rate (%)` (X-axis) vs. `POPE F1` improvement (Y-axis) to prove the intervention rate is the causal mechanism for score increases.
- **Figure 3 (Qualitative Failure Analysis):** Mandated heatmaps showcasing intervention limits:
  1. **BPE Stuttering Analysis:** Visualizing the actual top-5 token probabilities from the LM head for the suffix step, showing how the base model's original suffix probabilities drop after an overly aggressive prefix intervention, resulting in stuttering (e.g., "straw-straw-berry").
  2. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** An instance where the correct physical token was ranked $> M$ by the base model, rendering it invisible to TLRA.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its empirical execution. The scientific contribution is a positive, decode-time framework leveraging token-local visual evidence alongside VASM to prevent structural collapse. However, hard limitations define its operational boundaries:

1. **The Calibrator Asymmetry and Leakage Risk:** While TLRA operates as a base-model-frozen plug-in during inference, the $\Phi_{calib}$ projector is trained on generic external data. The scientific validity of TLRA relies entirely on the `TLRA_MeanPool`/`TLRA_RandomK` ablations and the Vocabulary Leakage Audit to strictly isolate local extraction mechanics from dataset memorization.
2. **Prefill Pruning Blindspot and Entropy Fallback Hypersensitivity:** Relying on prefill cross-attention is architecturally perilous. For long-horizon multimodal reasoning, objects emerge that were never in the prompt. Consequently, our entropy-driven fallback will frequently trigger, defaulting to dense evaluation. The "pruning efficiency" claim is thus heavily restricted to short-horizon targeted QA.
3. **Calibrator Data Boundaries on Spatial Relations:** An InfoNCE objective mapping generic objects to dense text inherently struggles to capture complex spatial prepositions. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` fundamentally bounds its spatial reasoning upper limit to the quality of $\Phi_{calib}$.
4. **WordNet Rigidity:** VASM's deterministic protection mechanism is strictly bound to the English WordNet database (`physical_entity.n.01`). It is highly brittle to novel compounds, modern slang, and non-English reasoning, inherently lacking multilingual scalability without parallel lexical databases.
5. **The KV-Cache Alteration and BPE Stuttering:** TLRA fundamentally relies on the base LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete BPE fragments. Artificially suppressing competing logits at step $t$ alters the hidden states added to the KV-cache. If the prefix intervention is too aggressive, suffix generation mathematically collapses, leading to observable "BPE stuttering".
6. **The OCR / OOV Paradox:** To guarantee functional syntax safety, VASM explicitly sacrifices intervention on out-of-vocabulary strings. TLRA is inherently ineffective for arbitrary OCR transcription, prioritizing absolute grammatical safety over in-the-wild text intervention.
