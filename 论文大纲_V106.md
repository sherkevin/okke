# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Semantically Initialized Decode-Time Routing Head for Visual Existence Verification

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently hallucinate when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during entity grounding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations modulate general model confidence, they lack explicitly trained cross-modal pathways. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects visual patches directly into BPE vocabulary logits to penalize unsupported entity tokens during autoregressive decoding. 

Recognizing the mathematical limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation and vocabulary sparsity, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **context-independent visual existence verifier** rather than a complex spatial reasoner. It reliably answers "Is this noun in the image?" to suppress pure hallucinations. To justify its $\sim$131M parameter footprint, TLRA is subjected to a strict evaluation against both standard instruction-tuned LoRAs and budget-matched restricted LoRAs. TLRA operates on three principles: (1) A semantically initialized projection head (`lm_head` anchoring) that solves the unseen-token Sparsity Trap while navigating a static vs. dynamic memory bandwidth tradeoff; (2) Absolute Evidence Scoring over bounded candidate windows, featuring a strict syntactic logit floor to prevent False Negative behavioral interference; and (3) Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM). VASM restricts interventions to physical nouns via an offline dictionary, employing an **Unambiguous Stem Restriction** to handle whitespace tokenization and semantic collisions (e.g., `_cat` vs. `catastrophe`). This work establishes a pre-registered, boundary-aware protocol for parameter-efficient decode-time visual routing, explicitly mapping its structural, morphological, and relational failure boundaries.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD) are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts. However, they cannot introduce new cross-modal alignment pathways. Conversely, standard parametric approaches (like LoRA) operate entirely in the prefill phase, compressing spatial evidence into the global context window without explicitly routing it to specific token generation steps. This leaves a critical gap: **How can we inject fine-grained, token-specific visual evidence directly into decode-time logits adjustment without destroying morphological syntax or hitting severe memory bandwidth walls?**

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is explicitly formulated as a late-fusion parametric fine-tuning head. TLRA extracts local spatial support to bias decode-time logits, strictly limited to entity-centric grounding. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a 50k masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit supervision that zero-shot methods lack. 

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly reject the framing of TLRA as a "context-aware spatial reasoner." Mathematically, TLRA acts as a **Global Visual Existence Checker**. By projecting patch features into the vocabulary space independently of the LLM's text history ($Y_{<t}$), TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. While this effectively suppresses pure hallucinations, it establishes a hard theoretical boundary: TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. We embrace this constraint, demonstrating that context-blind existence checking is highly effective for core hallucination reduction if properly bounded.

Furthermore, we dispel the "lightweight" illusion of vocabulary-space projections. For a standard architecture ($D=4096$, $V=32000$), TLRA introduces $\sim$131M parameters. To justify this architectural complexity against modern baselines, TLRA must survive a rigorous evaluation against both a standard mixed-instruction LoRA and a data-matched restricted LoRA. While a context-aware LoRA trained on 150k mixed instructions may excel on pure grounding, TLRA's structural advantage lies in its **Zero-Interference Guarantee on Weights**: unlike restricted LoRAs, which risk catastrophic forgetting of general instruction-following when overfitted to 50k captions, TLRA operates entirely post-hoc, theoretically perfectly preserving base reasoning capabilities. However, to ensure this weight preservation translates to *behavioral preservation*, we explicitly audit for False Negative suppression.

Finally, we confront the **Vocabulary Sparsity Trap** and **BPE Collisions**. Training a $4096 \times 32000$ matrix from scratch on 50k captions mathematically guarantees failure on unseen tokens due to random initialization. To solve this, TLRA leverages Semantic Initialization tied to the LLM's `lm_head`. Additionally, to prevent morphological corruption (e.g., penalizing `_cat` and inadvertently breaking "catastrophe"), TLRA introduces Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM) with strict Unambiguous Stem Restrictions. 

Our evaluation is structured as a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains and five defenses**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. Generic Collapse:** Evaluating standard and modern entity grounding (POPE, AMBER, MMHAL-Bench) while auditing for Morphological Error Rate (MER), Object Recall, and False Negative Rate (FNR) to prove TLRA is not merely silencing noun generation or becoming overly timid.
2. **Structure Preservation & The Sparsity Trap:** Auditing VASM's protection of general capabilities (MMBench) against the catastrophic forgetting of restricted LoRAs, and explicitly testing TLRA's Semantic Initialization on a held-out "Unseen Objects" vocabulary split.
3. **Local Evidence Value & Relational Boundaries:** Isolating spatial advantage on FREAK, while explicitly documenting TLRA's failure boundary on a Multi-Instance/Relational stress test and Top-M Unrecoverability limits.
4. **Semantic Boundaries and OOD Fallback:** Bounding the method via an Action/Verb stress test and verifying graceful degradation on Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOD) jargon.
5. **Hardware Bandwidth Physics:** A formal evaluation of the static vs. dynamic memory footprint, mapping real-world latency (TTFT and TPOT in ms), including scaling projections for 128k vocabularies.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Architectural Scope, Semantic Initialization, and the Late-Fusion Head

TLRA targets standard ViT + MLP projector architectures where spatial provenance is preserved across visual tokens $X_v$. TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage:

**1. `TLRA_calib` (The Parametric Routing Head):** A linear projection matrix $W_{calib} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V}$ maps cached spatial visual states of dimension $D$ directly to the lexical vocabulary size $V$. For a Llama-2 based model ($D=4096$, $V=32000$), $W_{calib}$ constitutes $\sim$131 million parameters. 
- **The Sparsity Trap and Semantic Initialization:** If $W_{calib}$ is randomly initialized, training on a restricted 50k caption subset leaves unseen vocabulary tokens mathematically un-updated (yielding pure noise). To enable zero-shot generalization, **$W_{calib}$ is initialized directly from the base LLM's pre-trained `lm_head` weights.** This grounds the projection in a semantically continuous space. 
- **Training Formulation:** $W_{calib}$ is trained on a bounded subset of 50k conceptual captions. **The base LLM and Vision Encoder remain entirely frozen; gradients flow *only* into $W_{calib}$.** The loss is strictly masked to backpropagate only on tokens where VASM $\gamma(y_t) = 1$ (physical entities). $\mathcal{L}_{calib} = -\sum_{t} \gamma(y_t) \log P(y_t \mid X_v, Y_{<t})$. 
- **The Parametric Baseline Contract:** Because 131M parameters significantly exceeds standard LoRA configurations, TLRA is audited against two controls: a "Standard LoRA" trained on a broad 150k mixed-instruction dataset, and a "Restricted LoRA" trained on the exact same 50k masked objective (to probe catastrophic forgetting).

**2. `TLRA_zero` (Representation Sanity Check):** Computes cosine similarity directly between frozen post-projector visual features ($X_v$) and the LLM's discrete text embeddings. Because ViT/MLP features align with the LLM's *input* layers rather than its output vocabulary space, this mapping is geometrically incoherent, proving that decode-time spatial routing requires the learned parametric projection.

### 3.2 The Hardware Bandwidth Tradeoff: Static vs. Dynamic Memory Footprint

At each decoding step $t$, TLRA maps spatial features $X_v$ to lexical biases for a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$. 

**A. Static Precomputation (Optimizing TPOT over TTFT):** During prefill, cached visual states are mapped to the full vocabulary, yielding a static bias matrix $B = X_v \cdot W_{calib}$. This incurs a one-time FLOP penalty degrading TTFT, but limits step computation to uncoalesced memory reads from High Bandwidth Memory (HBM).
**B. Dynamic Sliced-Matmul (Optimizing static footprint over FLOPs):** We dynamically compute the projection *only* for the Top-$M$ candidates at each step: $B_{step} = X_v \cdot W_{calib}[:, \mathcal{C}_t]$. This trades FLOPs to avoid HBM bandwidth saturation caused by uncoalesced gather operations. We mandate real-world ms latency measurements for this tradeoff.

### 3.3 The Context-Blind "Existence Checker" and the Absolute Logit Floor

Mathematically, TLRA acts as a **Global Existence Checker**. It computes a raw spatial support score by pooling the top-$k$ matching patches for a candidate token $c$:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} B_{j, c} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
Here, $\beta$ is a learned scalar calibration parameter. Crucially, $S_{raw}$ has no dependence on the text history $Y_{<t}$ outside of the candidate proposal window. It verifies if visual evidence for candidate $c$ exists *anywhere* in the image, theoretically guaranteeing failure on multi-instance/relational queries. 

To apply this evidence without forcing behavioral collapse (e.g., heavily penalizing a true positive noun until it falls below a verb due to a large base logit margin $\Delta_L$), we introduce an **Absolute Syntax Floor**. Let $L_{floor} = \max_{v \notin \mathcal{V}_{noun}} L_{orig}(v) + \epsilon$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token. The final adjusted logit is:
$$
L_{final}(c) = \max \Big( L_{floor}, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot \min(\Delta_L, \delta_{max}) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big)
$$
**Noun-Only Fallback Guarantee:** By bounding the penalty to never drop below $L_{floor}$, we mathematically guarantee that TLRA cannot alter the grammatical structure of the sentence (e.g., replacing a noun with an adjective), even if the visual existence score is low.

### 3.4 VASM, BPE Collisions, and the Unambiguous Stem Restriction

To preserve syntax, TLRA utilizes VASM, an offline deterministic binary mask ($\gamma \in \{0, 1\}^V$) extracted from English WordNet physical entity synsets. For a standard 32k Llama-2 vocabulary, the rigorous filtering isolates approximately 4,500 viable physical noun tokens ($\sim$14% of $\mathcal{V}$).

**Addressing BPE Tokenizer Leakiness:** BPE tokenization is exquisitely sensitive to preceding whitespace (e.g., `_apple` vs. `apple`) and semantic stem collisions (e.g., `_cat` acting as the prefix for "cat", "catastrophe", or "catalyst"). 
VASM enforces an **Unambiguous Stem Restriction**: 
1. **Whitespace Inclusivity:** Both whitespace-prefixed and raw tokens are mapped.
2. **Abstract Collision Filtering:** A subword stem is explicitly excluded from the VASM dictionary ($\gamma = 0$) if its top 10 most frequent completions in standard English corpora are abstract non-physical concepts. Interventions are restricted purely to unambiguous initial stems of groundable nouns.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict computational, structural, and relational boundaries. By explicitly reframing TLRA as a targeted, context-independent visual existence verifier, we identify the following mathematically rigid limitations:

1. **The Vocabulary Sparsity Trap and Semantic Initialization:** Training a $D \times V$ matrix on bounded data leaves massive portions of the vocabulary mathematically un-updated. While tying $W_{calib}$ to the `lm_head` provides a continuous semantic starting space, TLRA remains structurally vulnerable to semantic drift, multilingual tasks, and migrations to newer, larger tokenizers. Transitioning from a 32k to 128k vocabulary inflates the routing head to over half a billion parameters.
2. **The "Existence Checker" Fallacy (Relational Blindness):** The mathematical formulation of $S_{raw}$ pools patches independently of text context. TLRA cannot resolve multi-instance or relational spatial queries. It trades complex reasoning for robust, lightweight decode-time hallucination suppression.
3. **Behavioral Interference (The False Negative Risk):** While TLRA guarantees *structural* Zero-Interference (no base weights are altered), it risks *behavioral* interference if it generates a false negative and suppresses a correct token. We enforce the Absolute Syntax Floor in Eq 1 to prevent severe grammatical collapse, but aggressive $\alpha$ scaling will invariably induce model timidity.
4. **VASM and BPE Fragility:** Relying on an offline WordNet dictionary mapped to specific BPE stems inherently bounds scalability. Approximately only 14% of the standard 32k vocabulary maps cleanly to unambiguous physical stems, making TLRA an inherently incomplete, albeit highly precise, intervention.
5. **Top-M Unrecoverability:** The intervention is mathematically constrained by the base model's candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$. If strong language priors marginalize the ground-truth visual token below the Top-$M$ cutoff, absolute evidence penalties cannot rescue it.

Future execution of this pre-registered protocol will definitively confirm whether a semantically-initialized decode-time routing head can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities, sparsity traps, and relational blindness to provide scalable, targeted visual grounding.
