**1. Fatal Logical Flaw in Semantic-Fallback (Eq. 10)**
Your proposed "Confident Semantic-Fallback" mechanism is fundamentally flawed and actively promotes the exact problem it claims to solve. According to Eq. 8 and Eq. 9, the verification head outputs $\hat{y}^{(i)} \rightarrow 0$ when an object proposal is deemed invalid or a false positive. However, in Eq. 10, when $\hat{y}^{(i)} \rightarrow 0$, your system interpolates the prior back to $E_{text}(c_i)$, which is the pure textual embedding of the false object. By stripping the spatial features but explicitly retaining the textual semantic embedding of an object that *does not exist* in that region, you are forcefully injecting hallucinated textual concepts directly into the LLM’s context window. You are feeding the language model the word of the hallucinated object, practically guaranteeing that the LLM will hallucinate it in the output. This mathematical formulation contradicts the entire premise of the paper.

**2. Severe Train-Test Mismatch (Section 4.3 vs. 4.4)**
You claim a "decoupled" training strategy prevents the LLM from learning to hallucinate. However, this decoupling introduces a glaring train-test discrepancy. During training (Eq. 7), the LLM is exclusively exposed to uncorrupted, clean hybrid priors $\mathcal{P}_{hybrid}$. It never sees the interpolated, fallback priors $\mathcal{P}_{mod}^*$. Yet, during inference deployment (Eq. 10), the LLM is fed continuous interpolated vectors. The LLM has never been optimized to interpret or decode these mixed-state latent representations. The claim that this is a "mathematically sound residual gating" is unsubstantiated when the downstream LLM has not been conditioned on this distribution during autoregressive training.

**3. Naive Feature Extraction on Coarse Grids (Section 4.2.2)**
Applying RoI-Align directly on ViT feature maps (Eq. 5) demonstrates a lack of understanding of Vision Transformer architectures. The spatial resolution of later ViT stages (e.g., CLIP ViT-L/14) is notoriously coarse (typically 16x16 or 24x24). Applying continuous 4D bounding boxes via RoI-Align onto a highly quantized, low-resolution grid will result in severe feature aliasing. Small objects or heavily overlapping objects will yield identical extracted features. Claiming this provides "strict bounding" and "entirely bypasses error-prone global attention" is practically false, as the grid simply lacks the sub-pixel resolution necessary for meaningful RoI-Align operations. Furthermore, using a single global softmax weighting ($\alpha_l$) for layer aggregation assumes all spatial regions and object scales benefit from the exact same combination of semantic layers, which is empirically false.

**4. Uncalibrated Confidence Gating (Section 4.2.1)**
You treat the detector confidence $s_i$ as a reliable, monotonic scalar probability. External object detectors (e.g., YOLO, Faster R-CNN) are heavily uncalibrated. A confidence score of 0.6 from one class may be more reliable than 0.9 from another. Applying a universal linear projection $W_s s_i + \beta_s$ (Eq. 3) without any calibration or normalization techniques is mathematically naive. Furthermore, multiplying the entire textual embedding vector $E_{text}$ by this scalar suppresses the magnitude of the semantic vector simply because the detector is mathematically underconfident, artificially destroying the manifold structure you claim to protect.

**5. Trivial Synthetic Negatives (Section 4.2.3)**
Your "Strict Contextual Hard-Negative Synthesis" relies on trivially swapping bounding boxes of disjoint objects with IoU < 0.1. This does not represent the reality of LVLM hallucinations. Models hallucinate due to statistical co-occurrence biases (e.g., predicting a "mouse" because a "keyboard" is present) or complex visual occlusions, not because of random, non-overlapping bounding box permutations. Training the verification head on these artificial, easily separable synthetic negatives guarantees it will overfit to trivial spatial mismatches and fail completely on real, context-driven LVLM hallucinations.

Claiming this is a "realistic computational-latency trade-off" without rigorous theoretical FLOPs analysis or concrete hardware benchmarks is unacceptable for a systems-level claim.
