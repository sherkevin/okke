**1. The MLP Confounding Fallacy in Modality Decomposition (Equations 1 & 2)**
The fundamental premise of the Modality-Aware Residual Stream Decomposition (MRSD) is mathematically flawed. In Equation 2, you assign the entirety of the MLP layer outputs ($\sum \text{MLP}^{(l)}(h_t^{(l-1)})$) to the linguistic-sourced component $x_{t, \mathcal{T}}$. This demonstrates a naive understanding of deep multimodal Transformers. After the first layer, the hidden state $h_t^{(l-1)}$ is heavily entangled, containing both visual and textual information. The MLP projects this fused state through non-linearities. Arbitrarily classifying the entire MLP output as "textual" completely ignores the cross-modal fusion happening within the feed-forward networks. Consequently, your $x_{t, \mathcal{V}}$ is vastly underrepresented, and $x_{t, \mathcal{T}}$ is heavily contaminated with visual features, rendering the entire downstream "zero-intervention" claim invalid.

**2. Invalid Macroscopic Taylor Approximation (Equations 3 & 4)**
You propose a first-order Taylor approximation of the final LayerNorm ($\text{LN}_f$) using the Jacobian matrix $J_t$ to separate the logit attributions. Taylor expansion is only mathematically sound for *infinitesimal* or highly localized perturbations. The visual and textual residual accumulators ($x_{t, \mathcal{V}}$ and $x_{t, \mathcal{T}}$) are not small perturbations; they are massive, macroscopic orthogonal components of the hidden state $h_t^{(L)}$. Applying a first-order Jacobian to non-linear variance division over such large vector spaces guarantees catastrophic approximation errors. The claim that this "completely resolves the semantic gap" is unsubstantiated mathematical hand-waving.

**3. Fatal Logical Contradiction in Hallucination Indexing (Equation 6)**
Your definition of Visual Hallucination Severity (VHS) contains a fatal logical paradox. You use the final layer's raw attention allocation to the image ($w_t = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{V}} A_{t, j}^{(L)}$) as a weight to isolate visual concepts. However, if a model is genuinely hallucinating an object based *purely* on linguistic inertia (the exact scenario you are trying to penalize), its attention to the visual tokens at the final layer will inherently be extremely low ($w_t \approx 0$). By multiplying the grounding margin by $w_t$ in Equation 6, you mathematically suppress the penalty for the most severe hallucinations. The metric is fundamentally self-defeating. Furthermore, relying on final-layer attention weights assumes "attention is explanation," a hypothesis rigorously debunked in recent NLP literature (attention often targets functional tokens like periods or CLS, not semantic visual entities).

**4. Misleading Computational and Decoding Claims (Section 3.4)**
The claim of maintaining "strictly $\mathcal{O}(1)$ vocabulary matrix projections" while tracking accumulators natively is highly misleading. Splitting the residual stream requires modifying the core attention mechanisms of the LVLM to separately track $x_{t, \mathcal{V}}$ and $x_{t, \mathcal{T}}$ across all $L$ layers. Without custom, highly optimized CUDA kernels (which are not mentioned), this requires unrolling the attention mechanism in standard frameworks like PyTorch, resulting in massive memory bandwidth overhead and significantly reduced inference speed. Furthermore, your Modality-Guided Decoding (Equation 7) blindly adds a calibration scalar to the logits. Decades of contrastive decoding literature show that such unconstrained logit subtraction heavily degrades grammatical coherence, leading to repetitive or broken text, which you completely fail to address or bound.
