**Review Evaluation**

The proposed method exhibits severe theoretical flaws, mathematical inconsistencies, and naïve assumptions regarding Transformer inner interpretability. The claim of providing a "strictly mathematically consistent" and "purely visually isolated" intervention framework falls apart under rigorous mechanistic scrutiny. 

**1. Fundamental Theoretical Flaws in "Visual Isolation"**
*   **Contamination of Visual Tokens (Eq. 3):** The formulation explicitly assumes that aggregating attention directed at visual tokens ($i \in V$) isolates "pure visual residuals." This is a profound misunderstanding of deep Transformer dynamics. By layer $l > 1$, the hidden states of the visual tokens $\boldsymbol{h}_i^{(l-1)}$ have already undergone self-attention and MLP transformations, meaning they are deeply entangled with textual prefix information and other positional semantics. You are not extracting a "pure visual direction"; you are extracting a deeply entangled pseudo-visual state.
*   **The MLP Bypass Fallacy:** The paper acknowledges that "MLP layers introduce deep non-linear feature entanglement" and uses this as an excuse to completely ignore them. In modern LLMs, MLPs serve as the primary key-value associative memory that translates raw attention signals into vocabulary-space concepts. By bypassing the MLP sub-layers and directly projecting intermediate attention outputs ($\boldsymbol{v}_t$) to the logit space, the method relies on raw, untranslated representational noise. Intermediate attention vectors do not natively reside in the final unembedding basis.

**2. Mathematical and Architectural Inconsistencies**
*   **Hypocrisy in Normalization Claims (Eq. 5 & 6):** The authors heavily criticize previous methods for ignoring RMSNorm and claim their method "strictly adheres" to it. Yet, the solution provided (dividing the intermediate vector $\boldsymbol{v}_t$ by the final layer's variance scalar $\sigma_t$) is a rudimentary, well-known linear approximation used in basic Direct Logit Attribution (DLA). It is mathematically false that $\boldsymbol{z}_t^{vis} = W(\boldsymbol{v}_t / \sigma_t)$ respects the non-linear topology, as injecting or amplifying $\boldsymbol{v}_t$ would natively alter the variance $\sigma_t$ itself. The paper merely replaces one approximation with another while claiming absolute mathematical rigor.
*   **Naïve Attention Aggregation (Eq. 4):** Weighting layer-wise contributions dynamically by the gross sum of attention weights $m_t^{(l)}$ relies on the widely debunked "Attention is Explanation" fallacy. Not all attention heads process semantic visual information; many are responsible for structural, positional, or induction-head routing. Summing gross attention mass blindly conflates syntactic attention routing with semantic visual grounding, injecting massive noise into the residual vector $\boldsymbol{v}_t$.

**3. Vulnerabilities in Design and Filtering**
*   **Flawed Moving Average Filter (Eq. 8):** The adaptive syntactic filter relies on a moving average of relative visual magnitudes $\bar{\gamma}_{<t}$. This is a highly brittle, history-dependent metric. If a generated sequence begins with a dense listing of visual entities, $\bar{\gamma}_{<t}$ becomes artificially inflated, permanently suppressing the intervention ($\Phi_t = 0$) for the rest of the sentence and allowing hallucinations to occur. Conversely, a long, purely syntactic prefix will drive $\bar{\gamma}_{<t}$ to near zero, causing catastrophic over-intervention on the first semi-semantic token. 
*   **Logit Space Saturation (Eq. 7):** Adding scaled logits directly to the base unnormalized logits $\boldsymbol{z}_t$ via a static hyperparameter $\alpha$ is dangerously unstable. Because softmax is exponential, uncalibrated linear additions to the logit space frequently result in probability collapse (peaking), leading to severe repetition loops or grammatical destruction. The paper offers no mechanism to normalize or bound the injected bias $\tilde{\boldsymbol{z}}_t$ relative to the original entropy of the distribution.

**Score:** 1.5 / 5.0
