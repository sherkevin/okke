# Bounded Resonance Anchoring: Token-Local Logits Intervention for Multimodal Hallucination Mitigation

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are prone to hallucination when language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for grounded decoding. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalty) are highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers, but they do not directly offer a framework for injecting strictly **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving complex language structure. We present **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**, an inference-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted directly from the terminal logits space. 

BRA operates on three core principles. First, **token-local logits intervention** evaluates candidate-wise support computed from contextualized visual states at the LLM's final layer. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ Resonance** extracts stable local evidence from high-resolution spatial token fields without diluting the signal into a global average or sparse noise. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via a constrained $O(1)$ visual-concept dictionary and dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation inheritance. To address the input-output embedding asymmetry inherent in prefix-conditioned MLLMs, we explicitly separate a zero-shot variant (`BRA_zero`) from a lightweight calibrated bound (`BRA_calib`), enforcing strict fairness by comparing the latter only to similarly fine-tuned baselines.

Avoiding premature state-of-the-art claims, we organize this paper around a falsifiable, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol spanning three evidence chains: hallucination reduction (measured strictly alongside Average Generated Length to prevent truncation gaming), structure and reasoning preservation, and the specific necessity of local evidence over coarse aggregation and random noise. Evaluated across `POPE`, `CHAIR`, `MMBench`, `MMMU`, `FREAK`, and `DocVQA`, this protocol presents a rigorously bounded architectural proposition for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure is especially damaging in dense grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is fundamentally token-local rather than global.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), attention-based pattern penalty (e.g., OPERA), or layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa). These families represent distinct, highly competitive baselines that effectively regularize the generation process in orthogonal ways. However, none of them natively serve as a direct mechanism for the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, defensible positive hypothesis: an inference-time framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than purely language-side heuristics, provided that it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We term this framework **Bounded Resonance Anchoring (BRA)**.

Executing this is nontrivial for three mathematical and structural reasons. First, the **input-output embedding asymmetry**: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, making direct cosine similarity with output vocabularies mathematically invalid unless properly projected. Second, the **post-hoc entanglement risk**: assuming the final-layer hidden states of visual patches retain spatial locality requires overcoming deep unidirectional self-attention mixing, a massive gamble that varies severely across architectures (e.g., LLaVA's 1D sequence vs. Qwen-VL's 2D-RoPE). Third, the **structural collapse risk**: hallucination suppression easily overfires on Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities unless mathematically protected. A naive out-of-context POS tagger will catastrophically collide on ambiguous English tokens (e.g., "watch" as a noun vs. verb).

To address these challenges, BRA centers on three core mechanisms:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention**, mapping final-layer visual states to the terminal vocabulary space to evaluate localized patch support for text candidates.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance**, extracting localized spatial support from bounded candidate subsets, explicitly avoiding the dilution of global pooling or the sparse noise of random patch selection.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, a deterministic, structure-preserving protection layer utilizing an unambiguous, constrained visual-concept lookup and dynamic BPE continuation inheritance.

To ensure rigorous evaluation, we maintain a strict fairness boundary between a zero-shot probe (`BRA_zero`) and a calibrated fallback (`BRA_calib`). We structure our experiments as a stringent protocol testing five lines of defense: verifying spatial locality across specific architectures, proving hallucination reduction against length collapse, confirming structural preservation, demonstrating local-evidence necessity (against both mean-pooling and random-noise), and mapping computational boundaries and spatial washout limits.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Overcoming Post-Hoc Entanglement and Embedding Asymmetry: `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_calib`

A critical vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is the embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input embedding layer. To construct a valid local support metric, BRA evaluates the *contextualized* visual hidden states at the LLM's final transformer layer, $h_L^{(v_j)}$. 

We explicitly acknowledge the massive gamble of **post-hoc entanglement**: because these states have passed through deep self-attention, they implicitly mix with the entire prefix context. We hypothesize that residual streams preserve enough of the original patch's spatial bias to allow local extraction. We mandate proving this empirically across distinct base architectures—specifically contrasting Qwen-VL-Chat (which utilizes 2D-RoPE to preserve spatial grids) against LLaVA-1.5 (where 1D sequence flattening severely risks spatial washout). To handle projection cleanly, we split BRA into two distinct tracks:

1. **`BRA_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support by evaluating what vocabulary each visual patch would independently activate: $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`BRA_calib`**: A calibrated bound utilizing a lightweight projection matrix $\Phi_{calib}$. To avoid test-set leakage, $\Phi_{calib}$ is a single linear layer trained on a small, frozen set of 5,000 COCO image-patch to bounding-box label pairs. The LLM remains entirely frozen; we optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss.

`BRA_zero` serves as the fair comparison against training-free baselines. However, for decoder-only MLLMs, projecting past visual prefixes through the native `lm_head` carries a severe theoretical risk of yielding pure noise. If this zero-shot alignment fails (tested in Defense Line 1), we explicitly pivot to `BRA_calib`. Crucially, to maintain strict baseline fairness, if `BRA_calib` is utilized, we mandate comparing it against baselines (e.g., Base, VCD, OPERA) that have undergone Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT/LoRA) on the identical 5,000 COCO instances.

### 3.2 Bounded Candidate Filtering and Adaptive Top-$k$ Spatial Resonance

At decoding step $t$, BRA strictly bounds the intervention to a safe candidate set to prevent distribution collapse: $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=100$). For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, BRA computes local visual support. 

To prevent small objects from being washed out by background noise (which occurs in global average pooling) while ensuring sufficient coverage for larger entities, we apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)
$$
where $k_{min}$ acts as an absolute floor to capture single-patch objects without diluting the signal, and $\rho$ scales dynamically to accommodate dense inputs. The raw localized support for a candidate $c$ aggregates only its top-$k$ most resonant visual patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c]}{\tau_{sim}}
$$

### 3.3 Relative Penalty and VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)

The localized evidence is normalized into a relative distribution $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}(S_{raw}(c))$. The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens that lack local visual backing relative to their peers. Crucially, this penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$ to protect structural integrity:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

Hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing structural tokens or fragmenting multi-token entities. VASM deterministically gates the intervention strength via a strictly pre-computed dual-tier architecture:
1. **Root Token Expectation (Constrained Visual Dictionary):** A naive out-of-context POS tagger creates severe linguistic collisions in English (e.g., "watch", "train", "light" can be both visual nouns and verbs/adjectives). Instead of risking erratic semantic behavior, VASM employs a static $O(1)$ binary mask $\gamma$ where $\gamma=1$ *only* for a curated, unambiguous dictionary of visual object nouns (derived from COCO/LVIS categories) mapped directly to the vocabulary. All other tokens default to $\gamma=0$. The collision rate of ambiguous tokens is explicitly documented in the Appendix.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance (Dynamic):** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the static dictionary and inherits the exact deterministic $\gamma$ weight of its most recent root token. For SentencePiece (e.g., LLaVA), root tokens are identified by `_`; for byte-level BPE (e.g., Qwen-VL), via `Ġ`. This exact inference-time inheritance completely prevents the mathematical collapse of multi-token entities (e.g., complex OCR strings) during decoding adjustment.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because BRA introduces a specific structural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence, we organize the evaluation plan into three falsifiable evidence chains supported by five lines of defense. The protocol mandates explicit architecture specification, utilizing **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector, 1D sequence) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE) to prove generalization and map architectural limits.

### 4.1 Defense Line 1: Locality Verification and Zero-Shot Semantic Validity

**Hypothesis:** The final-layer visual hidden states $h_L^{(v_j)}$ retain spatial locality despite post-hoc entanglement, and occupy a semantically meaningful subspace.
- **Protocol A (Spatial Locality Check):** We will compute the **Attention-Rollout / Representation Similarity Alignment** between $h_L^{(v_j)}$ and ground-truth spatial bounding boxes on 1,000 COCO images across both target architectures to quantify how much spatial bias survives deep self-attention.
- **Protocol B (Semantic Overlap Check):** We measure the **Token Overlap Rate** (Top-1, Top-5, Top-10) between natively decoded words of $h_L^{(v_j)}$ and ground-truth object labels.
- **Execution Rule (Fairness Fallback):** If the Token Overlap Rate is $<5\%$, it proves that for prefix-conditioned MLLMs, `BRA_zero` is mathematically invalid. In this scenario, the evaluation strictly pivots to `BRA_calib`. All baselines in subsequent tables will be explicitly augmented with PEFT/LoRA fine-tuning on the identical 5,000 COCO instances to guarantee a strictly level playing field.

### 4.2 Defense Line 2 (Chain A): Hallucination Reduction vs. Length Collapse

**Hypothesis:** BRA mitigates object hallucination through localized evidence reweighting without causing pathological output truncation or length collapse.
- **Benchmarks:** `POPE` (Acc, F1, Yes-Ratio), `CHAIR` (CHAIRs, CHAIRi).
- **Baselines:** Base Model, VCD, OPERA, DoLa (all LoRA-adjusted if the fallback is triggered), `BRA_zero` (if viable), `BRA_calib`.
- **Mandatory Metric:** `AGL` (Average Generated Length).
- **Execution Rule (Table 1):** We mandate reporting standard metrics strictly alongside `AGL`. If BRA improves `CHAIR` metrics solely by generating significantly shorter responses (i.e., dodging hallucinations by truncating text, denoted by an AGL drop $> 10\%$), the core mitigation claim is falsified.

### 4.3 Defense Line 3 (Chain B): Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via local evidence does not degrade complex reasoning, provided that VASM's BPE inheritance actively protects structural and functional tokens.
- **Benchmarks:** `MMBench V1.1`, `MME`, `MMMU (Hard Subset)`.
- **Primary Ablation:** Base Model vs. `BRA_full` vs. `BRA_no_VASM`.
- **Execution Rule (Table 2):** `BRA_no_VASM` must demonstrate a catastrophic reasoning drop on `MMMU (Hard)`, while `BRA_full` preserves or improves upon the Base model's performance. This strictly isolates the constrained dictionary and BPE inheritance as indispensable mathematical necessities.

### 4.4 Defense Line 4 (Chain C): The Necessity of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting is significantly more faithful than coarse global visual aggregation, and performance stems from *accurate local evidence*, not just *sparse noise injection*.
- **Benchmarks:** `FREAK` (OCR, Position, Counting), `DocVQA` (evaluated strictly via the ANLS metric).
- **Primary Ablation:** Base vs. `BRA_MeanPool` (replacing Top-$k$ with a global average) vs. `BRA_RandomK` (selecting $k$ random visual patches) vs. `BRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Execution Rule (Table 3):** `BRA_AdaptiveTopK` must strictly outperform both `BRA_MeanPool` and `BRA_RandomK` by statistically significant margins. If Random-K performs equally to Adaptive Top-K, the locality hypothesis is falsified as mere noise injection.
- **Qualitative Audit (Figure 1):** We mandate spatial heatmaps tracking the Top-$k$ visual tokens selected during the generation of specific tokens. Showing the spatial activation over the image when decoding a structural token (e.g., "the") vs. a grounded token (e.g., "mug") visually proves VASM and local resonance.

### 4.5 Defense Line 5: Efficiency Audits and Unrecoverability Limits

To ensure BRA does not introduce prohibitive overhead, the protocol establishes:
- **Scaling Curve (Figure 2):** Generation Latency (Tokens/Sec) plotted against Visual Token Count ($N_v$). The bounded Top-$M$ candidate mask must constrain latency curves to remain competitive with base autoregressive generation.
- **Failure Case Analysis (Appendix E - Out-of-Candidate Misses and Spatial Washout):** Explicit documentation of two failure modes. First, *Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability*, where the ground truth visually grounded token falls entirely outside $\mathcal{C}_t$ (rank $> M$). Second, *Spatial Washout*, where final-layer visual states $h_L^{(v_j)}$ fail to align with the spatial ground truth due to deep attention mixing irrevocably destroying the spatial grid, empirically mapping the absolute boundary limits of post-hoc extraction.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of BRA relies on a clean, verifiable scientific contract: it intervenes at inference time, leverages token-local visual evidence over random noise or global pooling, preserves structural integrity via an unambiguous visual dictionary and BPE inheritance, and establishes transparent performance boundaries. However, execution of this framework carries specific theoretical limitations:

1. **Embedding Alignment and Fairness Loops:** `BRA_zero` tests the absolute limits of the residual stream's ability to overcome post-hoc entanglement. If the native `lm_head` fails to project visual prefixes into meaningful semantic spaces, `BRA_calib` becomes a strict architectural requirement. Utilizing it forces the evaluation to adopt lightly fine-tuned baselines to maintain scientific integrity.
2. **Context-Free Dictionary Collisions:** While VASM abandons naive POS tagging in favor of a constrained visual-concept dictionary, hardcoding static English tokens inherently risks minor collisions on polysemous words. Quantifying this linguistic limitation remains a necessary boundary condition for vocabulary masking.
3. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability and Spatial Washout:** BRA fundamentally operates by reweighting the Top-$M$ language priors. It mathematically cannot generate a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window $\mathcal{C}_t$. Furthermore, in architectures lacking strict 2D positional embeddings (e.g., LLaVA's 1D sequence), deep unidirectional self-attention creates the risk of *Spatial Washout*, where visual tokens become irreparably mixed, preventing localized extraction. 

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines **Bounded Resonance Anchoring**, a positive, falsifiable framework for mitigating multimodal hallucinations through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By explicitly identifying the embedding asymmetry risk, formalizing VASM with tokenizer-aware BPE inheritance based on a constrained visual dictionary, and proposing a testable protocol for post-hoc entanglement across architectures, we establish a rigorous foundation for decode-time control. The claims herein are strictly bounded by the proposed five-line evaluation protocol spanning locality verification, hallucination reduction against length collapse, reasoning preservation, and the empirical necessity of localized visual evidence over mean-pooling and random noise.
