# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: A Geometrically Grounded Visual Existence Checker for Autoregressive Decoding

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) frequently suffer from entity hallucination when strong language priors overpower visual evidence during autoregressive decoding. While zero-shot inference-time mitigations (e.g., DoLa, VCD) elegantly modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding or layer-wise distribution shifts, they lack explicit, supervised spatial-to-lexical mappings to route fine-grained visual evidence. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a late-fusion parametric head that projects post-connector visual features directly into a masked BPE vocabulary space to penalize unsupported entity tokens. 

Recognizing the algorithmic limitations of unconstrained logit manipulation, we explicitly frame TLRA as a **visual entity existence verifier**. It reliably answers "Is this noun anywhere in the image?" establishing a strict theoretical boundary between global entity grounding and targeted relational VQA vulnerabilities. To resolve parameter bloat and semantic distortion, TLRA operates on three structural principles: (1) **Geometric Manifold Preservation**: Instead of a fully fine-tuned projection that suffers from semantic drift, TLRA employs a frozen calibration matrix strictly tied to the LLM's Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$), training only a lightweight Visual Alignment Adapter to solve the unseen-token Sparsity Trap; (2) **Dynamic Activation Pooling** to resolve the spatial dilution inherent in static Top-$k$ patch selection across varying object sizes; and (3) A **Dynamic Syntax Floor equipped with an Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$)**, mathematically guaranteeing that hallucination suppression does not force irreversible grammatical collapse. To resolve BPE fragmentation and the "Greedy Pivot" absurdity, we introduce **Pre-Commitment Nucleus Scanning**, halting hallucinations before subword prefixes are committed. Finally, we explicitly confront the **Negation Trap**—the inherent failure of context-blind visual checkers to permit accurate textual refusals (e.g., "There is no dog")—by implementing a lightweight context kill-switch and pre-registering a rigorous evaluation protocol. We benchmark against prompt-injected object detection (e.g., Grounding DINO) to justify the necessity of late-fusion intervention over pure context-window prompt engineering.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), such as LLaVA-1.5 and Llama-3-Vision variants, exhibit remarkable generative capabilities, yet their autoregressive decoding behavior remains vulnerable to entity hallucination. Current inference-time mitigation strategies are powerful zero-shot methods that modulate general model confidence via contrastive decoding, attention-penalty heuristics, or distribution shifts. However, they generally lack explicit, supervised spatial-to-lexical mapping pathways trained specifically to verify spatial presence at the token level. 

We introduce **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**. Crucially, TLRA is *not* a zero-shot decoding trick; it is formulated as a late-fusion parametric routing module. By training a dedicated mapping from spatial features to lexical logits on a masked-caption subset—with the base LLM completely frozen—TLRA introduces explicit existence-checking supervision.

To maintain intellectual rigor, we explicitly downgrade our scope from general "hallucination suppression" to **Entity Existence Verification**. Algorithmically, TLRA acts as an existence checker for an offline-curated dictionary of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). By pooling patch features, TLRA robustly verifies whether a candidate noun exists *anywhere* in the image. This precise formulation forces us to confront three hard theoretical boundaries:
First, against **Prompt-Injected Object Detection**: if TLRA cannot outperform an off-the-shelf detector (e.g., Grounding DINO) appending a text list of objects to the prompt, its architectural complexity is unjustified. 
Second, TLRA risks **exacerbating relational hallucinations**. If a prompt asks "Is the dog on the sofa?" and the image contains a dog on the floor, verifying "sofa" exists will validate the token, inheriting the baseline LLM's self-attention vulnerabilities.
Third, the **Negation Trap**: A strictly context-blind visual checker will disastrously penalize correct negative constraints. If an image lacks a dog, and the LLM correctly attempts to generate "There is no dog," a context-blind TLRA will suppress "dog" and force a syntactic pivot (e.g., "There is no cat"). 

Furthermore, we confront three existential threats to late-fusion routing: **Geometric Manifold Drift**, **Spatial Dilution**, and **Grammatical Collapse**. 
First, initializing a projection matrix from Layer 0 input embeddings assumes the residual stream preserves the embedding manifold—a false assumption in modern LLMs. While mapping directly to the **Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)** solves this, fully fine-tuning this matrix induces immediate manifold drift away from the LLM's exact logit space. We resolve this by strictly freezing the $W_{out}$ anchor and training a lightweight Visual Alignment Adapter.
Second, static Top-$k$ spatial pooling fails across varying object scales; we replace it with **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**. 
Finally, naive subword logit suppression risks morphological destruction (e.g., penalizing a subword mid-prefix). We introduce **Pre-Commitment Nucleus Scanning** and an **Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$)**, formally conceding a hallucination rather than irreversibly destroying sentence structure when no viable syntactic alternative exists.

Our evaluation is a falsifiable audit organized into **three evidence chains**:
1. **Hallucination Reduction vs. The Negation Trap:** Evaluating entity grounding against zero-shot methods and Prompt-Injected Object Detection, while explicitly auditing True Negative Accuracy to measure how severely the method damages the LLM's refusal capabilities.
2. **Context-Blindness and Targeted VQA:** Mapping the exact failure boundaries of history-independent routing by splitting evaluation into Global Existence (POPE) and Relational Vulnerability (MMHal-Relations).
3. **Structure Preservation & Manifold Drift:** Auditing geometric calibration (Fully Fine-Tuned $W_{out}$ vs. Frozen $W_{out}$ + Adapter), anchored by an untrained `TLRA_zero` spatial heatmap visualization to prove the dynamic pooling extracts localized signal rather than random noise.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect abnormal generation patterns during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and carry different robustness and latency trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Internal Control

High-resolution MLLMs operate over large spatial token fields rather than a single compact visual descriptor. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should explicitly test that locality.

This is not a sweeping claim about all external baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when a task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support should be tested against coarser internal alternatives under matched conditions.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Function words, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role distinct from visually grounded entity tokens. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this via a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Connector Locus and the Manifold Preservation Principle

TLRA operates as a parametric intervention injected at the late-fusion (decode) stage, strictly limited to an offline-curated subset of physical noun stems ($V_{noun} \approx 4,500$). 

**The Connector Locus Requirement:** To successfully map visual features to the LLM's vocabulary space, the spatial features $X_v$ *must* be extracted **post-vision-language connector** (e.g., after the LLaVA MLP projector). Raw vision encoder features (e.g., CLIP) exist in an incompatible dimensional manifold, rendering late-fusion logit projection mathematically incoherent.

**The Sparsity Trap and Manifold Preservation:** To enable zero-shot generalization to unseen tokens, the projection must inherit semantic structure. Modern MLLMs heavily distort the residual stream via deep non-linearities, destroying the Layer 0-to-Layer $N$ manifold alignment. Thus, we utilize the base LLM's pre-trained **Unembedding Matrix ($W_{out}$)** sliced for $V_{noun}$ as our anchor matrix $W_{anchor} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times V_{noun}}$. 
Crucially, if $W_{anchor}$ is updated during training, its weights will immediately drift away from the LLM's exact logit space, breaking the geometric coherence required for late-fusion addition. Therefore, **$W_{anchor}$ is strictly frozen**. We instead train a lightweight, low-rank Visual Alignment Adapter $f_{adapt}(X_v)$ that operates on the spatial features *before* they are projected by $W_{anchor}$.

**`TLRA_zero`:** A strict, untrained baseline computing cosine similarity directly between the frozen post-connector features and $W_{anchor}$ (i.e., $X_v \cdot W_{anchor}$). This isolates the exact spatial localization capacity of the base model before explicit contrastive supervision ($f_{adapt}$) is applied.

### 3.2 Contrastive Calibration and the Semantic Margin

To calibrate $f_{adapt}$ and prevent mode collapse, we employ a **Contrastive Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) Loss** ($\mathcal{L}_{calib}$). For a given image with ground-truth physical nouns $\mathcal{P}$, we dynamically sample hard negative nouns $\mathcal{N}$ from $V_{noun}$ using corpus co-occurrence matrices. 
To prevent shattering the visual manifold by penalizing visually indistinguishable synonyms (e.g., ground-truth "mug" vs. negative "cup"), we enforce a **Semantic Margin $\phi_{sim}$**. A candidate negative $n$ is only accepted if its frozen CLIP text-text cosine similarity to all $p \in \mathcal{P}$ is below $\phi_{sim}$. During calibration, only the parameters of $f_{adapt}$ are updated; the LLM, Vision Encoder, and $W_{anchor}$ remain entirely frozen.

### 3.3 Dynamic Activation Pooling and Spatial Heatmaps

Visual patches are not bag-of-words. Static Top-$k$ pooling dilutes the existence signal of small objects while discarding context for massive objects. We utilize **Dynamic Activation Thresholding**. 
At decoding step $t$, for a candidate noun $c$, we identify the subset of active spatial patches $\mathcal{A}_c = \{j \mid (f_{adapt}(X_{v, j}) \cdot W_{anchor})_c > \theta_{pool}\}$. The raw spatial support is the mean of these dynamically selected active patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{A}_c|} \sum_{j \in \mathcal{A}_c} (f_{adapt}(X_{v, j}) \cdot W_{anchor})_c \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hat S(c) = \text{Clamp}\Big(\sigma\big(S_{raw}(c) \cdot \beta\big), 0, 1\Big)
$$
If $\mathcal{A}_c$ is empty, $S_{raw}(c) = 0$. To validate this mechanism, we mandate the extraction of Spatial Heatmaps prior to textual evaluation to prove that $(f_{adapt}(X_{v, j}) \cdot W_{anchor})_c$ mathematically localizes objects rather than pooling scattered noise.

### 3.4 BPE Lookahead, The Syntax Floor, and The Negation Trap

**Pre-Commitment Nucleus Scanning:** Modern tokenizers shatter words (e.g., `_re`, `friger`, `ator`). Penalizing a subword mid-prefix (the "Greedy Pivot") forces grammatical absurdity. Instead, TLRA employs a predictive lookahead. At step $t$, before sampling, we scan the LLM's top-$K$ logit distribution. If a hallucinated noun's initial subword is highly probable, we apply the penalty $\gamma(c)$ *before* the first subword is committed. If a word is already partially generated, the penalty is aborted to prevent prefix corruption.

**The $\tau$-Abort Syntax Floor:** Let $L_{floor}$ be the highest logit of any non-noun token currently in the top-$K$ candidate window. Forcing the argmax to fall to $L_{floor}$ is fatal if $L_{floor}$ represents a grammatically invalid token (e.g., a comma with logit -15). The final modified logit $L_{final}(c)$ enforces an **Intervention Abort Threshold ($\tau$)**:
$$
L_{final}(c) = 
\begin{cases} 
L_{orig}(c) & \text{if } (L_{orig}(c) - L_{floor}) > \tau \\
\max \Big( L_{floor} - \epsilon, \, L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)) \Big) & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
If the entropy gap exceeds $\tau$, TLRA aborts. Permitting a hallucinated noun is strictly preferable to irreversible structural collapse.

**The Negation Kill-Switch (Solving the Negation Trap):** A strictly context-blind checker mathematically destroys the LLM's ability to output accurate negative constraints (e.g., "No, there is no dog"). To bound this flaw, we introduce a lightweight look-behind heuristic: if an explicit negation token (e.g., "no", "not", "without") appears in the immediate generated context window $Y_{t-\delta:t}$, the TLRA penalty $\gamma(c)$ is forced to 0. This mechanism acknowledges that late-fusion context-blind routing cannot safely operate during explicit textual refusal.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

The evaluation is organized into three evidence chains prioritizing falsifiability. Results below are marked as "To Be Filled" (TBF) representing the pre-registered execution contract. 

**Data Isolation & Baselines:** The 50k training split for $f_{adapt}$ is drawn from Visual Genome. Baselines include Zero-Shot methods (DoLa, VCD), Untrained bounds (`TLRA_zero`), and **Prompt-Injected Object Detection** (Grounding DINO objects appended to the system prompt) to prove the necessity of late-fusion architectural complexity over pure prompt engineering.

### 4.1 Chain A: Global Grounding vs. The Negation Trap & Relational Limits

**Goal:** Determine if TLRA suppresses pure entity hallucinations better than prompt-based injection, map its relational boundaries, and rigorously quantify the damage to negative constraints.
- **Existence vs. Relational:** Evaluated across POPE (Global) and MMHal-Relations. We explicitly test the hypothesis that verifying global object existence exacerbates incorrect spatial relation priors.
- **The Negation Audit:** We compile a specific VQA subset designed to elicit negative answers (e.g., "Is there a car?" -> "No car"). We measure True Negative Accuracy to evaluate if the Negation Kill-Switch successfully prevents the model from hallucinating a pivot (e.g., "Yes, there is a tree").

**Goal:** Quantify the trade-off between hallucination suppression and grammatical collapse.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The viability of Token-Local Resonance Anchoring depends entirely on surviving strict linguistic, structural, and hardware boundaries. By reframing TLRA as a geometrically grounded entity existence verifier, we identify mathematically rigid limitations that future research must confront:

1. **The Negation Trap and Context Fragility:** The introduction of the Negation Kill-Switch is a necessary heuristic, but it is fundamentally a patch over a theoretical flaw. A context-blind existence checker fundamentally conflicts with an autoregressive stream attempting to generate negative constraints. If the lightweight look-behind window fails to detect a complex refusal phrase, TLRA will suppress the correct noun and force a grammatical breakdown.
2. **The Prompt-Injection Threat:** If appending a list of objects detected by Grounding DINO to the system prompt achieves comparable POPE and MMHal scores without inducing the Negation Trap, the architectural complexity of TLRA is functionally invalidated for offline tasks. TLRA's primary defense relies on bypassing attention-dilution limits inherent to massive prompt contexts.
3. **Relational Exacerbation vs. Global Existence:** Because TLRA pools visual patches independently of deep text history, it excels at global entity verification but actively risks *exacerbating* relational hallucinations. Validating an object's existence may inadvertently strengthen an incorrect positional claim made by the base LLM.
4. **The Grammatical Trade-off ($\tau$-Abort):** The $\tau$-Abort threshold formally concedes that late-fusion logit manipulation cannot always gracefully recover from hallucination triggers. If the sequence entropy is too low, the system must allow a hallucination to pass to prevent catastrophic grammatical destruction. 

Future execution of this protocol will definitively confirm whether a geometrically-coherent decode-time routing head—anchored by a frozen Unembedding Matrix and dynamic pooling—can overcome these inherent morphological fragilities, or if the algorithmic limits of late-fusion projection represent an insurmountable theoretical ceiling.
