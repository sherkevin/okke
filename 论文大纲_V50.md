# Dictionary-Gated Token-Local Visual Intervention for Hallucination Reduction in Image-LLMs

## Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to object hallucination when overarching language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense spatial grounding. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., DoLa, VCD, OPERA) successfully regularize global token probabilities or attention distributions; however, they are orthogonal to the explicit routing of spatial evidence. This paper investigates how to inject **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space at decode time without damaging functional language structure. We present a functional **Token-Local Visual Intervention** framework—a dictionary-gated logits adjustment utilizing localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

Strictly scoped to 2D Image-LLMs, this framework addresses three structural barriers. First, acknowledging the near-universal spatial washout inherent to deep self-attention, we utilize a zero-shot diagnostic (`Local_Zero`) that operationally necessitates a shift to a lightweight, dynamically calibrated bound (`Local_Calib`). We formalize `Local_Calib` as a **hybrid test-time adaptation** using an isolated 5k-sample prior. This prior is optimized via spatially-aware hard negative sampling, specifically constrained to preserve compositional coherence (e.g., avoiding the destructive separation of "rider" and "horse"). Second, to prevent decoding latency explosions, we extract localized support via an **Entropy-Scaled Adaptive Top-$k$** mechanism. To combat autoregressive threshold drift, we derive the threshold during the prefill stage and introduce a strict operational fallback state for long-context generation. Third, to protect functional syntax, we implement a **Dictionary-Gated Semantic Masking** module—a dual-tier shield utilizing an ~85k WordNet physical entity dictionary (Tier 1) and deterministic regex triggers for OCR structures (Tier 2).

To rigorously assess algorithmic overhead and genuine spatial utility, we propose a pre-registered evaluation protocol across three definitive evidence chains: (A) Hallucination Reduction, isolating routing efficacy against a strictly parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` control; (B) Structure Preservation, openly profiling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) intervention bypass rates to prevent conflating "preservation" with "inaction"; and (C) Local Evidence Value, ablating masking dynamics on dense tasks (FREAK, DocVQA). Alongside transparent defense lines tracking Inter-Token Latency (ITL) against generation step $t$, this framework establishes a verifiable proposition for spatially-aware, decode-time intervention.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable visual-linguistic capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to establish that these methods serve as highly successful, state-of-the-art regularizers that effectively discipline overarching language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. We tackle an orthogonal problem: spatial evidence routing. Because existing methods operate on global generation dynamics, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, functional hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce object hallucination faithfully, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We functionalize this intervention as **Token-Local Visual Intervention**. In this work, we strictly bound our scope to 2D Image-LLMs, deliberately discarding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims regarding spatial locality.

Executing this intervention requires mathematically overcoming three structural barriers. First, **spatial washout** in embedding spaces. Deep self-attention almost universally diffuses localized patch coordinates by the final layer. We formalize this via a zero-shot diagnostic (`Local_Zero`), which, upon expected failure, triggers a shift to a lightweight, context-preserving test-time calibration (`Local_Calib`). Crucially, this calibration requires spatially-aware contrastive sampling that does not inadvertently destroy naturally co-occurring compositional objects. Second, the computational complexity of **dynamic spatial pooling**. We replace static thresholds with an entropy-scaled dynamic activation derived during prefill, fortified by an explicit fallback mechanism to prevent threshold attrition during long-context generation. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments or OCR-critical entities. We resolve this via a dual-tier semantic mask, openly tracking when the mask actively preserves structure versus when it merely defaults to inaction.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, our experimental design is organized into a pre-registered execution protocol defining three primary evidence chains:
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mechanism value on POPE, CHAIR, and AGL against a strictly parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline, establishing a hard performance $\Delta$ required to prove decode-time routing efficacy.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving masking dynamics via MMBench and MMMU(Hard), openly profiling OOV intervention bypass rates segmented by task domains to isolate true structural protection.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the definitive superiority of localized evidence on dense tasks (FREAK, DocVQA) by strictly ablating Tier 1 vs. Tier 2 masking alongside the `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline, bounded by explicit Inter-Token Latency (ITL) limits.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Embedding Asymmetry, Spatial Washout, and Compositional InfoNCE

To construct a valid local support metric, we evaluate the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states corresponding to the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$. However, deep self-attention demonstrably diffuses spatial coordinates by the final layer. We formalize this via an operational boundary:

1. **`Local_Zero` (The Diagnostic Null-Hypothesis)**: We evaluate zero-shot bounding box retrieval using the model's native `lm_head` as the projection $\Phi_{zero}$. If this fails to achieve an average bounding box $\text{IoU} > 0.15$ on a 500-sample spatial validation set—which empirical precedence suggests is highly likely—spatial fidelity is mathematically declared washed out.
2. **`Local_Calib` (Hybrid Test-Time Adaptation)**: Upon triggering the washout threshold, the framework necessitates a structurally required shift to $\Phi_{calib}$, a 2-layer MLP projection ($\approx 4.2$M parameters, adding negligible FLOPs injected as a parallel stream strictly during candidate evaluation). This is optimized on 5,000 size-stratified Visual Genome pairs strictly disjoint from test sets. The base LLM remains entirely frozen.

**Compositional-Aware Hard-Negative Sampling:** We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. A naive spatial exclusion zone ($\text{IoU} < 0.1$) fails to disentangle overlapping objects. Therefore, valid negative patches ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) include *Hard Negatives* ($\text{IoU} > 0.5$, heavily overlapping with the anchor, differing semantic category). However, to prevent **Compositional Destruction** (e.g., pushing apart a "rider" and a "horse"), we enforce a *Compositional Coherence Margin*: hard negatives are strictly vetoed if the anchor and candidate share a parent-child relationship in the Visual Genome scene graph.

### 3.2 Entropy-Scaled Top-$k$ Pooling & The Attrition Fallback

At decoding step $t$, we apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{active}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{active}\Big)
$$
We derive an **entropy-scaled dynamic threshold strictly during the prefill stage**. We calculate the visual hidden-state entropy $\mathcal{H}_{vis}$ over the spatial grid, normalizing it by an empirically verified scaling factor $f(N_v)$ (defaulting to $\log(N_v)$ for fixed-resolution, but subject to empirical calibration for dynamic-resolution models like LLaVA-AnyRes). The threshold is: $\theta_{active} = \mu_A + \lambda\left(\frac{\mathcal{H}_{vis}}{f(N_v)}\right) \cdot \sigma_A$.

**The Threshold Attrition Fallback Mechanism:** Deriving $\theta_{active}$ at prefill prevents latency spikes but risks mathematical obsolescence during long autoregressive generation (e.g., $t > 100$). We introduce a prescriptive recovery state: we monitor the *Valid Activation Rate* ($\%$ of targeted terms surpassing $\theta_{active}$). If this rate decays below a critical bound $\epsilon$ (e.g., $<5\%$) for 3 consecutive tokens, the framework forces a fallback to an un-gated `Local_MeanPool` over the visual sequence for the remainder of the generation, ensuring the model does not silently collapse into zero-intervention mode.

### 3.3 Dictionary-Gated Semantic Masking

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing, gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$
Note that $\gamma=1$ *activates* the penalty, forcing visual grounding. We utilize a **Dual-Tier Protection Architecture**:
1. **Tier 1 (Root Dictionary):** Offline WordNet *physical entity* subtree ($\approx 85,000$ synsets).
2. **Tier 2 (Deterministic Format Triggers):** For DocVQA and dense reasoning, numerical strings and capitalized entities are assigned $\gamma=1$ via deterministic regex logic, forcing localized evidence checking on dates and numbers.

**Dynamic Continuation & The Broken OCR Concession:** Subword fragments inherit the $\gamma$ weight of their root via greedy string matching and a GPU-side bitmask. However, we explicitly acknowledge a severe limitation: broken OCR strings containing erroneous characters (e.g., "199$") will fail the Tier 2 regex, defaulting to $\gamma=0$. Consequently, the intervention is inherently bypassed precisely when the OCR engine struggles the most. We commit to quantifying these Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) bypass rates explicitly.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because Token-Local Visual Intervention introduces specific structural hypotheses—and acknowledges severe risks of Inter-Token Latency (ITL) bottlenecks, threshold drift, and regex brittleness—we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains. The protocol utilizes **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). All subsequent sections outline our pre-registered, hypothesis-driven execution.

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction and the Falsifiable Boundary

**Hypothesis:** The intervention mitigates object hallucination specifically through localized decode-time routing, not merely through the parameters exposed during test-time calibration.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (F1, FPR), CHAIR (Object), and AGL (Acc).
  - **Planned Output (Table 1):** 
    - *Columns:* Model | POPE (F1) | POPE (FPR) | CHAIR (Obj) | AGL (Acc) | **Intervention ITL Overhead (ms/token)**
    - *Rows:* Base | `Base + 5k LoRA` | DoLa* | VCD* | OPERA* | `Local_MeanPool` | `Local_Calib` (+Masking)
  - **Success Criteria:** We establish a strict falsifiable boundary. If the `Base + 5k LoRA` control alone closes $>90\%$ of the performance gap compared to the full `Local_Calib` intervention, the decode-time routing is rendered practically irrelevant. Furthermore, the ITL overhead must justify the F1 gain; a massive latency penalty for marginal gains will transparently invalidate practical utility.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure Preservation and the Inaction Conflation

**Hypothesis:** The dual-tier semantic mask protects structural tokens, but reliance on explicit dictionaries introduces a vulnerability where Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) terms bypass intervention entirely, conflating "preservation" with mere "inaction."
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Intervention Trigger Rate Profiling (Figure 1):** We will construct a scatterplot with **OOV Rate (%)** (X-axis) vs. **Accuracy Drop relative to Base** (Y-axis). Marker sizes represent Intervention Frequency (%), and colors denote task domains (MMBench, MME, MMMU). 
  - **The "Inaction" Boundary:** We establish a hard visual boundary on this plot at a 10% trigger rate. If the intervention rate on MMMU(Hard) drops below 10%, we will explicitly conclude that the mask is preserving reasoning primarily by disabling the intervention, rather than safely routing spatial features for abstract tokens.

### 4.3 Chain C: Local Evidence Value & Tier Masking Ablation

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting isolates specific evidence that diffuse global pooling natively washes out, provided Tier 2 regex triggers successfully catch OCR targets.
- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (Accuracy/ANLS) and DocVQA (ANLS).
  - **Baseline & Masking Ablation (Table 2):** Isolating `Base`, `Base + 5k LoRA` (to ensure the 5k prior didn't accidentally learn OCR heuristics natively), `Local_MeanPool`, `Local_Calib (Tier 1 Only)`, and `Local_Calib (Tier 1+2)`.
  - **The Strict Trigger Constraint:** Table 2 will include an **Intervention Trigger Rate (%)** column. A >3% gap between Top-$k$ routing and `Local_MeanPool` must exist to validate spatial locality over naive averaging.

### 4.4 Defense Lines: Algorithmic Bottlenecks and Threshold Attrition

**Hypothesis:** Dictionary dot-products dictate TTFT, Top-$k$ sorting bottlenecks ITL dynamically, and prefill-derived thresholds inevitably drift during long generation.
- **Evaluation Protocol (Figures 2, 3, & 4):**
  - **TTFT Ablation (Fig 2):** Ablating $\mathcal{V}_{dict}$ size (10k vs 85k) against TTFT and F1.
  - **Latency vs. Generation Step (Fig 3):** To prove the $M \times N_v$ sorting operation does not geometrically explode, we will plot ITL strictly against **Generation Step $t$** ($t=1$ to $500$) across expanding Candidate Window Sizes $M \in \{10, 50, 100\}$.
  - **Threshold Attrition Tracking (Fig 4):** Plotting Sequence Length ($t=1 \dots 250$) against Valid Activation Rate. The graph will explicitly mark the step $t$ where the fallback mechanism (`Local_MeanPool`) is computationally triggered to rescue the generation.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of Token-Local Visual Intervention lies in its structurally bounded proposition: injecting token-local visual evidence at decode-time while protecting language integrity via dictionary-gated tracking. However, its execution is governed by severe theoretical and operational constraints:

1. **The Falsifiability of Calibration:** Deep self-attention demonstrably washes out localized patch fidelity. The shift to the hybrid `Local_Calib` pipeline explicitly differentiates this from purely zero-shot regularizers (DoLa, VCD). Its validity rests entirely on surpassing the strictly parameterized `Base + 5k LoRA` boundary.
2. **Threshold Attrition & Masking Brittleness:** Deriving $\theta_{active}$ solely during prefill strictly requires an empirical fallback mechanism to survive long-context generation ($t>100$). Furthermore, deterministic regex gating (Tier 2) is highly brittle to broken OCR strings; fragmented tokens default to $\gamma=0$, bypassing visual intervention exactly when the vision encoder is failing the hardest.
3. **Latency Bottlenecks:** Executing spatial pooling and $M \times N_v$ Top-$k$ sorting at every decoding step incurs severe CUDA kernel overheads. While the dictionary dot-product induces a TTFT spike, ITL tracking against generation step $t$ remains the ultimate bottleneck, positioning this framework strictly as a high-precision analytical tool rather than a high-throughput serving API.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines a functionally precise framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through dictionary-gated, token-local logits intervention. By formalizing spatial washout (`Local_Zero` vs `Local_Calib`), integrating compositional-aware hard-negative sampling, engineering an entropy-scaled prefill threshold with an explicit generative fallback, and implementing deterministic semantic masking, we establish a technically rigorous foundation for decode-time visual routing. To prevent conflating inaction with preservation, the claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol—mandating exact Intervention Trigger Rate logging, ITL transparency, and direct baseline isolation (`Base + 5k LoRA`)—establishing a verifiable roadmap for empirical execution.
