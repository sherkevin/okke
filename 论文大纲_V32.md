# Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention: Token-Local Visual Evidence for Structure-Preserving MLLM Decoding

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) remain vulnerable to hallucination when strong language priors overshadow the localized visual evidence required for dense grounding. While existing inference-time mitigation strategies (e.g., contrastive decoding, attention pattern penalties) serve as highly competitive, orthogonal regularizers for the language or attention generation process, they lack a mechanism to inject strictly **token-local visual evidence** directly into the terminal logits space while preserving complex language structure. We present **Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention (TSLI)**, a decode-time method that reweights candidate tokens using localized visual support extracted from contextualized final-layer hidden states. 

TSLI operates on three core mechanisms. First, **token-local logits intervention** maps final-layer visual states into the LLM's latent dimension to evaluate candidate-wise spatial support. Second, **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling** extracts stable local evidence while mathematically preventing background patches from casting forced votes, explicitly avoiding the spatial dilution of global average pooling. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via a WordNet-lemmatized dictionary and dynamic Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) continuation inheritance. To rigorously handle input-output embedding asymmetry and spatial washout, we establish a strict evaluation boundary separating a zero-shot probe (`TSLI_zero`) from a lightweight, strictly linear calibrated bound (`TSLI_calib`). 

To validate TSLI, we propose a pre-registered, hypothesis-driven evaluation protocol restricted to 2D spatial grids, guaranteeing zero data leakage. Evaluated across three definitive evidence chains—(A) Hallucination Reduction against parameter-matched LoRA baselines, (B) Structure Preservation with exact Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) tracking, and (C) Local Evidence Value via geometric attention mapping—alongside rigorous transparent profiling of the inherent $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ latency overhead, this framework establishes a scientifically sound, structurally protected proposition for decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) exhibit remarkable zero-shot capabilities, yet their decoding behavior remains fundamentally susceptible to object hallucination. The model frequently generates text that is fluent and syntactically plausible but weakly supported by the actual image input. This failure mode is exceptionally penalizing in dense spatial grounding scenarios—such as OCR-heavy documents, small-object recognition, and complex spatial reasoning—where the relevant visual evidence is inherently token-local rather than diffuse.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene via noise-contrastive decoding (e.g., VCD), layer-contrast decoding (e.g., DoLa), or attention-based pattern penalties (e.g., OPERA). It is critical to clarify that these methods do not rely on global visual pooling; rather, they serve as state-of-the-art orthogonal regularizers that effectively discipline the language-side or attention-side generation heuristics. However, because they operate on generation dynamics rather than explicit spatial grounding, they are not natively designed to address the following structural question:

**How can we inject strictly token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, multi-token entities, or downstream reasoning ability?**

This paper advances a direct, defensible hypothesis: a framework that scores candidate tokens using strictly localized visual support can reduce hallucination more faithfully than pure language/attention heuristics, provided it incorporates explicit structure-preserving safeguards. We conceptualize this intervention as **Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention (TSLI)**. In this work, we explicitly bound our scope to Image-LLMs and 2D spatial reasoning, deliberately avoiding unsupported generalizations to spatiotemporal video domains to ensure tight, defensible claims.

Executing TSLI requires acknowledging and overcoming three structural barriers. First, **input-output embedding asymmetry**: raw visual tokens reside in the input space, rendering direct dot-product similarity with the terminal output vocabulary matrix invalid. Second, the **post-hoc entanglement gamble**: leveraging the final-layer hidden states of visual patches assumes localized support survives deep attention mixing, which risks spatial washout in 1D-sequence architectures. Third, **structural collapse**: hallucination suppression inherently risks penalizing Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) fragments, functional syntax, or multi-token entities.

TSLI resolves these barriers via three mechanisms:
1. **Token-local logits intervention**, mapping final-layer visual states to the LLM's pre-vocabulary dimension to evaluate localized patch support (`TSLI_zero` vs. `TSLI_calib`).
2. **Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ pooling**, extracting localized spatial support via abstention-aware patch activations. This internal design avoids the spatial dilution inherent in internally constructed global mean-pooling ablations.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)**, a deterministic, tokenizer-agnostic protection layer utilizing dynamic BPE continuation inheritance to shield structural language.

To ensure rigorous scientific boundaries, we deploy our experimental design under a stringent protocol encompassing three primary evidence chains: 
- **Chain A (Hallucination Reduction):** Validating mitigation on POPE/CHAIR alongside Average Generated Length (AGL) variance. Crucially, this compares against a parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline to isolate the true advantage of the decode-time mechanism over pure data exposure.
- **Chain B (Structure and Reasoning Preservation):** Proving the necessity of VASM and BPE Inheritance on MMMU(Hard) while transparently reporting benchmark Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rates.
- **Chain C (Local Evidence Value):** Demonstrating the geometric superiority of localized evidence via 3-way side-by-side attention mapping against natively flawed and internal mean-pooled baselines.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as VCD compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as OPERA detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as DoLa compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they focus on different intervention signals and induce different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large token fields that encode spatial or spatio-temporal layout. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often localized to a small region or a small frame interval. This makes token-local evidence particularly attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. BRA is designed around the claim that decode-time hallucination suppression should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `BRA_zero` vs `BRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded nouns or verbs. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. BRA addresses this through a probabilistic structural mask rather than through entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Overcoming Post-Hoc Entanglement: `TSLI_zero` vs `TSLI_calib`

A fundamental vulnerability in decode-time visual intervention is embedding space asymmetry. The LLM's output vocabulary matrix ($W_{vocab}$) resides in the terminal logit space, while visual tokens enter at the input layer. To construct a valid local support metric, TSLI evaluates the *contextualized* final-layer transformer hidden states of the visual sequence, $h_L^{(v_j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}}$. 

We explicitly acknowledge the gamble of **post-hoc entanglement**: deep self-attention implicitly mixes visual states with prefix context. To handle vocabulary projection cleanly without falsely claiming zero-shot alignment if the architecture (e.g., 1D-sequence models) implicitly washes out spatial fidelity, we define two distinct tracks:

1. **`TSLI_zero`**: A strict zero-shot variant where the projection $\Phi_{zero}$ is the model's native `lm_head`. We compute local support via $logit^{(v_j)} = W_{vocab} \cdot \text{LayerNorm}(h_L^{(v_j)})$.
2. **`TSLI_calib`**: If `TSLI_zero` fails due to spatial washout, we explicitly pivot to a lightweight calibrated decoding method. $\Phi_{calib}: \mathbb{R}^{D_{vision}} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{D_{llm}}$ is strictly defined as a **single linear layer (zero hidden layers)** projecting the visual state to the LLM's final hidden dimension *prior* to $W_{vocab}$. This simple linear mapping prevents $\Phi_{calib}$ from acting as an implicit memory bank. It is trained on a frozen set of 5,000 COCO image-patch to bounding-box label pairs, with its parameter count mathematically bounded to be strictly $\le$ the `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline. **Crucially, the base LLM remains entirely frozen, and these 5,000 images are guaranteed strictly disjoint from all downstream evaluation sets.**

We optimize $\Phi_{calib}$ via a contrastive InfoNCE loss. To resolve multi-token label ambiguity, we define the positive target $W_{vocab}[c^+]$ by extracting the **contextualized hidden state of the final subword** after passing the full ground-truth word $T$ through the frozen LLM. Negative samples ($W_{vocab}[c^-]$) are drawn from intra-image patches with an Intersection over Union (IoU) $< 0.1$ with the target bounding box. We enforce a **semantic negative constraint**: negative patches are explicitly filtered to ensure their ground-truth labels do not share the exact semantic class as the target. 

### 3.2 Threshold-Gated Adaptive Top-$k$ Pooling

At decoding step $t$, TSLI restricts intervention to a bounded candidate set $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$ where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=100$). A naive Softmax over $\mathcal{C}_t$ would force every visual patch to sum to 1.0, artificially inflating the voting power of irrelevant background patches. 

To prevent noise injection, we apply a threshold-gated Sigmoid activation, allowing patches with low semantic overlap to mathematically abstain:
$$
\tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c) = \text{Sigmoid}\left(\frac{logit^{(v_j)}[c] - \theta_{noise}}{\tau_{sim}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}\Big(logit^{(v_j)}[c] > \theta_{noise}\Big)
$$
We actively monitor $\theta_{noise}$ to ensure it is not overfitted to the 5k COCO calibration set. To prevent small objects from being washed out by background noise, we apply an Adaptive Top-$k$ rule: $k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_v \rceil\Big)$. The raw localized support for candidate $c$ aggregates only its top-$k$ most supportive activated patches:
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k} \tilde{P}^{(v_j)}(c)
$$
This is then normalized across the candidate set: $\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}_{c \in \mathcal{C}_t}(S_{raw}(c) / \tau_{norm})$. 

### 3.3 VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)

The final candidate logits are adjusted by penalizing tokens lacking local visual backing. Crucially, this penalty is gated by a deterministic semantic mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0,1\}$:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c))
$$

VASM deterministically prevents structural collapse via a dual-tier architecture:
1. **Root Token Expectation (WordNet-Augmented Visual Nouns):** $\gamma=1$ *only* for an unambiguous dictionary of visual object nouns. Out-of-dictionary terms default to $\gamma=0$, guaranteeing structural safety. By strictly limiting this to *nouns*, VASM consciously cedes attribute and relational hallucination mitigation to the base model's priors.
2. **BPE Continuation Inheritance (Dynamic):** Any subword token marked as a continuation fragment automatically bypasses the dictionary and inherits the exact $\gamma$ weight of its root token. Designed to be robust across tokenizers (e.g., Llama's SentencePiece `_`, Qwen's Tiktoken), this ensures words like "rhinoceros" (`_rhi`, `no`, `cer`, `os`) receive synchronized logit adjustment, mathematically preventing multi-token fragmentation.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Because TSLI introduces a specific structural hypothesis regarding decode-time local evidence, we organize the evaluation plan into predefined falsifiable evidence chains. The protocol mandates explicit architecture specification, utilizing **LLaVA-1.5** (MLP projector, 1D sequence) and **Qwen-VL-Chat** (2D-RoPE). 

### 4.1 Chain A: Hallucination Reduction vs. Length Collapse

**Hypothesis:** TSLI mitigates hallucination through localized evidence reweighting without causing pathological output truncation. These gains are strictly derived from the decode-time intervention mechanism, not mere parameter/data exposure.
- **Zero Data Leakage Guarantee:** The 5,000 COCO images used to train $\Phi_{calib}$ are strictly verified via MD5 hashes to have zero intersection with POPE, CHAIR, and downstream sets.
- **Planned Execution (Table 1):**
  - **Metrics:** POPE (Acc/F1) | CHAIRi | CHAIRs | **AGL (± std)**
  - **Zero-Shot Baselines:** Base, VCD, OPERA, DoLa, `TSLI_zero`. Even if `TSLI_zero` fails completely on 1D-sequence models, it will be reported as a scientifically valuable negative result highlighting spatial washout.
  - **Calibrated Baselines:** `Base + 5k LoRA` (fine-tuned on the exact same 5k pairs, explicitly targeting the LLM's Q/V projections, trained to convergence), `TSLI_calib`.
- **Success Criteria:** `TSLI_calib` must outperform both the zero-shot heuristics and the parameter-matched `Base + 5k LoRA` baseline. Average Generated Length (AGL) must remain stable, and a manual inspection subset will verify the model does not collapse into single-word ("Yes/No") degenerate loops.

### 4.2 Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation

**Hypothesis:** Hallucination mitigation via visual suppression does not degrade complex reasoning, provided VASM actively protects structural tokens and BPE Inheritance protects multi-token entities.
- **Planned Execution (Table 2):**
  - **Benchmarks:** MMBench V1.1 | MME | MMMU (Hard Subset) | **Average OOV Rate**
  - **Ablations:** Base | `TSLI_no_VASM` (applies penalty to all tokens) | `TSLI_no_BPE_Inherit` | `TSLI_full`
- **OOV Tracking Requirement:** We will explicitly report the Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) rate—the exact percentage of benchmark ground-truth targets where VASM defaulted to $\gamma=0$ due to dictionary exhaustion.
- **Success Criteria:** Unconstrained suppression (`TSLI_no_VASM`) must severely degrade MMMU(Hard) reasoning. `TSLI_full` must match or closely preserve Base model performance. If `TSLI_full` drops scores by $>3\%$, the failure mode will be explicitly analyzed.

### 4.3 Chain C: The Necessity of Local Evidence

**Hypothesis:** On dense spatial tasks, token-local visual reweighting geometrically isolates evidence that global pooling natively washes out.
- **Planned Execution (Table 3):**
  - **Benchmarks:** FREAK (OCR/Position) | DocVQA (ANLS)
  - **Ablations:** Base | `TSLI_MeanPool` | `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK`
- **Execution Rule:** `TSLI_MeanPool` serves as our internal ablation representing global visual pooling. It *must* use the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$ and VASM mask as `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK`. 
- **Qualitative Protocol (Figure 1 - 3-Way Heatmaps):** We mandate a 3-way side-by-side DocVQA failure analysis plotting: (A) The base model's *native cross/self-attention map* for the target token, (B) The `TSLI_MeanPool` 2D grid (diffuse), and (C) The `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK` 2D grid (sharp localization). 
- **Crucial Limitation Analysis:** We mandate the inclusion of **one explicit failure case** where `TSLI_AdaptiveTopK` locks onto the *wrong* local patch (e.g., a semantic distractor object of the same class) forcing a hallucination. This proves the mechanistic limits of the InfoNCE spatial projection.

### 4.4 Throughput Profiling and Latency Penalties

**Hypothesis:** Decode-time intervention imposes measurable latency that must be transparently profiled for system viability.
- **Planned Execution:** Operating at *every single decoding step* to calculate spatial Top-$k$ pooling, apply threshold gates, and perform dynamic BPE dictionary lookups introduces an inherent $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ overhead. We will benchmark and report exact **A100 Tokens/Sec** throughput. If TSLI reduces generation speed by $>50\%$, it will be explicitly defined as a high-precision, non-real-time analytical decoding strategy.

### 4.5 Boundary Profiling: Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability

- **Out-of-Candidate Limit (Figure 2):** We will explicitly document the absolute structural ceiling of TSLI. We mandate a qualitative visual example showing an "Out-of-Candidate" failure: visualizing the logits rank distribution where the correct visual token ranks at position $>M$ (e.g., rank 150 outside an $M=100$ window). Because TSLI mathematically reweights the initial distribution, it is fundamentally helpless to rescue tokens the base model completely suppresses.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strength of TSLI lies in its falsifiable, structurally bounded framework: intervening at decode-time using strictly token-local visual evidence, and mathematically protecting language integrity via VASM. However, its execution carries severe theoretical and operational constraints:

1. **The Post-Hoc Entanglement Gamble:** The core assumption that localized visual support survives deep self-attention in the final layers of an LLM is precarious. The text prefix heavily contaminates visual hidden states. If `TSLI_zero` completely fails and `TSLI_calib` yields only marginal gains over LoRA baselines, the premise of extracting clean "local states" from final layers may be fundamentally capped by architecture.
2. **Severe Latency Bottlenecks:** The dynamic BPE mask lookups, thresholding, and Top-$k$ spatial pooling introduce an $O(L \times |V| \times N_v)$ overhead per step. If throughput drops by over 50%, TSLI serves strictly as an offline analytical decoding tool rather than a real-time production inference strategy.
3. **VASM’s Noun-Centric Blindspot:** By restricting $\gamma=1$ exclusively to nouns to guarantee structural safety, VASM renders the intervention entirely blind to attribute hallucinations (e.g., "red" vs. "blue") and relational errors. This establishes a severe capability ceiling.
4. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** TSLI mathematically reweights the LLM's initial candidate distribution (Top-$M$). It cannot synthesize a visually grounded token if the base model completely suppresses it from the initial candidate window (e.g., rank $>M$), establishing a hard theoretical ceiling on all post-hoc logits adjustment strategies.

## 6. Conclusion and Future Execution

This paper outlines Thresholded Spatial Logits Intervention (TSLI), a rigorously bounded framework for mitigating MLLM hallucination through structure-preserving, token-local logits intervention. By mapping embedding asymmetry risks, utilizing threshold-gated Top-$k$ pooling to avoid background dilution, and engineering robust language protection via VASM BPE inheritance, we establish a technically precise foundation for decode-time visual control. The claims herein are strictly governed by our pre-registered evaluation protocol spanning Hallucination, Structure, and Local Evidence chains, awaiting full empirical execution.
