# Token-Local Resonance Anchoring: Structure-Preserving Decode-Time Intervention for Multimodal Hallucinations

## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) often hallucinate when strong language priors overpower the localized visual evidence required for the next decoding step. Existing inference-time mitigation strategies, such as contrastive decoding (VCD) and layer-contrast methods (DoLa), are highly competitive baselines, but they do not provide a direct framework for injecting **token-local visual evidence** into decode-time logits adjustment while preserving language structure. We present **Token-Local Resonance Anchoring (TLRA)**, a decode-time intervention framework that reweights candidate tokens using localized support extracted from contextualized visual states.

TLRA is built around three mathematical commitments. First, **token-local evidence reweighting** replaces coarse correction signals with candidate-wise support computed from localized visual patches. Second, **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Prompt-Conditioned Pruning** stabilizes local evidence extraction under a strictly bounded candidate window. To prevent evidence loss during open-ended generation tasks, pruning incorporates an attention-entropy-driven fallback mechanism—robust to architectural attention sinks—that retains full visual contexts when prefill attention is highly dispersed. Third, **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** protects functional syntax and multi-token entities via a deterministic, WordNet-backed whitelist. Because modern BPE tokenizers fragment words, VASM intervenes on the initial token of an entity, explicitly relying on the base LLM's autoregressive momentum to complete subword suffixes safely, while introducing string-length constraints to mitigate false-prefix collisions.

Because strict zero-shot intervention often collapses due to visual-lexical embedding asymmetry, TLRA utilizes a highly lightweight calibrator (`TLRA_calib`) trained solely on generic image-text pairs. Unlike purely unsupervised methods, TLRA explicitly introduces generic external data, but operates as a strictly **base-model-frozen plug-in**, leaving the underlying MLLM entirely unmodified. We organize the paper around a strict experimental contract: three evidence chains covering hallucination reduction (controlling for length collapse), structure preservation (validating VASM against perplexity spikes and utilizing document-understanding as a negative control), and isolating the distinct value of local adaptive selection against global pooling. The resulting evaluation protocol offers a positive, falsifiable, and rigorously bounded proposal for structure-preserving decode-time visual control.

## 1. Introduction

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly capable, but their decoding behavior remains vulnerable to hallucination: the model may generate text that is fluent and plausible yet weakly supported by the actual visual input. This failure is especially damaging in fine-grained visual grounding scenarios such as small-object recognition, spatial positioning, and counting, where the required evidence is distinctly local.

Current inference-time mitigation strategies typically intervene in one of three ways. Contrastive decoding methods such as `VCD` compare different input conditions to reshape the next-token distribution. Attention-based control methods such as `OPERA` monitor abnormal generation patterns and penalize them during decoding. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` exploit differences between shallow and deep model distributions. These are all legitimate, highly competitive baselines, but none of them directly answer the following positive hypothesis:

**How can we inject token-local visual evidence into decode-time logits adjustment without damaging language structure, subword morphology, or downstream reasoning ability?**

Executing this idea is nontrivial due to four foundational challenges. First, **embedding asymmetry**: visual states and output logits do not naturally live in the same space, making strict zero-shot lookup noisy. Second, **candidate limitation**: post-hoc logits intervention can only reweight candidates that the base model already keeps alive. Third, **structural collapse risk**: naive suppression of visually unsupported tokens easily damages function words and BPE-fragmented subwords. Fourth, **system cost at high resolutions**: computing patch-level support at every generation step scales poorly when modern vision encoders use dynamic patching ($N_v > 4096$).

TLRA therefore centers on three design commitments:
1. **Decode-time token-local logits intervention via a Base-Model-Frozen Plug-in.** We utilize a lightweight calibrator to bridge embedding asymmetry. We explicitly clarify our taxonomy: unlike purely training-free inference methods (e.g., DoLa), TLRA introduces generic external distribution data. However, it leaves the base MLLM 100% frozen. We mathematically isolate the value of localized decoding from this external data using strict parity ablations.
2. **Adaptive Top-$k$ resonance with Prompt-Conditioned Pruning** as the local evidence extractor. By filtering patches based on prefill-stage prompt attention and evaluating only the Top-$M$ logits, TLRA bounds decode-time overhead. We explicitly introduce a dynamic fallback mechanism based on attention entropy—carefully avoiding common architectural attention sinks—to disable pruning during generic, open-ended prompts, preventing the premature discarding of latent evidence.
3. **VASM (Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking)** as a deterministic structure-preserving layer. Built on a strict WordNet lexical whitelist, VASM defaults to bypassing intervention for structural and abstract tokens. We explicitly formalize two operational concessions here: the *OCR paradox* (VASM natively ignores arbitrary OCR strings to preserve general syntax) and *BPE Momentum Delegation* (VASM triggers on the first subword of a physical entity, relying on the LLM's autoregressive momentum to finish the word, while implementing bounds against false-prefix collisions).

To maintain scientific rigor, this paper avoids exploratory "fishing expeditions" and commits to a definitive evaluation protocol. We establish three distinct chains of evidence: hallucination reduction (controlled for length collapse), structural preservation (proving VASM's necessity via perplexity and using OCR tasks as negative controls), and local-evidence validation (isolating the value of Top-$k$ anchoring against global pooling).

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Decode-Time Hallucination Mitigation

Inference-time mitigation for MLLM hallucination has developed along several lines. Contrastive methods such as `VCD` compare logits produced under different visual conditions. Attention-based methods such as `OPERA` detect and penalize pathological generation behavior during the autoregressive process. Layer-contrast methods such as `DoLa` compare distributions from different network depths. These methods demonstrate that hallucination can be mitigated at inference time, but they rely on different intervention signals and involve different latency, robustness, and implementation trade-offs.

### 2.2 Local Evidence vs. Coarse Control Signals

High-resolution MLLMs no longer operate over a single compact visual descriptor. They operate over large spatial token fields, and in video settings they further inherit spatio-temporal structure. For dense grounding tasks, the useful evidence for a word is often concentrated in a small region of the image or a short temporal window. This makes token-local evidence attractive: if the next token is visually supported, the support is often sparse, local, and candidate-dependent. TLRA is built around the claim that decode-time hallucination mitigation should take that locality seriously.

This is not a sweeping claim about all existing baselines. It is a narrower methodological claim: when the task depends on local visual evidence, a framework that explicitly reweights candidate logits using localized support is worth testing against coarser internal alternatives. That claim is experimentally verifiable through internal ablations such as `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool`.

### 2.3 Structure Preservation in Decoding

A recurring difficulty in token-level intervention is that not all tokens should be treated equally. Functional syntax, discourse markers, and continuation fragments play a structural role that differs from semantically grounded object words or OCR-bearing entities. A practical decode-time method must therefore distinguish hallucination suppression from indiscriminate token suppression. TLRA addresses this through a deterministic structural mask rather than entropy-only heuristics.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Resolving Embedding Asymmetry: The Calibration Protocol

The first principle of TLRA is fairness transparency. In our preliminary viability probes, a strict zero-shot readout (`TLRA_zero`) mapping contextualized visual states directly to lexical logits yielded near-random overlap. The embedding asymmetry is too large. 

Therefore, the definitive intervention relies on **`TLRA_calib`**. To maintain a fair comparison against inference baselines, the calibration protocol is strictly constrained:
1. **Plug-in Location & Low Cost:** We train a single linear projection matrix, $\Phi_{calib}$, mapping the MLLM's visual tokens—*after* they have passed the native vision-language connector—directly into the LM head's pre-softmax vocabulary space. This projector is highly lightweight (typically $\approx 10-20$M parameters) and trains in under two hours on a single GPU.
2. **Generic Data via Dense InfoNCE:** $\Phi_{calib}$ is trained solely on a strictly limited generic dataset (e.g., a 100K subset of CC3M). To prevent bag-of-words collapse, we extract spaCy noun phrases, align them to pseudo-bounding boxes via a frozen GroundingDINO matcher, and train $\Phi_{calib}$ using dense token-to-patch InfoNCE. It is *never* trained on downstream task data.
3. **Frozen Base Model:** The vision encoder, vision-language connector, and the LLM remain 100% frozen. 

### 3.2 High-Resolution Efficiency: Prompt-Conditioned Pruning and Fallback

At decoding step $t$, TLRA must avoid evaluating the entire vocabulary against all visual patches, especially when visual tokens $N_v$ exceed 4096. We enforce efficiency via two mechanisms:

**1. Candidate Bounding:** We restrict intervention strictly to $\mathcal{C}_t = \text{Top-}M(L_{orig})$, where $M \ll |V|$ (e.g., $M=10$).

**2. Prompt-Conditioned Pre-decoding Pruning with Dynamic Fallback:** Unconditional ViT attention is notoriously noisy. We compute the aggregated cross-attention from the *text prompt tokens* to the visual sequence during the initial prefill stage, pruning visual patches that receive negligible attention to retain an active subset $N_{active}$. 

Crucially, in modern MLLMs, attention often collapses into architectural "attention sinks" (e.g., the `<image>` token, or newline characters) rather than semantic regions. We explicitly mask out known sinks before computing the prefill attention distribution. Furthermore, highly generic prompts ("Describe this image in detail") yield uniformly dispersed attention. To prevent catastrophic loss of evidence, TLRA enforces an **Entropy-Driven Dynamic Fallback**: instead of arbitrary length heuristics, we measure the normalized Shannon entropy $H_{attn}$ of the sink-excluded attention weights. If $H_{attn} > \theta_{fallback}$, pruning is bypassed entirely ($N_{active} = N_v$), preserving all patches for decoding.

For each candidate $c \in \mathcal{C}_t$, TLRA computes candidate-wise local support using an adaptive Top-$k$ rule over the active patches:
$$
k = \max\Big(k_{min}, \lceil \rho \cdot N_{active} \rceil\Big),
$$
$$
S_{raw}(c) = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{j \in \text{Top-}k}\frac{score(v_j, c)}{\tau_{sim}}.
$$
Calculating this exclusively for $M$ candidates strictly bounds the overhead to $O(M \times N_{active})$.

### 3.3 Relative Resonance Penalty

To convert raw local support into a stable logits intervention, TLRA computes a relative evidence score:
$$
\hat S(c) = \text{Softmax}\big(S_{raw}(c) \mid c \in \mathcal{C}_t\big).
$$
The final candidate logits are adjusted as:
$$
L_{final}(c) = L_{orig}(c) - \alpha \cdot \gamma(c) \cdot (1 - \hat S(c)).
$$

### 3.4 Structure Preservation via Deterministic VASM

A naive token-level intervention inevitably suppresses functional syntax, as isolated subwords lack visual grounding. TLRA introduces **Vocabulary-Anchored Semantic Masking (VASM)** to deterministically protect these tokens via a binary mask $\gamma(c) \in \{0, 1\}$.

VASM is algorithmically defined using a **strict WordNet fail-safe**:
1. **Deterministic Whitelist Generation:** A token is assigned $\gamma=1$ *if and only if* its string representation maps to a lemma subsumed under the structural synsets `physical_entity.n.01` or `color.n.01`.
2. **Polysemy Delegation:** If a token matches *any* physical sense in WordNet, it is flagged as $\gamma=1$. We rely on the base LLM's dominant original logits ($L_{orig}$) to naturally suppress grammatically incorrect verb usages (e.g., "table the discussion"), effectively delegating Word Sense Disambiguation back to the LLM. 
3. **BPE Momentum Delegation & Collision Avoidance:** Modern BPE tokenizers fragment words (e.g., "strawberry" $\rightarrow$ "straw", "berry"). VASM typically triggers $\gamma=1$ only on the valid initial prefix ("straw"). Once the initial subword is generated, VASM relies entirely on the base LLM's unmodified autoregressive momentum to complete the suffix tokens ($\gamma=0$). However, this introduces the risk of **false-prefix collisions** (e.g., the abstract word "bulletin" split as `['bullet', 'in']`, where "bullet" falsely triggers intervention). To mitigate this, VASM imposes a minimum string length filter ($L_{char} \ge 4$) for subword triggers and checks against a blacklist of high-frequency false-prefix fragments.
4. **The OCR Concession:** Because VASM explicitly defaults to $\gamma=0$ for out-of-vocabulary text to guarantee grammatical safety, it inherently bypasses arbitrary OCR strings. Preserving LLM fluency takes absolute precedence over hallucination intervention on out-of-dictionary text.

## 4. Evaluation Protocol and Experimental Design

Rather than presenting cherry-picked subsets, our experimental validation is governed by a definitive execution blueprint. The evaluation is structured into three interlocking evidence chains, followed by efficiency and sensitivity audits. All planned results represent the execution of this pre-registered protocol.

### 4.1 Evidence Chain A: Hallucination Reduction (The Length-Collapse Check)

**Goal:** Prove that TLRA reduces object hallucination, that the improvement is not a deceptive artifact of the model generating shorter responses, and that the performance gain stems from local extraction rather than just the generic external data in the calibrator.
- **Table 1 Target:** `POPE` (Accuracy / F1) and `CHAIR` ($CHAIR_s$, $CHAIR_i$).
- **Baselines:** Base MLLM, `DoLa`, `VCD`, `OPERA`, and **`Base + TLRA_MeanPool`**.
- **Mandatory Control Metric:** Average Generated Length (`AGL`) and **AGL Standard Deviation**.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will yield lower `CHAIR` scores and higher `POPE` F1 than all baselines, including `TLRA_MeanPool`. Outperforming `TLRA_MeanPool` mathematically isolates the benefit of decode-time token-local adaptive selection from the mere inclusion of the CC3M-trained $\Phi_{calib}$. Any configuration achieving SOTA CHAIR scores purely by collapsing AGL (variance > 5% from Base) will be flagged as an invalid structural collapse.

### 4.2 Evidence Chain B: Structure and Reasoning Preservation (The Negative Control)

**Goal:** Verify that local-evidence intervention does not irreparably degrade general multimodal cognition, prove that VASM effectively protects subword grammar, and validate the "OCR Concession" via a negative control.
- **Table 2 Target:** `MMBench V1.1` (Overall), `MMMU` (Hard), **Language Perplexity (PPL)** measured on WikiText, and `DocVQA`.
- **Ablation Setup:** Base model vs. `TLRA_no_VASM` vs. `TLRA_full`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_no_VASM` will exhibit severe perplexity spikes (indicating grammar destruction) and crash on complex reasoning tasks (MMMU). `TLRA_full` must recover these scores to baseline levels, ensuring PPL remains within 5% of the Base model. Furthermore, `DocVQA` serves as an explicit **negative control**: because VASM masks out OCR, `TLRA_full` should demonstrate $0\%$ improvement on `DocVQA` but, crucially, avoid degrading the Base model's native reading capabilities.

### 4.3 Evidence Chain C: The Value of Local Evidence

**Goal:** Validate the core thesis that *token-local adaptive selection* is superior to coarse visual aggregation, specifically for spatial and existence grounding. 
- **Table 3 Target:** `FREAK` (broken down into **Spatial Relations** vs. **Object Existence**) and `Object HalBench`. 
- **Ablation Setup:** Base vs. `TLRA_zero` vs. `TLRA_MeanPool` ($k = N_{active}$) vs. `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`. 
- **Critical Parity Constraint:** To ensure valid ablation, `TLRA_MeanPool` utilizes the exact same $\Phi_{calib}$ weights as `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK`.
- **Hypothesis:** `TLRA_zero` will perform poorly, justifying the necessity of $\Phi_{calib}$. For fine-grained spatial relations, `TLRA_AdaptiveTopK` will demonstrate a statistically significant margin over global aggregation (`TLRA_MeanPool`), proving that bounding the local match via $M \times N_{active}$ is mathematically necessary.

To map the operational boundaries of TLRA, the execution plan mandates the following diagnostic plots:

- **Figure 1 (Efficiency Scaling):** A line chart plotting `Tokens/Second` against Visual Token Volume ($N_v$ scaled up to 4096). We hypothesize TLRA's attention-entropy pruning will cap inference drops to $<50\%$ of base speed.
- **Figure 2 (Hyperparameter Sensitivity):** A grid showing `POPE F1` and `Perplexity` across varying $M$ (candidate window), $\alpha$ (intervention strength), and the **Entropy Threshold ($\theta_{fallback}$)**.
- **Figure 3 (Qualitative Failure Analysis):** We mandate heatmaps explicitly showcasing structural blind spots:
  1. **Out-of-Candidate Unrecoverability:** TLRA fails because the correct token was ranked $> M$ by the base model.
  2. **The False-Prefix BPE Collision:** A heatmap demonstrating the "bulletin" problem, where VASM mistakenly triggers on the subword `bullet`, illustrating the boundary of the string-length filter.
  3. **BPE Stuttering:** An instance where VASM correctly triggers on the first subword but turns off on the second, causing a stutter if the base LLM's autoregressive momentum is weak.

## 5. Discussion and Limitations

The strongest version of TLRA is achieved by tightly bounding its claims to its empirical execution. The core scientific contribution is a positive, decode-time framework that leverages token-local visual evidence while deploying VASM to prevent structural collapse. However, several hard limitations define the boundaries of this method:

1. **The "Training-Free" Distinction and Calibrator Asymmetry:** While TLRA operates as a base-model-frozen plug-in during inference, the $\Phi_{calib}$ projector is trained on generic external data. This creates a baseline asymmetry compared to purely unsupervised, zero-shot methods like DoLa. We rely entirely on the `TLRA_MeanPool` ablation to strictly isolate the decode-time local resonance mechanism from the dataset contribution.
2. **Attention Sinks in Pre-Decoding Pruning:** Relying on prefill cross-attention for visual pruning is vulnerable to architectural attention sinks (e.g., the `<image>` token). While our entropy-driven fallback mitigates this, highly miscalibrated base models may still trigger inappropriate pruning, forcing TLRA to default to unpruned, slower decoding.
3. **The False-Prefix BPE Collision:** VASM evaluates the string representation of subword tokens. This creates an unavoidable edge case where an abstract word (e.g., "bulletin") is tokenized such that its prefix forms a physical noun ("bullet"). While our length heuristics ($L_{char} \ge 4$) mitigate this, pathological tokenization can still trigger unwarranted visual intervention. TLRA fundamentally relies on the base LLM's unmodified autoregressive momentum to correct and complete BPE fragments.
4. **The OCR / OOV Paradox:** To guarantee the safety of functional syntax, VASM explicitly sacrifices intervention on out-of-vocabulary strings. TLRA is inherently ineffective for arbitrary OCR transcription, prioritizing absolute grammatical safety over in-the-wild text intervention, as verified by our DocVQA negative control.
